# Chapter 6 – The Summoned Evil Spirit

“VoooOOOo… oooo…!”

The evil spirit slammed its thick arms into the wall and landed on the
ground as it let out an otherworldly groan from the mouth on its torso.
A dull *thud* shook the cave violently, causing Shatia to instinctively
spread her arms to maintain her balance.

“Good grief, this has turned into quite a mess…” Shatia griped as she
used flight magic to dodge the evil spirit lunging from the ground.
Missing its target, the creature barreled toward the cave entrance,
blowing the nearby Skeleton Lord into fragments of bone in the process.

Though it had made solid contact, the evil spirit let out a displeased
cry upon realizing it had struck the wrong target. Watching from
mid-air, Shatia began a calm analysis.

“A massive amount of the mountain’s essence was collected for the
ritual, after all… It seems a fairly powerful one has been summoned.
This will take a bit of effort.”

The strength of an evil spirit summoned by a failed resurrection ritual
varies depending on the amount of mana used. Since the old man had
poured in a vast quantity in order to revive Shatifahl, a formidable
spirit had emerged—one possessing power roughly equal to Shatia’s own.
Shatia wiped a bead of sweat from her forehead with her arm.

“If I do not return soon, Mother will become suspicious. I shall deal
with you in earnest now.”

Realizing the hour was becoming truly problematic, Shatia decided to
fight with everything she had so as to return to the village quickly.
That said, she still had to restrain herself enough to keep the cave
from collapsing, yet she sensed that she could not afford to hold back
much against this particular foe.

Spreading both arms, she manifested spears of fire and hurled them with
a throwing motion. The high-speed projectiles pierced the evil spirit’s
body, but they seemed to deal no damage; instead, the fire spears were
slowly swallowed into the creature’s murky black mass.

“Gruuuuuuooooo!!”

“…Surprising. You even have the ability to absorb magic.”

Rather than taking damage, the amount of mana possessed by the spirit
even increased. Shatia quickly discerned its true nature. Whether this
specific individual possessed such a trait or if it was common to all
evil spirits was unknown, but it was clear that the enemy before her was
exceptionally troublesome.

As Shatia ruffled her hair in annoyance, the evil spirit made its move.
Kicking off the ground, it charged with even greater speed than before.
Shatia evaded it easily with her flight magic, retreating higher toward
the ceiling.

It appeared to lack the capacity to launch long-range magic attacks,
relying entirely on physical strikes. *In that case, I can still
manage,* Shatia calculated, aiming her palm at the spirit and swinging
her arm down.

“Hmph!”

“Guoooo…!!”

She unleashed the same gravity magic she had used on the Sorcerer
Skeleton. The spirit’s massive body was slammed into the floor, causing
cracks to spiderweb across the stone. Supporting her arm with her other
hand, Shatia continued to increase the gravitational load.

“Gugogogogogo…!!!”

Gravity magic was a magic originally meant to restrain an opponent’s
movement. However, because Shatia possessed such immense mana, she could
exert enough force to crush a Sorcerer Skeleton. But despite the
intensity of the spell, the evil spirit showed no sign of being crushed;
on the contrary, it began to step forward, slowly regaining its
mobility.

“…You can move? Under this gravity field…!”

“GOAAAAAAAAAAAAAA!!!”

Finally, with a piercing roar, the evil spirit forced its body up and
repelled the gravity magic. Caught in the backlash of the spell, Shatia
was blown back, spinning through the air.

After stabilizing her posture, Shatia stared at the spirit with eyes
filled with disbelief. Not only had it endured her specialty, gravity
magic, but it had possessed enough power to cancel it. This was a first
for her.

“…Haha! You are quite something!!”

Shatia couldn’t help but smile. Until now, not a single soul had been
able to endure her gravity magic. It was the source of her absolute
confidence. And yet, even as it was broken… she laughed. Because it was
through being surpassed that she could truly feel that the evolution of
magic had no end. For her, there was no greater joy.

“Then try this!!”

Shatia immediately swung her arms, levitating the nearby pillars and the
coffin. She intended to hurl them at the spirit. If magic wouldn’t work,
she would see how it fared against physical objects. However, the evil
spirit pulverized the incoming pillars with its fists and batted the
coffin away with a swing of its arm, completely unfazed.

“GUOAAAAAAAAAAAAAA!!”

“…Ugh!!”

As if declaring it was now its turn, the evil spirit leapt into the air
toward Shatia. It was faster than before, and this time, Shatia couldn’t
dodge perfectly. The sleeve of her dress was torn, causing part of her
illusion magic to flicker. In other words, even once the illusion was
dropped, her actual clothes would be torn in the same way. Shatia’s
expression darkened.

“Kuh… Mother will surely be furious if I show myself in these tattered
clothes.”

If it were just the shoulder, she might have been able to sew it back
together, but with a tear this severe, restoration would be impossible.
While thinking of a clever excuse to use once she returned to the
village, she turned back to face the spirit.

Its speed was clearly increasing. It was becoming accustomed to her
movements. A truly terrifying existence. If she didn’t settle this
quickly, things would become even more dire. Shatia turned both palms
toward the evil spirit.

“A gift in return. I shall let you taste my finest work.”

Even if it could absorb magic, surely there was a limit. Furthermore,
she could still land a direct hit. Gathering every ounce of her mana
into her palms, Shatia unleashed it all at once. A chaotic mass of
energy comparable to a typhoon erupted, carving through the cave as it
struck the spirit head-on.

“GUGOGAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA!!?”

A violent sound, like a thunderclap, resounded throughout the area,
followed by the wailing of the evil spirit. The creature was pinned
against the wall, smashing through the rock until it was buried deep
within. Once the wall collapsed and the spirit was buried under the
rubble, Shatia let out a small sigh and descended to the ground.

The old man, who had been hiding nearby, crawled out and patted his
chest in relief upon seeing the spirit buried. “W-What kind of monster…
And you, were you a mage!?”

“…After a fashion. Also, I should warn you—you had best stay hidden,”
Shatia cautioned the old man with a grim expression.

“What…?” Doubting her, he looked toward the pile of rocks. The rubble
shifted slightly. Then, a section was blown apart as the spirit’s arm
burst through. Pushing aside the stones, the evil spirit emerged, now
twice as large as before.

“You’ve grown even larger… Did you consume it? My mana?”

“GUOOOOOOOOOOOOO!!!”

Despite taking such a powerful blast, it was unscathed, and had even
further powered up by absorbing the energy. It was a truly dreadful
result. Shatia lost her composure slightly; even she hadn’t expected the
attack to be absorbed to that extent. Concentrating her focus on the
spirit, she gathered mana in her palms, ready to cast a spell at a
moment’s notice.

“GOAAAA!!”

In an instant, the spirit moved, and swung its arm down to crush Shatia.
She immediately formed a barrier to block it, but the force was so
immense that a portion of the shock passed through to her actual body.
It was enough to damage Shatia’s fragile childish form, and a weak cry
escaped her lips.

The spirit swung its fist once more, this time from the front. Shatia
hastily formed another barrier, but the impact forced her backward. The
spirit continued its assault, fist after fist, while Shatia continued to
block with her barriers. Gradually, Shatia began to be pushed back,
until she was finally driven all the way to the edge of the cave wall.

“Ngh, ugh…!”

“GOGOA GOGAGAAAAAAAAAAAA!!!!”

An endless rain of fists poured down from the evil spirit. Cracks began
to form in the barrier; Shatia was reaching her limit.

*Now, what shall I do?* Shatia’s thoughts remained calm despite the
sweat dripping from her brow. Maintaining her composure, never
panicking, she searched for a chance to turn the tables.

Standard magic had failed, her specialty gravity magic had failed, and
physical attacks had been repelled… How could such an enemy be defeated?
Shatia closed her eyes and dove into deep thought. She searched for a
way to defeat the evil spirit, a way to overcome this wall. Finally, she
arrived at a single conclusion.

“I have no choice but to use ‘that’…”

A certain spell. Unlike standard magic, it was performed through a
special formula. It was an anomaly even among Shatia’s repertoire—a
forbidden spell that even the other witches feared.

Given Shatia’s personality, she didn’t particularly want to use it.
However, in this situation, it was the only means she had left.
Resigning herself, she opened her eyes.

“GOAAAAAAAAAAAAAA!!!”

Before her, the evil spirit screamed in a frenzy, continuing to slam its
fists repeatedly to shatter her barrier. Looking up at her crazed enemy,
Shatia began to slowly recite the incantation. She must not rush. A
certain, precise, lethal spell to seal the enemy.

“Do not look so frightening. It has been a long time since I used this
magic… It may be a bit rough. Forgive me.”

Pacing herself, Shatia swept her arm to construct the formula, steadily
preparing the spell. Most of the barrier had finally been destroyed, and
the spirit’s punches grew even more savage. Perhaps sensing something,
the evil spirit began to swing its fists desperately to stop her. But
Shatia did not panic. Slowly, gracefully, she continued the incantation
while making light conversation as if it were mere small talk.

“You stand before the Witch of Wisdom, Shatifahl… Fear me, tremble, and
begone.”

Ensuring the hidden old man could not hear, Shatia placed an index
finger to her lips and softly uttered the words of power. In the next
instant, the area was bathed in light, which was then absorbed into her
palm. As the light suddenly vanished from the surroundings, countless
magic circles unfurled across Shatia’s hand.

“*Lullaby*.”

The magic circles scattered into a spray of tiny orbs of light. The
movements of the evil spirit, which had been whaling down with its
fists, began to slow until it stopped completely, as if its functions
had been cut. The spirit clutched its body seemingly in agony. Then, a
high-pitched scream erupted from the mouth on its torso.

“GOGAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA…!!?”

Soon, its body began to crumble, vanishing like dust.

This was Shatia’s ultimate spell: Lullaby. It was an extraordinary magic
that reduced the target’s mana to zero—a spell created exclusively for
Shatia alone.

Even the other witches feared this magic, and it was the reason Shatia
was called their leader. For mages and creatures that live on mana,
having the very source of their power erased is a terrifying prospect.
From the perspective of a witch, whose bond with mana is so intimate,
this magic could be called their natural enemy.

Thus, the evil spirit, which had been summoned using mana as a medium,
had its foundation severed and crumbled away.

“*Phew*… It has been a while, but it went well.”

Seeing the spirit vanish, Shatia sank to her knees in exhaustion. Her
body flickered slightly as her illusion magic began to slip.

Naturally, Lullaby had its drawbacks. It consumed a vast amount of mana
and required time to chant. To show such an opening in the heat of
battle would surely lead to defeat. Its use required specific
conditions. Of course, for someone as magically versatile as Shatia,
stalling for that much time was child’s play.

The old man peeked out from behind the rocks and rushed over to the
unharmed Shatia. “Y-You defeated it… Rather, who on earth are you!? What
was that final spell!? It felt as though the surrounding mana suddenly
vanished…!!?”

“Oh, still alive? How lucky you are. My apologies, but that’s a secret.
The circumstances are complicated, you see.” Though impressed that the
old man had remained unharmed despite such a fierce battle, Shatia
warned him not to pry any further.

“However, this provides a perfect excuse,” she continued. Looking at the
surrounding damage, Shatia clapped her hands as if she had just thought
of something. “You attempted a resurrection ritual and failed. The
summoned evil spirit destroyed the altar, and you were knocked
unconscious by the aftermath… Yes, this scenario should do.”

“W-What are you saying?” Unable to comprehend, the old man stared at
Shatia with a blank, open-mouthed expression.

“Forgive me, but I must ask you to sleep as well. I was happy that you
tried to resurrect me, but if the essence of the forest is stolen,
nature will be destroyed.”

Shatia swept her hand in front of the old man’s face and whispered a
spell. With just that, the old man collapsed on the spot and began to
snore.

“This will do,” Shatia nodded to herself. She surveyed the area once
more, checking to see if there were any oversights or mistakes, and
ensured that no traces of her presence remained. Once she was satisfied,
she finally stood up.

“Very well, time to go home.”

Shatia dispelled her illusion magic, returning to her childish form, and
set off back to the village.

On the way back, as she flew through the sky, she worried about how much
her mother would scold her for the state of her clothes.
