# Chapter 23 – That Which Awaited in the Vault

To the students of the magic academy, a visit to the royal palace was a
dream come true. The underground vaults of the palace housed numerous
special-class spellbooks, and the library contained a wealth of
organized information incomparable to that of the academy. Even if they
weren’t allowed to touch them, merely seeing them was, to the children,
like gazing upon treasures of shining gold.

Shatia’s eyes, too, were sparkling, though in a completely different
sense.

Under Professor Regis’s supervision, the students of Class 1 visited the
palace. Shatia walked the corridors alongside Laika. Giant paintings
adorned the walls, and every time Laika saw one, she would catch her
breath, completely captivated. Even the typically rowdy Leo didn’t say a
word, simply staring at the soldiers passing by.

Once Professor Regis led them to the great hall, he gathered them close.
Gesturing for them to approach, he cautioned everyone to keep their
voices down. The students, nervous in their grand surroundings, scurried
to huddle together.

“We will now have free time. You are only permitted to visit the floors
connected to the great hall. Under no circumstances are you to cause a
disturbance… Now, dismissed.”

Even the usually boisterous Professor Regis spoke as softly as possible,
his voice at the bare minimum volume for the students to hear.

No matter how grand the setting, children are creatures that become
instantly liberated once free time begins. The moment Professor Regis
dismissed them, they scattered toward the floors of their choice. Shatia
watched the students flow away like a river, tilting her head as she
considered her next move.

“Shatia-chan, they said some of the library’s bookshelves are open to
us. Wanna take a look with me?” Laika approached with the information
she had heard from Professor Regis.

Shatia nodded with feigned curiosity, “Indeed… I am certainly
interested. Let us go.”

The two headed to the library. Bookshelves were positioned everywhere
like a dense forest. The ceilings were high, and there were sections
where entire walls served as shelving units. The labyrinthine
construction caused Laika’s head to spin.

Upon reaching the shelves they were permitted to browse, the two picked
up various books. Laika couldn’t make heads or tails of the contents,
but to Shatia, every entry was fascinating.

“Uwaah, as expected of the royal library… it’s way bigger than the
academy’s,” Laika breathed out a sigh of admiration at the
well-maintained facilities.

And Shatia’s eyes sparkled as she carefully handled the books,
muttering, “Some quite interesting records are kept here. I would dearly
love to read them all.”

Even in this limited section, the books were packed with invaluable
information. Under normal circumstances, Shatia would have reached for
every single one. However, she restrained herself and gently returned
just the one book to its place.

“Laika, Shatia. I heard they’re holding a special lecture about the
palace. Wanna go with me?” Leo suddenly appeared, his hair as messy as
ever, and offered an invitation. It seemed to be a special presentation
for the magic academy students, promising interesting information.

“Leo-kun…” Laika seemed interested, her gaze shifting slightly in that
direction.

Shatia decided this was the perfect opportunity and gave a small nod, “I
shall pass. Laika, go ahead with Leo.”

“Eh… but what about Shatia-chan…?”

“I wish to look around here a bit more. Go on, enjoy yourself.”

With that, she gave Laika’s back a small push, half-forcing her to go
with Leo. Laika looked somewhat uneasy, but when Shatia gave her a smile
and a wave, she waved back.

Once the two were out of sight, Shatia let out a small sigh.

“Now then… shall I begin?”

The girl’s expression shifted into something stern. To fulfill her
purpose as a witch, Shatia moved her plan into action.

She had already memorized the palace’s layout through prior research.
She first used presence-concealment magic to move in secret, aiming for
the corridor leading to the underground vaults. Naturally, this was a
floor students were forbidden to enter, but Shatia reached it without
being noticed by anyone. Eventually, before her stood a door, firmly
locked. However, Shatia quickly picked it with magic and nonchalantly
descended the passage to the basement.

Despite being underground, the entire passage was lit with a pale blue
glow. It was likely some kind of magical effect. The atmosphere was
eerie, accompanied by a chilly sensation. Shatia ventured deeper and
deeper until she arrived before a single door—a massive, heavily
reinforced door shut tight. It wasn’t locked; instead, it was sealed
with a specialized magic. Likely a measure to prevent unauthorized
entry.

Shatia narrowed her eyes to examine the structure of the magic cast. To
her, a witch, it was an incredibly simple sealing spell. It was the kind
of basic magic used in place of a physical key. However, her expression
remained grim.

“Breaking the seal is trivial, but I sense mana beyond this point. Based
on the reaction, is it a magical weapon? Well, I shall break through
regardless.”

She could sense a faint mana signature from behind the door.
Furthermore, it was a mana distinct from that of an ordinary human.
Shatia surmised it was a magical weapon. She had expected a guard or two
given it was a vault, but she hadn’t anticipated a magical weapon lying
in wait. Though she looked annoyed, Shatia couldn’t turn back now. She
dispelled the sealing magic and opened the door.

What lay beyond was a different world entirely. Unlike the library, it
wasn’t filled with bookshelves. Rather, it was simply a vast, open
space. What met her eyes were books flying around in a circle before
her. Countless books were gathered in a sphere, rotating together. It
looked almost like the movement of a living creature.

Shatia briefly wondered why the books were moving in such a way, but
that was unimportant. What she had to do hadn’t changed: acquiring the
body creation magic. Just as she thought this and stepped inside, she
noticed an anomaly.

“That is…”

In the middle of the path leading to the swarm of books, a strange
object was lying on the floor. It was a humanoid magical weapon clad in
giant armor—what was generally known as a golem. But the strange thing
was that the golem wasn’t standing guard; it was lying on the ground
with a massive hole blown through its chest. It looked as if it had been
attacked by someone.

“A magical weapon… already destroyed?” Shatia muttered, her voice
trembling with rare agitation.

Looking closely, there wasn’t just one. There were golems whose armor
had been shattered to pieces, and others whose limbs had been blown off.
Clearly, some kind of battle had taken place in the vault. But why?
Shatia stood there in a daze, unable to comprehend what was happening.

Something was wrong. Perhaps there was an intruder other than herself.
Since she felt no mana signature, they might have already escaped, but
it was certain that something unusual was occurring. Regardless, Shatia
braced herself and, intent on achieving her goal first, walked past the
golems toward the many books.

“What an ominous feeling… It is almost as if someone knew I was coming.”

Shatia felt that this situation had somehow been prepared for her. It
was all too perfect. Whoever destroyed the golems hadn’t destroyed the
vault itself, nor did it seem like they had stolen any important
grimoires. What, then, was their goal? She didn’t know.

Determined to fulfill her primary objective, Shatia searched for the
body creation grimoire. She looked up at the books flying about and
narrowed her eyes. Using her intuition to discern which was which, she
gently reached out, and a book fluttered down from above. Catching it,
Shatia slowly opened the volume and flipped through the pages.

![](./images/0009_i-009.webp)

The first page contained warnings regarding the magic. Standard advice,
such as stating that only authorized individuals should view the book
and to use it with caution. However, every page after that was blank. No
matter how many pages she turned, there wasn’t a single description
concerning body creation. And when she turned to the final page, she
found words she could not ignore.

—*Unto the lowly has Wisdom fallen.*

What was inscribed there had been handwritten by someone. Shatia had a
memory of the handwriting, but she couldn’t quite place it. She tilted
her head, racking her brain.

On a hunch, she checked other grimoires, only to find several had been
replaced with blank ones. As she suspected, someone had come here before
her, defeated the golems, and swapped the books. Furthermore, they knew
of her existence.

A grave turn of events. Feeling a flicker of irritation, Shatia decided
she needed to withdraw for now. But just as she turned around, she
sensed a powerful mana reaction from above. This was followed by a sound
like the earth shaking. Realizing something was wrong, she hurried back
the way she came. She ran up the stairs and returned to the floor above
as if nothing had happened. There, she found the academy students and
other visitors screaming and running for their lives.

“Uwaaaaaah!!”

“T-This is terrible! The golems have gone berserk!!”

People shouted as they dashed toward the exit. Behind them, several
golems clad in the same armor she had seen in the vault were smashing
through walls and pillars, their eyes glowing red. Their movements were
clearly not normal. It was as if they were being controlled by someone.

“*Tch*… so that’s how it is,” Shatia muttered, her expression hardening.

This was undoubtedly the work of whoever had stolen the grimoires. To be
able to manipulate the palace’s golems, they must have a highly skilled
mage in their ranks. Shatia shook her head in exasperation.

“First, I must save Laika and the others. Finding the culprit comes
after… I shall make them deeply regret making a fool of a witch,” she
said with a complex, angry smile. Channeling mana into her fingertips,
she stood to face the approaching golems.

The first hurdle: repelling the golems while ensuring the safety of the
humans present.
