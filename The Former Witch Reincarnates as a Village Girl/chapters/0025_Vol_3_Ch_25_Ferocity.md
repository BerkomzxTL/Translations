# Chapter 25 – Ferocity

“Professor, please! Let me go! Laika’s still inside!!”

“I know. The soldiers are searching right now. That’s why we need to
evacuate.”

In front of the palace gates, Leo was shouting at Professor Regis, his
voice thick with emotion. Regis listened with a conflicted expression,
but his duty as a teacher came first. He could only shake his head and
refuse.

When they were evacuating from the golem rampage, Leo had seen the
moment Laika was attacked and separated from the group. He wanted to
turn back and help her immediately, but the other golems had blocked his
path, preventing him from reaching her. Ultimately, Regis had dragged
him back to the entrance, where Leo was now protesting, insisting on
going back to save her.

“But… if something happens to her…!!” Leo clenched his fists, unable to
finish his sentence. 

Regis understood his feelings perfectly, yet he remained firm in his
refusal.

He had already confirmed that two students were missing. He had
requested the soldiers to search the palace interior. He couldn’t allow
any more victims, so he had to bar Leo from entering. In truth, Regis
intended to go look for them himself. He ground his teeth, loathing his
own powerlessness for failing to protect all of his students.

At that moment, a massive explosion rang out from within the palace. The
ground shook, and something burst through the roof. Something that
looked like a brilliantly shining golden angel. It let out a great roar,
and glowing wings sprouted from its back.

“W-what is that…?”

Both Leo and Regis stared up in a daze. It was a sight beyond human
comprehension. Overwhelmed by its divine appearance, they even forgot to
speak. But Leo was certain—something catastrophic was happening inside.
Remembering Laika, he immediately bolted, slipping past Regis and
running into the palace.

“Ah! Wait, Leo!! Don’t be reckless!!” Regis realized what was happening
a beat later.

Floating high in the air, the golden angel looked down at the cityscape,
letting out a low growl. Ignoring it, Regis ran through the doors in
pursuit of Leo.

◇

Pushing aside the debris, Shatia crawled out from the mountain of
rubble. Despite how beautiful the palace had been, the area was now in
ruin. Beyond the shattered ceiling, the ancient golem floated in the
sky, mana wings spread wide. Shatia looked up and clicked her tongue in
annoyance before immediately searching for Laika.

“Laika…!!”

Laika had created a dirt wall to save Shatia. But she hadn’t been fast
enough to save herself, and she had been buried under the falling
stones. Shatia cleared the obstructing rubble with a pale face,
searching desperately. Her hands were scratched and bleeding from the
sharp fragments, but she didn’t care.

Finally, deep within the pile, she found Laika.

She was tucked into a small hollow that had formed, allowing her to
narrowly escape the worst of the impact. Though she was unconscious from
being hit by a few fragments, she didn’t seem to have any
life-threatening injuries. Seeing this, Shatia’s shoulders slumped as
she let out a long breath of relief. She pulled Laika toward her,
dragging her out of the space.

“My apologies… It was my fault. I have ended up owing you one,” Shatia
said to the sleeping Laika, her expression twisted with regret.

A momentary lapse in judgment. The enemy’s sudden transformation had
pulled her attention for just a second. Under normal circumstances, she
could have easily repelled the debris with a barrier. But Laika had been
faster. She had saved Shatia without any regard for her own safety.

After moving Laika to a safe corner of the floor and laying her down,
Shatia clenched her fists. Once again, she had failed to protect
someone. A surge of frustration welled up within her.

Looking up, she saw the ancient golem perched atop a palace tower.
Seeing it look down at the city, it likely intended to destroy the
capital. If a thing like that descended upon everyone, the people
wouldn’t stand a chance. Shatia gently stroked Laika’s cheek and stood
up.

“GOOOAAAARRRRHHH!!!”

“Wait here. I shall defeat that thing and return shortly…” Shatia said
while looking up at the roaring golem. Naturally, there was no reply
from Laika, but Shatia felt as though she saw a faint smile on the
girl’s face. Shatia allowed a thin smile of her own to appear, then
composed herself and took flight.

Stepping out onto the palace roof, she locked eyes with the ancient
golem on the tower.

Laika had sacrificed herself and resolved to stop that thing. Shatia
thought it unforgivable if she herself were to run away now. She, too,
steeled her heart, deciding to use every means at her disposal to
eliminate it.

“You have quite the nerve, injuring my student… This will cost you
dearly, you know?”

“GGGOOOO…”

Whether it remembered the battle before its fusion or not, the ancient
golem’s eyes glowed red through the gaps in its golden helmet as Shatia
fell into its vision. It let out a growl that sounded almost angry. But
Shatia felt her own anger rising; her gaze sharpened as she spread her
arms, preparing her magic.

“I shall settle this in a single blow.”

She no longer had any intention of holding back. There was no need to
analyze the enemy. She would end it with the first strike. The usually
cautious Shatia had vanished, replaced by a girl enraged that her friend
had been hurt. She held out her palms, and the surrounding light began
to gather toward her.

The moment it saw this, the ancient golem moved. Huge mana wings
sprouted from its back as it crouched down. Shatia had expected it to
charge and prepared to dodge while maintaining her incantation, but in
the next instant, it kicked off with enough force to shatter the tower,
hurtling toward Shatia like a localized gale.

“Lulla—gh!?”

“GGGRRRRUUUUAAAAARRRRHHH!!!”

Without even the time to trigger her magic, Shatia immediately evacuated
into the air using flight magic. However, the charge was powerful enough
to shatter the roof, and Shatia was struck by the flying shards of
stone.

She had no choice but to dispel the magic circles she had been forming
and put distance between them. The ancient golem pulled back the fist
that had smashed the roof and glared at Shatia with another low growl.

“This thing… can it use magic now?”

She had suspected as much when it destroyed the roof. It seemed the
ancient golem had gained magical capabilities through its fusion. With
its mana wings, it was now capable of high-mobility combat that would
have been impossible as a normal golem. It was an utter nuisance. Shatia
wiped the cold sweat beading on her forehead with her sleeve.

It moved again. Its golden armor gleaming, it swung its arm and formed a
mana spear. It was the same basic magic Shatia had demonstrated
earlier—a simple spell that involved condensing mana. Shatia had no
doubt the ancient golem was mimicking her.

Grasping the massive spear, the golem twisted its body to gain momentum
and threw it. The shot hurtled toward Shatia.

“*Tch*… It is learning, imitating my magic.”

She formed a mana shield to parry the spear, but her expression
darkened.

It was undoubtedly a mimicry of her magic. While an attack of that level
wasn’t particularly threatening, the thought that an enemy already
boasting such a resilient frame and immense physical strength had now
acquired magic was, admittedly, a bit disheartening. In any case, she
judged that it would be best to avoid a prolonged battle.

“GGGRRRRUUUUAAAAARRRRHHH!!”

As if it had a plan of its own, though, the ancient golem let out a roar
and launched a relentless assault. Flapping wings made of mana, it
soared into the air and lunged at Shatia; each swoop accompanied by a
shockwave. Dodging a downward punch, Shatia circled behind it. She
formed a massive mana fist and prepared to strike. But before she could,
the ancient golem shifted its stance, turned its head toward her, and
fired a beam of condensed magical energy from the gaps in its helm.

“You intend to prevent me from using magic, then…!”

She dispelled the mana fist she was about to launch and instead formed a
shield to block the beam. In that split second of opening, the ancient
golem regained its posture, closed the distance, and swung its fist from
point-blank range. Though she braced herself with her shield again, the
impact was so great that Shatia was blown back, crashing into the palace
roof.

“Ugh… it really does have intelligence… Did it understand my tactics
from the first fight? Honestly, how troublesome.”

Her arm ached slightly; Shatia’s hand dragged along the ground as she
stood back up. It wasn’t a major problem, she could cast magic even
without the use of her arms. The real issue was that she lacked the
leeway to do so. Shatia looked up at the sky and glared at the ancient
golem hovering above. It seemed to be preparing its next move, gathering
mana for a new attack.

As expected, it had intelligence. Shatia was certain of it now. Whether
it had it from the start or it had grown significantly through fusion,
it didn’t change the fact that it had become a formidable entity. The
ancient golem was wary of Shatia’s magic and was making sure not to give
her an opening. And with its newfound high mobility, it was succeeding
perfectly.

Shatia understood she had to seal its movements. But how? Aim for the
gaps in the armor like before? That was impossible. Such a delicate
method wouldn’t work against something moving so fast. What was she
supposed to do?

While she was thinking, the ancient golem released its stored mana,
raining down mana bolts on Shatia. She immediately formed a shield, but
because she hadn’t had enough time to extract sufficient mana, cracks
began to spiderweb across it. Eventually, the rain of mana turned into
explosions, pulverizing the palace roof. Shatia used flight magic to
escape the area and retreat into the sky.

“GGGRRRRUUUUAAAAARRRRHHH!!”

“…*Tch*!!”

The golem charged in pursuit immediately. It was a tackle reinforced by
a wave of energy. Judging that she couldn’t block it, Shatia dodged to
the side. However, she was caught in the aftershock and sent spinning
through the air. The ancient golem slammed into a tower with its excess
momentum, stopping only after its arm pierced deep into the stone. At
the same time, Shatia finally stopped her uncontrolled movement and
steadied herself in midair.

For the first time in a long while, Shatia was out of breath. Perhaps
because she hadn’t had a proper fight in so long, her body felt
sluggish. She laughed self-deprecatingly, wondering if she had grown too
soft soaking in the lukewarm water of the academy.

“Shatia!”

At that moment, a voice echoed. It wasn’t hers, nor was it the ancient
golem’s. It was a boy’s voice. Turning around, she saw Leo poking his
head out from the shattered roof; he had used flight magic to reach the
top. Shatia’s eyes widened, wondering why Leo was here.

“…Leo?” He had likely come this far out of concern for Laika. Then,
witnessing the situation, he simply couldn’t stand by and watch. Shatia
surmised as much.

In that moment, the ancient golem let out a low growl and shifted its
target from Shatia to Leo, unleashing a powerful mana blast in his
direction. “GGGRRRRUUUUOOOOOOO!!!”

Shatia instantly moved to Leo’s side with flight magic and formed a mana
shield. She took the blast head-on; a thunderous roar erupted along with
a shower of sparks. The golem had no intention of stopping, continuing
its onslaught.

“…! Why did you come, Leo!?”

“W-whoa… Shatia…! Because Laika was sleeping down there. There was this
crazy fight happening up here… Wait, are you seriously okay withstanding
that!?”

Shatia muttered that she obviously wasn’t okay as she took in the
situation. Truly, children with a strong sense of justice were nothing
but trouble. However, her lips curled up slightly, indicating that she
didn’t dislike such a thing. Shatia braced herself, putting strength
into her arms. She maintained the shield’s integrity, continuing to
block the consecutive blasts.

At this rate, they would both be finished. Setting aside her full power,
fighting while protecting Leo was difficult for Shatia. She might be
able to do something if she used her power as a witch, but she
hesitated. Would she be rejected again? Would she be seen as a loathsome
existence once more? Such anxieties crossed her mind.

“…Hey, Leo,” While maintaining the shield, Shatia spoke to Leo for no
particular reason. Despite being in a situation where she was far from
having any leeway, she opened her mouth. 

“Huh? W-what is it?” Leo gave an uncertain reply, suspicious of her
mysterious behavior.

“If I were to defeat that thing in an instant… would you be afraid of
me?”

It was an abrupt question. Even she didn’t understand what her own
intention was. It might have been a simple confirmation. If she
unleashed her full power as a witch and defeated the ancient golem, how
would humans look at her? Would they fear her after all? Even if she
fought to protect the city, would they feel terror at her abnormal
power? Recalling the past, Shatia couldn’t shake her anxiety.

Then, Leo said with a desperate expression, “As if that would happen!!
If you can beat it, just do it already!!!”

Seeing him practically begging her to defeat it now, Shatia blinked in
surprise. It was a response completely different from what she had
expected. The sheer unexpectedness of it almost made her lose her
strength for a moment.

“…Kuku… Haha, I see, I see. ‘If you can beat it, just do it already,’ is
it? You have a point. Indeed.”

“W-what is it? You have a way? Then hurry up and—”

“I’m going, then.”

“Eh?”

Shatia felt as if something had been cleared away. With a refreshed
expression, she channeled as much mana as she could, leaving the mana
shield sustained in place as she took flight. Leo watched her back in a
daze as she flew toward the ancient golem.

Still firing blast after blast, the golem noticed Shatia’s presence
closing in and let out a roar. However, it didn’t stop. It seemed to
have its eyes on Leo. But Shatia didn’t care. She had already prepared
her countermeasure. Swinging her arm, Shatia chanted her spell.

“GGGRRRRUUUUAAAAARRRRHHH!!!”

“Do not howl so much. I shall give you a special look at a witch’s
magic.”

As the ancient golem roared, it swung one arm toward Shatia. Mana
gathered there, preparing to fire another blast. But Shatia didn’t
retreat; she gave a small smile and activated her magic.

“*Shackles of Sin.*“

The moment Shatia moved her finger, magical restraints were clamped onto
the ancient golem’s body. The golem suddenly dropped its arm, its
collection of mana halted. It began to cry out, struggling in agony.

Shackles of Sin was a spell that drained the target’s mana. For a golem
whose power source was mana, it was nothing but pure torment.

“*Phantoms of Guilt.*“

Shatia then activated another spell. Dark smoke billowed from her body,
ensnaring the ancient golem. It coiled around the armor, binding it,
completely stripping it of its freedom.

Shatia’s magic didn’t end there. Black flames danced, pitch-black swords
reaped.

“*Flames of Stigma.* *Swords of the Darkened Skies.*“

Consecutively. The seven powerful magics wielded by the Seven Witches.
Each and every one pierced into the ancient golem, and its armor began
to crumble. Finally, Shatia used her own finishing magic.

“*Lullaby.*“

“GGOO, GGAAAAARRRRRRHHHHH…!!”

For a moment, all light vanished from the surroundings. In the next
instant, countless magic circles were released, and along with
scattering spheres of light, the ancient golem let out one final scream
before its brilliance faded. The golden armor returned to its rusted
state, and its red-glowing eyes were shrouded in darkness. Its wings
vanished, and the angel fell to the earth.

The golem slammed into the palace roof, its armor shattering into
pieces. Watching this, Shatia finally felt the battle had reached its
conclusion. Suppressing her trembling hands, she quietly landed on the
roof.

She had used all seven of the witch’s magics simultaneously. Even for
Shatia, who boasted inexhaustible mana, the accumulated fatigue was
impossible to ignore. She dropped to her knees and let out a long, long
sigh.

From a distance, Leo approached while waving his hand. Shatia didn’t
know how she should respond, so she simply gave a weak smile.

Regardless, she had shown power enough to repel an ancient golem. How
would the humans react now? Though she was anxious, Shatia decided to
leave herself to the flow of fate.
