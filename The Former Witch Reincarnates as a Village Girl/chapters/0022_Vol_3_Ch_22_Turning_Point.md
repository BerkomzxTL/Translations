# Chapter 22 – Turning Point

“The transfer student, Shatia-san… she placed first in the periodic exam
held by Professor Regis, her classroom attitude is excellent, and her
grades are outstanding… truly a student beyond reproach.”

A meeting was currently being held in the faculty room. It was a casual
exchange of information regarding Shatia, who had transferred in two
months ago. Given the suddenness of her transfer, it was a perfectly
normal meeting to confirm if there were any issues. The teachers
discussed her while holding documents distributed to each of them.
Professor Regis was among them, participating in the meeting while
recalling the exam he had conducted the other day.

“The other day, she pointed out a flaw in the structural formula of a
certain spell. When I looked into it, there was indeed room for
improvement. She possesses magnificent insight.”

“She seems to strongly desire a viewing of the spellbooks stored in the
royal palace… Given that she possesses this much talent, there should be
no problem in permitting her entry.”

Shatia’s reputation was high, and the teachers seemed to approve of her.
A talented student is, naturally, highly valued. Some teachers even
began to suggest granting her request to browse the royal spellbooks.
However, there were those who opposed it—teachers harboring various
anxieties; if a child who could not yet control her power got her hands
on a powerful spell, she would surely go out of control.

“…But, she has far too much talent. Was this child truly just an
ordinary village girl?”

One teacher raised a hand, interrupting the flow of conversation. His
expression was stern, and the atmosphere grew heavy.

“Indeed… her mana is somewhat of an anomaly. I feel something like a
bottomless power from her.”

“Rumor has it the Demon Kingdom has been acting suspicious lately. It
may be an exaggeration, but one could consider the possibility that she
is their plant…”

Stemming from that one comment, doubts about Shatia’s overwhelming
talent arose one after another, leading to speculation that she might be
an assassin sent by another country. Of course, it remained firmly in
the realm of *just* speculation. Yet, the teachers’ suspicions could not
be dispelled so easily.

“Regardless, she remains a student who requires close monitoring. A
golden egg, or the seed of calamity… we must discern the truth,” an
elderly teacher declared, and the others nodded in agreement.

For now, they would maintain the status quo. That was the conclusion
they arrived at. There was room for doubt, but they could not simply
accuse her. If she were innocent, it would be a devastating false
accusation. They needed to proceed with caution.

Professor Regis narrowed his eyes, recalling what he had seen during the
exam. The way Shatia had fought was that of a veteran. It wasn’t just
that her magic was strong; she was a master of its application. But
there was no way a child could wield such sophisticated techniques. Just
who was she? Was she truly Loreid’s pupil? The mystery only deepened.

◇

Generally speaking, the true nature of a “Witch” is unknown. Existing
since ancient times, living on without aging… They are deviant beings
who possess a vast amount of mana that a human body cannot contain, and
wield magic capable of shaking the continent.

Why they look human and why there are only women among them—all of it
remains shrouded in mystery. However, the fact that the Seven Witches of
history were terrifying entities is documented in great detail.

In fact, one of the witches, the Witch of Inquiry Cloak, used her unique
technology to create a terrifying magical weapon capable of blowing away
an entire city. It is said that even now, after the witch’s death, that
weapon exists somewhere in the world, and many nations are searching for
it.

“…Thus, even now that the witches are gone, their remnants remain
scattered across the world, and nations fight over them. Witches truly
are beings at the center of calamity.”

While bathed in the sunlight streaming through the window, Shatia
listened vacantly to the teacher lecturing about the history of witches.

Being a witch herself, she couldn’t find the slightest interest in
hearing about them from a human perspective. She slumped against her
desk, her head nodding up and down as she teetered on the edge of sleep.

The magic academy had a subject called history. It was a subject where
one primarily learned about the history of magic, and since she could
learn about human history that she didn’t know, it had piqued her
interest at first. However, a lesson on the history of witches had been
inserted halfway through, and the light quickly faded from her eyes.

To begin with, the history of witches as told by the teacher was mostly
dramatized by humans. To Shatia, it was full of things she had no memory
of; nonsensical tales like a witch making trees vanish from a mountain,
or a witch catching every single fish in a river—the supposed history
was filled with such absurdities. And yet, they framed witches as
symbols of terror, which was quite a nuisance. Shatia rested her chin on
her hand and let out a small sigh.

*If this continues, I really will fall asleep.* Thinking this, Shatia
decided to extract some information to make the lesson at least somewhat
beneficial to her. She raised her hand straight up, “Teacher, I have a
question…”

“Oh, what is it, Shatia-san?” The teacher smiled and turned toward her.

“Why were the witches viewed as evil beings?” She asked, tilting her
head slightly as if it were a simple, innocent question with no deep
meaning.

“Ah,” the teacher didn’t seem to mind, clearing her throat once before
speaking.

“It is because they each possessed power rivaling the Demon Lord, yet
belonged to no nation and exercised their magic as they pleased. People
who do not follow established laws and act selfishly are bad people,
right? It is the same thing.”

The teacher replied with a smile, as if it were the most obvious thing
in the world. Shatia reciprocated with a smile and nod of her own, but
inside, she harbored complex, murky emotions.

*Were we erased for such a trivial reason? Were we deemed dangerous
simply because we possessed bottomless mana, viewed as evil just because
we mastered magecraft according to the laws of nature?*

Shatia pondered. To begin with, witches such as herself could boast a
deep connection with essence, and thus their very existences were
inseparable from the concept of magic. For a witch, magic was synonymous
with breathing. And yet, humans declared that such a thing must be
suppressed, citing “laws.” It couldn’t be helped. Witches and humans
were different. 

Shatia found that her fists had been clenched. She released her tension
and gently stroked the doll tucked away in her clothes, calming her
heart.

In her eyes was an unwavering clarity.

<br />
After class ended and lunch break began, Shatia headed to the rooftop
with Laika. Usually they ate in the cafeteria, but today Laika had
brought a lunch box, and since Shatia had purchased bread and a drink,
they decided to eat on the roof.

Under the blue sky, Laika opened her lunch box and happily munched on
salad. Beside her, Shatia tore off pieces of bread and popped them into
her mouth, looking somewhat dazed.

“Shatia-chan, is something wrong?”

“…Hm?”

It was a sudden question. Laika looked somewhat worried, and since it
was so abrupt, Shatia stopped her hand in confusion. She dropped the
crumb of bread she was about to eat, letting out an “ah,” before
hurrying to pick it up.

“It’s just, you looked kinda sad…”

“Ahh, no… I was just thinking that the barrier between species is a
difficult thing… I wasn’t particularly sad.”

*I was just exasperated*, she swallowed and shook her head to dismiss
the concern.

It was something she had known for a thousand years—witches would never
be understood by humans. Even if their forms were similar, the contents
were as different as a mountain and a pebble. Even if they couldn’t tell
from afar, once they drew close, they would notice the abnormality and
flee at full speed. That was how alien witches were to humans.

“Hey, hey, did you hear?”

“Yeah, it’s super crazy, right~?”

Shatia suddenly overheard a conversation between some female students on
the roof. To distract herself, she tilted her ears toward the talk and
narrowed her eyes in focus. The girls continued with drinks in hand—

“The northern fortress invaded by demons… Isn’t that bad? Isn’t it
pretty close to the capital? You think a war or something’s gonna
happen?”

It was a rumor that had been circulating lately about the sudden
invasion by the demon race. Since it hadn’t been officially announced
yet, it couldn’t be confirmed, but it seemed the demons had seized the
northern fortress, which protected the route to the royal capital. Until
now, there had only been small-scale conflicts at the edge of the
continent, so this sudden movement left people bewildered.

Why? All of a sudden? No information had come in saying the front lines
had been broken. For some reason, demons had suddenly approached the
heart of the human territories and seized the northern fortress, the key
to their defense. This anomaly was still only at the stage of a rumor,
but it was enough to instill unrest in the people.

“And apparently the Demon Kingdom’s got a witch.”

“Ehhh? Weren’t they wiped out by the Hero-sama?”

“Don’t know~ it’s just a rumor… but if it’s true…”

They didn’t dare speak the rest; the girls looked at each other and
shivered with fear. The conversation ended there, and the two finished
their meal and left the roof. Just then, Laika and Shatia finished as
well, and Laika carefully packed away her lunch box.

Shatia narrowed her eyes. She recalled the conversation she had just
overhead, organizing the information in her head.

The movements of the demons lately were certainly ominous. There was the
mystery of how they seized the northern fortress, and the rumor of a
witch was also concerning. For Shatia, the start of a war between humans
and demons was of no consequence, but the matter of a witch was not so
simple. She had to determine if the demons were using a witch, or if a
witch was using the demons.

But she couldn’t move immediately, she could only bite her lip in
frustration.

In the past, she could have just gone to check using flight magic, but
now that she held the status of a student, she couldn’t fly around
outside so easily. She might be able to force her way through using
memory manipulation magic, but she didn’t want to resort to such
recklessness if possible.

*I came to this academy to save Emerald, and yet it has become a shackle
preventing me from searching for another witch… how ironic*, Shatia
chuckled.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
After finishing their lunch, Laika said she had business in the faculty
room and left. Now alone, Shatia muttered to herself while leaning
against the railing, “Time to start moving, I suppose.”

She hadn’t forgotten her original purpose—to browse the book on body
creation magic that was said to be in the royal capital. She needed to
master it to save Emerald. The proper way to do that was supposedly to
catch the eye of the court mages while at the academy, but from the
start Shatia had no intention of playing student for six years.

She was aware that some professors already harbored suspicions of her
power. Her immense well of mana could not be entirely concealed. Even if
Shatia measured things by her own scale, to them, it could feel like
something was off. She couldn’t risk staying at the academy for too
long, but that was fine. She only intended to stay for a short while, as
it was close to the palace.

“Tomorrow is the day of the royal palace visit… I shall be mastering
that body creation magic,” She turned and spoke while looking at the
palace visible from the roof.

The royal palace was surrounded by walls and heavily guarded. The castle
had several giant towers that reached the clouds; even from the roof,
one couldn’t grasp its full majesty without looking up. Usually, the
gates were guarded by soldiers, and one couldn’t enter without
permission. Archers patrolled the perimeter, and a faint barrier spell
was also in place. In other words, infiltrating without being noticed
was difficult.

However, the academy held a special lesson that involved touring the
palace, and it was precisely that lesson that was scheduled for
tomorrow.

Originally, this was a last resort. A drastic operation to be carried
out in an emergency. The only means left for if her true identity was
discovered. But the situation had changed. Now that the movements of the
demons were becoming increasingly frequent, and rumors of a witch were
circulating, Shatia had no choice but to act.

She made her resolve. Tomorrow, she would slip into the royal palace and
borrow that grimoire. Shatia placed a hand to her forehead and let out a
quiet sigh.
