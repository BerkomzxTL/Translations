# Chapter 32 – Wisdom and Inquiry

“To create a nation for witches… I see. Is that why you joined forces
with Belpheus?” Shatia asked with an air of composure while dodging the
lunging black snakes.

Cloak clicked her tongue at that carefree attitude and continued to
manifest more snakes without pause. What Cloak was most wary of was
Shatia’s Lullaby. The ability to empty one’s mana in a single breath was
a terrifying ability even to a fellow witch. Its only weakness was the
significant opening created during its activation. If she could just
crush that opening, the threat of Lullaby would vanish. Thus, Cloak kept
the black snakes coming, ensuring Shatia had no room to move.

“He wants to rule the world. Why wouldn’t I make use of that? When all
races are made to submit and he becomes the ruler of this world, the
ones standing beside him will be the witches. Witches will become beings
worthy of worship.”

As she swung her arm to direct several more black snakes, Cloak made her
confession. She spoke of her plan, of the dream she had envisioned. Her
form was full of life, her eyes sparkling with a rare intensity. She
twirled around like a dancer, her black coat fluttering. Synchronized
with her movements, the black snakes opened their maws and lunged at
Shatia.

Shatia formed countless mana spears and launched them at the incoming
snakes. The spears pierced the serpents and pinned them to the walls,
but they instantly dissolved into smoke, slipped through the spears, and
reformed into snakes to resume their assault.

“I see… quite an interesting plan. You always did come up with the most
unsettling things,” Shatia nodded in genuine admiration.

However, Cloak didn’t seem to care for that attitude; she offered a
mocking bow before ordering the snakes to attack again, “I am honored to
receive your praise, dearest mother.”

This time, they came from all sides at once. Trapped with no escape,
Shatia undauntedly formed mana shields around her. The black snakes
slammed into the shields and stopped, but immediately turned into smoke
once more. They drifted through the gaps between the shields, drawing
closer to Shatia before partially reforming into snake heads to bare
their fangs.

“Oops.”

Noticing them, Shatia fired orbs of mana from her fingertips to blow
them away. They turned back into smoke and retreated as if fleeing.

*As troublesome a magic as ever*, Shatia thought, her lips curling.
Originally, this magic was one Shatia had devised: Phantoms of Guilt.
However, the version Shatia created was a surprise-attack magic intended
only to obscure the enemy’s vision with smoke or put those who inhaled
it to sleep. Yet Cloak, to whom she had granted this magic, had refined
it in her own way. By giving it the form of black snakes, she made it
capable of attack, and by switching between the forms of snake and
smoke, she enabled a wide range of tactics. Furthermore, if bitten by a
snake, one would be struck by some kind of curse. It was a thoroughly
bothersome magic.

“To think I was the one who provided the catalyst for this… how ironic,”
Shatia murmured sadly, glaring at the black snakes.

The snakes that had retreated from the shields all turned into smoke at
once, obscuring Shatia’s vision. In the next instant, a single black
snake lunged from within and grazed Shatia’s cheek. Immediately, a wave
of dizziness came over her.

“*Tch*,” she clicked her tongue softly and placed a hand on her wounded
cheek. It was a tiny scratch, yet she had been hit by some sort of curse
or ailment. Likely a toxin. Her consciousness grew hazy, and her vision
wavered. It was probably a fast-acting poison—not one with high
lethality, but one meant only to immobilize prey. In that case, it
wasn’t a fatal problem yet. Shatia slapped her own cheek to snap her
consciousness back to alertness.

As the smoke cleared, Cloak stood a few paces away. She pulled the black
snakes back, making them wait at her feet. The snakes coiled and swirled
in circles. Shatia stared at them and let out a small sigh.

“Tell me, Shatia. You must think it too, don’t you? Humans are creatures
that deserve to die. Those bastards persecute us simply because we’re
witches.”

Cloak appealed to Shatia, clenching her fists tightly as she slowly
approached. Her beautiful face was distorted with hatred, and the flames
of revenge burned in her eyes. Unlike Emerald’s frenzied, unrestrained
madness, it was quiet, and cold. A killing intent backed by reason was a
terrifying thing. Cloak’s malice was truly vicious.

“We existed long before they built their cities on the continent. We
lived quietly in the forests, conversing with animals and monsters… that
was all we were! And yet, they interfered with that!”

Shatia could feel herself wavering as she listened intently to Cloak’s
emotional plea. Despite her head becoming hazy from the poison, she
carved every single word into her mind, not letting a single syllable
escape. She continued to listen in silence.

Cloak stopped a few steps away and opened her mouth with a small, bitter
smile. “Why are you protecting humans? You visit their cities and were
even living among them until just recently! Even though they’re the ones
who killed you… why can you do such a thing?!”

“…The ones who killed me were the Hero and the people of the royal
palace behind him. Even so, is it not wrong to generalize and label all
of humanity as an evil?”

“Shut up!!” Cloak howled, baring her teeth.

The black snakes rose as one, writhing like a wave as they lunged at
Shatia. Shatia poured every ounce of mana she could into her palms and
released it all at once. A dazzling light was unleashed, clashing with
the snakes. A powerful blast erupted, scattering smoke and light
everywhere.

“I am not necessarily protecting humans… To me, humans and demons are
equally precious living things. I simply do not wish to see anyone die.”

When the smoke cleared, Shatia was still standing there with her usual
dignified posture. On the other hand, Cloak showed slight signs of
fatigue. Her legs were bent, her shoulders slumped, and her breathing
was ragged. She was far from out of mana, but considering she had to
keep her ultimate weapon—translocation magic—in reserve, she could no
longer afford to be complacent. The look of ease vanished from Cloak’s
face.

“Even if our kin are killed? Are you saying you’re fine even if our
fellow witches are slaughtered by the Hero?”

“Of course not. It is a terrible thing. It feels as though my heart is
being torn apart. But I know that revenge is meaningless.”

“Ha! The old truly have a different way of speaking…”

Hearing Shatia’s words, Cloak laughed derisively.

Cloak was possessed by revenge. She understood that herself. But what
was so wrong with that? She wanted to ask. They had committed no crimes,
yet they were nearly killed for nonsensical reasons. There was no reason
not to be angry. To ensure such a thing never happened again, the humans
had to be taught a lesson. They had to be shown just how much of a
divine presence a witch was. That was Cloak’s ultimate goal. Never again
would they be killed. Never again would their comrades be slaughtered.

“Yes. Because I have sought revenge once before.”

Something unexpected spilled from Shatia’s lips. For a moment, Cloak
forgot to give orders to the black snakes waiting to lunge; her raised
arm remained frozen in mid-air. A silence fell over the room. Shatia’s
expression was soft—there was even a trace of a smile.

“…Huh?”

“I said that I, too, have sought revenge in the past. Well, it wasn’t on
a scale as grand as hating all of humanity like you… but even so, I have
laid hands on people.”

Shatia looked down, confessing as she stared at her own small palms. It
was a confession far too unexpected for Cloak. She lowered her raised
arm and signaled the waiting snakes to back off. The words were shocking
enough to make her lose her will to fight for a moment.

“I’ve… I’ve never heard a single word of that story?!”

“Everyone has things they do not wish to talk about. Witches are no
exception. This story happened before any of you were born…”

To Cloak, Shatia was truly like a mother. No, to all six witches, Shatia
was their mother-figure. She was the one who taught them what they were.
She was the one who taught them how they should live. It was no
exaggeration to say that Shatia was the entire world to them. To think
that such a respected figure had once killed others for revenge—it was
enough to bring even the revenge-driven Cloak back to her senses for a
moment. She showed visible agitation, as she had never been told such a
thing.

“It is a truly sorrowful thing. To lose a loved one… Even by destroying
every last person responsible, they will never return… Truly, truly
sad,” Shatia spoke in a whisper, a hand clutching her heart. Because her
head was bowed, her expression was unreadable.

Cloak had never seen Shatia look so vulnerable. The witch who had always
carried herself with the dignity of a master had never appeared so
fragile. She couldn’t understand it.

When Shatia raised her head, she held up both hands and sighed, “I did
not particularly want to tell this story. After telling you to stop your
revenge, it would not be very persuasive for me to say I have done it
myself in the past, would it?”

“…………”

Certainly, it was somewhat hypocritical to tell someone to stop their
revenge while admitting to having done it herself. However, Cloak didn’t
point this out; she simply remained silent, glaring at Shatia. Then,
Shatia delivered the most important part—

“However, as one who has experienced it, I will say only this: There is
nothing waiting at the end of revenge. All that lies there is ruin.”

Shatia seemed about to continue, but she placed a hand to her mouth and
fell silent.

*Just what happened in her past?* Cloak wondered. She herself had only
lived for about 700 years, whereas Shatia was a witch who had lived for
over a millennium. She had existed on the continent since before they
were even called the Seven Witches. That deep, distant past was
something even a fellow witch could not know. Cloak’s shoulders
trembled.

“Ahh, I see… Is that how it is?”

Regardless, Cloak forced herself to accept it, squeezing out the words
as she nodded.

What she had revealed was likely the truth. She didn’t lie to begin
with. Moreover, she didn’t have the personality to spout empty
platitudes before her beloved daughter. She wasn’t the type to use her
cleverness for petty deceit. If she continued this revenge, ruin truly
would be waiting. Cloak understood. However, she clenched her fists.

“But so what! I can’t stop now… I’ve already passed the point of no
return. The war has begun. ‘Everything is solved if the mastermind
dies’—life isn’t some fairy tale like that!!”

“…Cloak.”

Cloak swung her arm, gathering the black snakes. Behind her, the snakes
merged into a single pillar of smoke, eventually taking the form of a
massive black serpent. Its size was incomparable to the snakes from
before; it reigned there like a dragon.

“Live or die! That’s all there is! Fight me, Shatifahl! I will surpass
you and build a nation for witches!!”

She thrust her fist forward, and simultaneously, the giant black snake
lunged. It opened its bottomless maw, baring its fangs, and released a
pressure far greater than anything before. But Shatia did not retreat.
She simply looked up at the giant snake in a daze and raised her palm. A
dazzling light was unleashed, a single line of radiance that incinerated
all before her.

“…!!’

“The poison has already cleared… If that is to be your answer, then so
be it. I shall get serious as well,” Shatia spoke firmly, her eyes
sharpening.

Clenching her fists and releasing the mana within her body, the Witch of
Wisdom floated into the air. Her stage play was beginning—a tale of
chaos, crafted by a witch.
