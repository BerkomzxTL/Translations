# Chapter 12 – Casting Aside Emotion

Shatia was dreaming. She was looking back on those peaceful times when
all the witches were still alive, spending their days researching
magecrafts and delving into the workings of nature.

Long, beautiful silver hair fell over her shoulders in flowing strands.
Possessing a bewitching beauty, the woman leaned back in her chair with
her black pointed hat pulled low over her eyes. Nearby, small birds
chirped in the trees. It was the perfect day for basking in the sun.

“Shatifahlll!”

As Shatia sat in her chair quietly meditating, she heard a young girl’s
voice from out of nowhere. Finding it a bit noisy, she cracked open her
eyes to see a lovely girl with golden hair tied in two bunches, wearing
a long one-piece dress, walking toward her with teary eyes.

“Is something the matter, Emerald?” Though confused, Shatia caught the
approaching Emerald and calmed her down by stroking her head; her hair
was wet.

Through sniffles and occasional sobs Emerald explained, “Fantaretta
splashed water on meee.”

Apparently, the mischievous Fantaretta had been up to her old tricks
again. Shatia let out a heavy, exasperated sigh, “Good grief… always
getting carried away. She never changes.” Then, she snapped her fingers.
Instantly, Emerald’s wet hair was dry. Emerald touched her hair in
surprise.

“Listen, Emerald. Fantaretta didn’t want to hurt you. She just wanted to
play a little,” Shatia spoke as she wiped Emerald’s nose with a
handkerchief taken from her pocket.

“…Yes.” Emerald wore an expression showing she was unconvinced, but
since it was her beloved Shatifahl saying it, she nodded reluctantly.
Watching her, Shatia smiled gently.

“Good girl. You must never, ever retaliate. Make sure you remember
that.”
<br />  
<br />
<br />
The dream broke. When she came to, the blue sky stretched across
Shatia’s field of vision. She immediately tried to sit up, but a
staggering pain surged through her body. Looking closely, her clothes
were shredded once again. She looked as if she had been caught in a
literal storm. Shatia checked her condition and slumped her shoulders,
resting her hand on her forehead in exhaustion.

“…I’ve gone and had a bad dream,” she murmured sadly.

If this had been a dream she’d had yesterday, she could have cherished
it as a fond memory, but with timing, she could feel nothing but malice
in it. *Or did I have such a dream because I was thinking about
Emerald?* Shatia momentarily doubted her own heart. But there was no
time for such thoughts. Enduring the pain, she slowly pushed herself up
to assess the situation.

“I only blacked out for a moment, then… To think I’d be beaten so
soundly by my own pupil. I truly have grown old.”

Technically, she had become a child and should have been rejuvenated,
but given the circumstances, Shatia couldn’t help but mutter such a
thing.

For now, she felt grateful just to be alive. Even if she had been caught
off guard, taking a hit like that was a serious blunder. Shatia gave
thanks for her own toughness. Then, she chanted a flight spell and
returned toward the direction she had come from.

Arriving back at the spot where the forest had been carved into a
crater—the place where she had fought Emerald just moments ago—Shatia
discovered the knights frozen in place. Even when she touched them, they
didn’t budge an inch. It was as if they had truly become stone.

“Emerald’s curse… To break it, Emerald herself must release it, or…
there was one other way.”

Feeling sorry for the frozen knights, Shatia slowly closed her eyes in
thought.

To free them, she would have to have Emerald lift the curse. However,
she couldn’t imagine the current Emerald complying. That meant she might
have to resort to the worst-case scenario: killing the caster to force
the curse to break. That was the one thing Shatia didn’t want to do. But
she couldn’t leave innocent people standing here like statues.

As she racked her brain, Shatia suddenly noticed something. The mana
signature she usually felt was gone. The moment she realized this,
Shatia’s face turned pale, and she flew off in a specific direction.

“Moffy…!!”

When she reached the thicket where she had hidden Moffy, the girl was
nowhere to be found. As expected, or rather, as a result of her own
carelessness. Shatia bit her lip hard, cursing her own powerlessness.

She had said she would protect her. She had decided not to let her get
hurt. Yet, she had dragged her childhood friend into a clash between
witches. Shatia wanted to beat herself up for failing to prevent this,
but she forced herself to think calmly.

Moffy was gone, but that didn’t necessarily mean she wasn’t safe yet.
First, it was ten to one that Emerald was the one who took her. And her
goal was to make Shatia taste despair. If that were the case, she could
have just left a corpse behind. The fact that she didn’t meant she
intended to use another method. For instance, making her a sacrifice for
a ritual—trying to toy with Moffy’s death in a blatantly cruel way.

*There may still be time,* thinking this, Shatia thoroughly probed the
surrounding mana. There was a faint reaction from Moffy in the distance.
Emerald would surely be there as well.

“*Tch*… these shackles make it hard to move.”

She tried to head there immediately with flight magic, but her foot
stumbled and her posture tilted. The magic circles etched into her
limbs, the effect of the Shackles of Sin, were draining her mana and
binding her freedom. Finding it utterly bothersome, Shatia channeled
mana into her hands and thrust them directly into the magic circles.

“Don’t underestimate me, Emerald. Moffy, and you too… I will definitely
save both of you.”

With a sound like shattering glass, the magic circles binding Shatia
were all destroyed. A small flame ignited in her eyes as she made a firm
vow.

Because she didn’t want to lose anyone else.

◇

Emerald had arrived at an ancient mountain temple.

Already collapsed and reduced to ruins, the temple was in a disastrous
state; parts of it had crumbled, the ceiling was breached, and sections
of the walls had peeled away. However, for Emerald, this place provided
everything she needed for her ideals.

Cradling the young Moffy in her arms, Emerald reached the altar and laid
her down upon it. The quietly sleeping girl knitted her brow slightly as
she was suddenly bathed in sunlight pouring through the hole in the
ceiling. Emerald softly traced the girl’s cheek.

“Even with such a cute face… evil always lurks in the hearts of humans.
How tragic. Even a girl like you will eventually turn into a hideous
monster.”

She wasn’t so much lecturing as she was simply speaking to Moffy.
Naturally, there was no reply from the sleeping girl. But Emerald wore a
sad, pained expression as she clenched her fist.

“Shatifahl will surely try to save you. And even me… that’s just the
kind of person she is. So kind… truly kind…”

Emerald had foreseen exactly how Shatia would act. With her personality,
she would never resort to killing her. That was the one act Shatia
loathed above all else. Therefore, Emerald would strike at that
weakness, that naiveté. Clenching her fists, her lips trembled as she
continued to speak.

“That is why she must be made to know the ugliness of humanity. So that
she’s never betrayed by humans again, so she’s never toyed with by
humans again… I must be the one to prove it.”

Emerald slammed her fist onto the altar and glared at Moffy.

Precisely because she loved Shatifahl, because she was the one who
raised her, she didn’t want her to ever be killed by humans again. Thus,
even if it meant becoming enemies, Emerald had to prove it: just how
foolish and hideous a species humanity was.

Even if it resulted in her own death, it didn’t matter. If Shatifahl
could live without being interfered with by humans, that would be
enough. That was her only desire. A single tear fell from Emerald’s eye.
Even in a doll’s body, tears still came.

“You shall be my sacrifice. Through an ancient ritual… a devil’s rite
that amplifies the evil in one’s heart, you shall become a hideous
beast.”

Emerald brought her porcelain hand over Moffy’s face. What she was about
to perform was an old ritual. It was a remarkably simple rite, but a
terrifying one with prominent effects. That was why Emerald had come to
this ruined temple.

But as she gathered mana to begin, Emerald suddenly glanced at the
temple wall. She felt an ominous presence. She narrowed her eyes and
stared.

“…Reveal yourself.”

To the naked eye, it was clearly just an ordinary wall. But Emerald had
detected another presence. As she called out, the wall wavered and
distorted. From within the distortion, a demon woman wearing a black
cloak appeared.

“So I was noticed… As expected of one of the Seven Witches, I suppose?”

It was Sheris, the one Shatia had met in the forest. Sheris spoke
testingly while gripping the slender sword at her hip. As if that were a
signal, other demons began to appear from various spots.

“Demons… Why are you lot on this continent?” This was human territory.
Recalling this, Emerald heightened her vigilance as she spoke to them.
She tensed her arms, readying herself to unleash magic at any moment.

“No reason, we just have a small bit of business. It won’t take long,”
Sheris replied nonchalantly while drawing her sword.

Silence fell over the scene. The air was prickling with tension, a heavy
atmosphere that felt as though it might explode at any second. Then,
Sheris stepped forward with one foot and smiled provocatively.

“By order of the Demon Lord, we shall capture you. That is all.”

The moment she finished speaking, Sheris kicked off the ground with
explosive force and dashed forward. Her fellow demons followed suit. And
then, Sheris’s slender blade pierced Emerald’s body.
