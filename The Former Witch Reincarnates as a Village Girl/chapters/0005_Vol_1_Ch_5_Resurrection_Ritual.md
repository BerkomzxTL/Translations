# Chapter 5 – Resurrection Ritual

The place the Skeleton Lord guided Shatia was a cave deep within the
mountains. It was slightly chilly, and the area was deathly silent, with
no sign of any living creatures nearby.

Upon arriving before the cave, Shatia came to a sudden halt. Beside her
stood the Skeleton Lord. All the Sorcerer Skeletons had been left pinned
to the ground by her needle trap.

“Ah, I suppose it would be best to put on a disguise just in case.”

Realizing that being seen in her current form might lead to various
complications, Shatia decided to transform. After looking around the
area, she nodded as if to confirm her decision and cast a spell upon
herself. Shatia’s figure blurred as if shrouded in mist, and when she
reappeared, an adult woman with long, flowing silver hair stood in her
place.

“As for my hair color… I suppose there’s no real need to go out of my
way to change it. Indeed, I’ve certainly taken on a form quite different
from when I was a witch. Back then, I was more… ‘boom’ and ‘bam’ in
certain areas, as it were,” Shatia murmured her impressions while
looking down at her disguised self.

Truth be told, back when she was a witch, Shatia had been quite a
glamorous woman. She possessed a truly seductive appearance. However,
the witch in question had been somewhat oblivious to such matters
herself; she had worn clothes that were little more than revealing
undergarments—aside from her pointed hat—simply because they were easy
to move in. Perhaps partly due to this, she had left a strong impression
as a truly “wicked witch.”

In contrast, Shatia’s current disguised form was rather slender. Her
clothes were a safe choice—merely an enlarged version of the plain cloth
garments she wore as a child. Rather than looking like a full-grown
woman, she appeared more like an innocent young girl who had grown up
slightly and matured; that was the extent of the change.

“It’s not as if I did it consciously, but… perhaps this is what I will
look like when my current self grows up.”

It was the polar opposite of her previous life. Looking pleased, Shatia
felt a sense of novelty at her new appearance. Having confirmed that the
illusion magic was functioning perfectly, she prepared to charge into
the lair of the skeletons’ leader.

“Well then, preparations are complete. It is time to go knock on the
enemy’s door. I hope I find someone with interesting magic I’ve never
seen before… I shall trust you to guide me the rest of the way, Skeleton
Lord.”

“Gruuuu…”

The reason Shatia had come all the way to this cave was partly out of
curiosity. In truth, she had developed a deep interest in the one
controlling the skeletons. To possess the power to manipulate
high-ranking Sorcerer Skeletons and even place a Skeleton Lord under
one’s command… Was it some kind of innate ability? Or perhaps a form of
magecraft? That was what Shatia valued most. Thus, she headed into the
depths of the cave, certain she would find something there that would
satisfy her.

Inside, a long passage stretched out. Footprints remained, indicating
that it was a path that had been traversed multiple times. Shatia held
up a finger and manifested a small fireball. Even that tiny flame
provided enough light to illuminate the surroundings and allow her to
see what lay ahead.

Shatia continued deeper and deeper. Before long, she emerged into a
wide, open space adorned with decorations. With its high ceiling, it
gave off an air of mysticism, reminiscent of something akin to an altar.

“…Who goes there?”

And in the center of that space stood an old man. He had a wrinkled face
with bulging eyes, and a body as gaunt as a corpse. Clad in a
pitch-black robe, the old man slowly turned around as he noticed
Shatia’s presence.

“To enter this sacred place without my permission… how insolent. Have
you no understanding of the importance of this room?” The old man glared
at the intruder with hostility.

“Dear me, my apologies.” However, lacking all manner of caution, Shatia
responded as she nonchalantly approached the altar. “You see, I have not
had many opportunities to leave my village. I pray you can overlook
this.”

The platform was surrounded by pillars of fire and held what looked like
a coffin. Shatia immediately discerned that some kind of ritual was
likely about to take place.

“By the way, I came across this thing while taking a stroll through the
valley. Might it be the one you were controlling?”

“…You wretch. Are you saying you dealt with the Skeleton Lord?”

“Now, I wonder?”

Shatia simply smiled, neither confirming nor denying.

The old man took a closer look at the Skeleton Lord Shatia had brought
with her. It was battered, with marks on its armor as if it had been
skewered by something. Since the enigmatic woman before him didn’t
appear to be a swordswoman, the old man felt a surge of fear.

“Woman, are you of the Order of Knights? A comrade of those lowly
knights I drove back the other day?”

“I see, so that knight was fighting you. It seems I have found the right
person after all.”

“*Tch*… answer my question.”

This time, Shatia ignored him entirely, simply nodding to herself in
satisfaction. The old man felt irritated by her vague words, yet he
didn’t launch an attack immediately.

If this woman truly possessed the power to deal with a Skeleton Lord, he
had to be extremely cautious. The old man shifted slightly to the side,
maintaining his distance as he continued the conversation.

“For what purpose did you come here? To put a stop to my sublime plan?”

“Ohoh, a plan? By all means, do tell me about this plan of yours.”

Shatia finally offered a proper response, and the old man gave a faint
smirk. He seemed incredibly confident in his “sublime plan,” boastfully
raising his nose. Shatia, the curious soul that she was, seemed
unbothered; showing the composure to chat leisurely even in the heart of
enemy territory.

“Kukuku, you wish to know? …Very well, I shall enlighten you.”

The old man suddenly let down his guard. Placing his hands on the
platform, he held them out as if to show off the altar and opened his
mouth wide to speak.

“My plan is to resurrect the Great Witches who were destroyed by that
detestable Hero and create a world of darkness once more!!!” The old man
declared loudly, looking up at the ceiling.

Shatia had to pinch her own arm to keep from bursting into laughter.
After all, one of the “Great Witches” he was trying to resurrect was
standing right in front of him. Though she felt a complicated mix of
emotions, Shatia maintained a calm exterior and let out a breath of
feigned admiration.

“Hoh…”

“I shall have the magnificent Witches create an ideal world of sorcery!
And those ignorant, foolish humans will be made to vanish… Kukuku, that
wish is on the verge of manifesting! Through this ritual of
resurrection!!”

As the old man gestured toward the altar beside him, Shatia confirmed
that it was indeed a resurrection ritual.

*Even for a human, he’s a rather dedicated fellow,* Shatia thought,
revising her opinion of him. She had assumed that witches were
unanimously loathed by humanity, but here was someone who actually
worshipped them. It made her feel quite sentimental.

“Kuku, a woman like you surely cannot comprehend. How wonderful the
Witches are, how divine their existence is…” Mistaking Shatia’s reaction
for shock, the old man went on a boastful, rambling tangent.

As she listened, Shatia began to feel itchy and embarrassed. As someone
unaccustomed to being praised, having her former self called “divine”
and “wonderful” left her unsure of how to react. For the time being, she
covered her mouth to keep from laughing and managed to force a smile.

“My, oh my…” Once the old man finished his boasting, Shatia slowly
lowered her hand, revealing her face as she finally spoke. Her eyes
sparkled with a hint of genuine joy.

“What a truly magnificent plan! So, who do you intend to bring back? Is
it the talkative Fantaretta? Or Cloak, who did nothing but conduct
magical research? Personally, I would like to see Emerald resurrected…”
Forgetting all hostilities, Shatia leaned in and peppered the old man
with questions. She rattled off the names of the other witches one after
another, leaving him with a look of pure shock.

The old man was bewildered. How could this woman, who didn’t even look
of age, recite the names of the witches so fluently? And after making
some questionable leaps in logic, he concluded that Shatia must also be
a fellow worshipper.

“Hoho… I am impressed that one so young knows the names of the Seven
Witches…”

“Umu, well, I have had many opportunities to learn of them.”

Seeing that Shatia had lost all hostility, the old man’s tension
evaporated, and the previous atmosphere of conflict vanished. Even the
Skeleton Lord standing dazed at the entrance shrank back in confusion.

“However… the one I shall resurrect first is not among those you’ve
mentioned…”

The old man held up a single finger and walked toward the altar. Shatia
remained where she was, watching him without interference; rather, she
actually wanted to see the ritual through herself.

The old man stopped before the coffin, touched it, and looked back at
Shatia.

“The one I shall resurrect first is the legendary figure who led the
other six… the Witch of Wisdom, Shatifahl-sama!!!”

It took Shatia a moment to process what she had just heard. She hadn’t
expected her own former name to come up. She stared blankly at the old
man, who was busy puffing out his chest with pride.

When it finally dawned on her that the person he was trying to bring
back was *her*, she was overcome once again by an indescribable mix of
feelings.

“O-Ohh…”

“The one who led all other Witches. *She*, who rivals even the sages and
possesses power that stands head and shoulders above the rest!! Ohhh,
Shatifahl-sama, I shall resurrect you this very instant!!”

With entranced eyes, the old man called out to the ceiling in a
cloyingly sweet voice. But the reality was that the Witch Shatifahl was
none other than the woman right in front of him. Or rather, the young
girl using illusion magic. Shatia watched the prancing old man with a
complicated look on her face.

She was honestly happy to be worshipped. She had some reservations;
namely, his obsession, and that he had drawn essence from the mountains
for the ritual. But if she overlooked those, there was no doubt he was a
beneficial existence for the witches. However, even if he performed the
ritual here, Shatifahl would not resurrect. Because she already existed.

Knowing he wouldn’t believe her even if she told him, Shatifahl cradled
her head in her hands. In the meantime, the old man began his final
preparations, gripping his staff and channeling mana.

“Now, the time has come! The resurrection of the mightiest Witch,
Shatifahl-sama!!”

Converting the essence collected by the skeletons into mana, the old man
poured a massive amount of power into his staff. A pale light began to
leak from the altar, shining divinely. The Skeleton Lord shrank back and
hid in a recess of the cave. But even as a gust of wind from the altar
struck her, Shatia remained unmoving, gazing blankly at the old man.

*Now, what to do? By what means can I get him to stop the ritual?* It
was obvious that the ritual would fail. Should she guide him to
resurrect a different witch instead? But persuading such a devout
believer would be difficult. Perhaps she should just knock him
unconscious?

For the time being, Shatia decided to set aside such concerning thoughts
and called out to him, “Hey, I should tell you, you would be better off
stopping.”

“Fuhahaha! It is too late for that now!!”

Shatia waved her hand to catch his attention, but the old man wouldn’t
listen. To achieve his long-cherished goal, he was no longer interested
in hearing the voice of an outsider.

A whirlwind erupted around the altar. Though she had nowhere to escape,
Shatia remained carefree; showing no sign of panic, she drew closer to
the center of the altar simply to avoid being tossed by the wind.

The old man’s staff suddenly shone. He struck it against the ground
while chanting a spell. Light scattered with a burst, and the howling
wind vanished. The altar lost its glow, and the area was momentarily
shrouded in silence.

The old man, believing the ritual had succeeded, twisted his wrinkled
face into a smile. However, what emerged from the coffin was a black,
liquid-like hand.

“W-What is this…!?”

Instead of Shatifahl, there was a creepy black mass; the old man,
shocked, fell onto his backside and scrambled away from the spot. As he
fled to Shatia’s side, Shatia let out a small sigh and stared at the
black entity across from her.

At first, only hands emerged from the coffin, but soon the black entity
thrust out what appeared to be legs and manifested itself, walking on
two feet just like a human. However, it had nothing recognizable such as
a face—just a bizarre shape with limbs growing out of its center. It
didn’t even resemble a monster.

“Good grief, you’ve gone and summoned it.”

“W-What is the meaning of this!? I was supposed to resurrect
Shatifahl-sama!! W-What is that black thing!!?”

The old man pointed and questioned in distress at the entity that was
clearly not Shatifahl. And, looking as annoyed as ever, Shatia brushed
aside her hair from her forehead and slowly began to explain.

“If a resurrection ritual does not follow the correct procedures, it
summons a wicked soul… a malevolent creature born from the accumulation
of negative emotions such as hatred, resentment, and envy.”

The black creature reacted with a twitch. Suddenly, its body began to
bulge and transform: its arms became thick as logs, its legs grew sharp
claws like that of a dragon’s, and the middle of its torso split open
into the shape of a mouth, turning it into a true monster.

“Welcome, O Evil Spirit. Return to the other side as you are, and I,
too, shall do nothing. However, should you intend to cause harm, I will
respond accordingly.”

Shatia bowed politely to the black monster; the evil spirit. It was
unclear if her words were understood, but the evil spirit began to
tremble. In the next instant, black needles erupted from its body.
Shatia dodged the needles stretching toward her, and the old man behind
her scrambled to hide behind cover.

Gently tracing a finger over a needle that had passed just centimeters
away, Shatia’s lips curled into a small smile.

“A ‘no,’ is it? Then I suppose I shall pay you back in kind.”

Shatia clenched her hand into a tight fist, poured as much mana as she
could into it, and swung it down with great force. A wave of energy, as
powerful as a cannon blast, erupted from the swing, swallowing the evil
spirit and slamming it into the rock wall far behind the altar.
