# Chapter 13 – Devil’s Rite

“…Do you truly believe a witch dies from something of that magnitude?”

Pierced through the chest by the sword, Emerald slumped over as she
looked toward the heavens. But then, with an eerie mechanical swivel,
her head slowly rotated. Her eyes, glowing and stained with madness,
were now fixed on Sheris.

Sheris was struck with horror. No matter how terrifying the magecraft a
witch might wield, they shouldn’t be unharmed after having their body
run through with a blade. But evidence to defy that logic was right
before her. Emerald showed no sign of pain whatsoever, meeting her gaze
with a chillingly cold stare.

“Commander, fall back! We’ll handle this…!”

Seeing that being impaled had little effect, Sheris’s subordinates cried
out and stepped forward to shield their commander. Emerald, however, was
completely unmoved by their interference; she placed a hand over her
eyes and let out a soft, trailing laugh.

Sensing an imminent attack, Sheris immediately yanked her sword free and
retreated. Yet, her comrades remained frozen in place as they faced the
witch. Detecting something was wrong, she ordered them to withdraw, but
there was no response. Sheris found herself looking back at Emerald.
There, Emerald was letting out a sinister chuckle while wearing a
mocking smile.

“What did you do… to my men?”

“I merely let them sleep for a while… Though, I doubt they shall ever
wake again.”

When Sheris tested her with the question, Emerald revealed the cruel
truth. She indicated toward the motionless demons; Sheris followed the
gesture and saw their state—

Her men stood rigid, as if turned to stone. They were utterly
unresponsive to her voice, as silent as the dead. Sheris ground her
teeth, her grip tightening on her sword until her knuckles turned white.

“Pray, do not be conceited, O Demon. Even if your magical power is
somewhat superior to that of a human, from our perspective as witches,
the difference is trifling. Both are equally nothing but dust.”

Emerald directed those frigid words at the despair-stricken Sheris.

Sheris realized anew just how terrifying a witch truly was. Their
outward form might resemble a human’s, but the contents were something
else entirely. They harbored a frightening power that could only be
described as monstrous.

*There’s no way to win against such a thing*—Sheris’s intuition screamed
in warning.

Seeing Sheris’s reaction, Emerald’s expression turned sorrowful. “Ahh…
you, too, look at me with those eyes, as if viewing a monster. Even your
king, the Demon Lord, possesses similar power… So why then? Why is it
that us witches are so persecuted?”

Why were they so terrified by a mere difference in magical capacity?
Their own king possessed comparable power. Why was there such a vast
difference in treatment just because she was a witch? Emerald had
struggled to understand. But now she no longer cared to. 

Unreasonable humans. Demons who loathed the foreign. Disparate races…
all equally foolish. Therefore, they were to be eliminated.

“Yes, since long ago. Born with a power beyond comprehension, branded
with a devil’s curse… I have always been viewed as something *other*.
Feared, shunned… I desired friendship, yet I was met with stones and
contempt…”

Emerald raised her palm and slowly clenched it. Her doll-like body made
a weak, rattling sound, a testament to her tragic state.

“I shall no longer seek mutual understanding. If you intend to obstruct
me, then I shall be the one to cast you away. Into the depths of the
murky darkness.”

As she spoke, she channeled mana into her clenched fist. A tremendous
amount of energy accumulated, enough to make the very air tremble.

She had given up on making others understand witches. She had once
reached an understanding with the people of a town, only for them to
betray her on the orders of the kingdom’s men, leading to the witches
being slaughtered by the Hero. To Emerald, that event was more shocking
than death itself.

She never wanted to be betrayed again. She never wanted to be hurt
again. Driven by that desire, Emerald sought no more connections. It was
all a waste of time.

“*Shackles of Sin*.”

Emerald swept her fingers through the air, manifesting mana shackles
around Sheris’s body. Sheris struggled as her limbs were bound, but her
mana was drained away quickly, leaving her arms feeling as heavy as if
tethered to boulders.

“Ngh… ugh!?”

“The cursed eyes would freeze even your consciousness. I prefer you to
remain awake. Stay there and watch.” With that ominous remark, Emerald
headed toward the altar, her arms rattling with every step.

The Shackles of Sin was a spell primarily designed to restrict the
target’s actions. While it hadn’t had much effect on Shatia, she was an
outlier; it possessed enough potency to render an ordinary person
immobile. Robbed of movement by the shackles, Sheris could only bite her
lip in frustration. She was seized by the fear of being killed, but to
her surprise, Emerald turned her back on her. 

“What… do you intend to do…?”

“Merely killing you would not satisfy my rage. Therefore, I shall have
you witness the moment a human falls into hideous evil. Well, it is only
a child of a hostile race becoming a grotesque creature. Surely there’s
no need for you to be so concerned?”

Emerald answered Sheris’s question nonchalantly, tilting her head as if
seeking agreement. 

Sheris was left speechless. No matter how much they were enemies, she
couldn’t bear to watch a child who knew nothing of war be used as a
sacrifice. And yet, she couldn’t move an inch. Cold sweat poured down
Sheris’s forehead.

In the meantime, Emerald began the preparations for the ritual. Standing
once more before the sleeping Moffy, she raised her arms, closed her
eyes, and began to mumble an incantation.

“…You also came here seeking my power as a witch, did you not? How
foolish. You despise what we are, yet you covet our strength. Truly,
such greedy creatures.”

Stopping momentarily, Emerald cast a glance at Sheris and threw those
words at her. Sheris could say nothing in response; she could only watch
in silence as Emerald proceeded with the ritual.

“Now… the preparations are complete. All that remains is to wait for the
mana to accumulate… In the meantime, shall I tell you a story of long
ago?”

Having finished the incantation, a massive magic circle shimmered above
the altar. Emerald, a single bead of sweat trickling down her cheek,
nodded with satisfaction. The ritual was ready; all it needed was the
required amount of mana to be poured into that giant circle. Emerald
decided to kill time by talking to Sheris.

“A story… you say?”

“Yes. It’s like a common fairy tale. Though, it doesn’t have a happy
ending.”

Emerald placed one hand on her hip and waved the other as she gave a
warning. The “doesn’t have a happy ending” unsettled Sheris, but she
knew she had no right of refusal, so she waited in silence for Emerald
to begin.

“It was five hundred… no, seven hundred years ago? In those ancient
times, a single girl lived in a certain village.”

Emerald closed her eyes, speaking slowly, immersing herself in the
memory. Her voice was very gentle, yet it possessed a sorrowful,
ephemeral quality.

“The girl was different from others. She possessed immense amounts of
mana, grew slowly, and remained in the form of a young girl forever…
People called her a monster and looked down upon her.”

Emerald pressed her hand to her chest, her voice laced with sadness. In
fact, her expression looked as though she were crying. No matter how
broken she had become, her heart remained pure—though that purity was
now directed toward pure evil.

“Eventually, the villagers all died of old age, leaving only the girl
behind… Left behind by time, the girl suffered in loneliness and wept
alone, day after day.”

As she listened, Sheris felt it was a strange tale. A girl who did not
age. It was an odd protagonist for a fairy tale. It didn’t feel like a
fairy tale at all. Normally, a hero or a knight or *someone* should
appear to save her.

Sensing Sheris’s doubt, Emerald smiled faintly and reached the hand she
had held against her chest toward the sky.

“…However, a savior appeared before the girl. That person was the same
as the girl, possessing a mysterious power different from others. They
were searching for and gathering children just like the girl.”

For a moment, a light kindled in Emerald’s eyes. Her tone returned to
its former gentleness. She looked like an ordinary girl. Perhaps this
Emerald, with her gentle smile, was her true self. But the light quickly
vanished, and she lowered her arm with an inorganic clatter.

“The girl gained five sisters and a mother. It was a world more warm and
gentle than anything she had ever felt before.”

Emerald lowered her head. She didn’t continue for a while, remaining
silent as if lost in thought. Sheris didn’t try to speak either. Not
knowing what Emerald might do, she didn’t dare open her mouth.

“And yet… humans destroyed that gentle world. They killed the girl, they
killed her sisters, and they even killed the woman who was like a mother
to those children…”

Suddenly, Emerald’s voice dropped to a low, terrifying register. What
had started as a fairy tale had abruptly transformed into a sinister,
pitch-black, cruel story. Sheris’s eyes widened as she connected the
pieces.

“Could it be, that’s…”

Sheris tried to speak, but before she could finish, Emerald turned her
palm toward her and used magic to seal her mouth. Sheris let out a
muffled groan, her body trembling in distress.

“The girl despaired, but hope remained. Her mother had survived… So, to
ensure she would never be treated cruelly again, the girl vowed to
protect her… Well, that is the end of the story.”

“…!”

When Emerald lowered her hand and released the spell, Sheris gasped for
air, trying to steady her breathing. The sensation of being
suppressed—as if her throat were being squeezed—remained. Perspiring
heavily, Sheris looked at Emerald. She couldn’t see her expression from
where she stood, but the girl radiated an aura of profound sadness.

“Now, a question. To protect the mother, and to make the humans
understand their own foolishness, what should one do?” Suddenly
contorting her back and moving her face with a series of creaks, Emerald
posed the question to Sheris. Sheris couldn’t answer such a nonsensical
query. But she felt a terrible premonition.

And then, the massive magic circle above Moffy began to rotate, glowing
with a divine brilliance. Finally, it was fully charged. Seeing this,
Emerald’s smile deepened. She slowly opened her mouth.

“The correct answer was: ‘Turn the humans into monsters through a
ritual.’ Now! The mana is gathered. Let the ritual begin!”

“What… are you… You’re crazy… Insane…!”

Unable to comprehend how one could arrive at such a conclusion, Sheris
spoke as she shook her head slightly in terror. But her words did not
reach Emerald. Reaching her arms toward the glowing magic circle,
Emerald chanted the final words of the rite:

“Awaken, O heart of evil!!”

The magic circle stopped its rotation, and the runes within began to
shake violently. Emitting a blinding radiance, Moffy’s body vanished
into the light. The ritual had begun.

Through amplifying the evil that lay in her heart, her body would
transform into a hideous monster.
