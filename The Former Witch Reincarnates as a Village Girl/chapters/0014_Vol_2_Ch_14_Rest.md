# Chapter 14 – Rest

Enveloped in a dazzling light, the evil sleeping within Moffy’s heart
was amplified, transforming her very form into that of a monster… or so
it should have. However, as the light subsided, Moffy remained
unchanged, sleeping as peacefully as she had been moments before.

“…!?”

Emerald, who had been looking up at the heavens, regained her stance,
unable to believe her eyes.

While there were individual differences in how a subject transforms, it
was impossible for there to be no reaction at all. Why was nothing
happening? Had the ritual failed? Was the mana insufficient? Had some
sort of malfunction occurred? Emerald predicted and analyzed various
possibilities, but the cause remained a mystery.

“W-Why…?! Why is nothing happening?! The malice in her heart should have
amplified, transforming her into a beast… so why is there no change in
the girl?!”

Tearing at her golden hair, Emerald shrieked like a madwoman. She
stomped the ground, her irritation palpable as things failed to go
according to plan.

It was impossible. The ritual had been activated perfectly. There wasn’t
a single element that should have caused failure. It was certainly not a
lack of skill on Emerald’s part. Then why did Moffy remain unchanged?
Having a faint inkling of the truth, Emerald reflexively bit her lip
until it bled.

“Impossible… it’s impossible, impossible, impossible! There’s no way
this is happening! Something is definitely wrong, something is,
something…!!”

A stream of denials poured from Emerald’s mouth. Unable to accept the
reality before her, her eyes darted about as she repeated the same words
over and over like a broken doll.

At that moment, the temple shook violently, and a portion of the wall
was blown away by a blast. Debris scattered, and Emerald crouched low to
avoid the impact. When she lowered her arms and looked up, there stood a
silver-haired girl.

“Certainly, your ritual was indeed perfect. But Emerald… you made one
single mistake.”

Shatia walked through the temple, her footsteps echoing steadily as she
approached Emerald and the altar. Along the way, she spotted Sheris,
whose movements were still sealed by the magical shackles, and gave a
light wave of her hand.

“Sorry, Sheris, but I need you to sleep for a bit.”

“What… ngh…”

Before Sheris could even turn her neck to see who had appeared, Shatia
put her to sleep with a spell and continued toward Emerald as if nothing
had happened. Emerald’s vigilance spiked. She had managed to repel
Shatia temporarily once before, but the same trick wouldn’t work twice
against her. This time, luck would not be on her side. Emerald funneled
mana into her palm, hidden from Shatia’s sight.

“Shatifahl… a mistake? Exactly what kind of mistake do you claim I
made?!”

She needed to buy time. Besides, Shatia’s mention of a “mistake”
bothered her. Emerald swung her free arm as she spoke. Shatia stopped
walking, casually crossed her arms, and opened her mouth.

“It’s simple. Despite how much you were betrayed and how much human
folly you witnessed, there are still those who possess a heart as pure
as yours once was,” Shatia explained with ease.

Even after all that betrayal, even after seeing the depths of human
foolishness, she still believed in them. Emerald bit down harder on her
cut lip. Dark blood trickled down to her neck.

“Liar… there’s no way that’s true.”

“Adults may differ, but children almost all possess a pure heart. Surely
you remember? You used to play with the village children all the time.
You should know this better than anyone.”

“No… that can’t be… humans, humans are—!!”

Emerald vehemently denied Shatia’s words. Clutching her head, she
continued to deny it over and over like a child throwing a tantrum. The
sight was agonizing; for Shatia, who knew the girl she used to be, it
was heart-wrenching to witness.

“Wake up already, Emerald. What you are doing is a meaningless act that
only breeds further conflict—the thing I loathe most. Release Moffy this
instant.”

Shatia took a step forward and spoke with commanding authority. Emerald
let out a small shriek as if struck by the words themselves and
recoiled. She looked at the sleeping Moffy, then back at Shatia, shaking
her head in disbelief.

Emerald no longer had a clear purpose. Consumed by a desire for revenge
that had cost her her reason, she didn’t know how to free herself from
her suffering. She was simply committing slaughters as her madness
dictated. Thus, no matter how many humans she killed from here on, her
madness would never be quelled. She would be remembered by people as a
murderer for eternity, a Witch of Terror. Shatia could never allow that.
She would absolutely not let it happen. Shatia’s eyes sharpened, and she
clenched her fists.

“I… I just… aah… aaaaaah!!”

Emerald curled into herself, trembling violently. The mana she had been
storing surged to the surface, erupting from her body in the shape of
ominous, pitch-black shadows. It was a mana rampage. Having lost her
sense of self, she had lost control over the massive power she had been
containing.

The rampaging mana coalesced into a massive sphere and shot toward
Shatia. However, Shatia raised a hand and simply caught the mass with
her own mana. The sound of magical forces grinding against each other
shrieked through the air, sending vibrations rippling through the
surroundings.

“AAAAAAAAAAAAAHHHHH!!!!”

“A mana rampage… you’re acting just like a child, Emerald.”

Emerald collapsed to the ground, wailing. In contrast, the mass of mana
distorted further, and the power overflowing from Emerald’s body rained
down on Shatia. Like a hail of bullets, the massive clusters of mana
attacked all at once.

Shatia swung her other hand to deflect the attacks, then sliced through
the reaching mana vines, striding toward Emerald without a moment’s
hesitation.

“Stay back… stay back, stay back, stay BAAACK!!”

Rejecting Shatia’s relentless approach, Emerald shoved her arms forward;
it almost appeared like a cry for help. The mass of mana formed into a
giant human arm, reaching out to crush Shatia. But in an instant, the
mana arm burst apart. It had been erased by a powerful pulse of mana
emitted by Shatia herself. Again, Emerald let out a terrified shriek.

“Why… why… how?! Why is this happening?! I hate this. I just wanted to
live in peace with everyone again, without being bothered by anyone…!
It’s the humans who are wrong! Those stupid, wicked-hearted, greedy
monsters…!!”

Emerald’s mana thrashed frantically. It could hardly even be called
magic anymore. It was a violent, desperate style of fighting; simply
forcing her will upon the world with raw power. Naturally, such a sloppy
method could not defeat Shatia. No matter how many outbursts of mana
were thrown her way, Shatia parried and deflected them all with ease.
Step by step, she closed the distance until she was only a few paces
away.

“What did we do?! We were persecuted just for having a different power…
so we lived quietly, hiding in the forests and deep within mountains…
and yet, just because we were ‘dangerous,’ the humans betrayed us!!”

Creating countless whips of mana, Emerald swung both arms down. However,
they were stopped by a barrier. This time, Shatia channeled power into
her arms and pushed forward, hitting Emerald with a massive shockwave.
Though she wasn’t blown away, the protective mass of mana was stripped
from her, leaving Emerald’s body vulnerable. In that moment, Shatia was
right in front of her. Looking up at the small girl, Emerald began to
sob.

“Why… *Hic*, why was I born with this power?”

The words Emerald finally spoke were not of hatred for humans, but a
question regarding her own nature as a witch. Shatia froze. Those were
words she had heard once before. When they first met, Emerald had said
the exact same thing. Her expression then had been profoundly sad, just
as it was now. Shatia’s eyes wavered.

“Emerald…”

“…Ngh …AAAAAAHHHH!”

A moment of hesitation. Shatia had left an opening once again. The
moment she got close, she should have knocked Emerald unconscious or
otherwise incapacitated her. But Shatia had been shaken by Emerald’s
words at the last moment. As a result, the situation took a turn for the
absolute worst.

Emerald suddenly clutched her chest and let out a cry of agony. It was a
different kind of rampage from the one before. Something far more
terrible.

“This isn’t a rampage… no. Could it be… ‘Collapse’!”

Shatia broke into a sweat as she analyzed Emerald’s condition.

Emerald had forcibly used possession magic on a doll’s body she had no
particular attachment toward. In truth, it had been a failure. Perhaps
because she was a witch, she managed not to lose her soul to the
backlash, and she survived by transplanting parts of her original body.
But there were limits. And now, those limits had been reached, triggered
by the mana rampage.

The doll body, no longer able to contain the soul, began to crumble.
Parts fell away like peeling scales, and Emerald slumped to the ground,
unable to maintain her posture. Furthermore, because her mana was still
rampaging, immense power was constantly leaking from her body. It was
truly a worst-case scenario.

“AAAAAHHH! No… ahh… I don’t, want to… die…! AAAAAHHH!!”

“Emerald…!”

Forced to step back by a gale of mana, Shatia was nearly blown away. She
managed to endure, but Emerald continued to shriek, unable to suppress
either the mana or the physical collapse. At this rate, Emerald’s body
would disintegrate entirely; her soul would be lost, and she would truly
die. Even worse was the rampaging mana. It wouldn’t be surprising if a
self-destruction occurred that could level the entire area. Shatia
realized she had to do something and pushed through the forces swirling
around.

“Forgive me… It was due to my own lack of strength. I didn’t have the
capacity to save you,” Shatia placed a hand gently on the suffering
Emerald’s head and made her pained confession.

“Shati… fahl… no, it’s my… it’s my fault… I…”  Though struggling in
agony, there was no longer any madness. Clarity had returned to
Emerald’s eyes. “I… I couldn’t believe in humans until the end… I
forgot. I forgot the potential of the humans I once believed in… because
of my foolish desire for revenge.”

Emerald’s doll body began to crumble from the feet up. Losing her
balance and falling to the floor, Emerald looked up at Shatia. Each word
pierced Shatia’s heart, and she covered her mouth with her hand,
fighting back tears.

“I wonder why… why couldn’t I make it work…? I was supposed to want to
get along with humans… why did it turn out like this?” Emerald whispered
her final wish.

Once, she had desired coexistence with humanity and wished for peace
more than anyone. Where had she gone wrong? How had things become so
irreversible? She shed tears blackened by impurity.

“…Do not worry. When you next awaken, the world you desire will surely
be there.”

Shatia offered Emerald a motherly smile. Speaking in a tender voice, she
spread her palms and released a multitude of magic circles.

“So, for the time being, rest… *Lullaby*.”

Dazzling light scattered all around, dyeing the world white. Then, the
light converged into a single point, and everything vanished. Along with
the scattered spheres of light, Emerald’s mana rampage subsided. Having
lost the mana that served as her power source, her head slowly slumped
forward, and the light faded from her eyes.
