# Chapter 36: The Gale Dragonkin

Lizardmen. A race that worships the dragon said to have once enveloped
the earth in endless flame.

Born of the primordial dragon, the Lizardmen believed that dragon to be
their god. Their faith was so absolute and unwavering that they loathed
all other gods and sacred entities. Among these, the Witches—known as
anomalous existences—were the most detestable. History recorded a time
when the two forces once clashed.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
The Lizardmen of the Red Dragon Tribe, characterized by their crimson
scales, marched through the Mountain of Beasts in formation. There was
no idle chatter, they simply stared straight ahead and moved in silence.
Their procession was like that of a disciplined army.

Within this swarm of Lizardmen was one individual of particularly
massive stature. His body, already covered in hard scales, was further
clad in armor fashioned from more scales, and a scar ran across his
forehead. He was the chief of this tribe. Occasionally, he would let out
a guttural roar to urge his comrades forward, and upon hearing it, the
surrounding Lizardmen would quicken their pace.

“Gugorulululu!”

The Lizardman Chief flicked his long tongue and let out a call. He then
turned his face toward the rear of the line. There, members of the demon
race were being forced to walk, bound in chains and held captive. Since
the demons were all women or children, they could barely offer any
resistance, moving their legs in silence with pained expressions. The
Chief watched them and let out a satisfied growl, raising his arm to
command the march to continue.

“Gugoaaaahhhhh!!”

As the Lizardman Chief let out a war cry, the surrounding Lizardmen
responded in kind. They raised their arms, expressing joy, and
accelerated their stride. As if drawn by something, they pressed deeper
into the heart of the mountain. There was no hesitation in their steps;
they knew no fear.

Observing all of this from atop a cliff, Shatia let out a thoughtful
“hmm” and tilted her head. She shifted her gaze to the restrained
demons, placing a hand to her chin and contemplating the situation.

“What on earth do they plan to do with those demons?”

Shatia had been observing the Lizardmen’s movements for several minutes
and had managed to gather some information. For now, two things were
clear: they were heading toward a specific destination, and they
intended to do something with the captured demons. However, those facts
lacked specific detail. If possible, Shatia wanted more.

“They likely intend to perform a ritual of some kind… but can those with
so little mana even perform a ritual? Their objective remains unclear.”

Because they were transporting suspicious-looking tools, Shatia had
surmised that the Lizardmen intended to conduct a ritual. However, a
ritual should require a certain amount of mana. Naturally, as Lizardmen
possessed only trace amounts of the energy, they were ill-suited for
such tasks. This led to the hypothesis that they might intend to make
the demons perform the ritual—but would they, those who loathe other
races, truly try to utilize the power of demons? Since there were still
such ambiguities, Shatia had yet to reach a conclusion. Biting her lip
lightly, Shatia pondered her next move.

“…Hm?”

Suddenly, Shatia noticed the toxic-looking flora around her swaying. A
purple leaf fluttered down from a branch overhead, passing right beside
her face. Immediately after, a gale was unleashed toward her. The small
tornado let out a roar as if it were swallowing the world, scattering
the plants in its wake. Shatia evaded at the last second and turned her
eyes toward the assailant.

“Attacking without warning… I am not impressed. Who are you?”

“Gugyululu…”

It was an individual dressed quite strangely for a Lizardman. A slender
Lizardman wearing a feathered hat and a red mantle. His features were
sharper than the others, and his physique was rare for the Red Dragon
Tribe. In his hand, he gripped a long spear, its tip honed as sharp as a
dragon’s fang.

“Oh dear, I suppose I should introduce myself first. My name is Shatia.
Just a simple villager.”

Realizing she should name herself before asking for another’s, Shatia
lowered her head in an apologetic gesture. Whether the words were
understood or not, the Lizardman only let out a small growl. Shatia was
unable to hear anything resembling a proper response and shrugged her
shoulders in disappointment. Suddenly, the Lizardman kicked off the
ground and leaped instantly from the spot.

A gust of wind erupted. Whether it was a power inherent to the Lizardman
or some ability of the spear he held, he soared through the air on the
wind and lunged at Shatia. She instantly manifested a magic shield,
however, it was effortlessly shattered by the spear the Lizardman swung,
and Shatia was blown back slightly.

“Oh… My, my… As expected of a Lizardman. Such terrifying physical
strength,” staggering slightly but regaining her posture, Shatia spoke
with a twitch of her lips in amusement.

As one might expect from those who carry the blood of a dragon, their
power was immense. To destroy even a mana-infused shield with just a
spear meant that a direct hit to one’s physical body would be
devastating. While recognizing anew just how fearsome a race the
Lizardmen were, Shatia didn’t let her composed expression falter.

“A Gale Lizardman, is it… You have an interesting fighting style.”

“Gruaaaaahhhhh!!”

The Lizardman let out a roar, his eyes glinting from the gap under his
hat, and swung his spear down at Shatia. She used flight magic to
quickly retreat from the spot, evading the attack.

“You are fast… but I have some confidence in my speed as well,” she said
as she raised a hand and recited a magic. As she did, numerous magic
circles appeared behind her, and from them, thorns surged forth.

The Lizardman immediately kicked the ground to retreat backward, but the
thorns continued to pursue him. Resolving himself, the Lizardman gripped
his spear with both hands, swinging it to shred the incoming thorns into
pieces.

“Grululu…!”

Shatia whistled in admiration at his swift response. On the other hand,
the Lizardman was filled with growing agitation after completing the
maneuver.

In reality, he held the role of commander, the leader of the Lizardman
soldiers, a warrior of such skill that the Chief had personally granted
him the title “Knight of the Gale.” Furthermore, he was the leader of
the scouting team. After an anomaly occurred—losing contact with his
comarade—he had quickly realized an enemy was lurking nearby and bared
his fangs the moment he found Shatia.

Initially, when he discovered her, he was disappointed, thinking she was
just a child. But even if she were a child, he couldn’t allow her to
live after she had seen them. Above all, she was of a different race.
That alone was reason enough to eliminate her. He had swung his spear,
intending to kill her with the gale. But, to his shock, the child before
him had dodged his strike and was even using magic. In an instant, the
child transformed into a dangerous enemy.

Through this exchange, the Lizardman gradually came to understand the
staggering gap between his power and Shatia’s. He was fighting with
everything he had, yet the child before him was not showing even a hint
of her true strength. On the contrary, she had the composure to
intentionally prolong a fight she could end instantly, merely observing
and studying him instead. It was truly terrifying. To the Lizardman,
Shatia looked like nothing more than a monster wearing the skin of a
child.

“Gyululu…”

Even so, not once did he lower his spear. No matter how great the
difference in power, he was the one granted the position of commander by
the chief. He couldn’t allow himself to back down before an enemy. Thus,
despite understanding Shatia’s strength, he bared his fighting spirit, a
flame ignited in his eyes. Tensing his entire body and sharpening his
senses, he quietly leveled his spear.

“Grululuuaaaaaaaaaaaaaa!!!”

Then, letting out a roar so massive it seemed to make the earth tremble,
the Lizardman kicked off the ground. He kicked off trees and branches,
leaping through the area with total freedom. His greatest pride was his
speed. This was a fighting style that utilized it to the maximum. No
matter how great the power gap, it mattered not as long as he could
strike an opening. He continued to move at high speed, leaping about as
he drew closer to Shatia.

“Hoh…!”

Shatia’s eyes sparkled at the movement, which could almost be described
as beautiful. Even at this stage, her composure didn’t vanish. No matter
what power the Lizardman displayed, she was not the least bit
intimidated. Realizing he was lunging from behind, Shatia manifested a
barrier at her back without even turning around. A dull thud echoed, and
the Lizardman let out a groan. However, he utilized her barrier, kicking
off from it and retreating, beginning his high-speed movement once more.

“Interesting… Truly interesting.” To use the opponent’s magic to retreat
even when stopped—Shatia was impressed by his superb adaptability.
Finally, she opened her unmoving palm and clapped with her other hand.

She recognized the enemy before her as one worthy of his rank. With that
respect in mind, she began to exercise her magecraft.

“But I am sorry. I do not have the time to indulge in your acrobatics
forever,” narrowing her eyes and speaking coldly, Shatia activated her
spell.

A massive magic circle appeared behind her, and from it, a massive,
monstrous arm surged forth. Boasting overwhelming size, the arm
disregarded the Lizardman’s speed entirely; by simply swinging down, it
forcibly halted his movement. A thunderous roar resounded, and the
Lizardman, crushed by the arm, let out a groan as his body spasmed on
the ground.

“Gugo… Gah…!”

*Impossible. This can’t be happening,* the Lizardman protested in his
heart.

That he, born of the primordial dragon, should be cornered by such a
meager existence was something that should not be. Lizardmen were the
ones who stood at the pinnacle of living beings. There should never be
an existence that could shake that fact. There *must* not be.

The Lizardman raised his voice and forced himself up, “Rrr… Gaaaaahhh!!”

Gripping the spear he still held, the Lizardman took his stance once
more. Shatia watched, her mouth slightly agape in surprise as she
retracted the giant arm back into the magic circle.

The Lizardman thrust his leg forward. One step. Then another. He
approached Shatia steadily. To drive his spear home, he put strength
into his arm and pulled back his hip. However, his consciousness had
already flickered out. His legs buckled as if his strings had been cut,
and the Lizardman collapsed on the spot, his eyes rolling back. Even so,
the tip of his spear remained pointed firmly at Shatia, his resistance
remaining until the very end.
