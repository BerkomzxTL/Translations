# Chapter 20 – Exam

Being an academy, exams were an absolute certainty. Among them, the
exams designed to gauge one’s magical proficiency were quite
rigorous—sometimes so grueling that students would leave the academy
entirely. Such terrifying exams took place once a month. And this month,
the day of the exam had arrived once again.

All first-year students, including Shatia, were gathered at the
examination venue. The hall was vast enough to fit an entire castle,
with an endlessly high ceiling and meticulously maintained walls and
floors. Feeling her sense of perspective warping from the sheer scale of
the room, Laika felt a slight dizziness and moved to the very edge of
the class group, more withdrawn than usual.

“The exam is today… I wonder what sort of thing we will be doing?”
Shatia approached and struck up a conversation, sensing Laika’s
hesitance.

“Ah, Shatia-chan.” Laika’s shoulders gave a small jolt of surprise, but
she quickly offered a weak smile and moved closer to Shatia’s side.

A few minutes later, the teacher arrived. He was a man with a
powerful-looking face, a beard, and thick eyebrows: Professor Regis.

He appeared to be slightly older than Loreid, exuding an air of dignity.
His sharp, predatory eyes gave him the impression of an eagle. His
lessons tended to emphasize practical combat, and his overly intense
attitude made him somewhat feared by the students. Shatia, however,
found him interesting. Naturally, Laika was among the students who
feared him; the moment she saw Professor Regis, she hid behind Shatia.

“Quiet down. The theme of this exam is survival. I will now distribute
one bracelet to each of you. If you reach the goal while wearing one or
more of these, you pass.”

Professor Regis cleared his throat, drawing the students’ gaze as he
explained the contents of the exam. With a snap of his fingers,
bracelets appeared before the students, and he instructed them to put
them on.

Shatia and Laika did as they were told. It was a simple,
ordinary-looking bracelet with gold decorations. It wasn’t particularly
heavy, nor did it seem to have any specific magic cast upon it. The
students stared at them curiously.

“You can wear as many bracelets as you like. Let me just say that the
more you wear, the higher your evaluation will be… though of course, you
may simply reach the goal normally. By the way, there are no
restrictions. You are free to do whatever you wish.”

Hearing Professor Regis’s explanation, Shatia narrowed her eyes.

The way he spoke was provocative, as if he were explicitly telling them
to steal the bracelets from one another. Indeed, several students wore
knowing smiles; they had likely realized the true nature of this exam.
Shatia understood it as well, but she didn’t have the luxury of smiling.
She slowly shifted her gaze to Laika.

“Eh… so that means…”

In short, it was a survival match. In this abnormal situation where
every student was an enemy, they had to reach the goal while protecting
their own bracelets. For Laika, protecting herself was a far more
daunting task than taking a bracelet from someone else. Her expression
clouded with anxiety, and she looked back at Shatia with the same
concern.

“Professor Regis-dono,” Shatia raised her hand to ask something that had
piqued her curiosity.

“Hm? What is it? Do you have a question, Shatia?” As he was about to
start the exam, Professor Regis looked annoyed, but seeing it was
Shatia, he adopted a serious expression and listened.

“If a student reaches the goal without a bracelet, will that person be
expelled?” Despite the heavy question, Shatia asked casually. To her, it
was a simple question with no great intent behind it. However, the room
fell into a momentary silence.

Professor Regis paused for a beat, cleared his throat, and replied, “No,
that is not the case. They will simply be given a make-up exam.”

Hearing his answer, Shatia nodded with satisfaction and lowered her
hand.

The students looked relieved to hear there was no immediate expulsion,
but their faces clouded again at the mention of a make-up exam. The
re-tests conducted by Professor Regis were famously brutal; some even
wailed that expulsion would have been more merciful. The students told
themselves that they must absolutely avoid finishing with zero
bracelets.

“The exam will start shortly! The goal is to the northwest. There is a
gate there; pass through it to finish. Now… BEGIN!!”

With that, Professor Regis blew an exam whistle and swept his arm. In
that instant, the floor of the venue glowed, and a dense jungle expanded
around them in a flash. Foliage grew so thick that one could barely see
a few steps ahead, with vines and rocks obstructing the path.

Whether it was illusion magic or they had been teleported to a
pre-prepared location, the sudden appearance of the jungle meant the
students were started from scattered points. Naturally, Laika was
separated from Shatia and started from a completely unknown location.

“E-Eh!? No way… I can’t reach the goal alone…” Laika immediately hid
behind a rock and voiced her weaknesses while warily scanning her
surroundings.

No matter how much magic Shatia had taught her, Laika’s personality
remained as timid as ever. Even if she learned several offensive spells,
her will remained unchanged. If she fought someone now, it was clear she
would lose her nerve and collapse on the spot. Thus, Laika had no choice
but to hide while sneaking the goal.

“Shatia-chan, where are you? …Uuu, if only Shatia-chan were here…”

Hoping at least for Shatia’s presence, Laika clasped her hands tightly.

Professor Regis had certainly spoken in a way that encouraged stealing
bracelets. However, the lack of restrictions meant they didn’t *have* to
fight; they could also form alliances. But for Laika, whose only friend
was Shatia, cooperating with anyone else was impossible. She would have
to act alone at least until she could regroup with Shatia.

“Found you, Laika~!”

“…Eek!?”

After walking for a while, Laika encountered another student. It was one
of the girls who frequently bullied her. The girl let out an evil laugh
the moment she spotted Laika and approached.

Laika’s legs gave out, and she couldn’t move quickly enough to flee. She
scrambled backward on the ground until she was backed against a rocky
area.

“Fufufu, I knew it! Running away is all you’re good for, isn’t it?
You’re a failure, after all. You can just go get thrashed in the
re-test,” the girl spoke with the confidence of a victor as she cornered
Laika. She began channeling mana into her palm, aiming to knock her
unconscious.

Tears welled in Laika’s eyes. Was it going to end this easily? After
these past weeks of learning magic from Shatia, was she going to fail
without even using any of it and end up in the re-test? Laika cursed her
own helplessness. Then, in a moment of near-desperation, she clenched
her fist tight, swung it, and unleashed a pulse of mana at the girl.

“…Yah!!”

“…!!?”

With a gust of wind, the mana whistled past the girl’s face. Because
Laika had closed her eyes when she fired it, it didn’t hit, but the
power was enough that it certainly would have knocked the girl out if it
had connected. Feeling a tingling, numbing sensation, the girl widened
her eyes as if looking at something unbelievable. Once she snapped back
to her senses, her face distorted with rage and she kicked Laika.

“Y-You! Don’t get cocky! Shooting off a mana wave like a real mage… I
could have been hurt if that hit me!!” Ignoring the fact that she had
been about to attack as well, the girl shouted and kicked Laika several
times.

“Ugh… nng!” Laika curled up, desperately enduring the blows.

Eventually, the girl pulled her leg back, brushed her messy hair back,
and looked down at Laika with contempt. “I’m not letting you off easy…
I’m going to make this hurt!”

She raised her arm and funneled mana into it. A powerful orb of mana
began to form, crackling with sparks. Unlike a pulse, this was a basic
spell that prioritized accuracy over power. The girl intended to
thoroughly torment Laika.

“Uah… aaaaaaah!!”

Driven by terror, Laika suddenly lunged upward, pushed past the girl’s
legs, and bolted into the brush. Fortunately, the girl tripped from the
momentum and didn’t seem to be giving chase immediately. Laika vanished
into the jungle, covered in dirt. Her face was a mess of tears and snot,
an utterly pathetic sight.

◇

“I do not know if it was intentional, but these bracelets are made quite
thick… It seems one can only fit eight at most… hmm, I wonder.”

In a small clearing, Shatia was sitting atop a tree, assessing the
situation. She had taken her bracelet off briefly to examine it,
predicting that her arm could only accommodate about eight.

Whether it was to keep the number of failures low or to add a layer of
game-like strategy, it seemed some kind of safety measure had been put
in place. Shatia found that games made by humans could be surprisingly
entertaining.

“Ugh…”

“Nng… my head…”

“I have simply given you a little dream with illusion magic. Do not
worry, you will wake up in a few minutes.”

*Though by then, the exam will likely be over*, Shatia thought as she
looked down at the ground. Several students were lying there, groaning
and looking as if they were suffering from nightmares.

In truth, these students had plotted to work together to take down the
new transfer student, planning to ambush her while she sat in the tree.
However, Shatia had easily noticed them and turned the tables, leading
to the current result.

Shatia hopped down from the tree, landed on the ground, and rotated her
arm to check the fit of the four bracelets she now wore.

“Well, I believe this many should be enough to pass the exam… but what
to do,” she muttered to herself, crossing her arms and tilting her head.

She considered whether to simply go past the group of fallen students
and head for the goal to the northwest or collect a few more bracelets.
Just then, the nearby bushes rustled, and a male student appeared.

He was a boy with a very lion-like impression; messy navy-blue hair,
sharp eyes, and protruding canines that looked like fangs. Shatia knew
him well.

“Leo, was it…”

“Geh… Shatia.”

Shatia called the boy’s name and formed a smile as if she had found a
fun toy. In contrast, the moment Leo saw Shatia, his eyes darted to the
students lying on the ground. He understood the situation immediately
and his expression darkened with regret.

Leo was the boy who had been bullying Laika when Shatia first went to
look around the city. Since they were students, Shatia had expected they
might run into each other, but since they were both in Class 1, they saw
each other constantly. Shatia found it amusing, but Leo had developed a
serious complex about her.

“You… you’ve already collected that many bracelets?” Leo spoke with a
tone that went beyond admiration into exasperation.

In turn, Shatia raised her arm boastfully. The glittering gold bracelets
reflected in Leo’s eyes. “Indeed. They came to present them to me of
their own accord. I accepted them with gratitude.”

Reflexively, Leo clutched the single bracelet on his own arm.

“Do not be so wary. I have no need for more. This is sufficient,” Shatia
said as if sensing Leo’s anxiety. Her tone was gentle, the kind that
might put someone at ease, but to Leo, she remained an object of terror.
He, too, felt it instinctively—that Shatia was a being on a completely
different level.

She lowered her arm with a clink of the bracelets and turned to head
toward the northwest. 

Noticing this, Leo called out impulsively, “Hey! You’re going to the
goal?”

“…Hm? Yes, that is the plan.” Shatia tilted her head, clearly wondering
why he would even ask. Her back was turned to him. She glanced at him
over her shoulder, showing a completely defenseless posture.

Leo had no intention of taking advantage of it, but it irritated him,
feeling as though he were being looked down upon. However, knowing he
was no match for her, he kept his composure and continued, “What about
Laika? There’s no way she can reach the goal alone. She’s probably
having her bracelet taken right now.”

Hearing what he had to say, Shatia’s expression didn’t change; instead,
she looked puzzled as to why he would bring it up. It was true, as Leo
said, that it would be difficult for Laika to reach the goal alone.
Given that the exam was a survival match, a fight for bracelets was
inevitable. While cooperation was possible, it would be difficult for
someone like Laika, who was being bullied. Thus, Leo had assumed Shatia
would be going to help her.

But Shatia remained expressionless, tapping her finger against her lip
as if deep in thought.

“…True. Well, then it cannot be helped. Fortunately, it seems there is
no risk of expulsion. Laika will simply have to do her best in the
make-up exam.”

“You… are you even her friend?! She’ll never pass that exam! Go help
her!”

Seeing Shatia’s cold attitude, Leo argued back. He let go of his
bracelet and shouted, waving his arms as he made his plea. It was a
perfectly reasonable point, but Shatia merely smiled, acting as though
she had expected him to say that.

“Is a friend someone who helps you the moment you are in trouble? Is a
friend someone to whom you can leave all the difficult tasks? If that is
the case, Laika will never grow…”

Denying Leo’s words head-on, Shatia turned and looked directly at him.
Leo’s shoulders gave a small jolt. He had been walking towards her but
he stopped mid-stride. He froze, and the two stared at each other for a
moment.

“Sometimes, being strict is important. In order to ensure Laika does not
become dependent on me, she must work hard here. Well, if she fails…
then that is just how it is,” she said with a stern tone. It was
unchildlike, almost like a teacher, which gave Leo a moment of doubt.

At the start of the exam, Shatia had indeed intended to help Laika, but
she changed her mind once they were started separately. Before it began,
Laika had been staring at Shatia, clearly conveying her desire to act
together. That would not do. Not as long as she stayed someone who
solely relied on others. Thus, Shatia intended to reach the goal solo.

“It’s impossible… she can barely use magic…”

“Not necessarily… Besides, why do you care so much about Laika? If it
bothers you, why don’t *you* go help her?”

“H-Haah?!”

When Shatia offered this advice to the trembling, downcast Leo, he
snapped his head up. His mouth fell open, revealing his canines in
surprise. He looked more flustered than before, clearly shaken.

“The rules did not say we couldn’t cooperate. If that is the case, it
matters not who it is. You should just help Laika yourself.”

“W-Why should I do that?! I… someone like her…”

“Well, I have no plans to force you. After all, I have no intention of
helping her. Ah, poor Laika… abandoned by everyone…”

Despite saying moments ago that helping Laika was unnecessary, Shatia
suddenly changed her tune and acted worried. She paced around with her
hands clasped, looking like a mother worried for her daughter. Leo’s
shoulders shook at the sheer absurdity of her performance.

It was an obvious provocation. She wouldn’t involve herself, but she
wouldn’t stop Leo if he did. He felt a surge of anger at her
selfishness. But he didn’t give in to it; it was obvious he would be
trounced if he did.

“F-Fine! Dammit, I’ll do it!! *I’ll* help Laika! And I’ll prove it to
you. That Laika is someone who can’t do anything unless someone helps
her!!” Leo shouted desperately, stomping his foot. He wasn’t even sure
what he was saying anymore, but he had convinced himself he had to do it
to get back at Shatia.

Hearing his answer, Shatia burst out laughing, clutching her stomach,
“Kukuku.” After composing herself, she nodded with satisfaction and
revealed Laika’s location as a special favor, “…I see. Then do your
best. Laika is in the southeast.”

“You even know where she is?! Dammit… what a nice person you are!!” Leo
gave Shatia a resentful glare and ran off toward the southeast.

Once his figure had vanished into the jungle, Shatia shook her head and
let out a sigh.

“Children really do have such interesting personalities… taking such a
hateful attitude toward someone they like… Kuku, humans possess such
complicated hearts.”

Whether Leo himself realized it or not, Shatia had a sense of his true
feelings. It was likely one of those things unique to children—something
a witch like her didn’t quite understand, but Shatia found it quite
amusing nonetheless.
