# Chapter 31 – Determination

Cloak recalled that day. The day she was defeated, the day she was cast
down by the despicable hands of humans.

It happened abruptly. As she was conducting her usual research into
sorcery within her abode, a tower, Cloak sensed the presence of an
intruder. It wasn’t particularly rare. Adventurers seeking the head of a
witch were a daily occurrence. Assuming this was no different, Cloak let
out a sigh and went to intercept them.

To be honest, her true desire was to grind down the intruder’s bones,
render them immobile, and use them as a test subject for her
experiments. However, there were rules for witches. Rules established by
the leader of the witches, Shatifahl. *No matter the circumstance, one
must never harm another race. Senseless killing is also strictly
forbidden. We witches are to remain observers, quietly watching over the
world*—that was what she had been taught.

Cloak despised such rules as being too passive. Setting aside the demons
who simply desired dominance, humans clearly loathed witches. She
surmised that leaving them unchecked was dangerous. However, because she
owed Shatifahl for raising her, Cloak never voiced her objections.

She donned her jet-black coat as usual and headed toward the main hall.
Since she didn’t want her laboratory destroyed, she always made the hall
her chosen battlefield for intruders. Tying back her flowing blue hair,
Cloak quietly moved toward the area.

And there, a single boy was standing.

He had brown hair swept back, an ordinary face, and a quiet demeanor; he
looked like a human you could find anywhere, an ordinary boy completely
unsuited for conflict. Yet, he was clad in platinum armor and gripped a
radiant, glowing sword. From that alone, she could see clearly that he
was one of those wretches targeting witches.

Suddenly, Cloak felt a chill. She, who normally reigned at the pinnacle
as a witch, was intimidated by the ordinary boy before her. She hadn’t
had such an experience since her mock battles with Shatifahl.

She denied the reality of it, thinking it impossible. There was no way
that she, the Witch of Inquiry, could be intimidated by a mere human.
There was no way she could fear a boy with such an ordinary face. A boy
who looked as though he had never even held a sword. Cloak suppressed
her trembling arms and aimed her palm at the boy. That act, however, was
a mistake.

The next thing she knew, she was bleeding profusely from her abdomen,
her consciousness fading.

She didn’t understand what had happened. The battle had been decided in
a literal instant. The magic she had spent years refining was rendered
completely useless; the monstrous boy had struck her down.

“Die, Witch. You are an existence that must not be allowed to be,” the
boy recited while leveling his sword. He denied Cloak’s entire
existence, speaking with a tone as if his words were the absolute law of
the world.

Gritting her teeth and dripping blood, Cloak spat back a retort, “Don’t
screw with me! A mere human… killing *us* witches?! Like I’d let myself
be killed just for the sake of you humans’ convenience!!”

Cloak howled and headed toward the window. The boy gave chase, but
before he could reach her, she leaped out. It was a height that meant
certain death. However, Cloak showed a smirk, flicked her finger, and
cast a magic. Suddenly, the scenery in her vision shifted, and Cloak
collapsed onto an unfamiliar wasteland.

Translocation magic. An original magic devised by Cloak herself. A spell
that allowed her to move herself, others, or anything she wished to any
location she desired. Naturally, the mana consumption was intense and
several restrictions existed, but its power was such that even Shatifahl
took notice of it. To Cloak, this magic was the one and only achievement
she could truly be proud of.

“Gah… haah… haah…”

In the wasteland where a cold wind blew, Cloak gasped for air while
feeling the warmth of the blood flowing out from her body. She applied
first aid with healing magic and began to move, dragging her body along.

Because she had used the translocation magic randomly, she didn’t even
know where she had ended up. She tried to teleport again, but perhaps
due to her injuries, her mana failed to recover, leaving her with only a
minuscule amount. Regardless, she needed a place to rest. The sword the
boy used was likely a blessed one. Cloak judged that leaving the wound
as it was would be dangerous.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
Eventually, after wandering the wasteland for days, Cloak finally
arrived at a temple. The stone statues placed there were all unsettling,
and she could guess that it had likely been built by some other race.
She decided to take a rest there for the time being and searched for a
place to lie down while tending her wound. Then, she noticed that there
were several people in the temple. Based on the mana signatures, there
was one whose mana pool rivaled that of a witch, and then some others.
Feeling annoyed but hopeful, Cloak headed toward the source of the
signatures.

Reaching the depths of the temple, she found a man with pale skin. There
were others around him who appeared to be his subordinates. Perhaps they
were performing a ritual; they were offering prayers before a massive
stone statue as if worshiping it.

Since she sensed no mana flow associated with the ritual, it likely
wasn’t a summoning or anything of the sort. Whether it was some custom
or a law, it didn’t matter to Cloak, so she began walking toward the man
with the large amount of mana.

“You! Who are you?! We are in the middle of an important ritual!”

Naturally, Cloak’s path was blocked by the subordinates. They hid their
pale skin under black robes and had red eyes; it was then that Cloak
realized for the first time that they were demons.

It seemed she had translocated all the way to the Dark Continent. *Well,
this is actually convenient*, Cloak thought. Ignoring the demon who
blocked her, she lightly flicked her finger and blew him away.

Noticing the commotion, the demons began to raise their voices. Hearing
them say “Demon Lord-sama,” Cloak judged that the man must be the Demon
Lord and flashed a smirk. She blew away the demons leveling spears at
her with a pulse of mana before confronting the Demon Lord while
clutching her abdomen.

“Yo, hello there, O Mr. Demon Lord… My bad, hurting your subordinates
and all.”

“…What on earth are you?”

Being asked *what* she was rather than *who*, Cloak let out a small
“ah.” Though they possessed the same amount of mana and there wasn’t
much difference in their actual strength, they still looked at her kind
as alien.

Witches really were different. Different from sages, different from
demon lords, different from dragons. A completely freakish existence.
Cloak felt poignantly that they were beings that could never be
accepted. But that was fine. If they wouldn’t be accepted, then she
simply wouldn’t bother trying to get close to them either. Cloak decided
on her objective and gave the Demon Lord an exaggerated bow.

“I am the Witch of Inquiry, Cloak… O Mr. Demon Lord, will you not
conquer the world with me?”

Demon Lord Belpheus didn’t understand the meaning of Cloak’s words and
shook his head in confusion. However, from there, Cloak whispered sweet
words of temptation, drew out Belpheus’s desires, and promised to grant
them. Unaware that he was being manipulated, Belpheus easily came to
believe her. And so, the Demon Lord and the Witch joined forces.
Naturally, they didn’t tell the public. This was an important secret
shared only by Belpheus and Cloak.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
Belpheus was overjoyed at having gained the cooperation of a witch. To a
Demon Lord, obtaining a witch’s power was something the previous ruler,
Agito, had desired. The fact that it had been realized was nothing short
of a joy for him as the son.

*—If it’s me, I could make a witch submit.*

He had allowed himself to think such a thing. Later, hearing reports
that witches were being annihilated one after another, Belpheus wondered
if there might be other witches near death like Cloak and decided to
send out an investigation team. They then obtained information that a
girl who seemed to be the Witch of Purity had appeared on the human
continent.

When she heard the news, Cloak frowned. While rubbing her now fully
healed abdomen, she scoffed, “Capture Emerald? Don’t bother. She’s
probably gone mad after being betrayed by humans. The reports say she’s
near death, right? It’d be a mercy to just let her die peacefully…”

Despite her warning, Belpheus, sitting on his throne, refused to give up
on capturing Emerald and proceeded with the operation. Cloak simply
shook her head with a sigh and returned to her laboratory.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
Soon after, Cloak provided the Demon Lord with a plan to conquer the
human nations. She told him how to break through the front lines at the
edge of the continent—which had been in a deadlock for years—and take
the northern fortress, the key to the royal capital’s defense, in one
fell swoop. It was a strategy that Belpheus couldn’t fathom.

“Is such a thing truly possible? Transporting an entire army to the
northern fortress…”

“About a hundred people’s no problem. After that, we just repeat it over
and over. It’ll be a bit tough, but it’s within my limit.”

Revealing her specialty, translocation magic, she would use it to move
the Demon Lord’s army to the northern fortress all at once. By doing so,
they could take the fortress regardless of what was happening at the
front lines. That was the strategy Cloak had devised.

Naturally, there were concerns. Since she was thrusting soldiers right
into the heart of the enemy army, they could easily be surrounded or
overwhelmed by numbers. Cloak pointedly chose not to inform the Demon
Lord of such danger. The soldiers believed this operation would deal a
heavy blow to the human nations and didn’t give a single thought to the
possibility of their own deaths. Cloak utilized that. She chose to ride
the fervor of the people believing the Demon Kingdom would become the
world’s hegemon in order to plunge them into a vortex of chaos.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
“Cloak, let’s stop this already! A request for a ceasefire has come from
the human nations. It’s too dangerous to allow any more casualties!”

“Oi, oi, what’re you saying, O Mr. Demon Lord?”

One evening, while Cloak was reading through the spellbook she had
stolen from the royal capital in her room, Belpheus entered without
knocking and said those words. Cloak let out a sigh as she unfastened
her hair, looking at Belpheus with disdain.

“You made a declaration, didn’t you? To the masses, that the Demon
Kingdom would rule the world… Are you gonna tell them now that it was
all a lie? A rebellion would break out if you did. It’s too late for us.
We can’t stop. There’s no choice but to keep going.”

Cloak spoke as she stood up her chair and approached Belpheus, who was
taller than her. While she usually wore a thick coat, she was currently
dressed in sleepwear, draped in something like a thin veil. Her
appearance was undeniably alluring, and combined with her features, she
looked truly beautiful. Yet, Belpheus felt only terror. To him, the
beautiful woman approaching looked like nothing but a hideously formed
monster.

“Welcome to reality, O Mr. Demon Lord. This is what war looks like.”

To the dazed Belpheus, Cloak spat out those words while sticking out her
tongue.

The words were incredibly cold; they pierced Belpheus’s heart,
inflicting unbearable regret and endless suffering. Overwhelmed by those
emotions, Belpheus let out a small cry and fled from the room. Cloak
just watched his retreating back while cackling.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
That was quite recently.

And now, Cloak stood in the main hall of the Demon Lord’s castle. She
sat by the window, looking up at the sky. A beautiful, blackened sky. It
was covered in clouds, allowing not even a sliver of light to pierce
through. But Cloak preferred this. Light was blinding. It was annoying
and bothersome.

Before her, as she harbored such feelings, a single girl descended. A
girl with flowing silver hair and clear eyes. Contrary to her
appearance, her atmosphere radiated a dignity befitting a ruler.

“Gazing at the sky and acting all melancholic… You have become quite the
poet, Cloak,” Shatia crossed her arms.

“…Everyone has times when they just want to gaze up at the sky. Is it
what you’d call a homing instinct? You suddenly find yourself wondering:
‘Why I was born into this world?'”

Cloak moved away from the window and approached the center of the hall.
Her black coat flowed behind her, and a spellbook was gripped in her
hand. Shatia quietly narrowed her eyes upon seeing it.

“Why were witches born? We’re so hated and despised—do you think there’s
any value in us being alive?” She muttered as she walked. Whether she
was talking to herself or addressing Shatia, Cloak simply let the words
spill out flatly, her hair swaying in the wind blowing through the
window.

Shatia listened in silence. Cloak stopped a few paces away from her and
opened her mouth with a small smirk, “I’ll create that value myself.
I’ll remove everyone who gets in my way and build a nation for witches.
I won’t let anyone make a fool of witches ever again. That’s my goal.”

Gritting her teeth, Cloak swung her arm. Instantly, the shadow beneath
her feet expanded, dyeing the floor of the hall pitch black. And from
within that darkness, countless black snakes emerged. Cloak laughed—a
high-pitched, cackling laugh.
