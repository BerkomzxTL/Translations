# Chapter 28 – The Demon Lord’s Dream

Demon Lord Belpheus was dreaming. He was dreaming of his childhood, back
when his father, Agito, served as the Demon Lord and he himself was not
yet the ruler.

In those days, Belpheus did not yet possess a robust, powerful physique
like his father; he was an utterly ordinary boy. He simply spent his
days watching his father’s back, understanding nothing of politics or
the affairs of the state. While Belpheus wished to help his father, he
naturally understood that his powerless self was incapable of doing so.

*—”Belpheus, one day you shall succeed me and become a magnificent Demon
Lord. Do you understand?”*

*—”Yes, Father.”*

Whenever Agito spoke those words to his son, Belpheus would answer
confidently. Hearing this, Agito would laugh with satisfaction, a grand
laugh that bared his pointed fangs.

Agito was a Demon Lord with an incredibly strong sense of
possessiveness. He even bore the alias “Demon Lord of Greed,” and that
trait was particularly prominent even among the successive generations.
He aimed to one day dominate the human continent and establish the demon
race at the pinnacle of all living things. Belpheus respected his
father’s unwavering will and believed that if it were his father, he
could achieve it.

But then, one day—

“It has been a long time, Agito.”

She appeared suddenly. A woman of suspicious appearance, wearing a robe
fashioned like wings and a pitch-black pointed hat. Her form was
identical to that of a human, but the moment Belpheus felt her mana, he
knew she was a monster. A terrifying existence incomparable to a human.
He instinctively hid behind his father.

“Oh, Shatifahl! You’ve finally come!”

“You were all so persistent… I only came to receive the feast.”

Upon seeing the silver-haired woman, Agito raised his hand in joy and
called her by the name Shatifahl. It seemed Agito himself had invited
her to the Demon Kingdom long ago, and she was an acquaintance of his.
Belpheus knew his father had various acquaintances. Since he even held
discussions with those from enemy nations, Belpheus was no longer
surprised by such things. However, Belpheus sensed somewhere in his
heart that the woman named Shatifahl was an unprecedented, alien
existence.

“Hm? What is that little one over there?”

“Ah, that is my son. Come now, don’t hide, give your greetings.”

“H-hello… I am Belpheus.”

Even though he had hidden behind Agito, Shatifahl had easily seen
through him, and since his father had told him to greet her, there was
no escape. Belpheus bowed nervously and greeted Shatifahl. She placed a
hand to her mouth with interest and observed Belpheus intently,
comparing him to Agito before showing a somewhat amused smile.

“Hoh… quite a serious child… not at all like yourself,” she said while
laughing.

Agito twisted his mouth awkwardly and shot back, “Shut it.”

Belpheus couldn’t believe his ears. He had never seen anyone talk to his
father in such a manner. Agito was the Demon Lord, an existence
respected by everyone. Yet the woman named Shatifahl before him acted as
if she were actually admonishing Agito. She behaved as if she had known
Agito for a very long time.

“Well then, we have an important discussion to hold,” Agito’s expression
suddenly shifted into a serious one as he spoke to Belpheus, “Belpheus,
return to your room.”

“I-I understand,” since Belpheus hadn’t expected to participate in the
meeting anyway, he nodded obediently.

As the two headed toward the conference room, Shatifahl turned her head
back toward Belpheus and smiled brightly. “Let us meet again, future
Demon Lord,” she said before vanishing into the conference room along
with Agito.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
Belpheus’s consciousness surfaced. From within a long, long slumber, his
awareness gradually became clear, the darkness faded, and his dim
private chambers came into view. Lifting his body—which now possessed
powerful muscles unlike those of his childhood—Belpheus placed a hand to
his forehead and let out a small sigh.

“…A dream.”

His expression twisted as if he had seen a nightmare. His naturally pale
skin looked even more sickly, and faint dark circles had formed under
his eyes.

In truth, to Belpheus, that memory was nothing but a nightmare. An
existence that spoke as an equal to his father, whom he believed to be
the strongest in the world. No, she was more than that. Her mana was
bottomless, and its nature abnormal. For Belpheus, the existence of his
father, Agito, was already high above the clouds; yet, with the
appearance of Shatifahl, he had witnessed an existence so powerful it
pierced the heavens. That shock was not something the young Belpheus
could easily endure.

It was several weeks after their first meeting that Belpheus learned
Shatifahl was a witch. By that time, Shatifahl had already left the
Demon Kingdom, and he never met her again. It was then that Belpheus
came to fear the existence of witches. What surprised him even more was
the fact that there were six others like her.

According to what he had heard, the demon race was unable to attack the
human continent because the witches stood as a wall. However, Agito had
approached Shatifahl with a proposal to bring the witches to their side.
Belpheus didn’t know the outcome of that talk, but looking at the
current situation, he could guess that the negotiations had likely
failed.

Belpheus let out another sigh and slowly got out of bed. The room was
slightly cold, and he felt a chill against his skin.

“I’m back~, O Mr. Demon Lord~”

“…Cloak.”

Just as he thought he heard a familiar voice from nowhere, someone
opened the door without knocking and entered the room. A person who
showed no restraint even toward the Demon Lord—naturally, it was the
witch Cloak.

Despite possessing an appearance that even demons would call enchanting,
her behavior was catastrophically poor, leaving Belpheus at his wit’s
end. She strutted around the room with heavy steps, showing no reserve.
She collapsed roughly in a chair and crossed her legs, muttering about
how tired she was.

Curious, Belpheus asked, “Where have you been until now? You suddenly
left, saying you were going for a walk…”

Cloak then nonchalantly dropped a shocking revelation: “Yeah, I went to
see Shatifahl. Seems she’s heading this way just as planned.”

Belpheus’s eyes widened, and he was left at a loss for words. For a
while, no conversation passed between the two, until Belpheus regained
his composure and asked again with a trembling voice, “Wh… Shatifahl?
You mean *that* Shatifahl? The leader of the witches?”

“Yep. She’s like a mother to us.”

“Y-you… Why would you do such a thing!? I heard nothing of such a
plan!?”

Belpheus was confused by the sudden news. After all, his desire was to
dominate the human continent—a feat his father could not achieve. Just
as they were finally able to move because the witches had disappeared,
why was that very witch returning?

“Ugh, c’mon, you wanted the power of witches, right? It’s not like one
or two at this point’s gonna change anything in the plan.”

“Even so… That one is in a league of her own!”

It was true that Belpheus had coveted the power of the witches. While he
was glad they were gone, he had thought that if he could obtain their
power, even greater dominance would be possible. It was his desire,
inheriting the greed of his father. And then, the opportunity came just
once. By a stroke of luck, Belpheus had met the surviving Cloak and
decided to join forces with her. From there, he desired even more of the
witches’ power and targeted the rampaging Emerald. That was an
undeniable fact.

But Shatifahl was the sole exception. Belpheus’s arms trembled as he
recalled the image of Shatifahl burned into his mind. She was an
existence one must absolutely never get involved with. She was not
someone he could ever hope to defeat.

“Don’t worry. The reason you failed to capture Emerald was because you
didn’t listen to my advice. If you just do things according to my plan,
everything’ll go perfectly,” Cloak declared while laughing.

Where such confidence came from, Belpheus didn’t know. Cloak was skilled
at devising schemes. In fact, it was thanks to her that they were able
to take the northern fortress of the human continent. Belpheus
acknowledged her for that. If they had followed Cloak’s words, the
capture of Emerald likely would have gone well too.

But then, he wondered—if that were the case, would there be any meaning
in him being the Demon Lord?

“Now then, work hard, my dear Demon Lord. Shatifahl’s cooperation is
indispensable for dominating the humans. It’s too late to back out.”

Cloak stood up from her chair and extended a hand. Belpheus was choked
for words and could offer no reaction.

Plans were progressing one after another in places he didn’t know. While
Cloak’s objectives certainly aligned with his, her methods were far too
extreme. Belpheus worried that things might reach a point of no return,
but just as she said, he could no longer stop. The masses were waiting
with bated breath to conquer the human nations. The soldiers’ morale was
high. Even the king could not stop this momentum.

The Demon Lord following the Witch. It was a sight somehow reminiscent
to that of Agito and Shatifahl in the past.

After passing through the gaps in the dense trees and reaching the top
of the mountain with flight magic, Shatia finally caught sight of a
city. Larger and higher than the royal capital, in the center of the
many towers stood an exceptionally massive dark castle. One could tell
at a glance that it belonged to the Demon Lord.

“…I’ve finally made it,” Shatia murmured as sweat dripped from her brow.
Her clothes were soiled with dirt here and there, showing the hardships
she had faced to get this far. She sharpened her gaze while tidying her
disheveled hair.

Cloak was in that castle. Whatever that brat’s goal might be, it was
clear she was targeting Shatia. Shatia intended to meet her head-on.
After all, that was her duty as a mother. Steeling her resolve, Shatia
took a step forward.

“Just you wait, Cloak. I shall ingrain into your very being what happens
when you make me angry.”

Despite appearances, Shatia was the type to hold a grudge. Being
betrayed by someone she had raised like a daughter was quite a shock,
and she intended to put just as much effort into her retaliation.
Flashing a mischievous grin, she recited a flight spell and took to the
air, wasting no time heading toward the castle.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
Using presence-concealment magic, entering unnoticed was easy. Passing
over the walls and infiltrating the city, she saw many demons going back
and forth on the streets. It had been a long time since she had seen
such a sight. Shatia pulled her hood low just in case before descending
to the ground.

“Now then, the problem is how to enter the castle,” she muttered as she
looked up at the Demon Lord’s castle visible in the distance, resting a
hand against her mouth.

Entering the city was trivial. However, the castle’s security was much
more thorough. Furthermore, her goal this time wasn’t to “borrow”
something like she did at the royal palace, but to meet Cloak. Because
of that, she had to be thoughtful about how she handled this.

While she was thinking about these things, a noisy commotion suddenly
erupted from the market. It was followed by a sound like an explosion,
and Shatia, with a bad premonition, shifted her gaze in that direction.
There, a monster shaped like a giant wolf was letting out a roar.

“Whoa! Shit, the cage broke and the monster’s running wild!!”

“Y-you idiot! Do something, quickly! That’s important merchandise!!”

It was likely a shop that dealt in monsters as products. It seemed they
were being transported by carriage when the cage broke. The wolf monster
began destroying the nearby stalls while roaring, causing the commoners
to shriek and flee. The merchants frantically tried to calm the monster,
but their whips were easily swatted away by its tail, and the situation
had become impossible to stop.

“Ah… no, no, standing out here would surely be bad,” Shatia was about to
dash toward the scene but stopped her step at the last moment.

No matter the circumstances, standing out in the center of the Demon
Kingdom would be trouble. Even if she were to use presence-concealment
magic, its effectiveness would vanish if too many eyes were drawn to
her, and if her hood fell by some chance, her human form would be
revealed. Surely the soldiers would rush over soon; there was no need
for her to step in.

Shatia made that judgment, but unfortunately, she couldn’t help but see
it—a demon child about to be devoured by the wolf monster.

“Ah, I suppose it cannot be helped!”

Shatia pulled her hood down even tighter and started running. She used
gravity magic to stop the wolf monster’s movements, then blew it away
with a pulse of mana. The monster’s massive body flew through the air,
crashing into the carriage bed with a violent sound of destruction.
Shatia stood before the child and raised her arm.

Now that it had come to this, she had to do it. Shatia shouted with a
hint of desperation, provoking the groaning wolf monster, “Come, doggy.
I am your opponent.”

The wolf monster pushed itself up and let out an exceptionally loud
roar. Then, kicking off the ground, it bared its fangs and lunged at
Shatia from above.
