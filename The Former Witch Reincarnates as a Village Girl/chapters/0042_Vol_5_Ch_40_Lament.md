# Chapter 40: Lament

Shatia handled the Lizardman Chief—who attacked with his fighting spirit
bared like a wild beast—with absolute composure. When he lunged with
gleaming fangs, she parried; when he extended his sharp claws to strike,
she repelled with a barrier. The surrounding Lizardmen stood frozen in
shock, watching as every one of their Chief’s attacks was neutralized by
magic.

“Gruaaaaahhh… Aah!” The Lizardman Chief roared and swung his fist.
However, even that fist was blocked by a barrier, causing his scales to
crack and blood to spray.

Seeing this, Shatia directed a cold gaze toward him and urged him to
cease his attacks, “It is futile. I have already seen through your way
of fighting. Everything is meaningless before my magic.”

Shatia had already engaged in several battles with Lizardmen in her
time. Their methods of attack had long since become clear. For someone
like Shatia, with her superior observational skills, parrying a
Lizardman who possessed only common means of attack was a simple task.

“Gorua…!”

That said, the Lizardman Chief wouldn’t easily admit defeat. Behind him
stood dozens of his kin. As their leader, he couldn’t allow them to
witness his failure. Therefore, he clenched his fist. Even as blood
dripped from the gaps between his fingers, he ran straight toward
Shatia, attempting to land a punch before her magic could be activated.

“I said it is futile.”

This was the peak of speed for a Lizardman. Normally, it should have
been a speed a human could not react to. Yet, the Chief’s fist only
grazed Shatia’s cheek as she moved behind him, lightly pressing a finger
against his back. Suddenly, a silver flash erupted, and a sharp pain
shot through the Chief’s spine. Blown away by the impact, he collapsed,
rolling across the ground.

“Guh… oooah! …For a mere human…!!”

The Lizardman Chief roared and forced his body up. Pain flared
throughout his entire body, but he ignored it, his eyes gleaming with
rage as he glared at Shatia. But Shatia stood before him as leisurely as
ever, not even bothering to take up a combat stance.

Believing she was mocking him, the Chief displayed even more overt fury
and leaped at her. He poured strength into both arms, swinging his
claws.

“Ruguaaaaaaaaaaaaaah!!”

“No matter how much force you put into your appeal, there is no threat
if you can only perform the same attacks.”

Creating a barrier around her body, Shatia blocked the attack
completely. Then, she aimed her palm at the opening left by the Chief
and fired a massive mana blast. The Chief was blown away with a
thunderous roar, but he rotated in mid-air, landed on the ground, and
charged toward Shatia once again.

“Garurururuuu!!”

Twisting his body, he swung his tail and lunged. The destructive power
of a tail swipe delivered by a Lizardman’s massive frame rotating at
high speed was immeasurable, and Shatia avoided it at the last possible
second. The tail struck the ground, gouging the earth and sending soil
flying.

Even for Shatia, taking that hit would have been bad. While it likely
wouldn’t have been fatal, there was a possibility that some part of her
body would have been shattered. Despite this, a smile played across her
lips as if she were enjoying it.

“Hoh, that last one was quite good. I was a little surprised.”

“Grrr!! Do not… mock me…!!”

Because Shatia’s impression could vaguely be interpreted as praise, the
Lizardman Chief took it as being looked down upon and let out a roar. If
she had enough breath to speak, he would make her forget to even draw
it. The Chief put all his strength into his body and kicked off the
ground. He soared through the air, gliding down to deliver a kick.

“Whoops…!”

Forming a barrier, Shatia blocked it. However, the impact seemed
non-negligible, as she couldn’t immediately counterattack. Seeing this
as his chance, the Chief launched a follow-up. Lowering his center of
gravity, he put strength into his arm and swung his fist with all his
might. But it only cut through the air; Shatia had escaped into the sky
using flight magic.

She chanted an incantation, creating a light at her fingertip. That
light burst into tiny fragments, transforming into countless lines that
swarmed the Lizardman Chief. The moment the rays touched his scales, a
searing pain shot through him, and he immediately kicked the ground to
put distance between them.

“Rugua…!”

“What is wrong? Running away is an act unbefitting of a chief.”

Descending to the ground as she watched him, Shatia offered a
transparently cheap provocation. However, for the Lizardman Chief, the
word “escape” was the ultimate taboo, and it was more than enough to
enrage him.

Gritting his fangs, the Chief accelerated again. Fainting left and
right, he this time moved behind Shatia and swung his claws. But those
claws met no resistance; instead, her form broke apart like smoke and
vanished.

“Gugah…!!”

“Over here, Lizard-kun.”

Suddenly, a voice came from directly above the Chief. Turning around, he
saw Shatia suspended in the air with flight magic. He tried to attack
immediately, but before he could, Shatia released magical chains,
binding his arms and legs.

“Gogah…!!”

The Lizardman Chief tried to tear the chains apart at once, but these
were chains sturdily formed by Shatia’s mana, making them extremely
difficult to break physically. Looking down with amusement as her
opponent struggled, Shatia swept her arm, manipulating the chains. The
Chief was pulled along, hoisted into the air, and slammed into the
ground.

“Ruruaaaa…!!?”

“How does it feel to be tied up? You are my pet now. I shall have you
perform a trick or two,” she spoke with a gentle smile, as if playing
with a pet dog.

“Grrr… you…!!”

The Lizardman Chief felt an ominous premonition, his face turning pale
as he broke into a cold sweat. He grabbed the chains wrapped around his
limbs and pulled desperately, but Shatia swept her arm to control the
chains, dragging the Chief across the ground.

“Ruguaaa…!!”

Unable to offer any resistance, the Lizardman Chief let out a scream.
Shatia stopped her arm for a moment, then swung it up and back down,
slamming the Chief into the ground repeatedly. The sounds of his agony
echoed, and the Lizardmen watching him began to back away, seemingly
having lost their will to fight.

“Go… aaah…!”

“You are quite stubborn. More like a sandbag than a pet,” Shatia said
this while looking down at the mud-covered, exhausted Lizardman Chief.

The Chief’s consciousness flickered, but he somehow held on, letting out
a low growl as he raised his body, “A Lizardman… will not lose… to a
child… like you… We are… the chosen race…!!”

Because he believed they were the strongest race, the Lizardman Chief
did not fall. He carried a long history and the pride of the Lizardmen
on his shoulders. He could never simply cast that aside and run.
Therefore, no matter how many times Shatia tormented him, he bared his
fangs, extended his claws, and stood his ground.

“It is the pride of the Lizardmen… We shall be the rulers of the
world!!”

“Then you should just throw away such pride.”

In response to the roaring Chief, Shatia manipulated the chains without
mercy, swinging him high into the air and slamming him into the ground.
She swept him sideways, crashing him into trees and smashing him
violently against rocks. Finally, the Lizardman Chief spat out blood and
collapsed to the ground.

“The chosen race? Rulers of the world? Such things are nothing but your
delusions. You, who were birthed by the dragon, can never surpass it.
You simply wish to show off your own power.”

The Lizardman Chief was no longer moving, though still alive. Confirming
this, Shatia approached him and declared her judgment. During this time,
not a single Lizardman attempted to attack her, instead choosing to
silently witness the scene.

“Return to your dwelling. Every living thing has a place where they
belong. And this is no place for you.”

“Gu… ugh…”

With that, Shatia swept her arm to the side and deactivated the chains.
Realizing his limbs had become unbound, the Chief looked up at Shatia in
surprise. She simply maintained her cold gaze, slowly closed her eyes,
and turned to the side, intending to leave for the forest.

She would not engage in unnecessary combat. She had sufficiently
demonstrated her power. She had also warned him that if they fought
further, she would take appropriate measures. Shatia judged that there
was no more need to fight. Therefore, she released the chains and gave
him a path to escape.

But she had underestimated the tenacity of the Chief. To someone with
the pride of a Lizardman, it was synonymous with insult. To be shown
nothing but defeat and then be forced to live on in shame—it was crueler
than death. Thus, the Lizardman Chief raised his body and leaped at
Shatia, who had her back turned.

“GRUUUAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAH!!”

His claws aimed for her neck. But those claws passed right through her,
and her body once again vanished like smoke. The Chief’s eyes widened,
and he froze. From directly behind him, a cold presence drifted.

“Foolish creature.”

Before the Lizardman Chief could even turn around, Shatia once again
bound his body, launched him into the air, and slammed him into the
ground. Pulling the chains further to bring the Chief close to her, she
fired a mana blast at point-blank range, blowing him away once more. He
was then pulled back and thrown backward with incredible force. The
battered Lizardman was slammed into a rock, shattering it with the
impact. Finally, after crashing into a cliffside, silence fell.

“Go… ga… ugh…”

This time, he truly lost consciousness, and the Lizardman Chief did not
stand up again. Confirming this, Shatia let out a tired sigh and brushed
back her hair. Looking to the side, she saw the other Lizardmen backing
away in terror. She nodded with satisfaction and began to walk.

No one tried to stop her. No one *could* stop her. Leaving her mark
behind like a storm, she quietly walked away.
