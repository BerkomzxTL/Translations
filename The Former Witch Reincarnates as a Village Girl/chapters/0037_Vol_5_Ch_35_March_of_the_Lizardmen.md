# Chapter 35: March of the Lizardmen

Deep in a certain mountain range existed an area known as the Mountain
of Beasts. It earned this name due to the large population of monsters.
Even the local flora took on an unapproachable aura. A thin mist clung
to the terrain, and it was said that if a human wandered in, they would
never return—a truly terrifying mountain.

Inside a cave in the heart of these wilds, a single girl was conducting
what appeared to be a strange ritual.

“…Hmm. As I thought, something is missing,” the silver-haired girl,
Shatia, muttered as she peeked out from her grimy robe, placing a hand
to her mouth with a solemn expression.

She was perched on a rocky protrusion, and before her, a magic circle
used for rituals was drawn on the ground. However, no special power
could be felt from it; the ring simply existed there, without even a
drop of mana injected. Shatia tilted her head as she stared at it.

“The materials should be gathered… The incantation is not incorrect. It
is not possible for my memory to fail me… What is it? What is lacking?”

Laid out before Shatia were the bones and fangs of some creature, along
with herbs and tentacles the likes of which had never been seen before.

Normally, if one gathered these items, channeled the necessary mana, and
recited the incantation, the body creation magic should have activated.
Yet, for some reason, no matter how the materials were prepared or how
much mana was poured in, nothing happened. Shatia possessed absolute
confidence in her memory. Naturally, there was no possibility of her
making a mistake. And so, this phenomenon left her deeply puzzled.

“…This is troublesome,” she whispered sadly as she picked up the doll
she had kept by her side. Her eyes wavered as she gazed at it intently
before placing it back in its original spot. Then, clutching her
unmoving arm, she stood up and contemplated her next move.

At that moment, a wolf appeared at the cave entrance. It was a wolf with
beautiful silver fur and eyes that shone as brilliantly as the moon. It
was a species known as a Slade Wolf. These wolves were known as
particularly dangerous monsters, even within the Mountain of Beasts.
Once Shatia made this cave her dwelling, she had subjugated the Slade
Wolf pack and trained them to ensure no humans approached the vicinity.

“What is it? Has something happened?”

“Grrrrr…”

When Shatia turned only her face toward the entrance to ask, the wolf
lowered its head in a prostrate position and let out a vocal response.
This slade wolf was the leader of the pack, the one Shatia had
personally entrusted with surveillance. Since he had gone out of his way
to come here, it was certain that some kind of anomaly had occurred.

“What? A military force? …Humans? No? Then who on earth…?”

Shatia narrowed her eyes upon hearing the wolf’s report.

Originally, the Slade Wolves were tasked with driving away intruders so
that Shatia’s rituals wouldn’t be disturbed. The fact that an opponent
they couldn’t handle had arrived suggested that a force of considerable
scale had appeared. That would explain the “military force,” but the
next question was which nation the force in question belonged to.

The wolf answered with a low growl. The moment she heard it, Shatia’s
eyes widened, and she froze.

“Lizardmen, you say…?”

Lizardmen. Bipedal creatures with the appearance of lizards, roughly
twice the size of a human, and with bodies covered in scales hard enough
to deflect swords and arrows. Their physical capabilities were extremely
high; even without the use of magic, they possessed sufficient combat
prowess. In short, a dangerous race.

Unlike human nations or the Demon Kingdom, they didn’t hold large
territories. They always formed settlements in small numbers and usually
lived hidden in secluded regions surrounded by mountains. This was
influenced by their unique culture. Because Lizardmen believed
themselves to be a superior race, they avoided contact with “inferior”
other races.

Now, they had organized into an army and begun to move. For Shatia, this
report was more alarming than anything else.

“Why are they in a place like this… do they have some objective? I
cannot imagine there is much profit to be gained that would warrant them
taking action…” Placing a hand to her chin, Shatia muttered to herself,
lost in thought. However, no matter how much she pondered, no answer
emerged. She let out a weary sigh and ran her hand roughly through her
hair.

Nothing but complications. The resurrection of Emerald wasn’t
succeeding, and now there was this unexpected movement from the
Lizardmen. The situation was truly heading in a dark direction. She
wanted to change this state of affairs somehow. Shatia narrowed her eyes
and remained silent for a while, tapping her fingers against her
unmoving arm. Then, having come to a conclusion, she closed her eyes,
exhaled, packed up her ritual tools, and headed toward the mouth of the
cave.

After stepping outside, Shatia instructed the wolf that was sticking
close to her side, “Have your kin retreat deep into the forest. I shall
scout the situation.”

Hearing the order, the wolf growled in affirmation, kicked the ground
hard, and leaped onto the rocky area beside the cave to make its
departure.

Shatia turned her head, looking toward the thick trees before her.
Taking a deep breath, she probed the mana signatures in the area,
searching for the Lizardmen. After a while, she finally managed to sense
them. Because they didn’t employ magic, the mana within their bodies was
sparse, and their signatures extremely faint. Even for Shatia, it took
time to find such weak reactions within the mountain range.

Having located them, she immediately headed toward the source. To avoid
detection, she refrained from using flight magic and made her way
through the dense forest on foot. She pushed aside thorny, withered
vegetation as she advanced, before finally reaching the location of the
Lizardmen. It happened to be a cliffside, and below the cliff, the
Lizardmen were marching along a path. Shatia hid behind the rocks and
began to observe them.

“So those are them… The number is roughly a hundred. Red scales… They
belong to the Red Dragon Tribe.”

Every one of the marching Lizardmen had red scales. They were all
massive with sharp, jagged scales and a savage appearance. There are
various tribes among Lizardmen, and those Shatia was currently watching
were of the group commonly known as the Red Dragon Tribe.

An aggressive tribe. Even among the Lizardmen—already considered a
ferocious race—they were recognized as particularly dangerous. The way
to identify them was by their red scales; usually, if one saw them,
escaping immediately was the only solution. Of course, given the high
physical ability of Lizardmen, the means of escape were limited.

“Hmm… A mystery. In these past several decades, the Lizardmen have
rarely moved. Why has such a large force suddenly mobilized?” Shatia
voiced her doubt while tilting her head.

It didn’t make sense. Unless something extraordinary happened, the
Lizardmen would never leave the secluded lands that were their homes.
They looked down on other races and hated the outside world so much they
didn’t even want to breathe the same air. The fact that they had
appeared leading an army suggested something major was afoot. And Shatia
wanted to know what that was.

“Gugoruru…”

“—!”

At that moment, a Lizardman suddenly appeared from beside the rocks
where Shatia was hiding. He was wearing a small cloth, and his attire
gave him the appearance of a thief.

Because he had so little mana, Shatia had been late in noticing his
presence. Whether he was a scout or not, the Lizardman lunged at her the
moment he saw Shatia in her child-like form. He leaped with his arms
wide, his mouth agape to reveal rows of fangs. Shatia immediately swung
her arm and unleashed an ice spear.

“Gogwavvaaaaa!!”

“*Tch*… To think I would let one get so close…!”

The Lizardman was blown back by the spear and slammed into a nearby
tree. However, his cloth was merely torn, and he had no visible external
injuries. Conversely, the ice spear had shattered.

As expected, Lizardman scales were hard. Shatia pushed herself up, put
strength into her arm, and swung it down with force.

“Sleep.”

The moment Shatia spoke, a tremendous gravity bore down on the
Lizardman. No matter how hard his scales were, they meant nothing in the
face of gravity; rather, because of his solid body, escape was
impossible. The Lizardman collapsed. Prostrate on the ground and
groaning, he let out a small shriek before losing consciousness.
Confirming he was down, Shatia swept her arm to the side to cancel the
spell and let out a short breath.

“To think they even have scouts… Do these fellows intend to start a
war?”

At first, Shatia had wondered if the Lizardmen were moving to some other
land—abandoning their settlement for some reason to find a new base.
That would have made sense for a migration of this size. However, the
Lizardmen had prepared scouts and were radiating a clear fighting
spirit. Even for Lizardmen, was there a need to be so overtly hostile?
This was what nagged at Shatia.

“It seems best to observe a little longer… what their objective is. I
must make that clear,” Shatia concluded as she rummaged through the
unconscious Lizardman’s belongings.

Everything has a purpose. Whether on a small or large scale, at the
bottom of it all always lies an answer. As long as that remained a
mystery, there was no way to deal with the current situation. Shatia
decided that for now, it would be unwise to move boldly and she
continued to observe cautiously.

Gently pulling out the doll tucked in her robe, Shatia’s eyes wavered.
Then, closing her eyes as she put it back, she reopened them, resolving
to follow the marching Lizardmen.

“Lately, there’ve been rumors that townspeople are being kidnapped…
Well, even if I say townspeople, there are only demons around here.
Still, this is information that can’t be ignored,” Lili said while
fiddling with her red hair.

While traveling through the wasteland, Lili had been gathering
information in the towns they passed through. Hearing her comment, her
sister Mimi hid behind her in fear. The Hero, who was listening while
sitting on a rock, also took on a stern expression and leaned in to
listen.

“Demons are being kidnapped…?” He questioned.

Lili nodded, “Yes. It’s just a rumor, but they say monsters that look
like lizards are snatching demons away…”

Mimi seemed to have an inkling of what it could mean. She shook her
head. It seemed unbelievable, but she managed to speak haltingly,
“Sister, could that possibly be…”

Lili finished her younger sister’s thought, “Lizardmen.”

A lizard-like appearance, bipedal, massive—from that information, the
only possible answer was Lizardmen. Having reached that conclusion, Lili
took on a solemn face despite her young appearance, while Mimi visibly
trembled with fear. Amidst this, only the Hero looked lost.

“Lizardmen?” He doubted.

“Humans probably don’t know much about them. Lizardmen rarely show
themselves, and they live quite far from the human continent,” Lili
spoke as if it were obvious, her tone somewhat mocking.

In truth, it wasn’t that the Hero was completely ignorant of Lizardmen.
He knew such beings existed among the many races, but since he had never
encountered one, he couldn’t imagine what kind of race they were.

Sensing this, Mimi began to explain while remaining hidden behind Lili,
“Lizardmen are a ferocious race. Words don’t work on them, and they
reject contact with the outside world due to their own culture. That’s
why mutual understanding is difficult. To be honest, they’re a race I’d
rather not encounter.”

The Hero nodded, understanding such a race could exist.

Though all races had their own cultures, they all shared a common tongue
and could reach a certain level of understanding. Therefore, it was a
bit of a shock to the Hero that there was a race where words were
useless and conversation impossible.

“Even Shatifahl had a hard time with them. They used to worship dragons…
back when we clashed, it took a long time to suppress them,” Lili said
nostalgically as she sat down on the sand.

Mimi nodded in agreement while fiddling with her fingers, “That was a
difficult time… it was the golden age of dragons, after all.”

Unable to keep up with the conversation, the Hero simply watched the
exchange in silence, but he understood one thing: the witches had been
trying to maintain the world’s balance even in places humans didn’t know
about. As expected, his sin was heavy. Realizing this once more, the
Hero clenched his fist in regret.

“They’re definitely plotting something. Otherwise, they wouldn’t go out
of their way to kidnap people. It’s better to take action early,” Lili
concluded, striking the ground with her hand.

Mimi still seemed hesitant, her face clouded with anxiety as if she
couldn’t agree with the proposal, but Lili’s gaze was fixed on the Hero.
She was appealing to him—the Hero should be the one to move. And he felt
the same way. He unclenched his fist and placed it over his chest. His
eyes met Lili’s. Her eyes were as clear as those of the silver-haired
witch he had seen once before.

“It is time to perform your duty, Hero. Uncover the Lizardmen’s plot.”

“Yeah… I know.”

In response to Lili’s command, the Hero gave a firm nod and clutched his
chest.

The weight of his sins remained heavy. Surely this guilt would never
lighten for the rest of his life. Even so, the Hero would continue to
fulfill his mission—so that he might escape this guilt even just a
little. Praying for a night where he could one day sleep in peace, his
spirit continued to chip away.

And watching this sight was one of the twins, a look of sadness in her
eyes.
