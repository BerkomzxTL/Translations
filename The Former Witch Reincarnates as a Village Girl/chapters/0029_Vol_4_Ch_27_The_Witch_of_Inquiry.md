# Chapter 27 – The Witch of Inquiry

**Part 4: Kingdom in Chaos**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Shatia passed through the mountains, her robe fluttering around her.
Though crossing the continent using flight magic was efficient, the road
to the Demon Kingdom was a long one. At the very least, she would need
to cross three more mountains. 

“*Phew*…” Shatia let out a small sigh. Descending to the ground for a
brief reprieve, she took a water bottle from her bag and murmured as she
took a sip, “Well, even traveling by flight magic is tiring in its own
way.”

Even for one boasting inexhaustible mana, the mental strain of traveling
was significant. Moreover, it was only natural for any passerby to be
suspicious of a child like Shatia traveling alone. She had to proceed
with caution.

Tying back her silver hair with a string, Shatia took a deep breath. The
area was shrouded in mist, making it impossible to see more than even a
few steps ahead. If she moved carelessly, she risked crashing into a
tree.

“…I have run out of food.”

Realizing her hand met no resistance when searching her bag, Shatia’s
face clouded with concern. She had consumed her rations faster than
expected. More importantly, the lack of sweets she had so enjoyed was a
painful blow. Shatia rested a hand against her chin, pondering her next
move.

At that moment, a scream reached her ears. Simultaneously, she felt a
mana reaction and realized there were humans nearby. Several of them. It
wasn’t particularly strange; even the Demon Kingdom had villages, and
ordinary demons often traversed these paths. This area in particular had
many towns, so it was common for peddlers to pass through. However, the
fact that there had been a scream was the problem.

Though she felt it was a bother, Shatia couldn’t simply pretend she
hadn’t heard it. She hurried toward the source of the cry. As expected,
she found a carriage being attacked by bandits.

“S-someone help us!!”

“Heheh, if you value your lives, leave all your valuables!”

The merchants were two demons: a man and a girl who appeared to be his
daughter. They gripped the reins, their faces contorted with terror.
Facing them were about ten demon bandits. Each wore a red robe with a
hood pulled low, and they bore tattoos that seemed to be the symbol of
their gang.

*There really is little difference between humans and demons in this
regard*, Shatia thought absentmindedly as she observed the bandits. The
man who seemed to be the leader was well-built with a thick beard,
looking much like a bear. He appeared to be of a certain age, and seeing
as his subordinates precisely executed his orders, he must have been in
the business for a long time. After confirming this, Shatia nodded to
herself and pulled her own hood deep over her head.

“There is no need for an elaborate disguise…”

Being in the form of a child would actually make them lower their guard.
The merchants might find it strange, but since she had no intention of
getting too deeply involved anyway, Shatia stepped out from the brush as
she was.

The bandits showed surprise at the sudden appearance of a child from the
bushes, but once they realized she appeared weak, they felt no threat
and flashed arrogant grins.

“Huh? What’s this, a brat? You with these merchants?”

“You’d better hand over your valuables too. You gotta have at least a
little pocket change on ya, right?”

They approached Shatia without a hint of caution. One closed in,
brandishing a knife to intimidate her. Shatia didn’t respond with words;
instead, she quietly swung her arm downward. In that instant, the
bandits collapsed to the ground, overcome by a crushing pressure.

“Gwah…!! Aaaagh!!”

“W-what? My body’s suddenly so heavy… gfuh!!”

Unable to offer any resistance, the bandits all fell to the earth,
eventually foaming at the mouth and fainting from the sheer pressure.

After confirming that they were all unconscious and using her mana to
check if any other enemies were lurking nearby, Shatia finally relaxed.
She approached the fallen bandits and began rummaging through their
pouches and belongings.

“Umu… as I thought, just dried meat and potatoes… There are none who
would normally carry sweets, I suppose…”

Though not particularly surprising, Shatia still looked visibly dejected
when she confirmed the bandits didn’t have what she wanted. Beside her,
the merchant father and daughter, who couldn’t quite grasp what had just
happened, stared at her with wide, blinking eyes. Eventually, Shatia
stuffed the food she had “confiscated” from the bandits into her bag and
let out a small sigh as she stood up.

As she turned to leave, the merchant’s daughter hurriedly called out to
stop her, “U-um! Who… who are you, exactly?”

“…Hm? Oh, are you speaking to me?” Hearing the girl’s call, Shatia
stopped and pointed to herself while her face remained hidden by the
hood. The girl nodded tentatively.

“I am just a traveler. You may call me the Silver Traveler… as I have
silver hair.”

Deciding it unwise to reveal her name, Shatia gave herself a makeshift
title. The merchants, seemingly having no intention of prying, simply
nodded and accepted it.

Sensing the situation over, the father finally relaxed his face and
released the reins he had been gripping so tightly, calming the horse
that had been huffing with agitation. “Silver Traveler… thank you so
much for saving us. Please, let us give you something in return…”

“No, I have already received my reward from the bandits. Just think of
me as a passing storm and forget this ever happened,” Shatia waved her
hand and began walking again. 

The merchant’s daughter wanted to repay the favor, but she found herself
unable to follow. For some reason, her legs felt as if they were
paralyzed, refusing to move. Shatia vanished from their sight without
looking back.

After securing food from the bandits, Shatia noticed the sky was growing
dark and began preparing to camp in the mountains for the night. She
started a campfire with fire magic and used presence-concealment magic
to ensure no monsters would attack her. Leaning against a tree, she
began combing her silver hair.

![](./images/0010_i-010.webp)

“I should arrive in a few days… My, has it really been decades since I
last visited the Demon Lord’s castle? It has been quite a while,”
returning the comb to her bag, Shatia murmured to herself and tied her
hair back again.

In fact, Shatia had visited the Demon Kingdom in the distant past. It
hadn’t been as an ally; rather, the demons had been unilaterally
pursuing her. Her visits had been rare and very long ago. She mused that
the Demon Lord had likely changed by now, and the cityscape was probably
quite different, causing her to let out a small sigh. She stared at her
still-immobile left arm and bit her lip in frustration.

She was certain that Cloak was the one who had stolen the grimoires from
the palace. She likely intended to use them for research or some other
scheme. Furthermore, Cloak seemed to be aware of her existence. Given
the rumors that she was affiliated with the Demon Kingdom, there was no
doubt she was up to no good. Despite her beautiful appearance, she had a
foul mouth and was constantly plotting. That was just the kind of witch
she was. Shatia warmed her hands by the fire, thinking of her fellow
witch.

Suddenly, she sensed a massive surge of mana nearby. The flames of her
campfire flickered wildly, and the wind began to howl. Shatia narrowed
her eyes, her guard at its peak.

“…After such a long time, will you not even show your face, Cloak?”
Shatia spoke to a void between the trees.

“…Hehe. As expected of Shatifahl. Even though I worked so hard to hide
myself, I still got busted.”

The darkness there shimmered. A beautiful woman appeared, wearing a
black coat and with blue hair tied near her shoulders. Her eyes glowed
blood-red, and upon seeing Shatia, Cloak—the Witch of Inquiry—burst into
laughter.

“Heh… Hahahaha! Shatifahl, what’s with that look! It’s hilarious up
close… You… you had such a nice body when you were a witch… and now look
at that flat chest! …Puhaha!”

Cloak clutched her stomach and laughed uproariously, her beautiful face
contorted. She pointed at Shatia’s chest and slapped her own knee
repeatedly. Despite her stunning looks, her reaction was like that of a
drunken man. Even the usually calm Shatia felt her mouth twitch. In
truth, she had her own thoughts about her growth since being
reincarnated. However, she managed to maintain a calm exterior so as not
to let Cloak see her insecurity.

“I am a child right now, so it is only natural. You truly haven’t
changed… At any rate, try to laugh with a bit more grace.”

“Puhahahaha!! No way!!”

Cloak’s laughter only intensified until she began to choke and cough,
eventually collapsing. After a few moments, her laughter subsided, and
she stood up slowly, her hair disheveled and her breath ragged.

“Whew… *phew*… Ah, that was a good laugh. Damn, you’ve really taken on a
funny form, Shatifahl… or should I say you’re going by Shatia now?”
Cloak asked vaguely as she wiped tears from her eyes.

Shatia neither confirmed nor denied the name; she simply narrowed her
eyes.

What Shatia was most concerned about was how much Cloak knew. The
incident in the capital was undoubtedly her doing. However, she didn’t
know when Cloak had realized her existence and true identity. If Cloak
had known for a long time and had orchestrated this situation with a
plan, Shatia needed to be extremely cautious. Cloak’s most terrifying
trait was her ability to force her targets into her own pace through
schemes.

“…I shall be direct. Cloak, I know you stole the grimoires from the
capital. I want you to show me a portion of them,” deciding it was
better to get straight to the point, Shatia stated her business. Trying
to feel each other out would only give Cloak the advantage. Cloak was
the one who had lured her to the Demon Kingdom in the first place, so
she must have known Shatia would have a request.

Cloak continued to chuckle, moving her long fingers to stroke her own
cheek, “Ah… the grimoire for human body creation… You need it to bring
back that little miss straight-laced hanging at your waist, right?”

“If you know that, then…!”

“Sorry, but I decline. It belongs to me.”

To Shatia’s surprise, Cloak even knew about Emerald. This meant Cloak
had been aware of her existence since the incident in the village. *Or
rather*, Shatia realized, *did she discover me then?* Regardless, if
Cloak knew the situation with Emerald, there should have been no reason
to refuse. The rejection left Shatia in disbelief.

“Cloak… that’s enough of your games…!”

Even if they were fellow witches, if Cloak intended to abandon their
comrade, Shatia wouldn’t hesitate. She began to chant a spell to
activate Lullaby, but before she could finish, Cloak swung her arm, and
two black snakes emerged from the darkness.

“*Phantoms of Guilt*.”

Cloak chanted the spell. The two black snakes lunged at Shatia, forcing
her to stop her casting and leap away. The snakes slammed into a tree
behind her but instantly turned into smoke, floating up toward Shatia in
the air before transforming back into snakes. Shatia clicked her tongue
and swung her arm, blowing them away with a pulse of mana. When she
landed on the ground, she found that Cloak had taken to the air.

“Try it if you can. You were the one who taught us magic, but we’re the
ones who mastered it… Who wins: quantity or quality?”

With that taunt, Cloak swung both arms, causing countless black snakes
to emerge from the surrounding darkness. The snakes’ eyes glowed red,
their tongues flickering as they looked down at Shatia. Shatia’s gaze
grew sharp, but she remained still, staring intently at Cloak.

“…Hehe, well, I just dropped by to say hello. The main event’s in *my*
Demon Kingdom. I’ll have a feast waiting for you… dearest mother.”

“…Cloak!”

Cloak’s beautiful face broke into a wide smile. She gave a graceful bow
before being swallowed up by the black snakes. When they all vanished,
Cloak was gone.

Left behind, Shatia stood by the flickering campfire and looked up at
the night sky with a sorrowful expression.
