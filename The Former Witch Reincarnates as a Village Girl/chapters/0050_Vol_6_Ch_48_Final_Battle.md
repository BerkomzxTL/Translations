# Chapter 48: Final Battle

An old, dilapidated church. Holes were punctured the ceiling here and
there, allowing the light of the sunset to spill inside. It was a
long-abandoned building, the sort of place children might use as a
secret playground. It was one of the things Shatia had requested from
Kousal: a deserted location with ample space. At this hour, the children
were gone, and with the furniture removed, the interior was quite vast.
Shatia sat upon the church’s dais and quietly let out a breath.

“It has been a while… since I have had such an unpleasant dream,” Shatia
spoke painedly, placing a hand to her forehead.

Only a short while ago, she had sustained more injuries than expected in
her battle against the witch and had lost consciousness from exhaustion.
During that time, she had seen a nostalgic dream that left her feeling
melancholic. If that girl—the one who was always joking—had been telling
the truth in what she said back then… Shatia shook her head to dispel
the dark thoughts.

“Shatifahl.”

“…Emerald. Are your wounds better?”

“Yes, for the most part.”

Shatia looked up to see Emerald standing there. Wrapped in bandages, she
looked terribly frail, reminding Shatia of when they had first met.
There was a slight awkwardness to her gait; perhaps her injuries had not
yet fully healed. Though worried, Shatia said nothing and waited for
Emerald to approach.

Emerald asked, her hands clasped anxiously at her chest, “Um, when I was
fighting the monster, I felt something, if only slightly… Is it possible
that the one you were fighting, Shatifahl, was…”

“Yes. A witch,” Shatia answered without a shred of hesitation.

Knowing the truth, Emerald’s eyes widened and she froze, unable to speak
for a while. Shatia stepped down from the dais and ran a hand through
her hair as if to distract herself.

“I am almost certain of their identity. Well, it could be a bluff, so I
have a few things to verify… but to do that, I must first take care of
the monster,” ignoring the dazed Emerald whose mind couldn’t quite keep
up, Shatia continued. She already knew who it was, but she deliberately
chose not to tell Emerald yet.

“So, just like I was before, they’re trying to get revenge on humans…”

“No, that is not it. Their goal was decided from the very beginning…
Ever since that time, they likely intended to do this.”

“Eh…?”

Shatia spoke while recalling the words of that witch from her dream.

Back then, the girl had spoken as if she intended to eradicate all of
humanity. Because she was a prankster, Shatia had assumed it was just
another joke. But if it had been the truth, then she had been planning
and waiting for her chance all this time.

“Why do you think the witches were wiped out in the first place,
Emerald?”

“T-That was… by the hands of the Hero…”

“Indeed, that is correct. However, there are several things that feel
out of place. How did they discover the locations of the witches who
live in hiding? And to begin with, there should have been those among
the witches who were impossible to kill.”

“…Ah,” Emerald’s expression flickered with a realization, “The Undying
Witch… Vestalis?”

Shatia gave a slow nod at Emerald’s trembling words.

The Undying Witch, Vestalis. Just as her name implied, she was an
outlier even among witches, governing the power of immortality. It was
said that no matter how many times a sword rent her body, it would
reconnect, and no matter how large a cannonball blasted a hole through
her, it would simply close up. Because she was a being of both great
longevity and immortality, she was particularly feared.

“Correct. No matter how much of a Hero one might be, there is no way to
defeat the undying Vestalis… I asked him about that once…”

Shatia understood the power of immortality well. That was why she felt a
sense of incongruity regarding Vestalis’s defeat. She had asked about it
while traveling with the Hero, and remembering the answer, Shatia
couldn’t help but smile.

“…Apparently, Vestalis was defeated with a single blow.”

“…Huh? What? No… how is that possible?”

Emerald froze once more, her head tilting in jerky, mechanical
movements.

“Perhaps it is better to say she was weakened. As if she had been
prepared specifically to be defeated by the Hero…”

“T-Then… is Vestalis the one behind everything…?”

“Who can say. I am about to verify that now. I must also confirm what
state she is in currently.”

Shatia looked up at the ceiling riddled with holes. The sun had dipped
considerably; night would soon fall. *It is nearly time*, Shatia
thought, placing a finger to her lips.

“You should step back, Emerald.”

Leaving those cryptic words, Shatia walked toward the center of the
church. Emerald, not understanding the reason for her actions, simply
watched, but soon she felt a sense of wrongness. Something ominous. It
was a sensation Emerald herself knew all too well.

“…………HAAAAAAAH.”

What appeared was a monster shrouded in jet-black. Its sharp eyes
glowing alongside its dark breath, the monster stepped into the church
through the wide-open doors. Upon recognizing the presence of Shatia and
Emerald, it bared its fangs.

The monster growled, “Was it you… who was leaking this loathsome aura?”

Shatia replied with an exaggerated bow, “I thank you for coming,
monster. It seems the bait I scattered through the city was to your
liking.”

In the morning, Shatia had traveled throughout the city, scattering her
own mana. Although it was mana, she had merely placed concentrated mana
particles; they would have no effect on the human body. However, anyone
capable of sensing mana would recognize the scent. Furthermore, the mana
of Shatia—a witch—would be identified immediately.

“It seems you only target evildoers and those with power. Did you see me
as a ‘strong one’ as well?”

“Strong? You are far more than that; you are a deviant! One who
transcends the framework… A being like you must be crushed
immediately!!”

Kicking off the ground, the monster shouted its declaration. Its
attitude was that of seeing Shatia purely as an enemy. Furthermore, it
had seen through to her essence. It had instinctively sensed the power
of Shatia, who held immense strength even among witches.

“Then what of you? Do you know who you are, or how you were born?”

The monster fell silent at Shatia’s question. The turbulent aura
subsided, and it suddenly grew quiet, lowering the arms it had been
raising.

“That… I am searching for that myself. Therefore, I live according to my
instincts.”

“Then what will you do if those instincts were manipulated? If you were
designed to live in such a way… what would you do?”

“…What did you say?”

The monster’s shoulders shook in agitation at Shatia’s sudden question.
The hypothesis Shatia presented—that the monster’s very existence was a
fabrication—was a wild, startling claim. Tapping her foot, she began her
explanation.

“In this world, there exists such a thing as a ‘Curse’.”

Shatia raised her index finger. Seeing that finger, the monster’s intent
to attack strangely vanished; it retracted its bloodlust and listened to
her.

“There are many types of curses. Some affect others, like Emerald’s, and
some are like diseases that eat away at the body.”

She continued, casting a sidelong glance toward Emerald. Hearing herself
mentioned, Emerald awkwardly placed a finger near her eye.

“Among them, the most terrifying is what is known as the Curse of the
Reaper. Those afflicted cannot meet a normal death; it is said they are
transformed into grotesque forms.”

“…What does that curse have to do with anything?”

“I shall be blunt. You are someone whose form was changed by a curse.”

The monster froze completely for an instant. It was shaken by the
revelation of its true identity. Whether it was shock or sorrow, the
monster felt a fear similar to that of knowing the truth about itself.
It didn’t know why. Only fear began to envelop its being.

“…That’s impossible.”

“It is the truth. You were placed under the Curse of the Reaper and
became that form. Forgetting your memories and yourself… you ended up as
a monster that does nothing but repeat acts of destruction. A certain
witch orchestrated it so that things would move in her favor.”

The monster shook its head, placing its hands to its face as if to deny
the truth. However, Shatia continued to speak, hammering the truth home.
The monster took a weak step back.

“Shatifahl… then the enemy witch is…”

Realizing the monster was a person transformed by a curse, Emerald, who
had been standing back, approached Shatia. But in that moment, the
entire church suddenly began to shake. A black haze-like substance
poured in through the gaps in the ceiling, spreading out like a fog.

“So, you have come…!”

Shatia clicked her tongue in annoyance at the opponent’s movements,
which were faster than expected, and kicked off the ground. She managed
to evade the mist coming for her, but she had no time to protect the
monster or Emerald. The mist slammed into the floor, destroying
everything in the vicinity. The shockwave blew the monster away and sent
Emerald flying against the wall.

Shatia spun once in the air and leaped onto a large piece of rubble
embedded in the floor to assess the situation. Black mist was still
pouring from the ceiling. The monster seemed buried somewhere in the
wreckage, but she could see that Emerald was safe.

Shatia shouted her instructions to the bewildered Emerald, “Emerald! You
received the necessary items from Kousal, did you not? Break the curse!”

“Eh… ah, yes!”

The other thing she had requested from Kousal was to gather the
materials required to break the curse once she was certain the monster
was a victim. Those materials had already been gathered and prepared
behind the church. All that was left was to break it. However, a massive
amount of black mist swirled toward Emerald to prevent her from doing
so.

“I am your opponent…!”

Seeing this, Shatia lunged forward. Floating in the air with flight
magic, she unleashed a shockwave imbued with a massive amount of mana.
The mist was scattered, losing its form. Then, as the other mists
gathered, they turned their focus toward Shatia. Shatia gave a satisfied
nod and rose higher with her flight magic, evading the mist’s onslaught
in mid-air.

The mist condensed into sharp, pointed shapes and lunged at her. She was
flying toward the ceiling; there was no escape. Yet Shatia did not slow
down. At the very last moment, she moved sideways, causing the mist to
strike the ceiling directly. With a roar, the ceiling collapsed, and the
black mist lost its shape, scattering into the air.

“How violent. Do you want to defeat me so badly? It saddens me that my
daughters are all in such a rebellious phase.”

Leaping out from the smoke, Shatia landed on the church’s roof with a
light thud. The twilight illuminated the silver-haired girl, surrounded
by the dancing black mist.

“I shall settle this. You are a seed I sowed. I shall be the one to
pluck you,” Shatia made her declaration, looking up at the swirling
black mist.

In that instant, the mists moved in unison, changing their shapes. Some
became like thorns. Others took the form of spears. Some looked like
giant arms. All were painted in total black, writhing like fog. They
lunged at Shatia simultaneously, but she evaded them elegantly by flying
straight up.

“Do you think you cannot be caught because you have no form? …Naive!”

While soaring through the air, Shatia chanted a spell, generating chains
of mana in her hands. As she released them, the chains split into
countless copies, binding the black mist that had gathered at a single
point. The mist tried to use its nature to turn into haze and escape,
but, for some reason, it couldn’t break free. Upon closer inspection,
the mana chains were glowing, and threads of mana were flowing from
them. Shatia had captured the black mist by directly injecting her mana
into it.

Shatia yanked the chains with all her might, swinging the bound black
mist up, spinning it once in the sky, and slamming it onto the roof.
With a dull boom, the mist writhed and churned, and from within it, a
human figure painted in black mist tumbled out.

“Come out, you tomboy. I shall give you a thorough spanking.”

“Gugah…!!”

The girl of mist tried to hide herself again, black mist overflowing
from her body, but Shatia immediately unleashed a mana shockwave,
blowing the mist away.

The tide had clearly turned in Shatia’s favor. The mist girl’s face,
painted in black, contorted in a grimace as she let out a small click of
her tongue.

◇

“Quick, please stand here…! I’m going to break the curse now.”

“Guh…”

Meanwhile, inside the church, Emerald was dragging the fallen monster,
preparing to break the curse. Although the monster had been injured by
the black mist’s attack, it was still conscious, so there was no
problem. All that remained was to finish the necessary preparations and
begin the ritual.

Breaking a curse was not as difficult as one might expect. The only
grueling part was the massive amount of mana required, which was why
ordinary people couldn’t do it and why it was considered so difficult in
the first place. But for Emerald, a witch, there was no issue. She took
gemstones from the bag that had been prepared and began to draw a magic
circle on the floor.

“W-Why are you helping me… I am…”

“How should I know? I’m breaking your curse because Shatifahl told me
to.”

Emerald answered the monster’s question sharply but thoroughly, despite
her agitation. Her hands moved desperately to draw the circle, and it
was soon complete. All that remained was to place the gemstones in the
required spots and activate the ritual. Emerald wiped the sweat from her
forehead with one hand and turned her gaze toward the monster.

“I don’t know who you are… but, I’m begging you… the enemy witch is
strong enough to have defeated Shatifahl once… so please, save her…!”

Emerald, who possessed no offensive magic, made her plea with a pained
expression. The monster’s eyes wavered in bewilderment, and it looked up
through the exposed ceiling. A massive amount of black mist was dancing
there. It drifted across the sky, broad enough to blot out the light.
