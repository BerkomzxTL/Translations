# Chapter 30 – Secret Negotiations

To Demon Lord Belpheus, Shatia was a being who existed in a completely
different dimension from himself. Though he had only met her once, he
had felt her true power with his own skin and realized she was something
he could never hope to match. Yet, for the current Belpheus, Shatia was
a necessity. She was a military force he absolutely required to calm the
storm currently brewing within the Demon Kingdom.

Shatia sat on the sofa opposite Belpheus and crossed her legs, making
herself at home. Her lack of reserve was no different from when they had
first met. Though her form had changed, the presence of the Witch
Shatifahl was unmistakably there.

Seeing this, Belpheus formed a small, nostalgic smile and said, “You’ve…
shrunk quite a bit, Shatifahl.”

“I go by Shatia now. You should call me that as well. After all, this
form is merely that of a village girl named Shatia,” Shatia replied with
a smile.

Currently, she was the girl Shatia, not the Witch Shatifahl.
Furthermore, using the name Shatifahl here would cause various
inconveniences. Considering this, Shatia warned him, and Belpheus picked
up on her intentions and nodded.

“You, on the other hand, have grown quite large. You were such a lovely
child once, but now you’ve come to resemble Agito… How lamentable,” she
said as if grieving the fact that Belpheus had come to resemble his
father. She was familiar with the former Demon Lord, after all. She
placed a finger to her eye, mimicking a crying gesture. 

“Heh… You’re the only one who could say such a thing,” Belpheus smiled
wryly at the sight and shook his head as if to say, “You’ve got me.”

Everyone around him knew of the former Demon Lord. To the demons, Agito
was practically a hero. To call the fact that he resembled Agito
“lamentable”—even as a joke—was something only Shatia could do.
Recognizing once again that Shatia was an existence outside his
comprehension, Belpheus returned his gaze to her.

“How is that Agito doing? Surely that hunk of muscle wouldn’t die so
easily,” she asked, recalling Agito’s existence.

Belpheus answered flatly, “My father has retired and is living in a
secluded region… It seems his joints have been giving him trouble
lately.”

Since it seemed he wasn’t dead, Shatia felt a brief sense of relief.
However, since no further emotions sprouted beyond that, she simply
replied with a short, “I see,” she continued, “…You went through the
trouble of bringing me into such a deserted room. Surely you have some
business with me, Belpheus?”

Once the small talk ended, Shatia went straight to the heart of the
matter. She had nothing to hide, so she didn’t hesitate. Belpheus showed
a moment of surprise at Shatia’s characteristic bluntness, but then he
quietly narrowed his eyes.

“To get to the point… I want you to save the Demon Kingdom.” Belpheus,
believing that indirect speech would be an insult to Shatia, conveyed
his desire immediately.

“…Hoh?” And upon hearing this, Shatia placed a hand to her mouth in
amusement, her fingers moving as if she were appraising him.

“Currently, the Demon Kingdom is split in two… between the comrades who
support me, and the radicals who seek to dominate the human continent,”
he explained, holding up his fingers. He spoke desperately, trying to
convey the current state of the Demon Kingdom and make her understand
the magnitude of the crisis facing the nation.

Shatia listened in silence.

“The radicals are led by Cloak, the Witch of Inquiry… or rather, it’s
more accurate to say that the demons are latching onto a plan Cloak has
devised. Cloak intends to dominate the human world, and more than half
of the demons support her.”

“And those who oppose it are you and your followers? For a king to be
forsaken by his people… how terrifying.”

Cloak hadn’t publicly revealed herself as a witch, but she was plotting
the domination of the human world in her capacity as a researcher. Many
demons had agreed with her and desired invasion. The scale had become
something that even the Demon Lord Belpheus couldn’t stop, and the Demon
Kingdom had fallen into a state of near-secession. It wouldn’t be
strange if a civil war broke out at any moment.

What Belpheus feared was Cloak’s recklessness. Cloak’s actions were far
too unreasonable. Using her own abilities, she had led the demons to
attack the northern fortress and successfully captured it, but the
result was numerous casualties. While sacrifice is a part of war,
Belpheus, as a king, could not ignore it. He had to stop her—he had to
stop Cloak.

“Cloak is prepared to accept any sacrifice if it means destroying the
humans… Her actions have gone too far… I beg of you, please stop her…!”
For the first time, Belpheus bowed his head and pleaded with Shatia.

Seeing him like that, Shatia was reminded of Agito’s form in the past.
But Agito was a Demon Lord who would never, under any circumstances, bow
his head. Shatia closed her eyes quietly, her lips twisting slightly.

She understood Cloak’s general objective. To put it simply, it was
likely revenge against the humans. Cloak had high pride and loathed
being looked down upon. Therefore, she was using the demons to trigger a
war and aimed for the domination of the human continent.

If nothing else, Shatia could appreciate it as a very easy-to-understand
method. The war between humans and demons had continued since ancient
times. With a little push, one could easily ignite a fierce clash. If
the one providing that push was a witch, the intensity would be even
greater.

However, Shatia did not immediately nod. She placed a hand to her cheek
as if deep in thought and tilted her head slowly. She casually remarked,
“Stop her, you say… Who was it that first suggested joining forces?” 

Belpheus flinched. He didn’t open his mouth; instead he raised his
lowered head slightly, though he avoided meeting her eyes. That was all
the confirmation Shatia needed, and she let out a small sigh.

“Belpheus… you are very much like your father, Agito. He was boisterous,
so he was easy to read, but he was always trying to get his hands on the
power of the witches. With strong ambition, he sought to make everything
his own.”

Shatia spoke while picturing Agito’s face in her mind.

Agito was a man who, for better or worse, had no hidden cards.
Regardless of formalities, he would convey his demands directly and
often caused trouble for those around him. He was driven by an
unwavering ambition. To others, it was a nuisance, but some felt a
strange charm in his boisterous personality and followed him.

But Belpheus was different. He had a quiet personality and possessed a
composure truly befitting a king. On the surface, he was the opposite of
Agito, but his heart had firmly inherited his father’s ambition. And
because his heart was the opposite of his personality, he had a
troublesome nature.

In short, Shatia’s assessment of Belpheus was that he was a troublesome
person who, despite his reserved personality, would quite slyly sneak in
his own demands wherever possible.

“At the very least, you desired it too, did you not? To have the power
of a witch in your hands… and then you met Cloak. I do not know which of
you brought up the idea, but you two built a cooperative relationship.
Do you not think it a bit selfish to want to back out now?”

Shatia tilted her head slightly. It was like a scene of a teacher
instructing a student on the right and wrong of a matter. Belpheus
raised his head, his lips trembling. He tried to say something, but the
words that reached his throat vanished without taking form. A single
bead of sweat rolled down his pale cheek.

“…It is true that I met Cloak and formed an agreement.”

Finally, Belpheus began to speak. Admitting his own actions, he started
to tell the story of when he first met Cloak. Since it was a story
Shatia was interested in, she listened in silence.

“She told me… that if I wished for it, I could make the Demon Kingdom
the strongest nation. That it would become an invincible country that
wouldn’t lose to any other… That’s what she told me.”

“…………”

Belpheus clenched his fists and spoke with a bitter look on his face.
Shatia knew why he was reacting that way.

To make the Demon Kingdom the strongest nation was a dream shared by all
demons since the first Demon Lord. “Strongest nation” was somewhat
lacking in specificity, but in short, it meant the Demon Kingdom would
become the country that ruled the world. That was why they sought to
destroy humanity, their greatest obstacle. Shatia speculated that Cloak
had likely taken advantage of that desire.

“But her methods are far too violent… so you want to act as if the
promise never happened?”

“…! Ever since the invasion of the human continent began, hundreds of
casualties have appeared one after another! The masses haven’t realized
it yet. That they are tightening the noose around their own necks!”

Belpheus slammed his fist onto the table. It wasn’t that he was feeling
anger. Rather, his expression was twisted with a sense of frustration.
Shatia let out a small, exasperated sigh and crossed her arms.

“You should be well aware that sacrifice is a part of war. No matter how
faint-hearted you are, surely you understand that?”

“But there is a limit! If it were just a simple war, that would be one
thing, but Cloak has begun to reach out beyond the human nations. Her
goal isn’t domination—it’s destruction!”

Here, Shatia finally understood what Belpheus was afraid of. For him,
the objective was domination, not war. Rather than destroying enemy
nations, showing his power was the most important thing. But Cloak’s
focus was not there; it was destruction to achieve her revenge. And that
desire was increasing in extremity, reaching beyond just the human
nations.

At this rate, rather than becoming the rulers of the world, the demons
would likely find themselves isolated with every country as their enemy.
Belpheus was afraid of that outcome.

“Hmm… I believe I understand the gist of the situation,” Shatia nodded
repeatedly while organizing the information in her head. Then, with a
satisfied smile, she looked at Belpheus, “I understand that you are
having trouble handling Cloak… However, my objective is the grimoire
Cloak stole. I cannot afford to get involved in the affairs of the Demon
Kingdom.”

Hearing Shatia’s refusal, Belpheus let out a small grunt of frustration.
However, he didn’t argue, nor did he seem like he had anything to add.
He hung his head in resignation, his shoulders slumping in obvious
dejection.

Shatia uncrossed her arms. Then, strangely, she broke into an eerie
smile.

“But well, it seems my daughter is being quite a nuisance. I suppose I
must give her a bit of a scolding.”

It was by no means an act of kindness. It was simply that her fellow
witch was acting up, so she would stop her. It was truly nothing more
than that. But to Belpheus, it was a welcome helping hand; he raised his
head with a bright expression, as if the fog had cleared.
