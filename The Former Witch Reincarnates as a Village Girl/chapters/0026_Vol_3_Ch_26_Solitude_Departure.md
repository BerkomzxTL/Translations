# Chapter 26 – Solitude, Departure

“Ahh, dammit. The golem got trashed… I thought a high-mobility type that
gives the enemy no time to attack would do the trick… I guess things
never go quite as planned.”

A woman stood suspended in the sky above the ruined palace. Looking down
at the deactivated ancient golem and the two children beside it, her
face was twisted in a look of profound disgust. She clutched several
grimoires tightly to her chest, as if they were precious treasures, and
let out a heavy sigh.

“The subordinates I received from that Demon Lord guy got themselves
captured by the soldiers, too. No choice, then… gotta pull out. Well, I
did get my hands on the grimoires, so I suppose I’ll call that a win.”

The woman surveyed the area outside the palace. Soldiers were gathering,
forming ranks. Judging it too dangerous to remain any longer, she
decided to abandon her subordinates rather than try to rescue them.

Regardless, her primary objective had already been achieved. Releasing
the seal on the ancient golem had merely been a whim. It didn’t
particularly matter if it was defeated. Looking at the girl who had
taken it down, the woman’s lips curled into a mocking sneer.

“Then, I shall be waiting for you in my country, the Demon Kingdom…
dearest leader of the Witches, Shatifahl,” Cloak, the Witch of Inquiry,
murmured as she bowed deeply and politely. With her crimson eyes burning
quietly, she resolved to surpass Shatia the next time they met.

In the next instant, Cloak vanished from the spot like smoke. All that
remained were the ruined tower and the broken palace, with wind blowing
softly through the wreckage.

◇

The day after the golem rampage, a rumor began to spread through the
royal capital.

It was said that a single student from the magic academy had repelled
the golems singlehandedly. At first, no one believed it, but the
existence of the golem carcasses and the testimonies of the soldier
witnesses proved the rumor true. Under normal circumstances, the public
would never believe a child could defeat a golem, academy student or
otherwise. While they harbored doubts about the child’s power, they were
simultaneously filled with gratitude. The city had been saved because of
her. To express their thanks, people visited the mansion where she
stayed or sent letters.

On the other hand, some raised voices of dissent, questioning if the
child might have been involved in the incident herself. However, the
soldiers explained that the palace attack was the work of demons and
that the culprits were already in custody.

Questions remained as to how demons—who they were supposed to be
fighting on the front lines—had suddenly managed to infiltrate the
palace, but it was announced that these details would be investigated in
due time.

In any case, demons were involved. Human society had suffered a major
blow. Several spellbooks had been lost, and the ancient golem, which had
been sealed away for years as a secret weapon, had gone out of control.
Some considered this a declaration of war and argued for a return to
conflict with the demons.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
“They invaded our country and caused immense damage… This is clearly a
declaration of war! We must take up our swords once more!”

“But how did they infiltrate the palace? It was the same with the
northern front… They appear out of nowhere. What manner of trick are
they using?”

Heated debates broke out in the palace conference room. Some desired
war, some were suspicious of the nature of the demons’ invasion, and
some even suggested surrendering. All were anxious. This attack had
provided a justification for war. Just when the world was finally free
of the witches, it was on the verge of being swallowed by chaos once
again. It was a fate they desperately wanted to avoid.

“In any case, it is a miracle that there were no casualties this time.
We should not agitate the situation further; we must not let the public
fall into a state of panic,” an elderly man declared, causing the others
to fall silent and retreat back to their seats.

Despite the scale of the incident, it was indeed surprising that there
had been no deaths. There were injuries, of course, but having no
casualties in the face of such an event was nothing short of a stroke of
luck. This outcome was due entirely to the girl who had defeated the
ancient golem.

It was hard to believe, but a young girl from the magic academy had
defeated it all by herself. If that thing had reached the city, the
defenseless populace would have been wiped out in a tragedy. The old man
sensed something abnormal about the girl’s power, but he couldn’t simply
ignore her achievement.

He cleared his throat quietly and said, “We must show our gratitude to
the girl. The capital was saved because of her… The palace can be
repaired. For now, it is best not to reopen old wounds.”

There was no need to cause an uproar or declare war to an already
anxious public. For now, the priority was to increase vigilance against
demon infiltration and to strengthen their defenses. It was not yet time
to act. They needed to rebuild and solidify their foundations. If war
was to come, it would be after that.

With that conclusion, the turbulent meeting came to an end.

◇

“Eh? Shatia-chan… You won’t be coming to school for a while?”

Concerned about Shatia, who had been injured during the incident, Laika
had come to the mansion to visit her. Laika herself had been injured by
the debris, but her wounds weren’t severe; she only had a few bandages
on her arms and legs. Surprisingly, however, Shatia had been badly hurt,
with her left arm currently in a cast.

“Indeed. It seems that when my left arm was hit, I was exposed to some
miasma… The wound simply won’t heal,” Shatia explained while stroking
the cast on her arm.

It appeared that the ancient golem’s fusion had granted it a
constitution that produced miasma—a toxic form of essence, which Shatia
had absorbed when she was hit. Because of this, the wound wouldn’t
respond to healing magic, leaving her arm immobile.

“I shall be departing on a journey of recuperation for a while. My
teacher told me that soaking in a fairy spring should heal it, you see.”

“I see… then I won’t be able to see Shatia-chan for a while.”

Hearing that Shatia wouldn’t be attending school for a while, Laika’s
expression darkened significantly. She fell into her old habit of
fidgeting with her fingers, the atmosphere around her turning somber.

“Don’t fret,” Shatia chuckled and reached out with her good arm to pat
Laika’s head. She reassured her, “You will be fine even without me.
You’ve gained a strong spirit… I don’t doubt you will stand tall
regardless of any bullying.”

Hearing this from the person she respected, Laika seemed to regain a bit
of confidence; her eyes wavered, but she nodded firmly. However, a
lonely shadow lingered on her face as she looked down. “But still, it’s
going to be a little lonely…”

“Kuku, for me as well,” Shatia agreed, though she knew it was childish.

Though it had only been two months, the time she spent with Laika had
been truly enjoyable. It felt as if she had returned to the days back
when she was a witch. Thinking of the past she could never return to,
Shatia quietly closed her eyes.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
After seeing Laika off, Shatia prepared for her journey in her room.
Because it was for medical treatment, her absence from school had been
excused. Since she was the hero who suppressed the incident, she had
been granted special dispensation.

Shatia looked at her immobile left arm and tilted her head. She
murmured, “Surprisingly… it is possible to be accepted.”

The experience of having her great power accepted was different from her
time as a witch. She was a little surprised by the turn of events.

She had expected to be restrained or abducted as an abnormal existence,
but instead, she was hailed as a hero. While she was being treated at
the mansion, visitor after visitor came to offer their thanks. She
hadn’t known how to react, and for once, Shatia was left bewildered.

She let out a small sigh as she stroked the doll at her waist. If
Emerald could see this, how happy would she be? For someone who desired
coexistence with humans, this was the outcome she would have wanted
most. In truth, Shatia would’ve had the opportunity to revive her
through this incident, but according to the soldiers, the grimoires had
been stolen by the demons.

The culprits behind this incident—the demon race. They had used some
unknown method to suddenly infiltrate the palace and cause the golems to
go berserk.

“…There is a mastermind behind them,” Shatia narrowed her eyes as she
murmured.

Certainly, demons were involved in this incident. But that wasn’t all.
There was the rumor that the demons possessed a witch. Despite fighting
on the edge of the continent, they had suddenly captured the northern
fortress, a place close in proximity to the capital. And now, this
sudden infiltration of the palace. Shatia had a good idea who would be
capable of such things.

Cloak, the Witch of Inquiry. Contrary to her beautiful appearance, she
loathed humans and truly believed that witches were the pinnacle of all
living things. She was a dedicated researcher of magic who often
challenged Shatia to duels in the past.

Judging by the handwriting in the grimoires and the nature of the
infiltration, she was the only one who could be the mastermind. Shatia
was certain of it.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
“Shatia-chan, are you ready?”

Loreid knocked on the door and entered. He had a bag in hand, prepared
for travel. Naturally, he intended to accompany Shatia on this healing
journey. Since he was technically Shatia’s guardian, he felt obligated
to go.

However, Shatia’s expression was grim.

“Indeed. I am almost finished, teacher.”

She turned toward her tutor.

“I am truly grateful to you, teacher. You brought me to the capital and
allowed me to attend the academy. You have my heartfelt thanks.”

“Eh, what’s with the sudden formality, Shatia-chan?”

Loreid looked puzzled by Shatia’s sudden seriousness. Ignoring his
confusion, she walked slowly toward him, held up her right hand, and
chanted a spell. A magic circle appeared before his eyes and emitted a
blinding light.

“But I am sorry. My destination is not a fairy spring… but the Demon
Kingdom.”

As she made her declaration, Shatia moved her fingers and manipulated
Loreid’s memories.

Under the influence of the memory manipulation magic, he would believe
that Shatia had gone on her healing journey alone, and that he had plans
to stay in the capital to continue his research. Since he was an
existence that couldn’t officially appear in public, the populace
wouldn’t realize Loreid had stayed behind. Laika and the others would
believe Shatia was on her trip, never suspecting for a moment that she
was heading for the Demon Kingdom.

Shatia bowed slightly in apology to her teacher standing in a daze. On
the desk, she left a large pile of gold coins, her reward for resolving
the incident, which she intended for him to use for his research
expenses. 

Picking up the bag from her bed, she slowly trudged out of the room with
a lonely expression. Leaving behind the mansion where she had been cared
for for two months, she used presence-concealment magic to vanish into
the crowd.

Shatia’s next destination was confirmed—the Demon Kingdom, where tragedy
and turmoil awaited.
