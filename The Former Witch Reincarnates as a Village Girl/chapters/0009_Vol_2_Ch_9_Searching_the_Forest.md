# Chapter 9 – Searching the Forest

After playing with Moffy, Shatia immediately headed for the village
chief’s house. She found a crowd gathered there already, and peering
inside, she saw the knight from the other day. He was accompanied by
several comrades, and they carried an air as if they were about to march
off to war.

*He came back to give his thanks recently, so he should have no business
left in this village,* Shatia thought, tilting her head as she observed
them.

“Then… Are you suggesting a witch has been resurrected?”

“It’s yet to be confirmed for certain. However, eyewitness reports
suggest one has appeared in this region. We’re conducting an
investigation, but please, remain vigilant.”

The knight and the chief were already deep in conversation with grim
looks on their faces, creating an atmosphere that made it impossible to
interject. Reacting to the word “witch,” Shatia’s brow twitched.

The ritual Barbas performed had ended in failure. Therefore, no witch
should have been resurrected. *Then why is that word coming up?* Shatia
scrutinized the knight’s expression. He didn’t look like he was lying.

“I shall take my leave, then…”

“Indeed. May the protection of the village spirits be upon you all.”

Once the talk concluded, the knight donned his helmet and departed the
village with his men. The villagers’ faces were clouded with unease, and
even the chief looked somber. Even Shatia, hearing the word “witch” left
her with a nagging, unsettled feeling. To fulfill her original purpose,
she approached the chief inside the house.

“Chief, who were those people?”

“Ah, Shatia. That was the knight from the other day… He says that while
it isn’t certain, someone believed to be a witch has appeared nearby.”

Shatia maintained her usual detached manner, but the chief, who had
known her since she was a baby, welcomed her warmly without a second
thought. He began to recount what the knight had told him.

“Is that information reliable?”

“They only said it was someone who *might* be a witch. The knights seem
to be investigating to confirm the truth of it.”

Apparently, reports of a witch-like figure had reached the capital, and
the knights were ordered to investigate. Since the truth of the rumor
was still unknown, the chief didn’t have any more to say. However, for
Shatia, this was not something so light as to brush off. A fellow witch
might be alive. If that were true, she couldn’t afford to sit still.

“And one more thing. It seems demons are also on the move. I’m not sure
if it is because of the rumors of the witch, but… I must warn the
villagers not to go outside for a while.”

“Demons… you say?”

Shatia’s eyes widened in surprise at the unexpected news.

The demon race. A species hostile to humanity, possessing high physical
capabilities and immense amounts of mana. They dwelled in the region
known as the Dark Continent. To Shatia the Witch, they were just
creatures from a different race, not so different from humans. But for
reasons beyond her understanding, humans and demons had been at war
since ancient times.

For such demons to be near this remote village would no doubt be a
terrifying prospect for the villagers. Between Barbas and this, the
village had been far too noisy lately. If a witch were truly resurrected
on top of that, all would surely fall into chaos.

Shatia narrowed her eyes, contemplating her next move.

*Was the mana I felt earlier from a fellow witch? …Or was it…*

Resting her chin on her hand, Shatia recalled the sensation she had felt
earlier. That unpleasant, viscous feeling—like a lingering grudge. Yet,
it also felt strangely nostalgic. She didn’t know if it belonged to a
fellow witch, but she felt this was something she had to investigate
herself.

Shatia decided to depart immediately. Fortunately, she had told her
mother she would be playing with Moffy, so as long as she told Moffy a
quick lie, she wouldn’t be caught. After ensuring no one was watching,
she used flight magic and soared out of the village in an instant.

However, once out, Shatia realized she didn’t actually know where to go.
The unpleasant presence she felt had been fragmentary; she couldn’t
trace which direction it had come from. And she couldn’t rely on the
knights’ information either, as she didn’t have the details. *What to
do?* Shatia landed in the forest and racked her brain.

“Well then, where should I begin my search?”

While tapping her head with her finger, she suddenly picked up the
presence of the knights. It seemed they were investigating this part of
the forest. *If I follow them, I might encounter the witch,* she
thought. Suppressing her presence and blending into the foliage, she
headed in their direction.

“For now, I shall follow the knights. If I do happen upon a witch, I can
simply knock them unconscious. It shouldn’t be a problem.”

Once the knights were in her sight, Shatia trailed them cautiously. She
nearly tripped over a tree root once or twice, but thanks to her flight
magic, she maintained her balance and could avoid making any sound.

It seemed even the knights didn’t know the exact location of the witch;
they were searching blindly. *Perhaps there was no need to follow them
after all,* Shatia thought with a yawn, but at that moment, she detected
an ominous mana from afar. The thought that it might be the witch 
briefly crossed her mind and Shatia instinctively braced herself, but
upon grasping the aura more closely, she realized it was different; it
was a sinister mana steeped in pure malice. Shatia let out a small sigh
of relief.

“The presence of demons… so they truly are nearby. Hmm, perhaps I shall
show my face for a period.”

To ascertain the demons’ goal, it would be wise to make contact. If
their target was indeed the witch, she would have to reconsider her
options.

Leaving the knights behind, Shatia used flight magic to rise above the
forest canopy. She headed toward the demons and cast illusion magic
along the way. Transforming into her adult form, she descended into the
forest where the demons were gathered.

“Well now, hello everyone. The weather is quite splendid today, is it
not?”

“!? …Who goes there?! Where did you come from!?”

Standing there were several demons clad in black cloaks.

All of them had blue skin, red eyes, and the wings and tails
characteristic of their race. A woman who appeared to be their
leader—sharp-eyed, with black hair tied in a single bunch and a slender
sword at her hip—confronted Shatia.

“Oh dear, my apologies. I didn’t intend to startle you. I am merely a
traveler. I happened to be passing by,” Shatia introduced herself
casually.

“…You see us and think nothing of it?” Faced with Shatia’s nonchalance,
the demon woman gripped her cloak and retorted with a slight
awkwardness. She clearly meant, *“How can you stay so calm when you’re
facing demons?”*

Shatia tilted her head and touched her finger to her lip.

“You mean the fact that you are demons? Unfortunately, I’ve seen beings
like you many times before. I’ve even seen things far more impressive…
if I were to be startled every single time, there would be no end to
it.”

In truth, Shatia had met demons before, and had even been their enemy at
times. Her own history went back quite a ways, and facing a few demons
now meant nothing to her. The demon woman’s eyes widened in surprise.
Her sharp edge softened slightly, and she lowered her guard.

“…It seems you have not come to slay us.”

“Of course not. I have no desire to waste my mana. I merely came for a
bit of small talk.”

Shatia disliked conflict by nature. Raising both arms to show she was
unarmed, she stated her business. The demon woman momentarily kept her
hand on her sword out of unease, but eventually, she let out a small
sigh and released it.

“You sure about this, Commander? A woman like this, even just the rest
of us could…”

One of the demon men whispered his suggestion to the woman, but she held
out a hand to signal him to step back.

“Stop. You lot could not defeat her. Can’t you tell? Look at the sheer
amount of her mana…”

The demon woman had felt it prickling against her skin since the
beginning—the overwhelming amount of power flowing from Shatia. In
reality, that was only the amount Shatia was failing to suppress. Her
true strength was far higher than the demon woman imagined.

“Shall we talk, then?”

The demon woman finally gave in.

Shatia replied while playing with her hair, “I heard a rumor on the wind
that a witch has been resurrected… I wished to see her out of curiosity.
Do you know anything?”

Shatia planned to first get information about the witch. What the demons
were actually after could wait. At worst, she would simply utilize the
information to stay one step ahead. However, the demon woman showed no
real reaction to the word “witch.” Whether she was hiding something or
truly didn’t know, Shatia couldn’t tell.

“I… do not know. I’ve heard nothing of a witch being resurrected. Though
if that were true, it would be a terrifying thing indeed.”

“Kukuku, so even demons fear witches?”

“Of course. They’re calamities. More than even we can handle.”

Shatia was surprised to hear such a timid remark from the demon woman.
And when the word “calamity” came up, Shatia was made keenly aware once
again how dangerously their kind was viewed by other races.

“I see… That is truly a sorrowful thing.”

Shatia looked down and spoke with genuine sadness. But she quickly
returned to her normal expression and raised her head, masking her
feelings from the demons.

It was something she already knew. Witches were harbingers of calamity.
Few would ever understand a pariah like a witch. Shatia caught herself
wishing she could laugh like Emerald at a time like this.

“Umu, you have my thanks. That would be my business with you concluded…
Though if I may prod, why are you demons in a forest such as this?”

Shatia clapped her hands to signal the end of the matter and casually
asked about their goal. She shifted her feet as if she were about to
leave at any moment, acting as if she weren’t particularly interested.
The demon woman brushed the hair from her forehead before speaking.

“And if I told you, what benefit would there be for us?”

“…Kuku, none at all. My apologies. That was tactless of me, I was merely
curious.”

“Oh dear,” Shatia said, dropping her shoulders in mock disappointment.
It seemed the demon woman was more formidable than she had expected.
Deciding it would be unwise to overstay her welcome, Shatia turned to
leave. But she glanced back over her shoulder one last time.

“One last thing, then. Could you at least tell me your name?”

Shatia didn’t know if they would ever meet again, but she wanted to know
the demon woman’s name. It was a selfish request since she hadn’t even
introduced herself, but she wanted a name to call her by. Even a fake
one would do.

The demon woman looked surprised, looked down for a moment as if
considering, then slowly raised her head and gave her answer.

“Sheris.”

Hearing the name, Shatia let out a satisfied “Hoh.” She didn’t know if
it was her real name, but she liked it regardless and nodded in
approval.

“I see. A very fine name. Well then, Sheris. Let us meet again, should
fate allow it.”

With that, Shatia ran off and left the area. Several demons tried to
pursue her, but were left stumped as she escaped into the sky with
flight magic and vanished instantly.
