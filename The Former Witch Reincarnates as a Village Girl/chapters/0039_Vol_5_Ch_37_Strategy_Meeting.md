# Chapter 37: Strategy Meeting

“Now then… allow me to have a look into your mind.”

After defeating the Lizardman, Shatia carried him to a dense thicket of
trees to conduct her investigation. She had mastered mind-reading magic,
and, true to its name, it was a magic to read the mind of a target.
However, it didn’t allow for a complete reading of the subject; rather,
it allowed her to experience fragments of the subject’s emotions and
memories as if they were her own. While it was a magic with limited
utility, it possessed the advantage of working even on those who spoke a
different language, making it perfect for a situation like this.

“*Open*.”

With the incantation, Shatia activated her magic. She placed one hand on
the Lizardman’s forehead, from which a magic circle emerged. By
synchronizing her senses, she began to read.

Various pieces of information flooded into Shatia’s mind. A
reddish-black landscape where sandstorms swirled incessantly, and within
it, Lizardmen were marching. They were seeking something. Their desire
was palpable—something they wished to achieve even at the cost of their
own lives. A massive black shadow appeared before them. With giant
wings, massive arms, and sharp, elongated claws, its form was exactly
like that of a dragon.

The dragon was shrouded in a black shadow, so its full appearance was
unclear, but Shatia felt it looked familiar. A malevolent existence,
often called the incarnation of darkness. The monster that had once
blanketed the earth in flames. The Ancient Dragon.

The mind-reading concluded, and Shatia deactivated the magic. Exhausted,
sweat dripped from her forehead. She toyed with her hair, her expression
heavy and grim.

“…I see.”

She had predicted it to some extent, but having it confirmed made
Shatia’s mood grow heavy. She straightened her body and adjusted her
clothes to distract herself while she organized the information.

“Their objective is the resurrection of the dragon. Can they not simply
wait for their master to awaken? Good grief, what an impatient lot…”

The Lizardmen’s goal was the resurrection of their creator, the dragon.
While the dragon’s resurrection was something originally destined to
happen, it seemed the Lizardmen desired its return much sooner than
scheduled. Shatia shook her head and placed a hand to her forehead,
sighing at the unwelcome news.

They likely intended to use the demons for the rite. It was a classic
ritual. The demons had been kidnapped solely to be offered as
sacrifices. Whether this was truly necessary for the dragon’s awakening,
or if it would even actually work, was unknown, but it would be a
disaster if the dragon were to awaken by some fluke. After synthesizing
the information, Shatia furrowed her brow and let out another sigh.

“In any case, I must stop them.”

She judged that this ambition had to be thwarted. If the dragon
awakened, vast amounts of nature would be destroyed. That was the one
thing she wanted to prevent. Shatia turned toward the direction the
Lizardmen were marching and began preparing to follow.

“…Is someone approaching?” Just then, the grass ahead rustled, and the
sound of footsteps approached. Sensing mana signatures at the same time,
Shatia realized someone was drawing near.

Judging by the signatures, there were two people. Furthermore, they
weren’t Lizardmen. One possessed an enormous amount of mana, while the
other had only a trace—a truly anomalous combination.

Moreover, the reactions were strange; the one with the massive mana felt
as though they were being suppressed or capped, and the one with the
trace mana was erratic. Shatia wore a dissatisfied expression at the
hard-to-grasp signatures and tilted her head. Heightening her vigilance,
she raised her hand, ready to activate magic at a moment’s notice.

The bushes parted significantly, and two figures emerged. Upon seeing
them, Shatia’s face filled with shock. For both were individuals she
knew very well.

“You idiot Hero! You’re truly useless! Moron! Blockhead! Slowpoke!!”

“Ugh… S-Sorry. I’m sorry.”

One was a person whose angry outbursts Shatia had seen many times: Lili,
one of the Witch Twins. As always, she was a cute girl with fiery red
hair tied to the side, wearing black-and-white decorated clothes. Shatia
had often doted on her back when they were both witches.

The other was an individual she could never forget: the man who had
killed her, the Hero. Compared to when Shatia had last seen him, his
features had become slightly more mature, and his eyes appeared somewhat
swollen. His aura, too, was somewhat lonely.

“Lili… Hero…?” Without thinking, Shatia let the words slip, her hand
still raised to cast a spell.

Hearing her, Lili and the Hero took notice of the young girl standing
before them for the first time and stopped in their tracks, surprised.
Initially, they didn’t know who she was and wore puzzled expressions,
wondering why a young girl was in a place like this. But gradually, Lili
realized that the mana she felt from Shatia belonged to Shatifahl, and
her fingertips began to tremble.

“Eh… No way… Shatifahl?”

The form was completely different. But through the reaction of the mana
within her, Lili couldn’t help but know that the girl before her was
Shatifahl. The Hero, watching from the side, also began to catch on and
suddenly broke into a cold sweat.

“No, no, no, you’re kidding, right? …I mean, she’s shorter than me now,
and no matter how much illusion magic she used, there’s no way Shatifahl
would disguise herself as such a runt,” Lili shook her head in
disbelief, initially refusing to accept it because the revelation was so
shocking.

“Sorry for being a runt. Just like Cloak, it seems your mouth is as foul
as ever; I am glad to see it, Lili.”

But when Shatia smirked as if she had expected it and spoke of things
only a witch would know, she proved she was Shatifahl. Lili finally let
tears well up in her eyes and let out a sob she could no longer hold
back.

She rushed toward Shatia, tears streaming down her face. In Shatia’s
current form, Lili was taller, but she didn’t care. Accepting her,
Shatia straightened her back and gently stroked her head.

The sight was so childish it was impossible to imagine from the usually
aggressive Lili. The Hero, watching from behind, stood dazed.

Eventually, Lili stopped crying and stepped away from Shatia while
sniffling. Shatia smiled gently as she straightened her disheveled
clothes, then turned toward the Hero, indicating it was his turn.

“It has been a long time… Hero. From the look of things, it seems you
took my words to heart.”

“…Shatifahl.”

The Hero also became certain that Shatia was Shatifahl and slumped his
shoulders as if he had given up on something. Seeing him like that,
Shatia smiled at him reassuringly.

“So, why are the two of you traveling together? More importantly, what
happened to Mimi, Lili?” Shatia snapped her fingers—since she couldn’t
clap—and shifted to the main topic.

There were many questions. The fact that the Hero and two witches were
traveling together, the absence of Mimi who was always stuck to Lili’s
side, and the fact that their mana signatures were strange. To confirm
each point, Shatia sat down on a rock.

“T-That is…” The Hero stuttered with an awkward expression. 

Disregarding him, Lili began her attack as if she had been waiting for
the chance, “It’s all this incompetent Hero’s fault, Shatifahl. Because
of the guilt he feels for hunting us, he can’t use his powers properly.”
Lili approached the Hero and gave him a light punch in the stomach.
Because it was the strength of a child, it didn’t hurt much, but the
Hero let out a pained groan.

Seeing how practiced she was at it, Shatia immediately realized the two
had been traveling together for a long time. “He can’t use his powers
properly?”

Lili explained, “His power as a Hero, his magic, his swordsmanship—it’s
all become half-baked. Have you ever heard of such a thing? Being unable
to use your strength because of a ‘heart problem’?”

“Hoh… That is quite interesting indeed,” Shatia nodded with interest,
resting a hand on her chin.

Supposedly, the Hero could use magic normally at first. He had even been
able to fend off monsters that attacked during their journey. However,
once he began to be tormented by nightmares, his ability to handle his
power gradually declined. First, his magic stopped activating correctly,
then his power as a Hero followed. It seemed he couldn’t even use his
specialty, swordsmanship, to his satisfaction.

“Thanks to that, the seal placed on me and Mimi hasn’t been completely
broken, so we can’t use our powers as witches.”

“Haha… I see. So that is why the mana signatures felt so strange.”

Shatia nodded in understanding, having found the cause of the oddness
she felt from the two. Simultaneously, she grew curious about the
details of why the Hero’s heart was preventing him from using his power.
However, because there were other things to be done, she swallowed the
question and indicated for them to continue.

Taking over the explanation, the Hero began to speak in fragments, “We
were investigating the Lizardmen. But during the investigation, we were
spotted by Lizardmen soldiers and ended up in a fight… because of that,
Mimi-chan was—”

Just as he mentioned Mimi, Lili cut him off, “Don’t add ‘-chan’!!” She
kicked his leg while yelling at him for his “outrageous” informality.
She must have hit a sensitive spot, as the Hero clutched his leg and
groaned.

“She was taken away as a sacrifice… is that it?” Gathering the rest of
the explanation, Shatia lowered her hand from her chin, her eyes
wavering as she fell into thought.

“I messed up. To think *we* would be the ones targeted… Ugh, by mere
lizards,” Lili continued to mutter complaints while kicking the ground
in frustration.

For now, she understood the situation. What surprised Shatia was that
Lili was letting the Hero live even after being released from her seal.
There might be a reason, such as his power being incomplete, but the
fact that they were traveling together was quite a shock. Shatia felt a
bit of admiration for Lili’s attitude and let out a small whistle.

“Now then, what to do next…” She stepped down from the rock and racked
her brain, diving into a sea of thought.

For now, the objective was to rescue the kidnapped Mimi and to thwart
the Lizardmen’s ambitions. Since Mimi was taken as a sacrifice, there
was likely no immediate concern for her safety. Still, it would be best
to save her as quickly as possible. However, the problem was the
Lizardman swarm. If it were one-on-one there would be no issue…

“…Hm? Ah, I was careless…”

Suddenly, Shatia sensed a faint presence from behind. Realizing what it
was and understanding what had happened, she furrowed her brows in
regret and slapped her own cheek. She hurriedly started running, darting
through the trees. Surprised by Shatia’s sudden dash, Lili and the Hero
followed after her.

“Hey… what’s wrong, Shatifahl?”

Despite Lili calling out to her, Shatia didn’t look back. Reaching her
destination, she scanned the surroundings. As expected, her target was
gone. In its place was a massive indentation in the ground—a Lizardman’s
footprint—and Shatia let out a small click of her tongue.

“To think he would wake up already… I underestimated him slightly.”

“This footprint… No way, a Lizardman?! Shatifahl, were you keeping a
Lizardman captured?”

Shatia corrected her, saying she wasn’t exactly keeping him captured,
while she placed a hand on the ground to sense for his presence. But as
expected, she couldn’t track the presence of a Lizardman who possessed
only trace amounts of mana. She brushed back her bangs and sighed.

Letting the Gale Lizardman escape was a significant blow. He had
prioritized reporting to his allies over defeating his enemy and had
fled the scene. Because she hadn’t expected a Lizardman to be capable of
such judgment, Shatia regretted that she had miscalculated completely.

“Forgive me, you two. With this, the enemy will have become completely
aware of our presence.”

“…So what’re we gonna do? If it’s you, Shatifahl, you can easily beat
those guys, right?”

“You certainly overestimate me. Hmm, well now…”

Shatia kept her back to the two, her hand on her chin as she groaned in
thought.

The enemy knew of their presence. That meant the Lizardmen would proceed
with even greater vigilance. They would be extremely aggressive during
combat, making the struggle much harder. No matter how strong Shatia
was, facing a large number of ferocious Lizardmen would be exhausting.
Shatia started brainstorming a simpler plan to wipe them all out in one
fell swoop.

“…I suppose I do have a plan. Hero, I shall need your cooperation as
well.”

After finalizing her thoughts, Shatia lowered her hand and looked at the
Hero. The Hero’s eyes widened in surprise at being addressed. He shifted
uncomfortably, bewildered.
