# Chapter 38: Conversation

After explaining the plan, Shatia gave Lili her mission and they parted
ways temporarily. In the meantime, Shatia and the Hero were to follow
after the Lizardmen together.

As expected, the swarm, seemingly having received word from the Gale
scout, had quickened their pace. It appeared they intended to cross the
mountain and move on to the next. Shatia, for her part, wanted to settle
matters before they could make it over the peak.

The Hero, who was concerned about Lili as they moved, asked, “Is it
really alright? Letting Lili go off alone…”

However, Shatia didn’t answer. She was much more focused on how the Hero
addressed the Witch Twins, “…It is ‘Mimi-chan’ for one, but just ‘Lili’
for the other? Why the difference?”

Taken aback by the unexpected inquiry, the Hero wore a blank expression
and offered a troubled reply, “Huh? No… I didn’t mean anything deep by
it…”

“There is no issue. Even if she is alone, it is only reconnaissance, and
even Lili would not make such a blunder,” Shatia answered while walking
straight ahead, “…probably.”

Though she sounded confident, he didn’t miss what she said at the end,
and the Hero couldn’t help but do a double-take. Shatia’s eyes were
clear and beautiful. The moment he saw her face, the Hero lost the urge
to ask any further questions.

“What concerns me is whether the plan will succeed. In my estimation,
there are no flaws… but there is always the possibility of the
unexpected.”

“…And am I the one who has to make up for that ‘unexpected’?”

“Precisely.”

Shatia turned around and stated this firmly. She pointed a finger at the
Hero, fixing him with a sharp gaze as if to remind him that there was no
escape. The Hero flinched for a moment and stopped walking. Shatia also
stopped, continuing to stare at him.

“…I don’t think I’m up to such a major role,” he replied, casting his
eyes downward as if it were difficult to say. He placed a hand over his
heart with a lack of confidence, exuding a weak aura.

Shatia let out an exasperated sigh, “Certainly, you are much weaker now
than when we met before. However, that does not mean you can run away.
You will fulfill it, will you not? Your duty as a Hero.”

“…………” The Hero couldn’t offer an answer. He simply watched her, his
eyes wavering with a lonely light. Shatia quietly accepted that gaze.

“Do not worry. If it comes to it, I shall handle things,” she said,
giving her chest a reassuring thump at the end.

She meant it to be comforting, but to the Hero, the sight of an
incredibly small, young girl puffing out her chest could only be seen as
a somewhat indescribable scene. He almost let out a laugh and placed a
hand over his mouth, causing Shatia to narrow her eyes.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
“I would like you to tell me a little bit.”

They continued walking for a while longer until they reached their
destination—a place where the bushes grew thick. Since they now only had
to wait for Lili, whom they had promised to meet here, Shatia sat down
on a rock, and the Hero asked what had been on his mind.

“Hm, what is it?”

“…I want you to tell me… what… you Witches actually are.”

The Hero managed to get the words out despite his nervousness. His voice
trembled in places, and he was gripping his fists tightly; he must have
been mustering a great deal of courage. Shatia didn’t answer
immediately, instead tilting her head as if thinking, twirling a strand
of hair with her finger.

“…Hmm.”

For a while, silence flowed between the two, and neither spoke a word.
Shatia continued to toy with her hair, her gaze lowered in deep thought.
The Hero simply watched, waiting in silence for her response.

Eventually, Shatia lowered her hand and looked up at the Hero. Her eyes
were as clear and pure as ever, making him even more nervous.

“Answering that question is exceedingly difficult. Just as one cannot
answer why humans were born, I can only say that we exist simply because
we are alive,” she spoke lightly, opening her hand wide.

Indeed, when asked about the meaning of one’s existence, there isn’t
much of an answer to give. However, the Hero wore an expression that
suggested he wasn’t satisfied.

“In the first place, you have traveled with Lili and Mimi for a while
now, correct? Did you not ask the two of them during that time?” Shatia
asked out of curiosity.

With his hands raised, the Hero answered, “Mimi-chan told me a little…
but Lili is, well, the way she is.”

As expected, it seemed he couldn’t get any proper information out of
Lili, and Mimi, being as shy as she was, hadn’t been able to say much
either. In all likelihood, the Hero himself had his own reservations and
hadn’t been able to ask much. Thinking this, Shatia placed a hand to her
neck, wondering how she should phrase it.

“Hmm… well then, if I were to explain it just a little…”

Since she couldn’t tell him everything, Shatia pondered for a moment,
and once she had organized her thoughts, she began to speak tentatively
while touching her neck.

“‘Witch’ is nothing more than a common name you all have given us. We
are merely ‘a certain race,’ and by no means special entities called
Witches.”

Shatia spoke in fragments, her eyes closed. Her presence was gentle and
warm, like a mother singing a lullaby to her child.

“A certain race…?”

“An ancient race. There are likely very few survivors left. I wonder how
many are still alive now…”

Shatia stopped there. The Hero’s expression darkened instantly. He
understood what she meant.

The Seven Witches were merely a gathering of people from a certain race.
The Hero had hunted them. While some were sealed or resurrected, and
others survived, it didn’t mean everyone was safe. There was a
possibility that some were truly dead. Realizing this, the Hero bit his
lip hard.

“Well, it was a fate destined for ruin anyway. It is only natural for an
ancient race to vanish; it is a return to the source,” Shatia patted her
knee and stood up from the rock. She brushed off her clothes, signaling
the end of the conversation and began preparing for the operation.

The Hero remained standing in a daze, staring at the ground before
opening his mouth impulsively, “…Do Mimi-chan and the others know?”

Shatia reflexively turned back, “…….Why do you ask such a thing?”

The Hero looked steadily at Shatia and took a step closer. His gaze
captured her as if searching for something, not letting her escape.

“The two of them said you raised them since they were old enough to
understand things. You have lived the longest among the witches, and
everyone looks up to you like a mother, right? …Why don’t you tell them
about their own identity?”

Hearing the Hero’s words, Shatia remained silent, facing forward for a
while. Eventually, she let out a small sigh and shook her head as if
giving up.

The Hero’s intuition wasn’t that far off. Shatia was indeed different
from the other witches. She was the one who had lived the longest and
had raised the others. Therefore, it wasn’t strange for Shatia to know
things the others didn’t. However, that didn’t mean Shatia was obligated
to answer him directly.

“…There are things in this world that are better left unknown, Hero.”

Shatia did not answer. The truth remained hidden, shrouded in darkness.
In the end, the Hero couldn’t learn what he wanted to know, and his
conversation with Shatia was cut off there. Shatia made no move to say
anything further; instead, a sad expression flickered across her face.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
Within a few minutes, Lili rejoined them. According to her scouting, the
Lizardmen were following the path Shatia had predicted and would arrive
in this vicinity within a few minutes. They were right at the boundary
between two mountains. If they didn’t stop them here, thwarting their
objective would become increasingly difficult. Shatia braced herself and
gave her cheek a soft slap.

“So, how are we gonna outsmart the Lizardmen, Shatifahl?” Lili asked
while scratching her head. Her eyes were sparkling, clearly believing
that Shatia must have some incredible plan.

Shatia found the sight dazzling. She cleared her throat once and held up
a single finger. Crossing in front of Lili and circling near the Hero,
she explained, “Our objective is not necessarily to defeat all the
Lizardmen. We need only to rescue the hostages so the ritual cannot be
performed.”

To begin with, there was no need for them to engage in an all-out war
with the Lizardmen. If they could just take back the hostages needed for
the ritual, that would be enough.

“I intend to draw their attention. While I do that, Lili and the Hero
shall go and rescue Mimi and the other sacrifices.”

Of course, that didn’t mean they could avoid all combat. As long as they
confronted them, conflict was inevitable, and there was no way the
Lizardmen would quietly watch as their hostages were taken back. That
was exactly why Shatia said she would act as the decoy to keep the
fighting to a minimum, and tasked Lili and the Hero with the rescue.

“Got it… wait, Shatifahl. What’s that magic circle?” Lili pointed with
her finger, noticing that Shatia had raised her arm and was preparing to
activate a spell.

Shatia, who had been deploying the magic circle, looked as if she had
just remembered something and swung her arm, moving the circle in front
of her.

“I was merely considering erasing the mountain,” she replied with a dry
chuckle.

Lili’s cheek reflexively twitched, and the Hero beside her wore a
complicated expression. Shatia curled her lips in amusement and gently
moved her fingers.
