# Chapter 7 – An Encounter With a Sage

Upon returning to the village, Shatia was scolded by her mother, just as
expected.

Although she hadn’t been gone for long, it seemed coming home in
tattered clothes was quite a serious matter. Shatia’s mother, her face
filled with worry, cautioned her daughter deeply, telling her that while
playing outside was fine, she must take great care not to get hurt. This
time, Shatia honestly admitted her own fault; she lowered her head and
apologized with a sincere “I’m sorry.”

Eventually, the knight regained consciousness and explained the
situation in the northern valley—

It appeared the knight was the captain of a unit dispatched by the
kingdom. They had headed to the northern valley on the advice of a court
mage who claimed the energy in the area was unusual. Once there, they
encountered an old man named Barbas—a former mage turned heinous
criminal—who had overwhelmed the knights with an army of skeletons. Near
death, the captain had somehow managed to reach this village before
losing consciousness.

“So that old man is named Barbas… A heinous criminal? What a pity.”

Having changed into new clothes given by her mother, Shatia was once
again playing in her room with Moffy. It seemed the adults had to
discuss future matters with the knight, and the children had been told
to stay quietly at home. Shatia, having already resolved the incident,
seemed disinterested.

With nothing to do, Moffy was reading a book she had borrowed. When she
caught Shatia’s muttering, she tilted her head in curiosity.

“What’s wrong Shatia?”

“No, it’s nothing… I merely thought it a shame to lose such a promising
individual.”

It was a rare thing to find a human who actually liked witches; if he
was a criminal, then it couldn’t be helped if the knights took him away.
Shatia looked out the window at the scenery, sounding genuinely
regretful.

However, this incident had not been without gain. Meeting the old man
Barbas had revealed the existence of magic used to enthrall monsters,
and she now knew there were humans seeking to resurrect the witches.
This gave her hope that she might one day meet her kin once again.

Following the discussion, it was decided that the knight and several
young men from the village would head to the northern valley.
Apparently, they intended to confront Barbas once more; everyone left
the village clutching weapons with awkward, unpracticed grips. Shatia
didn’t think much of it, assuming they would simply encounter the
still-unconscious Barbas. However, Moffy was very worried about her
father, so Shatia stayed by her side to play and keep her calm.

A few hours later, the knight and the villagers returned, bringing the
bound Barbas with them. Just as Shatia suspected, the knight was
convinced that Barbas had brought about his own ruin through a failed
ritual and intended to return to the kingdom at once. There was talk of
a reward or some such, but Shatia, who cared for nothing but magic,
didn’t bother listening.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
Peace returned to the village, but only a few days later, another
visitor appeared. An old man clad in a dusty green robe, his eyes hidden
by a hood and his face adorned with a long beard. The man, who looked
almost like a beggar, visited the village and asked the chief for a
single night’s lodging.

Curious about the suspicious visitor, the villagers quickly gathered
around the chief’s house.

“Hoh… how unusual. A sage, is it?”

Shatia, together with Moffy, joined the crowd and observed the old man
from the doorway. She immediately recognized him as a sage and smiled in
amusement.

“Sage? What’s that?”

“I believe you read about them in that book the other day. They are
those among mages who possess exceptionally superior magical abilities
and have mastered their craft,” Shatia explained while chiding Moffy
slightly by reminding her they had discussed this before.

Unlike ordinary mages, sages possess extraordinary skill. Their power
was said to rival that of the gods, enabling feats such as communing
with nature and communicating with animals.

While the general public didn’t know strictly what defined a sage,
Shatia recognized him instantly from the faint magical aura he was
leaking.

Shatia had only met sages a few times during her previous life as a
witch. Since both were masters of their own respective magecrafts, sages
and witches didn’t actually get along poorly. As a witch, Shatia had
always harbored a slight resentment over why sages were not viewed with
hostility by humans while witches were, but she had more or less
resigned herself to the fact that there was no use complaining about it.

After being ushered away from the village chief’s house, Shatia wondered
if there wasn’t some way she could meet with the sage. For Shatia, who
wished to know all magic, the ancient spells used by sages were more
interesting than anything else.

She went to bed early after deciding that the dead of night when
everyone was asleep would be her best chance.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
Slipping out of her room at midnight, Shatia used flight magic to exit
through her window and head toward the chief’s house. Instead of using
the front door, she used magic to open a window and slipped inside.
There, she was met by the sage—who had removed his hood to reveal a kind
face—waiting for her.

“Hm, so you were aware of my presence?”

“Well met, O Anomalous One.”

Shatia internally raised her vigilance and showed a brief flicker of
surprise, but the elderly sage simply smiled kindly and beckoned her to
come closer. Judging that there was no hostility, Shatia sat down on a
floor cushion as invited.

“‘Anomalous One,’ is it? Quite an apt description.”

“What else would one call one who possesses such outsized mana while
dwelling in a human frame? Though, I suppose I’m not one to talk.”

With that, the sage produced two wooden cups. They were empty, but when
he held his staff and chanted a brief incantation, warm tea appeared in
the cups in an instant. Shatia’s eyes widened in surprise. She had seen
magic to summon items, but she had never seen magic that actually
*created* tea like that.

Shatia observed the cup she was given carefully before taking a sip. She
couldn’t quite discern the taste.

“So… who might you be? Or rather, what manner of soul has stained your
being?”

“Kukuku, a sage indeed. To guess even that much. However, alas—I am a
coward. I am not so kind as to introduce myself on a whim.”

There was no real problem with identifying herself, but as a precaution,
Shatia didn’t reveal she was a witch. Since she was living in this world
as Shatia, a village girl, she didn’t want her true identity to leak. If
it were known, she might be loathed by humans once again.

Despite her refusal, the elderly sage showed no sign of being
particularly bothered; his face remained as kind as ever as he let out a
soft “hoho.”

“I see. Well, no matter… I shall introduce myself, at least. I have not
told the villagers, but I am the Sage Vezal. Keep it a secret, I pray
you.”

It seemed he, too, didn’t particularly want others to know his true
identity. *I already told Moffy, but it should be fine*, Shatia thought
while nodding her head.

“And? Why has such a Great Sage come to this remote village?” Shatia
asked while finishing her tea and toying with a strand of her hair that
fell over her shoulder.

Sages rarely appeared in public. Some would even hide their identities
in order to blend into crowds. Shatia was curious why such a mystical
being had come to a mere ordinary village.

Vezal swirled the tea remaining in his cup and began to speak slowly,
still clutching his staff in one hand, “I have been led here by
prophecy… It seems a sinister aura was flowing near the northern
valley.”

Hearing Vezal’s explanation, Shatia thought, *Oh, that*, and crossed her
arms behind her head disinterestedly. If the sinister aura referred to
Barbas, then the incident had already been resolved.

However, Vezal continued, “But that is not all. The northern valley is
but the beginning… Before long, history shall once again be shaken.”

“Ohoh?” Shatia was surprised that there was more to the prophecy and
showed a hint of interest. She sat up straight and leaned toward Vezal,
listening intently so as not to miss a word.

“What do you mean by that, exactly?”

“One cannot say a prophecy is absolute… However, the world today, devoid
of the Seven Witches, is filled with chaos. Eventually, the world shall
once again be enveloped in the fires of war.”

According to Vezal, another war was coming. A struggle for territory
between demons and humans, conflicts between different races, the
awakening of dragons—chaos would erupt once again. Once, the Seven
Witches had been part of that chaos. But now they were no more. As
Shatia thought of the world falling into turmoil again, her eyes wavered
with a hint of sorrow, realizing that conflict never truly vanishes.

“Kuku, humans never stop fighting, and the demon race is too proud.
Other races look down on one another, and eventually, the dragons shall
wake… Conflict is truly something that never goes away.”

“Indeed. But it cannot be helped. We can only pray that those who suffer
are as few as possible…”

Vezal agreed with Shatia and gave his staff a light flick. A sphere of
light burst from the staff, and countless tiny orbs of light ignited
within the room. Some floated near Shatia; when she gave one a little
poke, it popped and vanished.

“Magecraft deludes people. With great power, one loses sight of the
ground beneath them, and the heart becomes clouded… It is a sorrowful
thing.”

With a tap of his staff on the floor, Vezal extinguished the glowing
orbs. In the moment before the light vanished, Shatia saw that Vezal
wore a look of profound sadness. Though partially hidden by his beard,
she saw clearly that his kind face was stained with grief.

“Possessing such mana, what is it that you desire? Wealth? Power? Or
perhaps gold?”

“…Me?”

Vezal abruptly asked Shatia such a thing. Shatia placed her cup on the
floor and clenched her hands tightly. Then, with a slight smile, she
answered firmly:

“My desire is the endless pursuit of the magical arts. To master every
magic in this world, to know them all, and to savor them. That is all.”

She spoke boldly, spreading her arms wide. Her eyes sparkled like those
of a young girl. No, Shatia *was* a young girl. But for a fleeting
moment, to Vezal’s eyes, she appeared as an adult woman with long silver
hair. Possessing a colossal amount of mana, she emanated an aura as vast
as a mountain; an unreachable existence. But he quickly snapped out of
it, returning to his senses to gaze at the girl before him.

“Hoho, truly quite the greedy one, aren’t you?”

“Indeed, I am greedy. I am a person so covetous that a single lifetime
is not enough.”

In truth, Shatia had reincarnated and obtained a second life because she
had been driven into a corner. While such a thing warped the laws of the
world, Shatia suspected she would have done the same even if she had
simply been about to die of old age as a witch. At the very least, she
had no intention of dying until her desire to pursue the path of magic
had been sated.

“…Well then, I shall take my leave. Our conversation was quite
interesting.”

After talking a while longer, Shatia realized the sun would soon be
rising and decided to return home before her mother woke up. She stood
up from the cushion, waved to Vezal, and headed for the window.

Vezal waved with a smile, seeming a bit reluctant to see her go.
“Indeed, I too have enjoyed a pleasant conversation for the first time
in a long while. If possible, I should like to cross paths with you
again somewhere.”

Shatia returned the sentiment with a smile of her own. “If we are both
still alive when that time comes.”

After all, sages are long-lived because they have mastered their own
magecraft, and since Shatia was still a child, she wasn’t likely to die
anytime soon. They would likely meet again. With that thought, Shatia
hopped out the window and flew back home with her flight magic. 

Watching her go, Vezal slowly closed the window and muttered, “…What a
strange child. To have such a youthful appearance, yet possess the air
of one who has seen more years than I.”

Vezal had thought she had a peculiar way of speaking from the moment
they met, but through their conversation, he felt that it was truly her
natural manner. Her atmosphere and bearing were clearly those of someone
older than him, making him feel even more strongly that she was no
ordinary girl.

Vezal pondered. Just who was the girl who had alighted before him
tonight? Was she perhaps a creature made of mana sent by the spirits? A
child of the spirits? Or perhaps a fellow sage? He didn’t know. But he
felt certain he would never forget that girl as long as he lived.

“Now that I think on it, there was a continuation to the prophecy… ‘When
the earth is stained red and color vanishes from the sky, the Seven
Witches shall gather once more…’ so it said.”

Vezal spoke the rest of the prophecy—a part he had told no one and which
he alone knew.

The prophecy stated that the world would once again be caught in the
vortex of chaos. But in truth, the following lines contained the even
more terrifying claim that the dead witches would gather again.

Vezal had kept this part of the prophecy to himself for fear that
spreading this would cause panic even before any wars began. But the
witches were supposed to be dead. How could they possibly return? A
resurrection ritual? Or perhaps reincarnation magic? If such magic had
been used, did it mean the witches were already surviving in this world
as children?

“Surely not…”

For some reason, the image of Shatia surfaced in his mind; her beautiful
silver hair reaching her waist, her skin as white as snow, her body so
small it felt as if she might fly away and vanish at any moment, and her
eyes as clear as if they could see through anything. Vezal had sensed
the aura of a witch from her. But he quickly dismissed it as a mistake,
letting out a small sigh before stroking his beard.

Having drained the last of his tea, Vezal lay down to get a little more
sleep.
