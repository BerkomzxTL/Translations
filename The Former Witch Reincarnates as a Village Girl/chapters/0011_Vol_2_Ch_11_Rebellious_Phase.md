# Chapter 11 – Rebellious Phase

“Ngh…!”

Pain was a sensation Shatia rarely experienced. She lived with a thin
veil of defensive magic constantly deployed, preemptively neutralizing
any threat. Consequently, she almost never suffered injury. At worst,
her clothes might become tattered or torn, but that was the extent.

That was precisely why Shatia understood that the pain she felt now was
the embodiment of Emerald’s rage. For someone who so detested hurting
others, someone who specialized solely in healing magic, to unleash an
offensive spell of this caliber—Shatia understood anew the situation was
even more dire than she had imagined.

Extricating herself from the splintered trees, Shatia gently laid Moffy
onto a patch of grass. Fortunately, Moffy was unhurt. Having cast a
defensive spell at the very last second, her body was devoid of a single
scratch. In exchange, Shatia herself had taken significant damage, but
she was satisfied as long as her friend was safe.

After hiding Moffy to keep her out of the crossfire, Shatia flew back
toward Emerald. There stood a doll, possessing a twisted expression far
removed from the pure girl she once knew.

“So you persist in turning on me… Me?!!”

“Yes… I shall be the one to stop you.”

Emerald’s blue eyes wavered at those words. The curse didn’t seem to
have activated yet. But since there was no telling when it might
trigger, Shatia heightened her vigilance.

Emerald moved first. With a swing of both arms, she unleashed a powerful
pulse of mana. To ensure no harm came to Moffy hiding in the forest,
Shatia took the attack head-on. She layered defensive magic over her
palms, enduring the impact by trying to envelop it. But Emerald’s attack
was beyond her expectations. In a desperate maneuver, Shatia parried the
strike, redirecting the trajectory of the mana toward the sky.

“…*Tch*!” Shatia clicked her tongue, realizing Emerald’s offensive magic
possessed greater potency than her own defense. If possible, she wanted
to sever the mana source and negate the attack, but it seemed Emerald
was truly intent on killing her. *I cannot afford to hold back either,*
Shatia thought, her expression pained.

“You would do well… not to underestimate me!!”

With a series of clatters, Emerald contorted her body in impossible
directions, moving with an eerie, supernatural fluidity. Suddenly, her
arm thrust forward, and a mana bolt erupted from it. Shatia immediately
deployed her defensive magic as before. However, the bolt twisted
mid-air, striking Shatia from below.

“…Ugh!?”

Blown into the sky, Shatia used flight magic to regain her posture. But
Emerald was already there, soaring through the air with her own flight
magic, closing the distance until she was right in front of Shatia’s
face.

“Did you think that because I specialized in healing magic, I could not
fight? That’s unlike you, Witch of Wisdom Shatifahl.”

With her moon-bright golden hair fluttering, Emerald’s deathly pale face
drew near. Then, her lips curled back to reveal her gums in a demonic
grin as she swept her fingers through the air.

“*Shackles of Sin*.”

As Emerald spoke, small magic circles flew from her fingertips, touching
Shatia’s limbs. In that instant, Shatia’s body became as heavy as lead;
despite her flight magic, she felt as though she were about to plummet.
She immediately unleashed a pulse of mana to blast Emerald away in
retaliation.

“Haa… haa…” After pushing her away, Shatia clutched her shoulder,
letting out a pained, ragged breath.

“Those shackles bind you and absorb your mana… You remember them, don’t
you? This is the magic you taught me, after all,” Emerald placed a hand
to her mouth, speaking with a sense of nostalgia.

Of course, Shatia remembered the spell. She was the one who had
conceived and created it, and she had taught it to Emerald because she
had the best aptitude for it. It was, so to speak, a spell of their
shared memories. Shatia bit her lip in sorrow, realizing she had become
the target of her own creation.

“Though, for Shatifahl, who boasts inexhaustible mana, such shackles are
hardly a burden, are they? At most, they simply make your body feel
heavy.” Emerald clapped her hands together and spoke lightly, as if
discussing the weather, as if it were of no great consequence.

In truth, having her mana absorbed was not a particularly painful ordeal
for Shatia. Even during her battle with the evil spirit, she had been
unbothered by having her mana absorbed. However, for Shatia in her
current form of a child, weight was the ultimate nuisance. Being
shackles made of pure mana, she felt their heaviness even while using
flight magic, making every movement a struggle. Cold sweat trickled down
her face.

“What’s the matter? Can you not bring yourself to harm your former
comrade? It’s only natural. You are so very kind. To you, who led us
witches, we were like daughters…”

Seeing Shatia remain still, Emerald spoke chidingly. A malevolent smirk
played on her lips, and her eyes narrowed in mockery.

Shatia could say nothing. Emerald’s words had hit the mark.

“But I am different.”

Emerald suddenly unleashed a devastating pulse of mana toward Shatia. It
was the largest amount yet. She forced her heavy arms up to intercept
it. However, due to the weight of her body, she couldn’t exert her full
power. This time, she couldn’t redirect the shot; Shatia was sent flying
by the impact of the blast.

“Ngh, guh…!”

“If you take the side of the humans, then even if you were like a mother
to me, I will kill you without hesitation. Do you understand? My rage…
even you cannot stop it.”

Her clothes were torn once again, and Shatia’s expression darkened.
Thanks to her defensive magic, she had avoided physical injury, but she
was steadily being backed into a corner.

Looking up at Emerald, who looked down upon her while she spoke, Shatia
contemplated her options.

Fueled by rage and resentment, Emerald was exerting more power than ever
before. No—she had always possessed the potential. It was only her
gentle nature that had directed that power toward healing rather than
combat. But the fallen Emerald of now was different. She used magic
solely to kill humans, seeking to destroy everything. The potency of her
magic in this state was staggering.

Seeing Emerald’s resolve was genuine, Shatia wiped away the sweat from
her brow. She stole a glance toward the forest to confirm where Moffy
was. So far, she was out of harm’s way. She decided it should be safe to
use a bit more of her true power.

“Kuku… kukuku…” Suddenly, Shatia clutched her stomach and began to
laugh.

“…?” Emerald frowned at this bizarre behavior, her vigilance increasing
as she wondered what she was planning.

With tears of laughter in her eyes, Shatia wiped them away and spoke,
“So this is what they call a ‘rebellious phase’ for a daughter… I see, I
see. It is indeed quite a handful.”

“…What are you talking about? Have you lost your mind?”

“Fufu… Yes, perhaps.”

Shatia thought of her own mother. Her mother, who was raising a daughter
as willful as Shatia, must surely be going through a great deal of
trouble. Feeling a pang of guilt, Shatia closed her eyes, and in the
next instant, she vanished, reappearing behind Emerald.

“…!?”

“It is true that it pains me to harm you.”

Shatia deployed a magic circle, creating her own chains of mana that
then coiled around Emerald. Then, channeling mana into her arm, Shatia
punched her with all her might. Emerald was sent flying into the
distance, only to be yanked back by the chains. The moment she returned
to her original position, Shatia struck her with another fist.

“Ugh!?”

“But even more than that, I do not wish to see you harm people.”

Yanking the airborne Emerald back with the chains once more, Shatia
slammed her into the forest floor. While Emerald remained bound and
immobilized, Shatia unleashed a burst of mana with every ounce of
strength she could muster. A blinding light descended upon the forest,
followed by a roar like a thunderclap as a massive explosion erupted.

When the shock subsided, Emerald lay there in a tattered state. Her robe
was scorched, and cracks had formed in her doll-like body. That she was
still intact was likely because she had deployed a defensive spell at
the last possible moment.

Maintaining high alert, Shatia descended slowly to Emerald’s side.
Emerald let out a pained groan, struggling to push herself up.

“Ka… hah… Fufu, as expected… you’re strong, Shatifahl,” Emerald spoke
weakly, blood trickling from her mouth.

*Why does blood flow from a doll’s body?* Shatia wondered. She
hypothesized that Emerald was using mana to replicate her living state
from the neck up. Then, another thought suddenly struck her—since the
current Emerald was a doll, what was the source of power moving that
body?

As she pondered this, Shatia shifted her gaze back to Emerald. The girl
was smiling as if she were plotting something.

“However… because you are strong, you sometimes fail to pay attention to
what is beneath your feet… How ironic. Is this the carelessness of the
powerful?”

Shatia didn’t understand what Emerald meant. Was she talking about
Moffy? No, she had maintained a sufficient distance. She had confirmed
her safety. There was no problem. Was it a simple lie, then? A trap to
cause agitation? No, Emerald was not known for such tactics. Then what
did those words mean?

As Shatia puzzled over it, she heard footsteps from behind.
Simultaneously, a familiar voice reached her ears.

“I saw a light coming from this direction… Gh! What is this!?”

“C-Captain! That woman’s body, it’s… a doll…!?”

Shatia didn’t need to turn around to know who was behind her. It was the
knights. They cried out in shock at the scene, pointing at Emerald in
bewilderment.

Shatia immediately tried to hide or use illusion magic. But before she
could act, Emerald struck her directly with a burst of mana, blowing her
far away from the area.

“That is your weakness. You fail to notice the presence of those who
possess only a tiny amount of mana… They’ve been approaching this whole
time, you know? But your own immense capacity acted as a shroud,
preventing you from noticing.”

Emerald’s lips twisted into a mocking sneer.

Shatia possessed a truly anomalous amount of mana. If she released it,
the sheer intensity alone would influence her surroundings. Therefore,
she usually suppressed it. But in the heat of battle with Emerald, she
hadn’t had that luxury. Because she had unleashed her power, her own
vast mana had clouded her senses, preventing her from detecting the
knights approaching from a distance. Emerald had been aiming for that
singular moment of carelessness and vulnerability.

“You… could you be a witch!?”

“Silence, human. A filthy creature like yourself has no right to know my
name.”

The knights drew their swords and faced Emerald. But Emerald looked upon
them with eyes of contempt, as if looking at dust. Then her blue eyes
turned pitch black. In that instant, the knights were rendered unable to
move, freezing where they stood.

After releasing her curse state, Emerald’s shoulders slumped. She
exhaled a breath of pure exhaustion.

“…Ugh… As expected of the one who led us witches… I managed to push her
far away, but knowing her, she will likely return immediately,” Emerald
muttered weakly.

She herself was far from unscathed. Despite her defensive magic, she had
taken the full brunt of Shatia’s attack. The damage was severe.
Furthermore, though she had blown her away with a blast of mana, she
knew Shatia would return in no time. The battle was not yet over.
Feeling this, Emerald steeled her resolve, stood up, and gazed toward a
certain part of the forest.

“But… that is enough. In the meantime, I shall cast you into the depths
of despair… using that girl.”

With a sinister smile, Emerald began heading toward where Moffy lay
sleeping with a faint, unsteady gait.
