# Chapter 46: The End of Shatia

It burst with a refreshing sound, as if a single mass of water had been
popped.

At the very last second, Shatia had slammed a mass of mana against the
black mist that lunged at her, blowing it back. The mist drifted through
the air like a lingering haze as it dissipated, eventually vanishing
entirely. There was no sign of the enemy’s true form. Had they retreated
in that split second? Shatia sharpened her senses, heightening her
vigilance.

“Now then… I wonder which one you are?” She muttered quietly, her
concentration unbroken.

Which one was it? There was no doubt that the identity of this enemy was
a witch. The problem was which of them it was. Since her reincarnation,
Shatia had met four witches; that left two remaining. Given the way this
one hid, it was certain they harbored some form of hostility.
Unfortunately, in Shatia’s memory, both remaining witches were eccentric
enough to be candidates. Though she came to a conclusion, Shatia prayed
she was wrong.

Suddenly, the mist attacked from behind her again. Without a solid form,
a cluster of small black particles approached, moaning like a ghost. To
which of them did this magic belong? Shatia wondered idly as she evaded
it using flight magic.

“Do you truly believe you can defeat me with such mere mist? Then allow
me to teach you your error,” Shatia threw the question out while keeping
her distance.

“…………”

Naturally, there was no reply. It was doubtful whether the mist
attacking her now was even the true body. It was likely a spell created
by combining illusion and water magic. In theory, any witch could use
such a magic. However, there weren’t many who could use it so perfectly
to hide their true form.

As a test, Shatia created a sphere of fire in her palm. She split it
into countless fragments and sent them flying around her, creating a
barrier of flames. The fire would ignite upon the slightest contact. If
the true body was hidden within the mist, she would find it immediately.

“Come out. A daughter should show her face to her mother. I will not
tolerate this selfishness forever.”

She flicked her fingers, moving the countless flames. In response, the
mist scattered around them, as if fleeing. Shatia immediately swept her
arm, sending the flames in pursuit. But the mist vanished into the
shadows and disappeared from sight.

Shatia halted the movement of her flames for a moment. Though the figure
was gone, a faint trace of mana remained. There was no doubt they were
still close. She sharpened her senses to discern where they were hiding.
The opponent was skilled at concealing their mana—somehow, they even
surpassed Shatia’s own detection abilities. Even so, if they were this
close, she shouldn’t miss them. If she just concentrated, she would know
instantly. But right then, Shatia felt a sharp presence plunging down
from directly above. A powerful mana reaction. Reflexively looking up,
she saw a vast, spreading shroud of mist.

“So, that is your move…!”

“…………——”

Instead of hiding, it was an ambush from directly above. Being hit with
this so suddenly delayed her reaction. Even the great Shatia felt a
surge of panic, yet strangely, a smile played upon her lips. Even though
a single moment of carelessness would result in injury, even though even
Shatia wouldn’t escape unscathed at such close range, she still smiled.

“You would do well not to underestimate me…!”

Her clear eyes flashed like a beast’s, and she swung an arm surging with
mana. Instantly, an impact equivalent to a storm was hurled into the
sky. The diffused mist was blown away in an instant, losing its form
once again. Yet, there was no sense of a solid hit. Shatia bit her lip
slightly as she pulled back her arm. How tedious. It was meaningless
unless she struck the true body. For a moment, she considered sending
out a pulse of mana to level the entire surrounding area. But she
remembered she was in the middle of a city and immediately struck down
the thought.

She didn’t know if the enemy was counting on this, but she couldn’t
afford to use flashy magic. Furthermore, the people were already in an
uproar because of the explosion. If she did anything to further terrify
the populace here, it would be a headache later. Deciding this, Shatia
thought to end the battle as quickly as possible. However, it would not
be so simple. She felt a sharp pain running through the arm that had
just unleashed that massive attack. A numbing sensation. She immediately
rolled up her sleeve to check; her arm was being eroded by the black
mist.

“It grazed me…”

The erosion wasn’t happening from the surface of the skin, but from
inside the arm. She didn’t know when it had entered; there had been no
sensation of being touched. Or perhaps even a tiny fraction of the mist
coming close was enough to cause erosion. Shatia pondered, but failed to
reach a conclusion. She tried applying healing magic for the time being,
but since it wasn’t a physical wound, it wouldn’t be particularly
effective. It was a drastic measure, but she decided to flood her arm
with a massive amount of mana to forcibly halt the erosion. Either way,
she needed to settle this immediately.

“Enough hide-and-seek. I shall smoke you out,” Shatia muttered, tearing
her clothes to bind her arm just in case.

There were plenty of subtle magics that were powerful yet would go
unnoticed. For Shatia, who had mastered many, she had her pick. She had
only hesitated because she was thinking of contingencies. No matter how
“subtle,” Shatia’s mana could turn a simple fire spell into a grand
magic that breathed the flames of hell. Thus, she had hesitated. But in
this situation, it couldn’t be helped. To settle this, she could no
longer afford to be choosy about her methods. Shatia concentrated the
mana of her entire body and created a small silver crystal at the tip of
her hand.

“*Dance, Silver Bird*.”

As Shatia leaned in and whispered softly, the silver crystal shattered,
and a tiny bird emerged from within. A silver bird born of mana. It was
a simple creation spell, yet it was a powerful magical creature birthed
by the mana of a witch.

Shatia let the bird take flight from her hand. In that instant, the bird
sped away at high velocity. Scattering silver particles, it headed
toward the black mist. The mist scattered as if to escape, but upon
realizing that no special magic was imbued within the bird, it
immediately enveloped and began to erode it. The silver bird turned
black and soon began to lose its shape. But at that moment, the mist
wavered slightly.

“……——!”

“You fell for it. Did you think it was just a little bird?
…Unfortunately, it was rigged with a powerful explosion spell. Well, it
is too late to tell you now.”

Shatia smiled. The opponent noticed and tried to put distance between
themselves and the silver bird, but Shatia had already snapped her
fingers. Suddenly, the bird was enveloped in light and let out a
tremendous, deafening roar. Silver particles blew out in all directions,
swallowing the black mist.

“…………!!”

There was no form. No figure. Yet the mist thrashed as if in pain,
immediately trying to tear away the silver particles. Was the true body
there, or were they sharing sensations? Regardless, there was a
reaction. Shatia moved into the air, staring intently at the mist whose
movements had slowed.

“Now, allow me to see your face. Who are you? My dear daughter.”

“…………”

The black mist’s movements were being sealed by the silver particles.
Using the same components, Shatia had countered the mist by scattering
particles imbued with mana. It was a direct hit and proved effective.
Earlier, the mist would simply vanish when it fled, but now it was
unable to escape. Which meant, after all, that the true body was within.

Shatia smiled and quietly stared at the black mist. What was hidden
within that shroud? Who was it that was feigning this form? Shatia
wanted to know.

“Gah… hah…”

Taking a blow from the iron fist, Emerald collapsed headfirst onto the
ground. Her head became dull, as if it had turned into a heavy stone,
making normal thought impossible. The pain traveling from her cheek
raced to her jaw, her neck, and then throughout her entire body. The
only reason she remained conscious was likely the thin mana barrier she
had draped over her body. Beside the near-death Emerald, the creature
laughed.

“Hmm. You’re unexpectedly sturdy… You’re using some little trick, aren’t
you? How annoying. If you had just taken it like a good girl, you could
have gone down quietly…”

“Ha… ha… guh…”

The monster tilted its head, wondering why Emerald wouldn’t lose
consciousness. But upon realizing she was using some kind of magic to
mitigate the damage from its fists, it let out a bored sigh. Emerald
glared at the monster in her agony, looking for any chance to
counterattack. But the monster was not an existence to be dealt with
easily. The enemy before her was a monster to humans and witches alike.
Yet, that was exactly why she wondered. Why did such a monster exist?
Why had such a monster suddenly been born?

Rumor had it that the grotesque being had suddenly appeared a few years
ago. It repeated acts of destruction without purpose. It claimed to be
eradicating evil, but the radical way it wielded its power was
impossible to ignore. Emerald could not understand how such a being had
come into existence. The monster didn’t belong to any specific race, nor
was it a magical beast born from a mutation. It was strange that such a
creature had never been discovered once in history. That was why she
couldn’t comprehend it—the fact that a monster with such preposterous
power existed. Furthermore, it possessed enough power to surpass her, a
witch. Why? Only a witch… could create such a creature.

A bead of cold sweat rolled down Emerald’s forehead. Such a thing was
impossible. It was too far-fetched. Certainly, some witches possessed
the magic needed to create living things. In fact, Shatia, the mother of
all witches, loved magical beasts and used to raise them. They even
created familiars, so it wasn’t an impossible story. But there was no
reason to create a creature so powerful, immune to magic, almost as if
it were designed specifically to kill mages. It shouldn’t be. But what
if this hypothesis was the truth? If there were someone who hated humans
as she once had, and even thought of killing their fellow witches…
Emerald gasped at the thought.

Immediately after, the monster’s kick flew into her stomach. Emerald
flew through the air, spinning once before falling to the ground with a
painful thud. Her face was stained with blood, her tied golden hair came
undone, and her bangs fell over her eyes.

“Guh… ah… haa… haa…”

The monster spoke coldly, “Cease this futility. It is time you went to
sleep. If you push yourself further, you will truly lose your life.”

“Ha… ha… ah…”

Emerald breathed raggedly, forcing oxygen into her lungs over and over.
But the tightness in her chest wouldn’t fade. On the contrary, it became
even harder to breathe, and her head grew heavier. Emerald reached the
point where she could no longer stand, let alone channel mana properly.
The monster approached her slowly. At the same time, Emerald forced her
head up, her body trembling.

“Who… exactly… are you…?” Biting her lip, Emerald managed to squeeze out
the question. In a raspy, weak voice, she used the last of her strength
to ask.

The monster stopped moving, tilting its head slightly in silence as if
thinking. But it soon rippled its body with lack of interest and raised
its fist.

“Who knows. I’d like to know that myself.”

Answering flatly, the monster unleashed the final blow, this time for
certain, without a shred of emotion. With a dull sound, Emerald’s head
fell, and she collapsed to the ground. Her mana barrier was completely
stripped away, and having taken the monster’s blow with her bare body,
Emerald did not get up again.

◇

“…………!!”

In that instant, Shatia received a powerful shock. Being highly skilled
in mana detection, she knew exactly where Emerald was. Thus, she knew
Emerald had followed her orders to head toward the explosion and had
taken action there.

Shatia felt Emerald’s mana reaction suddenly vanish. However, she was
not so naive as to show an opening while an enemy was right in front of
her. She remained calm and composed. She was composed… or she should
have been. But the moment she felt Emerald’s mana vanish—that split
second of feeling—dulled Shatia’s movements. Because she felt it, her
body reacted, stopping its motion for just a fraction of a heartbeat. In
that instant, a black mist arm pierced through her body.

“Ah… guh…?”

“…Heh, heh.”

The black mist had taken the shape of a human arm. Though it was blurred
in places, it was certainly functioning as an arm. It was protruding
from her body. Which meant, which meant… she had been hit. Understanding
this, Shatia spat blood from her mouth. She hadn’t intended to be
careless. She hadn’t intended to show an opening. It was a mere instant.
She had been surpassed by a difference of a barely perceptible instant.
And the fact that they could perform such a feat meant the opponent was
undoubtedly a witch.

Hearing a woman’s laughter from behind her, Shatia tried to turn her
face in that direction. Perhaps because she was losing so much blood,
she couldn’t hear the sound correctly. Her vision wouldn’t transmit
correctly to her brain. Heat was draining from her body. There was no
time.

“Soft as ever. Way too soft on your own family, Shatifahl… Just crushin’
Emerald with the monster, of course you’d react. That right there’s your
weakness.”

A unique way of speaking, a familiar tone. There was no doubt. However,
Shatia’s mind was unable to function correctly, and she couldn’t reach
the answer. The owner of the voice moved the mist arm as if in mockery,
toying with Shatia’s body. The squelching sound of overflowing blood
echoed, and Shatia let out a groan. Then, as the owner of the voice
yanked the arm out with all her might, Shatia heard a sound like a
string snapping inside her, accompanied by agonizing pain.

“Goodbye, mom. The one I loved.”

Unable to even maintain her flight magic, Shatia fell as she heard the
woman’s voice. As she plummeted toward the ground, the figure of a girl
with golden hair flickered at the edge of her vision.

*Ah, it was you.* For some reason, she thought this idly and a smile
appeared on her face. Then, right by her ear, she heard a sickening
crunch, like a piece of fruit being crushed.
