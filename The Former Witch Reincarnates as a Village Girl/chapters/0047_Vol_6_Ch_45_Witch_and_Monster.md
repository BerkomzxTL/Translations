# Chapter 45: Witch and Monster

As Shatia had instructed, Emerald headed toward the source of the
explosion. What met her eyes was a gruesome sight: people dressed as
knights lay scattered across the ground. Their armor was dented in odd
ways, making it clear they had been defeated by blunt force rather than
magic or special abilities. Fortunately, they were still breathing, but
they were nonetheless in critical condition.

Emerald hesitated. If she didn’t use healing magic here and now, these
people might die. But was using healing magic truly the right thing to
do? Humans were not to be trusted. They would betray in an instant. If
she used her extraordinary healing magic, these knights might become
suspicious and try to capture her. Such anxieties swirled within
Emerald. But then, she reacted to the voice of a man slumped nearby.

“Help… me…”

“…!”

The man seemed unable to even recognize who was in front of him; he
simply reached out an arm, pleading for help. He was so badly injured
that he was forced to beg for help from a small girl. Emerald’s body
moved before she could think. She approached the fallen man and cast her
healing magic. The pained groans subsided shortly after, and the man
closed his eyes peacefully. Seeing this, Emerald let out a sigh of
relief in spite of herself. Then, feeling bewildered by her own
reaction, she stared at her trembling hands.

*I truly am a fool*, she thought. Despite being betrayed time and again,
despite swearing revenge, she still ended up helping humans like this.
Acknowledging her true nature, Emerald let out an exasperated sigh. She
immediately moved to the other fallen and cast healing magic on all of
them.

She didn’t know if this was truly the right thing to do. Perhaps she
would just be betrayed again. But even so, she would act so that she
wouldn’t have regrets later. Emerald decided to follow her heart, just
as Shatia had told her.

After finishing healing everyone, Emerald headed toward the direction of
the fighting. Leaping over a fence, she landed on the shattered remains
of a house, where she saw the grotesque monster fighting a silver-haired
knight. Emerald was startled by the monster’s bizarre form and felt a
sense of doubt regarding its strange mana. It was an eerie mana she felt
she had encountered somewhere before. Guardedly, Emerald observed the
battle.

The silver-haired knight was defeated and on the cusp of being finished
off. In that instant, Emerald stood up, channeled mana into her arms,
and unleashed an oversized mana blast. At this distance, it should have
been a direct hit, yet the grotesque monster leapt from the spot with
agility belying its appearance, completely evading the full-power
attack.

With a look of surprise, Emerald jumped down from the wreckage and
landed on the road to face the monster. She announced, “I will not
permit the harming of people. I shall… eliminate you.”

Emerald was by no means a witch specialized in combat. Of course, she
remained a being that transcended human knowledge—one that even a group
of ordinary mages could not hope to defeat—but among those
transcendents, she was weak enough to be called the weakest.

To begin with, her repertoire contained few offensive magics. Because
her aptitude and personality specialized her in healing, she hadn’t felt
the need for them. Therefore, she had no effective means of attack
against the monster before her. It already looked incredibly sturdy, and
its movements were swift. All she could do was channel her immense mana
and fire it. To be blunt, it was going to be a grueling fight.

“A mere brat…!!”

The monster sprinted forward. It lunged with a massive sound that shook
the ground, closing the distance in an instant. Emerald immediately
created a barrier in front of her, but the monster’s swinging fist
shattered it effortlessly. The impact blew Emerald away, sending her
crashing into the wall of a house. She could hear a sickening sound,
like something breaking inside her body. Blood trickled from her mouth
as she pressed her hand to her stomach and cast healing magic on
herself. A full recovery—but the pain didn’t fade instantly.

“Guh…”

Seeing Emerald still standing despite the damage, the monster spoke with
interest, “Hoh, you use interesting tricks. But do you really think you
can defeat me with such a thing?”

Its attitude was one of calm superiority; even sensing the mana of a
witch, it showed no fear. Emerald, on the other hand, had no room for
composure. She hadn’t even been hit directly, yet she took this much
damage just from the shockwave. Fortunately, she could recover
instantly, but the destructive power this creature possessed without
even using auxiliary magic was terrifying.

Emerald’s expression grew grim, and a bead of sweat rolled down her
forehead, but she did her best to maintain a defiant stance so as not to
let it show, “You shouldn’t get too ahead of yourself, Monster-san. You
will soon learn of an existence above you and run away with your tail
between your legs like a rat…”

“Khahaha! I look forward to that,” the monster simply laughed off
Emerald’s provocation, its composed attitude remaining unshaken. 

Thanks to the comfort of knowing Shatia would back her up, Emerald could
manage a defiant front for now, but she couldn’t help but think of the
worst-case scenario; *what if Shatia and this monster are evenly
matched?* Shaking her head to dispel the thought, Emerald moved. She
gathered her maximum mana into her palms, forming it into a sphere, and
fired. Because it was condensed, its speed was high. But again, the
monster evaded it and closed in right before her eyes.

“Then, before that happens, I think I’ll crush you to pieces.”

Through the gaps in its face covered in black tentacles, the monster
showed sharp fangs. It swung up an arm that looked like a mass of muscle
and brought it down toward Emerald without mercy. This time, she managed
to dodge, kicking off the ground to retreat. But the monster responded
instantly, moving at a speed invisible to the eye and circling around
behind her.

“…!!”

It was too fast. Far too fast. Unable to react, Emerald took a kick and
was slammed into the ground. Her vision inverted, and her mind went
blank for a moment. Though her consciousness threatened to flicker out,
Emerald immediately jumped back, placed her hand on her body, and
activated her healing magic. Her back and head were damaged. With just
one blow. If a follow-up attack had landed, she might have lost the
consciousness required to even cast the spell.

“Ha… ha…” Emerald’s expression darkened slightly, and a ragged breath
escaped her lips despite herself.

Healing magic was originally intended to be used to carefully heal the
wounds of an injured person. It was by no means something intended to be
used instantly during combat. That she could do so was thanks to her
skill and vast mana capacity, but even then, if the casting time was too
short, the healing became sloppy. Even if shattered bones were returned
to their places, if they were even slightly misaligned, it would cause
serious trouble later. Therefore, Emerald had to avoid relying on her
healing as much as possible.

Whether it realized this or had sensed Emerald’s lack of composure
through her growing panic, the monster let out a low, distorted chuckle,
“What’s the matter, little girl? Your face has lost quite a bit of its
confidence.”

“Thank you for your concern… but don’t worry, I was just letting my body
get used to things,” Emerald, showing disgust at being pitied by the
monster, retorted with a furrowed brow. She wiped the sweat from her arm
and stood up.

She had to stop this monster here. She didn’t know its goal, but it was
a threat capable of defeating a group of Holy Knights single-handedly.
She likely couldn’t kill it, but she could at least restrain it. If she
did that, Shatia could use her magic to peer into its mind once they
regrouped and learn its purpose. Forming this plan, Emerald gathered the
mana from her entire body into her fingertips, and as she moved her
fingers, she activated her trump card.

“*Shackles of Sin*…!!”

Along with a flash of light, magic circles burst forth, binding the
monster’s limbs. Surprised by the sudden inability to move, the monster
tried to jerk its arms, but it was a futile struggle; it couldn’t move
at all.

Success in the binding brought a smile to Emerald’s lips, but at the
same time, she felt a massive drop in her mana and her shoulders slumped
from the exhaustion.

“Nugh! …What is this?! I can’t move!”

“It’s useless. Those are inescapable mana restraints created with my own
power… you won’t break free easily.”

Magical restraints, the Shackles of Sin, were physically impossible to
escape. They could only be destroyed by something that could directly
interfere with mana, like Shatia’s Lullaby—or so it should have been.
But perhaps there were exceptions. For example, when the power of the
one bound far surpassed that of the caster.

The monster seemed to feel irritation at the fact that its arms wouldn’t
move no matter how much strength it applied. It shook its body, trying
to escape. Eventually, its anger seemed to reach its peak, and it let
out a roar, fangs bared from its hideous face.

“FOOLISH! Do you really think you can restrain me with such a thing?
AAAAGGGGHHHHH!!!”

It was like fire; the monster emitted something akin to heat from its
entire body as its muscles bulked up. Cracks rapidly spread through the
restraints until they finally reached their limit and shattered like
glass. Emerald froze, stunned that her trump card had been broken.

“What… eh…”

“Khahaha! Fragile, little girl. In the end, that is all you amount to!”

Emerald no longer possessed a means to oppose the monster. Her mana
blast wouldn’t hit, the creature’s offensive power surpassed her
healing, and it had even broken through the Shackles of Sin. She was
outmatched in every way.

Emerald remembered the words she had spoken earlier. *‘An existence
above you.’* That was, without a doubt, the monster currently before her
eyes. This grotesque monster possessed strength far exceeding her own, a
witch. The moment she realized this, Emerald lost the will to resist.

“But you fought well… as a reward, I shall sink you into the depths
without suffering. Now, it is time for the end.”

Noticing that Emerald had lost her will to fight, the monster didn’t
bother with further brutality. It approached Emerald leisurely and
raised its fist toward her small frame. A dark, pitch-black fist.
Emerald looked up at it blankly, only able to watch in silence as it was
swung down.

◇

Shatia was tracking the other anomalous mana signature, separate from
the one at the explosion. It was hidden very carefully. She leapt onto
the roof of a house and continued her search by following the lingering
scent. Finally, she found the source. Atop the tallest building, it was
hidden, blending into a black mist. She approached it while floating in
the air with flight magic.

Quietly, her caution at its peak, Shatia whispered, “…You are a witch,
are you not?”

It was hidden very carefully, but this was undoubtedly the mana reaction
of a witch, just like herself. When Shatia first noticed it, she
couldn’t believe it. She didn’t understand the reason for hiding, nor
why it was so ominous. Therefore, she hadn’t been able to call out
immediately and was only doing so now, with full vigilance.

The black mist, noticing Shatia’s presence, wavered and turned its
face-like portion toward her, scattering itself as if in mockery.

In the next instant, the black mist exploded, scattering all around. It
opened its maw wide as if to swallow Shatia whole. Shatia widened her
eyes and stared blankly, her vision dyed pitch black soon after.
