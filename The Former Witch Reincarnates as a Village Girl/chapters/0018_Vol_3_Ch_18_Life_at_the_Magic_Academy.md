# Chapter 18 – Life at the Magic Academy

A transfer student was coming. This was shocking news for the students
of the magic academy.

Unless there were exceptional circumstances, transfers rarely occurred.
Even if they did, it was usually a noble’s child or someone arriving
under a special dispensation. Thus, the children in the class were in an
absolute uproar, wondering what kind of child would be arriving.

The classroom door opened, and the usual teacher entered. He told the
students to take their seats. Then, with a clap of the hands, he
announced that a transfer student had arrived. The room immediately
began to buzz once more as the students resumed their debate over what
kind of person was joining them.

The teacher cleared his throat to quiet the students, then gave a signal
and called for the transfer student. The door opened, and a lovely girl
with beautiful, long silver hair appeared.

“I am Shatia. I look forward to working with you,” the girl stood
elegantly before the podium and spoke with confidence, showing no hint
of shyness.

To the students, she looked like a fairy—silver hair shining like the
moon, refined features, eyes as clear as pure water, and a slender frame
with porcelain skin. She possessed a perfect form, like a doll crafted
from glass.

“Geh…”

But one boy let out a groan, looking uncomfortable. Shatia noticed him
as well, narrowing her eyes with a hint of amusement. He was the boy who
had been bullying Laika the other day. Laika was also in the same class,
and when she realized the transfer student was Shatia, her eyes widened
in surprise and joy.

By the teacher’s instruction, Shatia’s seat was placed next to Laika’s.
Shatia greeted her with a smile, “We meet again, Laika.”

“Shatia-chan…!” Laika, happy to have someone she knew nearby, smiled
back. However, her smile was a bit stiff and somewhat hesitant. “I’m
really surprised! I didn’t know the transfer student was Shatia-chan…”

“Indeed. I look forward to the next six years.”

After a quick handshake, Shatia took her seat. Basic lessons began, and
during the break, Laika gave her a tour of the school building. In
truth, Shatia had already memorized the layout, so she didn’t need a
guide, but she accompanied Laika to deepen their friendship.

Occasionally, passing students curious about the new student would speak
to Shatia. Shatia brushed them off with non-committal replies, but she
noticed something: none of the students would meet Laika’s eye. They
didn’t ignore her in an extreme way, but their awkwardness was
noticeable. Laika was the same. There was a sense that both sides were
trying to maintain a policy of non-interference.

*Hmm*, Shatia thought, crossing her arms. Human society was proving much
more difficult than she had imagined.

“Um, this is the cafeteria. They have everything and it’s delicious. And
the bathroom is over there…”

“Laika. That is the boys’ bathroom.”

“Eh!? W-w-wha!”

Laika had pointed to a bathroom in the corner of the cafeteria, but she
was heading straight for the men’s side. It was a good thing Shatia
pointed it out immediately, otherwise, it would have been quite the
embarrassing sight for the girl.

Even more startled, Laika tripped as she tried to back away, landing
hard on her backside. Looking down at the pained Laika, Shatia let out a
breath that was more of an impressed sigh than mockery.

“Poor at magic *and* clumsy… you are quite the character, Laika.”

“Uuu… I’m sorry.”

Not out of malice, but with a sense of amusement, Shatia reached out a
hand. Laika looked a bit hesitant but apologized and took her hand,
patting the dust off her uniform as she stood up.

Then, footsteps approached. Multiple sets. Shatia turned around without
any particular tension. A group of female students had gathered, and all
of them had sharp eyes and malicious smirks.

“As usual, Laika is such a klutz. I’d appreciate it if you didn’t show
the transfer student such embarrassing things,” one girl stepped forward
and spoke coldly to Laika. She crossed her arms and shook her head with
a weary gesture.

Laika clutched her own arm fearfully and shrank back. Shatia watched in
silence.

“That’s so true~. Laika can barely use magic, and she’s a total failure
at sports. What *can* you do?”

“The other day, she tripped over nothing just like that. At this rate,
won’t you just tumble right down the stairs?”

The other girls surrounded Laika, throwing out barbs. It was clear
mockery, but not direct physical bullying. *How tiresome*, Shatia
thought idly. She glanced at Laika, who was trembling with her head
down. Her spirit was completely broken in their presence.

“It must be hard for you too, Shatia-san? Being stuck in the seat next
to a failure like this. If you want, we can tell the teacher to change
your seat,” one girl asked with a smirk.

Startled by the sudden question, Shatia snapped back to reality. It was
a strange question. Shatia didn’t see why that would be a reason to
move, but she decided she should give some kind of answer.

She predicted this was likely intended as an “invitation.” If she
agreed, they would cruelly judge that she was on their side. If she
refused, she might be treated like Laika. Shatia sighed inwardly at
their coldness and spoke, “No… I shall pass. I quite like that seat.”

“Oh? Is that so? Well, fine then.” The girl was visibly displeased, but
quickly put on a fake smile.

The group turned to leave as if they were done with Shatia, but as one
girl passed by, she noticed something tied to Shatia’s waist.

“Oh my~, what’s this? Wow, it’s a doll~”

She reached out and grabbed the doll tied to Shatia’s waist. The girls
who were about to leave gathered back, making a fuss and looking at the
object with amusement.

“No way, Shatia-chan, do you seriously still play with dolls?”

“What a weird doll. It’s kinda scary.”

Shatia’s silver hair swayed, her bangs hiding her face, making it
impossible to see her expression. But the girls didn’t stop. They called
the doll weird, said she had bad taste, and told her to get a cuter one.
They handled it roughly, pulling the hair—so violently it looked like it
might break.

“We’ll make it cuter for you. Ah~ or maybe you should just throw this
thing away and I’ll give you one of my old ones?” The girl put a hand on
Shatia’s shoulder mockingly. But then, she froze. The others stopped
moving, and even Laika went stiff.

Her face still hidden by her bangs, Shatia spoke softly, “Give it back…
That is a precious memento.”

Shatia was perfectly calm, but her voice was terrifyingly low. It was a
dark, guttural sound, as if dragged from the throat of a corpse, far
beyond what could be described as anger. The girls felt as if they were
physically bound by something, unable to speak. The girl holding the
doll, eyes welling with tears, managed to whisper a small, “Yes,” before
returning it with trembling hands.

“Hmm… Ahh, no, sorry. This was given to me by a friend. If it is
damaged, they would be angry. Forgive me for ruining the mood.”

“Ah, ah… is that so… no, it’s us who should be… um, apologizing.”

Shatia took the doll and smiled once she confirmed it was safe. But no
one could smile back. The girls fled as if running for their lives.

After carefully putting the doll away in her chest, Shatia let out a
small sigh and cleared her throat awkwardly.

“Shatia-chan… that was really scary.”

“Sorry. I didn’t think I could produce a voice that low. Kuku, it seems
I am still quite immature.”

Shatia cursed herself for losing her composure. She was supposed to
avoid doing anything that might stand out, yet she’d done this on her
first day. Rumors were bound to spread. *I must learn to look at things
more objectively*, she thought, tapping her head.

“But, you really treasure that doll… Is it, um, really a memento from a
friend?”

“Yes… it is a precious treasure from a dear friend. Ideally, I should
keep it in a wooden box, but I am poor at tidying. I fear I would lose
it.”

Shatia didn’t elaborate, instead giving a vague reason to establish it
as a valuable item that she always carried with her. After all, she
certainly couldn’t say a witch’s soul resided within it.

“Anyway, it seems you really are bullied, Laika. Is it always like
that?”

“Uh, y-yeah… well… since Shatia-chan was here today it wasn’t as bad…
but usually, yeah.”

Hearing this, Shatia put a hand to her chin. “I see. Very well.”

Shatia was pleased she had been able to guide the conversation away from
the doll. Fortunately, it seemed she wouldn’t need to resort to memory
manipulation. She instead brought up Laika’s bullying, and although she
didn’t look eager to talk about it, Laika answered honestly. 

“Alright… teaching someone once in a while might serve as a pleasant
diversion. Laika, I shall teach you the mysteries of magic,” Shatia
smirked.

“Eh?” Not understanding what was going on, Laika just tilted her head. 

On a whim, the witch of wisdom decided to raise the girl. The legendary
witch who once taught the six witches was picking up the baton once
again.
