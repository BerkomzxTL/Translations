# Chapter 33 – Settling Grief

Shatia was getting serious—that is to say, truly exercising her magic.

It wasn’t that she didn’t normally use magic. Simple fire spells or
flight were perfectly fine magics in their own right, and she could
create magical swords and shields at will. However, she usually handled
most situations simply by hurling masses of raw mana—in particular when
it came to offense. Because Shatia’s mana capacity was so astronomically
high, that was usually more than enough. Consequently, she had never
truly exerted herself. Her true worth only began to manifest when she
exercised her countless refined spells.

“Burn, O flames; rage, O storm; let the souls bound in chains now be
unleashed… My name is the Witch Shatifahl. Magic, obey me.”

As Shatia uttered, a gargantuan amount of mana gathered toward her.
Though overwhelmed by the intense pressure, Cloak didn’t back down; she
manifested her black snakes and launched them at Shatia. However, with a
flick of Shatia’s finger, magic circles deployed, and countless chains
of mana surged forth. Caught in the chains, the black snakes writhed in
agony. They tried to twist their bodies to turn into smoke, but perhaps
because they were being suppressed by the mana, they were unable to
change their form.

“Dammit…!”

Cloak clicked her tongue at the sight and moved into an offensive
stance. There was no telling what kind of spells Shatia would unleash
now that she was on the attack. In any case, the best course of action
was to move before her opponent could act. That was her logic, but the
decision came too late. Before she knew it, thick, pitch-black
smoke—rather than shadows—had drifted beneath her feet. From within,
blackened, corpse-like hands reached out and seized Cloak’s legs.

“What is this…!?” Cloak let out a small shriek and kicked her legs to
shake them off.

“They are the hands of the dead. Do not be too rough with them, or they
will drag you down to Hell.”

Though Shatia warned her to stay still, Cloak was in no mood to listen;
she channeled mana into her hands and unleashed a blast of flame. In the
next instant, a bizarre shriek echoed from the smoke, and countless more
hands swarmed Cloak.

“Hii…!”

She tried to summon more black snakes, but she had already pushed her
limit, and those she had summoned were pinned by the chains. She
couldn’t call forth any more. Cloak swatted at the crawling arms,
whipping up a gale, desperately trying to escape. She managed to clear
several of the hands, and used that opening to retreat, however, in the
meantime, Shatia had already finished another incantation and deployed a
new magic circle.

“Right then, the real show starts now.”

A massive magic circle expanded, and something bizarre emerged from it.
Cloak watched, breathless. It was a mass of flesh—or perhaps more
accurately, a part of something larger. It was clearly a high-level
summoning spell. That meant whatever had been summoned would possess a
devastating effect. Cloak braced herself, on high alert.

The mass of flesh bulged out from the magic circle, finally revealing
its shape. A giant arm. Unlike the hands of the dead from before, this
was clearly non-human—an ugly, monstrous limb. The arm swung upward as
if to crush Cloak and came crashing straight down.

With a thunderous roar, a massive crater was gouged into the hall.
Debris flew everywhere. Cloak managed to dodge at the last second, but
the shockwave sent her tumbling back against the wall. She clutched her
body, enduring the pain.

“Guh, gah… What kinda magic is that!?” Dripping blood from her mouth,
Cloak glared resentfully at Shatia.

It was too alien to even call it summoning magic. What was that giant
arm? Was that the creature’s true form, or just a fraction of it? If
that thing had a main body, just how enormous of a creature was it? It
was a magic Cloak had never seen, and her ignorance of Shatia’s magic
filled her with terror. However, she couldn’t back down. Cloak let out a
roar and channeled every last drop of mana into her arms.

“D-Daaaaammnnn yoooouuuuu!!”

It was a fire spell of the highest lethality. A massive pillar of fire
erupted with a blinding light; the monster’s arm flinched as it was
baptised in the flame and retreated back into the magic circle. But
Shatia’s magic didn’t end there. She had already deployed a magic circle
on the ceiling of the hall, from which a literal waterfall came crashing
down. The pillar of fire was extinguished in an instant, and Cloak was
caught in the deluge.

“Ngu, aaaa… Translocation!”

Hammered by the water, Cloak immediately activated her translocation
magic. The scenery shifted, and Cloak, now soaking wet, appeared on the
roof of the Demon Lord’s castle. The cold wind bit into her skin, and
she collapsed on the spot, coughing. Immediately after, a section of the
roof was obliterated by a pillar of fire, and Shatia rose through the
hole using flight magic. The girl was wearing a smile, which struck
Cloak as deeply unsettling.

“Something wrong? Is that all?”

“…Gu, ku…!!”

Faced with Shatia’s completely composed expression, Cloak gnashed her
teeth and triggered her translocation again. This time, behind Shatia.
She used her larger adult body to overwhelm the girl, slamming her on
the roof and pinning her down.

“Hoh, brute force? Quite a blunt approach for someone like you. So, what
now?”

“Haah… haah… I’ll kill you! You’re… finished…!”

Cloak was already battered and exhausted. She howled, her breathing
ragged and her wet hair disheveled. But Shatia showed no sign of panic.
On the contrary, her expression softened with amusement as she watched
Cloak resort to physical force.

Certainly, for a magic user to resort to brute strength was
questionable, but with such a difference in physique, it was a logical
advantage to exploit. Furthermore, an ambush following translocation was
a sound tactic.

Shatia analyzed the situation calmly. Then, with her composed expression
unchanged, she continued, “I asked *how* you plan to do it. Will you
snap my neck? Or will you crush my bones to immobilize me? Perhaps
cutting out my tongue is an option?”

Cloak frowned, unable to comprehend what Shatia was saying. Eventually,
Shatia snapped her fingers. Instantly, Cloak’s arms were seized by
magical threads. But they didn’t pull her away; on the contrary, they
guided Cloak’s hands around Shatia’s throat.

“What… are you…?”

“You were going to defeat me, were you not? Then keep choking me. Snap
my bones, and your goal will be achieved.”

Cloak couldn’t understand any of Shatia’s actions. Why would she do this
to herself? She could kill her with just a little bit of force, so why
was Shatia walking toward her own death? Was there some hidden purpose?
She didn’t know. She didn’t know anything. Cloak simply shook her head
in confusion and rejection. Shatia looked at her with a cold gaze.

“Do it, Cloak. Kill me with your own hands. Engrave the sensation into
your memory.”

Her hands trembled. In contrast, the manipulation of the magical threads
became even more aggressive, forcing Cloak’s hands to tighten. Cloak was
lost in a daze, and before she knew it, tears were streaming down her
face. She didn’t understand anything. She didn’t know what to do. She
didn’t even know what she truly wanted to do with Shatia anymore.

She screamed in rejection, “Aa, ah… AAAAAAAAAHHHHH!!”

With a sharp snap, the threads were torn apart. Cloak scrambled away
from Shatia, retreating to the very edge of the roof. Shatia sat up,
coughing slightly, and looked down at the terrified Cloak. The witch
looked like nothing more than a small, frightened child.

“That is ruin. Everything disappears. You lose sight of what you want to
do and writhe in suffering. Can you endure that?” Shatia walked slowly
toward her.

“Haah… haah… No… I don’t want to…” Cloak shook her head while clutching
it with her hands. Her previous bravado had vanished entirely, and she
curled into a ball, trembling.

Looking down at the frightened girl, she spoke, “Then accept everything.
I will not say you have to do it immediately. Take your time, and find
what it is you can do.”

She no longer intended to fight. Shatia calmed the girl down as she
gently stroked her head. Cloak looked up at Shatia with tear-filled
eyes. She caught a scent that felt nostalgically familiar—a gentle,
motherly scent.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
Afterward, Shatia took the spellbook from Cloak. She searched for the
pages detailing the method of artificial body creation, flipped through
them, and then closed the book. Satisfied, she returned it to Cloak,
warned her to return it properly to the royal capital, and turned
around.

At that moment, Cloak reached out and called out to her, “Shatia… were
you able to endure it? This… this urge for revenge?”

“…………”

Shatia stopped in her tracks. She didn’t look back, but tilted her head
as if in thought while facing away.

It was a simple question. Shatia had said she once threw herself into
revenge. But only ruin waited at the end of that path. If the moment
Cloak just experienced—the moment of losing one’s purpose—had also come
for Shatia, did it mean the woman standing here now had endured it?

Finally, Shatia turned only her head toward Cloak, a lonely look in her
eyes.

“No. That is why I lost them. What was precious to me.”

Shatia returned to the castle without another word. Cloak didn’t try to
follow; she simply hung her head and vented frustrations that no longer
had an outlet.

The battle between witches had concluded. There was no victor—only an
unspeakable sadness remained.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
After the battle, Shatia returned to Belpheus. It was already the dead
of night, and as the world outside was shrouded in darkness, the
citizens began to stir over the incident at the castle, with rumors
circulating that the humans had attacked. However, the soldiers Belpheus
had placed on standby quickly quelled the chaos, and the incident was
officially summarized as a mere accident during a magical experiment.

In the room where they had first met, Shatia tidied her disheveled hair
and clothes before facing Belpheus.

“I leave the rest to you, Belpheus. You were once in step with her. See
this through to the end.”

“…Yes, I understand. If there’s anything I can do, I’ll do it.”

It was a point already agreed upon during the initial negotiations, but
after the battle with Cloak, it was decided that Belpheus would look
after her. Since she already held the position of the Demon Lord’s aide,
it wasn’t particularly strange. However, Shatia asked Belpheus to
provide as much care as possible, knowing that Cloak was likely wounded
after falling into the depths of despair. Since it was a mess of his own
making, Belpheus accepted the task readily.

“You decide what to do about the human nations. Accept a ceasefire,
propose a peace treaty, or even continue the conflict if you wish.”

“No, I’ll pass on that. It seems I need some time to think. It looks
like I should prioritize unifying my own kingdom over ruling the world.”

Hearing Belpheus’s reply, Shatia let out a thoughtful “Hmm.” Tilting her
head, she approached him and looked up into his eyes. Seeing his serious
expression, she let out a satisfied smile and gave a firm, vigorous nod.

“What… what will you do now?” Belpheus asked.

Shatia looked slightly pensive.

She had the body creation magic memorized. She could likely begin as
soon as the necessary preparations were in place. She slowly pulled out
the doll she had tucked away and stared at it.

“I have plenty to do myself. I am a busy person, believe it or not. For
a while, I shall be holed up somewhere where no one can disturb me.”

Answering thus, Shatia pulled up her hood and began walking toward the
exit. Belpheus watched her small back in silence. It was a back
incomparably smaller than the one he remembered from when she was known
as a witch, yet to Belpheus, it seemed immense; its greatness no less
than his father’s.

Shatia opened the door and stepped into the hallway. Finally, glancing
back at Belpheus from beneath her hood, her lips curled ever so
slightly.

“Let us meet again, Demon Lord Belpheus.”

With that, Shatia closed the door and vanished from Belpheus’s sight.

An existence in a place that could never be reached. Yet, an existence
endlessly warm and bottomlessly kind. He couldn’t envision a future
where he stood as her equal. Belpheus let out a sigh and leaned back
into the sofa.
