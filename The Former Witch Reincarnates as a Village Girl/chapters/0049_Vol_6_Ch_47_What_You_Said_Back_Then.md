# Chapter 47: What You Said Back Then

“Head… right arm… bones do not appear to be broken… The abdomen,
however, will take some time to repair.”

Amidst the cries of the people, Shatia calmly assessed her own condition
while desperately maintaining her flickering consciousness. The damage
was severe. Blood flowed incessantly from her pierced body. The only
reason she remained conscious was that she had managed to activate
healing magic at the very last second. However, it did not possess the
miraculous restorative power that Emerald’s did; it was merely meant to
stop the bleeding and prevent further deterioration.

“Y-You! Are you alright? We need to treat you immediately—”

“It is not a problem. Forget everything you have seen here, and go
somewhere safe.”

Seeing a man rush toward her, Shatia sat up and pointed a finger at him.
The panicked man’s face instantly became calm, and he walked away as if
nothing had happened. The people crowding around also stopped their
commotion and returned to their respective business. Shatia glanced up
at the sky. The black mist was gone.

“…As expected, she is no longer there. Chasing her would be a waste of
effort. I must get to Emerald.”

While checking the state of her wounds, Shatia stood up, shifting her
focus.

A moment ago, she had felt Emerald’s mana suddenly vanish. She didn’t
know if the girl was dead, but she was certainly weakened to the point
where her mana could no longer be sensed. During the battle, she had
maintained her composure despite being caught off guard as a result of
that, but the truth was that she was worried. Shatia forced her sluggish
body to move, clutching her arm as she began to walk. Just then, a man
appeared before her.

“…Who are you?”

“Ha… ha… Are you the child named Shatia?”

A man with silver hair dressed as a knight. He was already battered, and
on his back, he carried a person Shatia knew well. It was Emerald.

“My name is Kousal, Captain of the Holy Knights… I heard about you from
this girl. She said that if it is you, you would know the truth behind
this incident…”

Kousal looked as if he were about to collapse at any moment. Hearing
this, Shatia understood the situation and realized that things were far
more serious than she had imagined. Silence fell over the city.

One battle had ended, and the next was about to begin.

For the time being, they had decided to leave the area. Since their
injuries were severe, they had to go somewhere they could receive
treatment. Naturally, that was Loreid’s mansion.

While treating the heavily injured Emerald, Shatia exchanged information
with Kousal.

“Why did you believe her? That this child was telling the truth.”

“I was saved by her. That is why I chose to believe… besides, the Knight
Order is virtually decimated. I need help if I’m going to take down that
monster.”

“Hmm… I see.”

Shatia nodded as if satisfied by Kousal’s reasoning and wrapped a
bandage around Emerald’s forehead. Since Emerald had activated healing
magic on herself initially, her wounds had partially recovered. All that
was left was to provide mana from the outside.

After finishing the first aid, Shatia checked her own wounds. Since she
couldn’t rely on Emerald’s healing, a full recovery wasn’t to be
expected.

“Then allow me to tell you one thing. What you saw was likely not a
monster, but someone whose form was changed by a curse.”

“…What?”

The monster that had destroyed the city was not merely a beast, but
something transformed by a curse. Kousal felt fear rather than surprise
at this revelation. If that were true, why did such a thing happen?
Kousal shook his head in confusion, unable to make sense of anything.

“While this child was fighting the monster, I was fighting the one who
seemed to be the mastermind controlling it… though this is the result of
having my opening exploited,” Shatia spoke with a self-deprecating laugh
as she wrapped her own wounds in bandages.

Kousal felt a sense of incongruity at her behavior, which was far from
that of an ordinary young girl. However, ever since Emerald had
appeared, he knew they weren’t normal humans, so he wasn’t particularly
taken aback.

“I have an idea of who the mastermind is. And the monster as well… If my
theory is correct, this is merely a ‘prank’ played by that mastermind.”

“A prank… you say? Creating such a monster and destroying the city is a
‘prank’!?”

“Exactly. From that person’s perspective, at least.”

Shatia had a sorrowful expression. During the battle with the black
mist, she had heard the true voice of the mist at the very end. Her
consciousness had been hazy then and she couldn’t hear it clearly, but
now she was beginning to suspect whose voice it belonged to. She was
also starting to see the trick behind this incident.

“I will catch the monster and the mastermind… but first, I must find the
monster.”

Driven by a strong sense of responsibility, Kousal volunteered his help,
“I-In that case, I must assist! As a knight, I cannot simply stand by
and watch!”

But Shatia brushed the proposal aside, rejecting him, “Do not be
reckless. You are injured yourself. There is no need for you to follow
me. You should rely on the kingdom’s soldiers.”

Kousal, however, would not be easily dissuaded and clung to her with his
request. Occasionally, one encounters such earnest humans. Shatia let
out a tired sigh and ran a hand through her hair.

“…Fine. In that case, there are things I need you to prepare. A place
where there are no people, and several materials,” deciding to yield,
Shatia saw Kousal bow gratefully. She took out a piece of paper, listed
the necessary materials, and handed it to him. 

“I-I understand! Leave it to me. I will prepare them immediately,”
Kousal nodded, took the paper, and hurried out of the mansion.

Watching the door close, Shatia let out another quiet sigh, “Good grief…
if only every human on this continent possessed a kind heart like his…”

Exhausted and looking slightly pale. Sweat poured from her forehead even
though she wasn’t hot. Staggering across the room, she collapsed onto
the sofa and gripped her arm tightly.

“Guh… kh… I was hit worse than I thought.”

When she unwound the bandage, Shatia’s right arm was stained pitch
black. As if it were disintegrating, the blackened flesh of her arm was
crumbling away, a wound clearly distinct from normal injury. At that
moment, Shatia had taken the black mist to her arm for just an instant.
At the time, there was no great effect, but now a sudden change had
begun. It was likely a type of curse. Because of that, even healing
magic could not restore it. Shatia buried her face in the sofa,
desperately enduring the pain radiating from inside her body. Her
consciousness began to fall into a dark abyss.

Shatia’s consciousness drifted into the past. A dream of the past, a
story from the days when she still lived as Shatifahl, the Witch of
Wisdom. She was standing on a hill side-by-side with a young girl. The
girl’s face was hidden by something like a black mist and could not be
seen. But Shatia knew her well.

The witch beside her spoke while watching the human armies on the
distant plains, “The humans’re expanding their territory again, y’know,
Shatifahl? Is this okay? Just leaving it like this.”

“It cannot be helped… we witches are forbidden from interfering with the
actions of humans. We must leave it to nature.”

However, Shatia, who had lived as part of the witch race for ages, chose
not to take any action even as she watched, opting instead to follow the
rules she had set and silently leave things to fate.

“Haa… that ‘commandment’ again? From my perspective, that’s a buncha
nonsense,” the witch beside her voiced her dissatisfaction.

Shatia spoke as she hopped down from a rock she was using as a stool,
tapping her head with a finger, “I am much older than you… my mind has
become quite twisted.”

“Whoa, you’re actually sayin’ that yourself? Now that’s really twisted,”
though the other witch’s expression was invisible, she put a hand to her
mouth and let out a repulsed sound.

Seeing this, Shatia gave a bitter smile and shook her head with a “good
grief.”

“Hey, Shatifahl. The reason we witches are feared is ’cause a few of the
clan ran into humans back in the day and ended up fighting, right?”

While they were walking, the other witch asked as if suddenly
remembering. Shatia’s face contorted as if to ask where this was coming
from, but a child’s questions had to be answered properly. Despite her
annoyance, Shatia replied thoroughly.

“Yes, that is right. While humans were exploring unknown continents,
they had the misfortune of encountering witches who claimed those lands
as their territory. And they were in the middle of a magical experiment
at the time… As a result, a fierce battle broke out with the humans who
feared the witches… It is an unforgettable event.”

Shatia had a pained expression. This happened long before the witches
Shatia was currently raising were even born. It was a story from the
time when witches still existed as a unified clan. This incident was the
trigger for witches to become known to—and feared by—humans. The humans
advanced further, seizing the witches’ territories. That was why the
witches tried to minimize contact with the outside world as much as
possible, to prevent their numbers from dwindling any further.

The other witch put a hand to her mouth and hummed, seemingly lost in
thought. *What is she doing?* Shatia wondered, narrowing her eyes as she
watched her.

“Hmm… so that means the only other pain in the neck’s the Lizardmen,
yeah? That Demon Race tries to stay outta our way, and they’ve got a
rapport with you.”

“…What have you been talking about for a while now?”

“No, no, I just had a little thought, y’know.”

Shatia looked puzzled by the witch who had been saying strange things.
What exactly was she trying to say? Why was she asking all these weird
questions? As if to answer, the girl broke into a grin so wide it was
visible even through her pitch-black shroud.

“If we wiped ‘em all out, I wonder if we witches could finally live in
peace.”
