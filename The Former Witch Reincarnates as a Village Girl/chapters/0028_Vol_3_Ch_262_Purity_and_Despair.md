# Side Story 2: Purity and Despair

—A story from before the girl fell to impurity. Innocent to human greed
and filth, she believed in the light of people and held hope for the
world.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
Far from the royal capital lay a town that was not particularly large.
Surrounded by mountains and serenity, it was a place home to a certain
girl. She rented a room at an inn, where she lived day to day.

Curling up in her small bed and hiding under the blankets, the girl let
out a soft sound as the sunlight filtered through the window, “Mmm…
nngh… is it morning already…?”

Her voice was clear like a bell. With long, flowing hair that shone like
gold and eyes as blue and clear as the sea, she possessed a beauty so
refined she looked like a doll. Her porcelain skin was without a single
blemish, her legs were long, and her fingers were slender. This
beautiful girl let out a cute yawn as she rose from her bed and set her
feet upon the floor. The chilly sensation made her face twitch slightly.

Her name was Emerald. Known to people as the Witch of Purity, she was a
kind-hearted witch who specialized in healing magic.

There were seven witches in total, and Emerald was a rarity among them
who maintained interactions with humans. Generally, witches were
persecuted by humans; their existence was not looked upon kindly.
However, in this town, thanks to Emerald’s long years of effort, people
had come to understand witches and engage with her. That was why she was
able to live here now, sleeping comfortably in an inn bed.

“*Fwaah*… did I have any work scheduled for today?” Still drowsy,
Emerald walked across the room while yawning. She checked a paper pinned
to the wall to confirm her schedule for the day.

In this town, Emerald used her healing magic to help people, and because
it was the magic of a witch, the reputation of witches was excellent.
Consequently, there were so many people desiring her healing that she
was always booked solid. She had to check her schedule constantly to
manage her time.

Yet, Emerald never found this situation difficult, rather, it was a joy.
People understood the power of a witch and relied upon it. This was what
Emerald had wanted most, and it was the very definition of happiness for
her. The peace she had finally grasped—she had no time to complain about
being busy if she wanted to maintain it. After checking her schedule,
she stood before the mirror, tied her hair into twin tails, and let out
a breath.

“Alright, let’s do our best again today!”

She cheered herself on in the mirror. Then, her beautiful blue eyes
shimmering, she stepped out of her room to the start of a new day.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
“Emerald-chan, over here too, please!”

“Please help me as well~, Emerald-chan!”

“Coming, coming! Just wait a moment, please!”

The place where Emerald usually worked was the church in the center of
town. Beds were lined up, used by the injured and ill who visited.
Today, too, many people had come, and Emerald was being called from one
side to the other. But this was no burden. She didn’t think it was a bad
thing to be needed, and above all, it made her happy to be relied upon.
Though she was sweating, she continued to heal people without losing her
smile.

“Big sister, I scraped my leg… please help,” A small boy sitting on a
bed spoke to Emerald and rolled up his trousers to show the injury. He
had clearly fallen somewhere; the scrape was bleeding.

However, Emerald knelt down, patted the boy’s head, and gently
admonished him, “You shouldn’t rely on magic for something like this.
Healing magic is meant only for illnesses that truly can’t be cured
otherwise or for essential situations. If used recklessly, it can
actually drain your body’s life force.”

Healing magic was an extraordinary art. Consuming mana to drastically
boost the body’s natural recovery, it could take any injury and remedy
it in an instant. However, this was effectively overworking the body.
Excessive healing magic was considered dangerous. For such a reason,
Emerald typically declined using it for minor injuries.

The boy made a dissatisfied face and puffed out his cheeks in a pout,
“But… it hurts.” He hated pain, and had wanted Emerald to fix it
instantly. But because he was refused, his mood soured and he looked
down.

“I’ll apply proper medicine for you. Come now, look up.” Emerald gave a
troubled smile and made the boy lift his head. Taking a first-aid kit,
she applied medicine and put on a bandage. Then, looking up at the boy
who still looked grumpy, Emerald said with a mischievous grin, “If
you’re a boy, you mustn’t whine. I’ll cast a little charm for you…
there, ‘Pain, pain, fly away!'”

Gently placing her hand over the scrape, Emerald recited the
“incantation.” It wasn’t magic or anything of the sort, just a “spell”
to distract him. However, the boy suddenly turned red with embarrassment
and jumped down from the bed.

“I-I’m fine now! Big sister!”

“Is that so? Please tell me if it really hurts. Can you get home by
yourself?”

The boy didn’t even bother to answer; he opened the church door and
hurriedly ran out as if escaping. Watching his retreating figure,
Emerald giggled and whispered, “How cute.” Long ago, she too had been
small and energetic like that. Recalling those times, she briefly felt
sentimental. But she quickly shook her head to dispel those thoughts.
She had a life to live now. She didn’t have time to dwell slowly on the
past.

Emerald continued to heal people after that. Because many in this town
had jobs involving manual labor or facing monsters, there was no end to
those seeking healing. Naturally, Emerald herself felt fatigued from
consuming too much mana, but she didn’t mind because of the joy of being
useful. As long as someone relied on her, Emerald would keep pushing
herself. That was her resolve.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
“Thank you as always, Emerald-chan. Thanks to you, the people of this
town are safe.”

“No, it’s nothing… I’m just healing everyone’s wounds.”

When noon arrived, Emerald took a break from work and rested in a room
within the church. The sister who managed the church was there as
well—an older, gentle-looking woman. It was through this sister’s
assistance that Emerald was able to do her current work.

Drinking the tea the sister had brewed, Emerald lightly massaged her own
shoulders. No matter how much mana she possessed as a witch, physical
fatigue was inevitable. She tried her best not to let it show on her
face, but she felt the exhaustion even during work.

“By the way, there were quite a lot of people coming for healing today.
Did something happen?” To avoid letting the sister realize how tired she
was, Emerald changed the subject.

It was true that many people had sought healing. While some usually came
just to see Emerald, there were many injuries even excluding them.
Emerald wore a slightly worried expression, wondering if something was
wrong.

“Ah, actually… monsters have started appearing in the southern forest
lately,” the sister answered with a look that suggested she found the
topic difficult. Wanting to avoid worrying Emerald as much as possible,
she hadn’t wanted to talk about it. But since she was asked, she
answered honestly.

Emerald almost dropped her cup in shock, “Monsters near the town?”

The sister explained, “They aren’t that numerous, but perhaps they’ve
made a nest somewhere. The men of the town are currently out dealing
with them. That’s likely why there are so many injuries.”

“I see…” Emerald put a hand to her chin.

A few monsters wandered near the town, but the danger level was
relatively low. Consequently, there had been no major damage until now,
but this time, it seemed monsters strong enough to cause injuries were
on the move. As a healer, Emerald wanted to do something about this
situation.

“Um, in that case, I’ll…” Suddenly, words spilled from her mouth almost
before she realized it. She mostly used magic specialized in healing,
but she could still handle magic more powerful than an ordinary mage.
After all, she was one of the Seven Witches; she could estimate that her
combat power was sufficient.

But the sister, looking a little sad, shook her head and said, “No,
Emerald-chan. We can’t possibly ask any more of you. Just having you
heal everyone’s wounds is already a huge help.”

From the sister’s perspective, having Emerald cast healing magic was
more than enough. She knew that even if Emerald had the strength to
subjugate monsters, they couldn’t simply rely on her for everything.

“I see… but please tell me if anything happens. I want to be of help to
everyone,” Emerald requested with a sad face. She had interpreted the
sister’s refusal as her not being dependable enough.

When asked so earnestly, the sister couldn’t refuse. Though their
thoughts differed, both worried for the other and tried their best not
to cause concern.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
Emerald continued working into the night before finally finishing her
duties and closing up. As she expected, there were several people who
showed signs of having fought monsters during the day, making her
increasingly concerned. However, if she did something unnecessary, it
might cause trouble for the townspeople. Fearing being hated more than
anything, she found herself unable to act and ended up doing nothing.

After returning to the inn, she finished her meal, took a bath, and
immediately crawled into bed. Tomorrow would be another early day. To
fully recover her mana, she needed plenty of sleep. Closing her eyes,
her consciousness soon grew hazy as she drifted into the world of
dreams.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
It happened in the middle of the night; Emerald suddenly awoke. She had
sensed mana filled with hostility. It wasn’t a complex mana reaction
like a human’s, but a basic response of pure hunger. Emerald realized
instantly that it belonged to a monster. And that there were multiple
such reactions.

“Ngh… don’t tell me, monsters in the town…!?”

Her concerns realized, Emerald jumped from her bed, threw on her robe,
and darted outside. Screams echoed from the direction of the plaza,
followed by sounds of destruction. Emerald headed straight in that
direction. There, she found giant monsters shaped like boars running
rampant. They had sharp horns and tusks, distorted muscles, and eyes
stained red; true monstrosities.

“Uwaaaaaagh! Help meeee!!”

“Someone! My child… the monsters… aaaaaagh!!”

Screams could be heard from everywhere. Some were trapped under
destroyed buildings, others were injured and unable to move. Emerald
wanted to cast healing magic immediately. But before that, she had to
defeat the boar monsters in front of her. Emerald gathered mana in her
palms and clenched her fists tight.

“I will not allow you to run wild in this town!!”

Unlike Shatifahl, Emerald prioritized human life over that of monsters.
Even if they were only attacking the town out of instinct, that was of
no concern to her. She aimed her palms and released her mana. In that
instant, with a thunderous roar, a blast was fired, blowing one of the
boar monsters far away.

“BUUMOOOOOOOOOOOO!!”

Angered by a human fighting back, the boars let out a roar. They stamped
their hooves, took aim, and charged at Emerald. However, Emerald didn’t
dodge; she repelled them with a barrier, and then bound one of them with
chains of mana, flinging it toward another. The two collided and
shrieked. Emerald immediately turned her gaze back to face the remaining
creatures. The monsters were now completely wary of her, surrounding her
while closing in slowly.

Emerald raised her vigilance, ready for an attack at any second. Then,
in the next moment, they lunged all at once. Without flinching, Emerald
released a pulse of mana at the ground, using the impact to blow them
all back. They slammed into buildings and lost consciousness. Confirming
the threat had passed for the time being, Emerald let out a sigh.

“*Phew*… I’ve used too much magic, it seems,” she murmured as she wiped
the sweat from her brow.

She had been using healing magic from morning until night. Because of
that, she was considerably exhausted. On top of that, using offensive
magic—which was not her forte—had pushed her even further. But she was
the only one who could save the people here right now. She couldn’t
afford to collapse.

“E-Emerald-chan! Please! My child’s gone missing…!!”

Suddenly, Emerald felt her sleeve being tugged at; a woman from the town
was begging her to find her child. The woman was the mother of the boy
who had come to the church that morning.

“Ngh… I understand! I’ll find him right away!!” Feeling a terrible
premonition, Emerald left the plaza and combed through the streets
searching for the boy. She could still sense the presence of monsters.
Perhaps he was being attacked. Her anxiety growing, Emerald continued
her search.

Running while defeating any boar monsters she encountered, Emerald kept
going. Finally, in a corner of the town, she found the boy. He was
covered in wounds and cornered by a single boar.

Emerald leaped, showering the monster in mana blasts in mid-air. The
monster flinched, and in that opening, she landed in front of the boy to
shield him.

“Big sister!!” The boy cried out looking like he was about to burst into
tears. His face was covered in grime, and his clothes were torn in
places. It was clear he had been through a terrible ordeal. 

Emerald bit her lip and faced the monster with a sharp expression. “It’s
okay… Big sister will protect you.”

She had already consumed a vast amount of mana. Considering the healing
she would need to do for the townspeople afterward, she wanted to avoid
further consumption. But a monster stood before her. With Emerald’s
current physical strength, she couldn’t even pick up the boy and run.
Realizing she was cornered, Emerald’s expression clouded as she wondered
what to do.

“BUUMOOOOOOOOOO!!”

But indifferent to her struggle, the monster roared and lunged at her.
She dodged the charge while protecting the boy, rolling across the
ground. Emerald breathed heavily while keeping the boy behind her.

“What should I do…”

Understanding just how much of a pinch she was in, panic began to show
on Emerald’s face. Since she wasn’t skilled at combat to begin with, she
couldn’t respond properly. At this rate, both she and the boy would
fall.

The monster charged again. Emerald tried to dodge, but her legs tangled,
and she took the hit head-on. A dull pain spread through her body as she
was blown back into a building. Crashing into the wall and feeling
intense pain in her back, Emerald didn’t lose consciousness but let out
a pained groan.

“Big sister!!” Seeing Emerald blown away, the boy let out a cry.
Fortunately, the monster’s horn hadn’t pierced her body, but she had
still taken major damage. Her body refused to listen due to fatigue and
pain; she couldn’t even manage to stand up.

She thought she would be finished by the monster, but for some reason,
perhaps convinced of its victory upon seeing the immobile Emerald, it
turned its target toward the boy. Paralyzed by fear and despair, the boy
couldn’t even run; he trembled with tears streaming down his face.

“S-stop… that child is…!!”

She called out to stop the monster, but of course, her voice didn’t
reach it. Emerald’s outstretched hand fell hollowly to the ground. She
tried to put strength into her legs, but they gave out immediately, and
she collapsed back onto the earth. In that time, the monster moved
steadily closer to the boy.

“Stop… it…!!”

No. That was the one thing that must not happen. Emerald’s voice turned
harsh as she crawled along the ground. Someone she had to protect.
Someone she had to save. She had to prevent anything from hurting him,
no matter what. Emerald felt anger. Why did it have to be like this? Why
did the peace she had finally grasped have to be violated by something
like a monster? This must never be allowed to happen. A fierce rage
enveloped her. Gradually, her blue eyes began to stain black, until they
were painted over in pitch darkness.

“Freeze.”

A cold command was released—one that didn’t seem possible to have come
from Emerald’s mouth. In that instant, the monster in Emerald’s sight
became as immobile as if it had turned to stone; it even stopped
breathing and ceased all activity. Seeing the unmoving monster, the boy
finally came to his senses and collapsed in tears.

“Hah… hah… Big sister?” The boy didn’t know what had happened, but
thinking Emerald had saved him, he turned toward her. However, there he
saw Emerald crouched over, clutching her eyes in pain and saying things
he didn’t understand. For some reason, the boy felt fear at that sight
and remained unable to move.

“Ngh… hah… k-uh… don’t look!” Emerald writhed on the spot. After much
suffering, she finally calmed down and sat up. Her eyes had returned to
their usual beautiful blue, and she breathed heavily while drenched in
sweat.

“*Phew*… I’m sorry. Are you all right?” She finally regained a calm
composure and asked the question while smiling gently to reassure the
boy. 

“Y-yeah…” Relieved that she had returned to her usual self, the boy
answered while still a little frightened.

Emerald was overjoyed from the bottom of her heart that the boy was
safe, and she sank to her knees again in exhaustion. This time, the boy
ran to her side and held her shoulder. Seeing this, she smiled quietly
and thanked him.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
The incident was suppressed thanks to Emerald’s efforts. Many in the
town felt gratitude, but some felt fear toward that power. In
particular, there were several witnesses to the power Emerald showed at
the very end to freeze the monster, and some argued that witches were
terrifying after all. However, it was a fact that many were grateful,
and it was certain that Emerald had saved the town.

She would continue to live and heal the people, wishing for peaceful
days to continue.

\*\*\*

A group was walking along a road. One was a boy clad in silver armor,
radiating an atmosphere different from that of an ordinary human.
Several companions followed him, acting as escorts. The boy stopped
suddenly and looked down at a town visible from the hilltop. He took a
map from his breast pocket, confirming that they were indeed at their
destination.

“So that is the town from the reports… and a witch is there…” The boy
murmured while looking at the map.

His eyes were fixed on the town; they were straight, focused eyes that
had cast aside all doubt. A boy enveloped only in light, knowing nothing
of darkness. He was heading toward the town in order to erase a certain
existence.

“Wait for me, Witch of Purity… I, the Hero, shall cut you down,” the
boy, the Hero, murmured as he touched the sword at his waist.

The people surrounding and following the Hero for some reason wore
wicked smiles. No semblance of justice could be found among them.

And so the Hero boldly stepped toward the town without hesitation.
Shamelessly, and full of ignorance, he pushed forward. Toward tragedy,
toward despair.
