# Chapter 4 – Battle against the Skeletons

Shatia continued to dodge the endless volley of lightning strikes, her
eyes reflecting a sense of detached enlightenment. Evading left, right,
her flight magic compensated for the physical limitations of her
child-like form. Occasionally, she blocked unavoidable strikes with a
barrier.

For Shatia, dodging the lightning of the Sorcerer Skeletons was a simple
task. Her true concern lay elsewhere.

“There.”

In the intervals between attacks, Shatia flicked her finger to release a
lightning strike of her own. It struck one of the Sorcerer Skeletons,
silencing it instantly. Shatia had been firing back like this
repeatedly, but the number of Sorcerer Skeletons showed no sign of
thinning. On the contrary, the group that had started at twenty now
numbered nearly forty; somehow increasing.

“Good grief, there is no end to them, no matter how many I fell…” This
tedium caused Shatia’s expression to cloud over with annoyance.

At this rate, the villagers would notice her absence. Shatia decided
that taking any more time would be dangerous, but at the same time,
simply fleeing felt unsatisfying. She swiped at the air in vexation
before retreating high into the sky to take a brief moment to rest.

“Now, what should be done? I have no concerns about mana… but if their
numbers never dwindle, even I shall eventually tire.” Shatia crossed her
arms in the air, looking down at the Sorcerer Skeletons as she pondered.

Striking at the source would surely be the most straightforward action,
but she didn’t know the source’s whereabouts. Even the talented Shatia
couldn’t act without that knowledge. As she rested her chin on her hand
and tilted her head, her silver hair swaying, she peered down below. To
her surprise, the Sorcerer Skeletons were gone.

“Hmm?”

Uncharacteristically startled to the point of nearly falling, Shatia
hurriedly looked around. She found them gathered at a single point among
the rocks, raising their staves and channeling mana. Seeing this, a bead
of sweat rolled down Shatia’s forehead.

In the next instant, the area was enveloped in light. For a fleeting
moment, the world fell silent, followed by a violent roar. When the
light faded, the Sorcerer Skeletons were holding their staves aloft,
forming a massive sphere of lightning.

Fusion Magic. A method where multiple mages recite the same incantation
to amplify a spell’s output. Its potency increases with the number of
participants. Naturally, the amount of energy gathered from nearly forty
skeletons was staggering.

The sphere of lightning was launched toward Shatia. Its movement was
slow. It was possible to dodge it. However, it would be dangerous to
leave such a thing alone, not knowing where it might land. In the worst
case, the damage might even reach the village. Reaching that conclusion,
Shatia constructed a powerful barrier and intercepted the lightning
sphere head-on.

“Guh…!?”

The moment her barrier collided with the lightning sphere, a tremendous
burden pressed down upon Shatia’s body. Its power was immense,
unsurprising for a spell possessing the mana of forty individuals.
Shatia began to be pushed back, her heels digging into the ground. The
barrier could be heard creaking and cracking as its structural integrity
weakened. Yet, Shatia endured. She began to compress the sphere, intent
on neutralizing it.

“…Hup!”

Enveloping the mass of mana with even greater amounts of her own, Shatia
slowly erased the internal energy comprising the spell. Deprived of its
power source, the lightning sphere grew smaller and smaller until it
finally vanished with a pop. Confirming this, Shatia slumped in
exhaustion and let out a small sigh.

“*Phew*… You certainly pulled off something interesting.”

“Gigigiggi…!?”

As expected, the Sorcerer Skeletons seemed to have depleted their mana,
as they made no move to follow up. Rather, they appeared terribly
flustered that even their most powerful strike had been neutralized.
Shatia slowly opened her palm, manifesting a small sphere of mana.

“Now, it is my turn.”

With a smirk, Shatia channeled even more mana into the sphere,
transforming it into an energy mass even larger than the lightning ball
she had just received. Swinging her arm down, she hurled it straight
toward the group of skeletons.

The skeletons in turn each attempted to form barriers to protect
themselves, but the overwhelming pulse of mana swept their barriers away
in an instant, and the Sorcerer Skeletons were blown away along with
them.

“I would be pleased if you would finally stay annihilated… Ah, I suppose
that was too much to hope for. You will not admit defeat so easily, will
you?”

The surrounding rocks and gravel had been vaporized, leaving a small
crater. However, Sorcerer Skeletons began swarming once again from
between the trees and bushes, and soon a horde of skeletons stood before
Shatia just as before.

Shatia sighed wearily at the sight. But then, suddenly, the Sorcerer
Skeletons parted, and a single, massive skeleton emerged from the
center.

“Hoh… the Skeleton Lord makes its appearance.”

“Gwwoooh…”

Unlike the others, this skeleton had blackened bones and armor that
radiated a dark, oppressing aura. It carried a staff embedded with what
looked like a monster’s eyeball. A Skeleton Lord—a special class of
skeleton that occupies a leadership position. Shatia narrowed her eyes
at the sight.

“It does not seem… that you are the one giving the orders. Are you
merely providing temporary leadership for the pack?”

Shatia’s initial assumption was that the Skeleton Lord was the
mastermind, but she didn’t believe a monster like a Skeleton Lord was
capable of issuing such complex commands. She concluded that it was
likely to also be a puppet. Once she organized her thoughts, a knowing
smile played across her lips.

“Which means, if I deal with you, I can reach the one behind all of
this.”

“GUOOOOOOOOOOO!!!”

Hearing her declaration, the Skeleton Lord let out a roar and raised its
staff. The eyeball embedded within rolled around and emitted an eerie
purple light. A sharp pulse of mana was fired toward the sky. Shatia’s
posture wavered slightly upon impact, but not enough to make her fall.
She immediately recovered and swung her arm in kind to release a pulse
of her own.

“Hmph… Quite a greeting!”

The Skeleton Lord blocked the incoming attack simply by waving its hand.
Shatia let out a breath of admiration at the sight.

The mana it possessed was clearly on a different level than the Sorcerer
Skeletons. Though most notable was the staff it held. Shatia deduced it
was a special staff imbued with some kind of enchantment, used to
bolster its spell output. *In that case, it’s necessary to target the
staff first,* she thought, but the Skeleton Lord moved first.

“GUOAAAAAAAAAAAAAA!!”

With that piercing roar, the eyeball on the Skeleton Lord’s staff turned
bloodshot and emitted a dazzling flash. Countless purple lightning bolts
surged toward Shatia. She formed a barrier instantly, but one strike
managed to graze her shoulder. Fortunately, it only tore her clothes,
but Shatia bit her lip slightly at the sight.

“Good grief, this is a precious outfit I received from Mother… I shall
be scolded later if I bring it home in tatters.”

For Shatia, who feared being scolded by her mother more than being
injured, damage to her clothes was her greatest concern. Magic couldn’t
restore the fabric; if it were ruined, the loss would be permanent.

“This will cost you dearly,” Shatia vowed while holding her torn
shoulder. She increased the distance between herself and the Skeleton
Lord.

“GUOOOOOOOOOOOOOO!!”

“Ah, no need for concern. I shall collect the compensation from your
leader.”

Shatia raised her free hand and formed three orbs of water. Floating
them around her, she closed in on the Skeleton Lord and launched the
first sphere. The Skeleton Lord batted it away with its staff and began
chanting a spell to fire lightning back. However, Shatia blocked it with
the second water sphere and slammed the final one into the Skeleton
Lord.

“Truth be told, fire magic would be more effective, but it would be
troublesome if the forest burned down,” Shatia muttered as she watched
the Skeleton Lord get blown back into the rocks by the impact.

Without pause, she swung her hand again, and the orb that had driven
into the Skeleton Lord burst apart. The scattered water soaked into the
surrounding ground.

“Now, I shall teach you something interesting. Regarding the three orbs
of water I launched just now… they were imbued with a special magic.”

Shatia landed on the ground and turned her gaze to the surrounding
Sorcerer Skeletons. They clutched their staves and were slowly closing
in, surrounding her so she couldn’t escape. Despite this, Shatia made no
move to flee and instead began an out of place explanation.

“Water magic has a particularly high degree of freedom among the many
magics…. Its greatest merit is that the water can be freely manipulated
even after the spell has been cast.”

Stretching out her hand, Shatia began to explain water magic as if she
were giving a lecture. The Sorcerer Skeletons were puzzled by this
nonsensical behavior but ignored it, channeling mana into their staves
to finish their enemy.

Shortly after, the Skeleton Lord, which had been thrown against the
rocks, regained its footing and joined the encirclement, raising its
staff.

“The orbs of water from earlier burst and soaked into the ground. Now,
does anyone here understand what that means? I shall warn you
beforehand—watch your step.”

“!!?”

The only one to realize was the Skeleton Lord. It immediately lowered
its staff and leaped away, taking refuge atop a rock. In the next
instant, water in the shape of sharp blades erupted from the ground,
skewering the skeletons and creating a forest of needles.

“Giaaaaaa… Aaa…!?”

“Oh dear, so only the Lord noticed? Impressive. However, a word of
caution for you as well. The scattered water is also clinging to your
armor.”

“GUO!!?”

With that, Shatia flicked her finger lightly. At that moment, the water
on the Skeleton Lord’s armor turned into blades, piercing it. It let out
a groan and collapsed on the spot.

The skeletons skewered by the field of needles were also screaming in
agony. They shouldn’t feel pain, but between the impalement and the
damage to their bones, they were likely unable to maintain their forms.
Shatia wove her way through the gruesome scene of skewered skeletons and
approached the Skeleton Lord lying upon the rocks.

“*Phew*… Finally, we can have a proper conversation. Well, I do not know
if I can communicate with a skeleton, but for now, you…”

“Gugu… u…”

Seeing that no more Sorcerer Skeletons were appearing, Shatia was
certain the battle was over. She hopped onto the rock and looked down at
the Skeleton Lord. She looked refreshed, as if she had just found an
excellent experimental subject.

Showing the smile typical of a young girl, Shatia opened her mouth. “Why
don’t you guide me to your leader? Surely a Lord is capable of that
much.”

Having completely lost its chance at victory, the Skeleton Lord could
only nod weakly. The world of monsters is one of survival of the
fittest; the weak are devoured by the strong. This time, Shatia was the
stronger of the two.
