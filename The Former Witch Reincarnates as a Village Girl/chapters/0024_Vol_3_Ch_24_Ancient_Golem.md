# Chapter 24 – Ancient Golem

Shatia kicked off the ground, using flight magic to slip through the
gaps between the approaching golems and move toward the ceiling. Once
she distanced herself enough so as to be out of reach, she held out her
arm, and countless orbs of mana rained down like a storm.

“I have no time to deal with you. Begone.”

One after another, the golems were pierced by the spheres, ceasing to
function and crumbling to the floor. After confirming their defeat,
Shatia left the area and began running to find Laika and the others.

The students of the academy had been exploring various floors during
their free time. Everyone likely wasn’t in the same place when the
golems went berserk. There was a possibility that Laika and her group
hadn’t managed to regroup with Regis. Feeling uneasy about the
situation, Shatia pushed through the crowds and headed toward the floor
where Leo had mentioned the lecture was being held.

“Kyaaaaaaa!!”

When she reached the floor, she heard Laika’s scream from above. Looking
up, she saw Laika cornered by a golem halfway up the stairs leading to
the second floor. She reacted instantly, firing an arrow of mana. The
arrow struck the golem’s arm and shattered, transforming into a web of
mana that bound it. After blowing the immobilized golem away with a mana
pulse, Shatia landed beside Laika.

“Are you alright, Laika?”

“Shatia-chan…!”

Shatia checked to see if she was injured. Her uniform was a bit
disheveled, but fortunately that was all. There didn’t seem to be any
major injuries. Laika also looked relieved that Shatia had come for her,
her expression softening as tears welled slightly in her eyes.

“What of the other students? What is Professor Regis doing?” Shatia
asked, trying to get a grasp of the situation.

“Professor Regis immediately took everyone and evacuated when the golems
started going berserk… but I got separated along the way…” Laika
explained while her shoulders trembled slightly.

It seemed the other students had already finished evacuating, but Laika
alone had been attacked by a golem and separated from the group. Shatia
was just glad Laika was safe and considered their next move.

Ensuring Laika’s safety was the top priority. Since the other visitors
had already evacuated, as long as she got Laika outside, everything
should be fine. Shatia reached that conclusion while resting a hand on
her chin, then spoke as she turned her back to Laika, “Come with me,
then. I shall take you to a safe place.”

Laika nodded vigorously and pressed herself close to Shatia’s side.

The two began running toward the exit. However, on their way out of the
floor, several golems got in their way. They stood there, blocking the
escape route, staring down Shatia and Laika with ominous glowing red
eyes. Shatia clicked her tongue and tried to head for another exit to
avoid an unnecessary fight, but more golems were positioned there as
well. Before they knew it, the entire floor was surrounded.

“These things… do they actually intend to trap us?” Shatia questioned,
tilting her head. Of course, there was no way she would get an answer.
Instead, Laika, seemingly terrified by being surrounded, hid behind
Shatia’s back.

Golems were beings typically only capable of moving in set patterns.
They shouldn’t be able to do something so sophisticated. There had to be
some kind of purpose—something to be gained by trapping them. As she
thought this, Shatia detected a powerful mana coming from the second
floor.

Sure enough, standing there was a massive golem, visibly different in
size from the others. Unlike the other golems guarding the royal palace,
it wore rusted, thorny armor, with severed chains dangling from various
parts of its body. And in its thick arm, it gripped a sword even larger
than itself.

“An ancient golem… Humans, you actually had something like this sealed
away?”

Shatia identified the unusual appearance and let out a weary sigh.

Ancient golems were naturally-born magical creatures that lived in the
wild about five hundred years ago. Not only was their size immense, but
their destructive power was also unfathomable. Their numbers had
dwindled significantly after a certain point in time. Shatia never
imagined a surviving golem still existed. She surmised that humans had
likely kept it sealed. The boundlessness of human greed left Shatia with
her shoulders slumped.

“What a messy situation. If that thing reaches the city, the royal
capital will be laid to waste.” 

The ancient golem was currently being controlled by someone. Since it
was mostly in a berserk state, it would likely engage in indiscriminate
destruction. The scale of it wouldn’t be limited to the palace; it would
destroy the city as well.

“What!?” Laika cried out in surprise, “Then we have to stop it
somehow…!!”

Shatia let out a small chuckle upon hearing her naive outburst. “Come
now, how do you expect children like us to stop that thing?”

“B-but… the palace soldiers have their hands full with the other golems.
If we can hold it back even a little…” Laika argued.

“I’m telling you, its combat power is hundreds of times greater than
that of the average monster… Do you still intend to go through with
this?” Shatia simply stated the facts, causing Laika’s face to cloud
slightly.

Anyone with common sense could see it. No matter if they were students
of the magic academy, they stood no chance against an ancient golem,
even if they could handle standard ones. Even stalling it would be
difficult. The sound decision would be to run. Even Shatia considered
fleeing because it was such a bother. But for some reason, Laika—whose
power should be far below Shatia’s—chose a different option.

“I’ll do it. Because if I don’t, the city will be in terrible trouble,
right? So I’ll do my best to hold it back, even just a little.”

Laika gazed at Shatia, unwavering. There was no hesitation in her voice.
If it were the old Laika, she would have been terrified and suggested
they run away immediately. But she had changed. Even knowing it was
reckless, she had developed a positive personality—one that pushed her
to do her absolute best with what she could accomplish in the moment,
even while knowing it was reckless.

Though pleased by that change, Shatia let out a “hmmm” and tilted her
head. It was a completely foolish choice. There was nothing for her to
gain by challenging it. Why do humans expend such wasted effort? She
couldn’t understand. But that was what made humans interesting. Shatia
let a smirk play across her lips. It didn’t change the fact that Laika
was being reckless. However, Shatia knew that she could turn that
foolish act into something courageous.

“Very well. Then I shall do what I can as well.”

“Shatia-chan…!” Hearing her response, Laika looked happy. She had gained
a powerful ally.

“Though I was planning to stop it anyway,” Shatia followed up softly.

And as if it had been waiting for the two of them to resolve themselves,
the ancient golem began to move. It kicked off the ground and leapt down
to the first floor where Shatia and Laika were. They immediately braced
themselves. As it landed, a heavy sound echoed throughout the area, and
a shockwave surged.

“GGGOOOOOAAAAARRRRHHH!!!”

The helmet portion of the ancient golem split open, revealing a
mouth-like shape as a roar resounded. Though raspy and metallic, it was
more than effective for intimidation. Shatia steeled herself, silently
recognizing that this wouldn’t be easy. Fortunately, the surrounding
golems were only blocking the paths and showed no signs of attacking.
She narrowed her eyes, thinking they might still be able to manage.

“You’re noisy, doll. Unfortunately, I am not very good at playing with
dolls. I tend to break them by accident, you see,” Shatia said lightly
while preparing her magic as the ancient golem swung its sword with a
massive, rattling sound.

Her typical basic mana attacks wouldn’t be effective. Golems were known
for their sturdiness, and this was an ancient golem at that. Even if its
armor was rusted, its defensive power hadn’t diminished. Shatia decided
she first needed to damage the armor itself and generated a massive
spear of mana.

“…Pierce it!”

“GOOOOOO!!”

The spear Shatia launched clashed with the greatsword swung by the
golem. Sparks flew, and a tremendous shockwave scattered. Then, with a
dull sound, cracks appeared in the mana spear, and it was slammed down
onto the floor. The ancient golem rested the tip of its sword on the
ground and let out a low growl.

“What terrifying physical strength… Laika, set up a dirt wall.”

“Y-Yes.”

Shatia calmly analyzed the ancient golem’s strength while giving
instructions to Laika beside her. Then she began to run, closing the
distance before firing a highly focused mana blast. A blinding light was
released, concentrated on a single point. However, the golem simply held
up its greatsword and used it to deflect the trajectory of the beam.

Shatia used flight magic to rise into the air, attempting an attack from
above this time. She formed countless mana spears and fired them from
every direction. Seeming to understand that it couldn’t parry them all,
the ancient golem began to move to evade. But immediately after, a wall
of dirt appeared at its feet.

“Good decision, Laika.”

With a smirk, Shatia looked down at Laika. She had her hands pressed to
the ground, generating a dirt wall from a distance. Thanks to that, the
ancient golem’s reaction was delayed ever so slightly as its leg caught
on the wall. A moment later, countless mana spears pierced its armor. A
dull screech, like iron scraping against iron, reverberated through the
hall.

“GGAAAAARRRRHHH!!”

“Still enduring? My, golems really are frighteningly sturdy.”

The damage seemed to be getting through. However, despite being pierced
by so many spears, the ancient golem didn’t fall. Once the rain of
spears stopped, it quietly regained its stance. Shatia also descended
back to Laika and landed on the floor.

“What are we gonna do, Shatia-chan? Nothing’s working!” Laika spoke
anxiously.

Shatia, however, remained somewhat detached, as if everything was going
as planned. She stood there with her arms crossed, looking up at the
ancient golem. “Hmm, well, I expected as much. Now then, what trick
should I use…”

It was impossible to destroy the armor even with spears containing
increased piercing power. The mana blast had been parried, and there was
no way to make it drop the greatsword. The only option left was to
attempt internal destruction. She couldn’t use Lullaby… that was a last
resort. Something more physical and simple would be ideal.

“I intend to go for the joints of the armor. We shall seal its
movements,” she briefly stated her objective before taking flight again.

“Eh… what do you… Ah, Shatia-chan!!” A confused Laika called out and
reached toward her, but Shatia was already closing in above the ancient
golem’s head.

This time, she formed thin mana blades instead of spears. She placed
them on her palm and launched them with a burst of force. The blades
pierced into the gaps of the armor, and the ancient golem let out a
deep, thick cry.

“G-GGGOOO!!!”

“Is it working? Then let me give you some more presents.”

Seeing its reaction, Shatia judged it to be effective and formed even
more blades. She launched them all at once, piercing every gap in the
armor. The blades that pierced the leg joints extended further, pinning
it to the floor. Completely immobilized, the ancient golem finally
dropped its greatsword in its desperate struggle. However, the more it
moved, the more its body was sliced by the blades, and eventually, it
stopped resisting.

“One more for good measure.”

Finally, as a precaution, Shatia formed one massive blade. It pierced
into the gap of the ancient golem’s helmet; its head tilted forward with
a jerk, and the golem slumped over.

It hadn’t ceased functioning, but after being skewered like this, it
shouldn’t be able to move. Having reached that conclusion, Shatia let
out a small sigh.

“It stopped…? We did it… We did it, Shatia-chan! That was amazing!!”
Seeing that the ancient golem had stopped moving, Laika seemingly
thought they had won and began jumping for joy despite still being
surrounded by standard golems.

“Hmm, that went surprisingly well… Now, let us find an escape route
while we—” Shatia landed next to Laika and was about to take her away to
escape immediately. But at that moment, she noticed something strange.
The golems that had been blocking the paths were starting to gather
around the ancient golem.

At first, Shatia thought the golems were trying to remove the blades
that were binding it. She quickly formed a spear, but then realized that
wasn’t it. For some reason, the golems were overlapping and sticking to
the ancient golem. Some were climbing up to cling to its back and
shoulders. And when the ancient golem lifted its face, it bit into and
crushed one of the golems that had come near.

“Cannibalism…!?”

It chewed it along with its armor, swallowed whole. It was a clearly
abnormal sight. Terrified, Laika grabbed Shatia’s arm and stepped back.
The eeriness likewise made Shatia more alert. In the next instant, the
ancient golem’s rusted armor began to peel off, and the golems clinging
to it began to merge. They unified, shifting fluidly before settling
into a single set of armor. There, the ancient golem became clad in
shining gold.

“This… is it fusion?” Shatia murmured as she looked upon the golem,
which now shone brilliantly like an angel.

It was likely some kind of fusion, one of the functions golems
possessed. A simple means of gaining powerful strength by unifying
individuals. In short, a power-up. *This has become troublesome*, Shatia
thought, biting her lip.

“GGGOOOOOAAAAARRRRHHH!!!”

Immediately after, the ancient golem let out a roar and blew away all
the blades that had been piercing it. Furthermore, it aimed its arm at
the ceiling and began firing pulses of mana, indiscriminately destroying
the palace.

With her full attention on the transformation, Shatia was slow to react.
She failed to notice the pieces of ceiling crumbling down; debris was
plummeting toward her from directly above.

“Watch out, Shatia-chan!!” Noticing this, Laika shouted and struck the
ground, forming a dirt wall. The wall covered Shatia’s head,
successfully blocking the debris. But in exchange, Laika’s figure was
swallowed by the avalanche of rubble.
