# Side Story 1: Childhood Friend

—At a time when peace reigned, when Shatia was still living in the
village. Having only recently regained her memories as a witch, Shatia
spent her days practicing magic.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
As it was dangerous to do so inside the house, Shatia always practiced
in the meadow where she used to play with her childhood friend, Moffy.
In truth, she had intended to practice alone, but unfortunately, Moffy
caught her as she was leaving, and the two ended up going together.

Upon reaching the meadow, Shatia began her usual light magic practice.
She started with spells that anyone could perform with a bit of
training—making pebbles levitate or causing flowers to bloom. But even
that seemed utterly fantastical to Moffy; she stared at Shatia with her
round eyes sparkling.

“Amazing, amazing! Shatia can use so much magic!”

“Anyone can do this much with practice… provided they are a mage, that
is.”

No matter how much praise Moffy lavished her with, it was a trivial
matter to Shatia. Rather, she was frustrated with herself for being able
to demonstrate only this much power. Since she had only recently
regained her memories from her time as a witch, her mind was still in a
state of confusion. When one’s imagination does not function perfectly,
it hinders the ability to visualize magic. Shatia clenched her hand,
pouring mana into it.

“Must be nice… I wish I could use magic too.”

Unaware of Shatia’s inner thoughts, Moffy watched the magic practice
from behind with an envious expression. Since it wasn’t a performance,
Shatia would have preferred for Moffy to go home, but she couldn’t speak
harshly to a child. Furthermore, if for nothing else but the sake of
appearances, she couldn’t simply brush off her only friend in the
village.

“Perhaps even you could do it if you practiced, Moffy,” Shatia murmured
those alluring words while glancing at Moffy out of the corner of her
eye. 

“Eh, really!?” Immediately, Moffy jumped up and began swinging her arms
up and down excitedly, begging her to say more.

However, Shatia just chuckled lightly, covering her mouth with her hand
as her shoulders shook with a hint of teasing. She began counting off
her fingers, “Well, it would likely be impossible unless you first
listen to what adults tell you, stop being a picky eater, and apply
yourself to your studies.”

“Ugh, Shatia, you sound just like mom!” Moffy covered her ears and
gnashed her teeth in annoyance. Since there were many points that hit
close to home, it was a painful subject for her.

Afterward, Shatia continued her magic practice with quiet focus, and a
peaceful time passed as Moffy watched. Occasionally, Moffy would grow
bored and chase butterflies flying through the field or wander off on
her own to pick flowers, causing Shatia some worry, but ultimately the
time passed without incident.

By the time Shatia finally finished, Moffy had fallen asleep lying in
the grass. With a resigned sigh, Shatia hoisted the girl onto her back.
While she could have used magic to make her float for transport, she
felt like carrying her home instead. Moffy’s soft breathing tickled her
ear. Occasionally, the girl would snuggle closer as if seeking comfort,
which gave Shatia a strangely ticklish sensation in her heart.

After delivering Moffy to her home, Shatia returned to her own house.
Her mother had just finished the housework and was taking a moment to
rest, having removed her apron and poured some tea.

“Welcome back, Shatia. Were you playing with Moffy-chan?”

“Yes. I intended to practice my magic as usual, but she followed me of
her own accord…”

“Fufufu, that just shows how much she likes you, Shatia.”

Shatia conversed with her mother in a tone that was quite un-childlike.
It wasn’t that she held complex feelings toward her mother; rather,
Shatia spoke like this with everyone ever since her memories returned.
Those around her simply assumed she was “that kind of child” and
accepted it.

“Still, I was quite surprised that you could use magic. I wonder if you
got it from your dad’s side?”

It was only recently that Shatia had confessed she could use magic. At
first, she had considered hiding it, but she realized it would
eventually be discovered by the villagers since everyone lived so close
together, so she chose to tell her mother first. As a result, she wasn’t
treated with much suspicion; her mother simply assumed it was inherited
from her father and didn’t think much deeper on the matter.

“Who knows…” As she trailed off, Shatia recalled her father. She
questioned her mother, “Speaking of which, is Father away for work
again?”

“That’s right. He should be heading toward the city about now,” her
mother replied with a smile while resting a hand against her cheek.

Because Shatia’s father usually worked in the city, he was rarely home.
Even after Shatia gained awareness of her surroundings, she had only met
him a handful of times. Consequently, she didn’t truly know what kind of
person her father was, nor did she know the nature of his work. However,
judging by her mother’s reaction, he seemed to be someone she could
trust. Shatia sat in a chair with a complex expression.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
When dinner time arrived, Shatia was eating with her mother as usual.
Suddenly, her mother paused, speaking as if she had just remembered
something, “Oh right, it’s almost beast season, so you can go as far as
the usual meadow, but you mustn’t wander outside the village.”

“Beast season” was a dangerous period when animals and monsters appeared
at high frequency and became aggressive enough to harm even passing
travelers. In short, it was simply a warning to be careful because many
monsters would appear. Occasionally, village youths would rush out to
test their strength. Though no one died, it would still often result in
injuries.

Shatia let out a small “ah” of acknowledgement, tore off a piece of
bread, chewed it a few times, and swallowed. She asked, “That time
already… Does that mean the adults will be heading out again?”

“Yes, that’s right. I heard that this year, Moffy-chan’s father and
Uncle Rob’s second son will be going,” her mother answered while tapping
a finger to her head as she recalled the details. She was a bit ditzy
and child-like in that regard.

During beast season, it was custom that several adults must protect the
village. While the monsters usually didn’t try to leave the forest
despite their aggression, a few would occasionally wander out. At those
times, the villagers—who possessed no weapons—would have no way to fight
back. To prevent this, the adults would build a barricade at a location
a slight distance away from the village. They would make their stand
there to ensure no monsters came near.

It was a simple task. From Shatia’s perspective, she could solve the
issue by simply casting a barrier spell, but she couldn’t afford to
reveal that level of magic. She sipped her soup while feigning the
innocent behavior of a child to ensure nothing seemed amiss.

“…Moffy’s father, then,” Shatia murmured softly while clearing away the
dishes after dinner.

Since Moffy’s father was an ordinary villager, he didn’t possess great
power. However, he met the physical standards of an adult, so there
shouldn’t have been a need for great concern. Yet the source of her
anxiety wasn’t him, but Moffy. Knowing her, she was likely crying and
clinging to her father out of worry.

The construction of the barricade was scheduled to begin tomorrow, ahead
of plan. Apparently, monsters had already been spotted in the vicinity.
Even after hearing this news from her mother, Shatia didn’t offer much
of a reaction, she simply nodded and returned to her room. She took one
of the books from her shelf, making it levitate as she flipped through
the pages. Her mind wouldn’t focus. Feeling a slight irritation, Shatia
couldn’t understand why she was harboring such restlessness.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
The next day, Shatia walked through the village, notably without
encountering Moffy. Today was a day of rest from practice. Since she now
had the body of a child, overexerting herself carried the risk of being
consumed by mana and dying. Moderation in all things was important.

She eventually reached the village chief’s house and, having nothing
better to do, decided to pay him a visit.

“Good morning, Village Chief-dono.”

“Oh, Shatia. Early as always, I see. Is something the matter today?”

“Nothing in particular. I simply found myself with some time to spare.”

Even though Shatia had arrived unannounced, the village chief didn’t
show the slightest sign of annoyance and happily invited her inside. He
prepared tea and sweets to serve her. Shatia offered her thanks and
gratefully ate them.

“My, my. Between you suddenly coming over and Moffy waking up early for
once… it is a strange day indeed.”

Shatia’s hand stopped dead at the words the village Chief muttered while
sipping his tea. Hearing something she couldn’t simply let alone, she
doubted her ears for a moment and snapped her head up to look at the
Village chief.

“Village Chief… what did you just say?”

“Hm? Oh, I just saw Moffy running past the house around dawn… I just
thought it was a rare sight, is all.”

The village chief didn’t seem to think much of it, but to Shatia, those
words were highly problematic. The chief likely assumed Moffy was just
playing. But Shatia knew better. Moffy never played alone. She always
played with *someone*—either her father or Shatia herself. And since she
wasn’t with Shatia today, that meant… Shatia stood up abruptly.

After giving a quick thanks to the village chief, Shatia bolted outside
and hurried toward the village outskirts. Fortunately, the villagers
hadn’t come out in force yet. With no one to spot her, she passed
through the gate and used flight magic to head toward the forest.

“That girl Moffy… she couldn’t possibly…!”

As she flew, Shatia bit her lip. Perhaps she was overthinking it. Maybe
Moffy really had just woken up early to get some exercise. She couldn’t
imagine such a scenario, but perhaps by some whim she had decided to
lead a healthier lifestyle. But Shatia’s intuition was sharpened only
toward the worst possibilities.

What if Moffy had headed into the forest out of concern for her father?
Even if her father and the others were at the barricade, the path there
wasn’t necessarily safe. In the first place, it was possible to get lost
in the woods. It was far too dangerous for Moffy to go alone.

Shatia continued to fly straight through the forest. It wasn’t that
great a distance. Since the goal was to block the path leading to the
village with a barricade, there would be no point in it being too far
away.

Soon, Shatia spotted Moffy’s father and another adult building the
barricade. Moffy wasn’t with them. For a moment, she let out a sigh of
relief, thinking she had overreacted, but suddenly a small cry reached
her ears.

“…!”

It was a truly faint scream. A barely audible sound, like the whistling
of the wind. It seemed the adults hadn’t heard it, as they continued to
carry timber and build the barricade with practiced indifference.

Shatia immediately changed direction, heading deeper into the forest
toward the source of the sound. She had a terrible premonition.
Simultaneously, she felt her heart beginning to race. Why was she so
nervous? She couldn’t understand.

When she finally reached the source of the sound, she found Moffy being
attacked by a giant spider.

“H-help mee! Daddyyy!!”

“Moffy!”

Moffy’s legs were caught in the spider’s silk, leaving her unable to
move. Shatia immediately descended, landing between Moffy and the
spider. Raising her arm, she unleashed a massive pulse of mana. The
giant spider was sent flying through the air, its numerous legs
flailing, until it slammed into a tree.

“Are you all right, Moffy!?”

“Sh… Shatia…?”

Rushing to the fallen Moffy, Shatia checked her condition. Fortunately,
she wasn’t injured, only restrained by the silk. Shatia formed a blade
of mana at her fingertip and sliced through the threads. But despite
being able to move, Moffy was still trembling with fear; she widened her
eyes and pointed behind Shatia.

Immediately after, spider silk stuck to Shatia’s arm. She felt a pulling
sensation and a feeling as if the strength were being drained from her
limb. It was likely this special silk that had rendered Moffy immobile.
However, Shatia simply channeled mana into her arm and released a small
amount of mana to blow the silk away.

“How strange… I am usually quite kind to monsters… but for some reason,
I am feeling very angry right now,” As she stood up slowly, Shatia spoke
to the giant spider with her back still turned toward Moffy.

It was rare for Shatia to feel the emotion of anger. She typically
viewed most things objectively and held only detached impressions of
most incidents, but for some reason, she was feeling a fierce anger this
time. While bewildered by the emotion welling up from her chest, Shatia
clenched her fists.

“Begone. Never show your face before me again,” she said with her eyes
sharpened menacingly, causing the giant spider to rapidly back away. Its
many eyes darted around as if it were feeling terror, and eventually it
curled its body and fled into the depths of the forest.

Once the enemy had vanished, Moffy fainted, perhaps because her tension
had finally been relieved. Shatia rushed over and gently stroked her
cheek.

Why had she been so angry? Until a moment ago, she had been consumed by
the urge to tear that giant spider apart. For a monster-lover like
Shatia, that should have been the most impossible of emotions.

Hoisting Moffy onto her back, Shatia began the walk back to the village.
Along the way, hearing Moffy’s soft breathing in her ear, Shatia’s
expression suddenly changed to one of realization.

“Ah, I see… I truly do care for Moffy.”

Shatia turned her head slightly to look at Moffy’s sleeping face. A very
small girl. To her former self as a witch, she would have been an
insignificant existence. Back then, she could never have built a
friendship with such a being. Perhaps that was why she had come to
treasure her so much. Without realizing it, her childhood friend Moffy
had formed a place in her heart.

“Perhaps I am just as simple as you are, after all… Moffy,” she
murmured.

“Nn… mmgh…” Moffy let out a small sound, though whether it was a reply
was anyone’s guess.

Shatia chuckled and continued her walk.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
After returning to the village, Shatia delivered Moffy to her mother.
She spouted an excuse, saying they had been playing in the meadow as
usual and Moffy had fallen asleep, then Shatia returned to her own home.
Her footsteps felt somewhat lighter than usual.
