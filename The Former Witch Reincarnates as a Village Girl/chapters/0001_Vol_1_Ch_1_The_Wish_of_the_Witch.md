# Chapter 1 – The Wish of the Witch

**Part 1: Village Life**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Shatifahl the witch collapsed on the spot, coughing up blood.

Under a black pointed hat and long, disheveled silver hair, a beautiful
woman calmly looked up at the man standing before her.

“I ask of you, O Hero… why must we Witches be hunted?”

“Because your kind are evil existences. Possessing heretical powers and
belonging to no nation, you are nothing but a threat to the people.”

The man known as the Hero answered Shatifahl indifferently while
pressing his blade against her throat. Offering no resistance, Shatifahl
quietly listened. With a faint smile playing on her lips, she even
appeared somewhat enlightened.

“Then, by what criteria did you deem us evil? Has a witch attacked a
human city? Has a witch joined hands with a demon? For what sins must we
pay for?”

“…………”

To this, the Hero could not answer. His eyes wavered, and the tip of his
sword trembled slightly. Observing this, Shatifahl continued.

“Humans have deemed us evil simply because we possess unknown power. If
that is the case, what of yourself, Hero? Is that power of yours not
also unknown?”

“Silence! Unlike your kind, I am trusted by the people. It is because
*I* am their hope!”

“That is but a fleeting trust. When you have finished hunting every last
witch… what do you think the people will come to fear next?”

Shatifahl smiled as the Hero’s eyes shook violently. As if the path he
had walked until now had suddenly begun to crumble, his body staggered,
and he lowered his sword. Shatifahl merely watched, her eyes narrowed
calmly and coldly.

“Realize this, Hero. Have you seen this world with your own eyes, walked
it with your own feet? Were the witches you’ve seen truly evil
existences? Were those you have cut down until now truly rebels? Are
your comrades, who are not here now, truly people worthy of your trust?”

The Hero’s companions were not here; they had opted not to storm the
witch’s mansion, offering instead to keep watch outside. The Hero had
agreed to this and had charged in alone. At first, the Hero thought this
was a strategy—that his allies were standing guard to ensure no outside
enemies intruded. But what if that wasn’t the case?

“I… I…”

“…I must now leave this earth. My only wish… is that you would… erect
graves… for my kin whom you have laid to rest… until now……”

Shatifahl steadfastly gazed at the Hero, his trembling hands revealing
his inner turmoil, and made her final request while clutching her
pierced abdomen. Then, with a sudden drop of her head, she quietly
breathed her last.

\*\*\*

Thus, Shatifahl, the Witch of Wisdom and the last of the Seven Witches,
met her end.

Having finished hunting all the witches and fulfilled his duty, the Hero
was praised by the people as a champion. He was supposed to receive his
promised reward and enjoy a life in the royal capital… but for some
reason, one day he suddenly vanished without a word.

Why did the Hero disappear? For a time, the capital was abuzz with
rumors and he became famous as the Vanished Hero. Only the Hero himself
knew the truth. And so, the history of the Hero’s fierce battles came to
a temporary close.

\*\*\*

And then, five years passed. Memories of the witch hunts and the
vanished Hero began to fade little by little, and the world’s clock
ticked on in peace.

Far from the royal capital existed a village deep in the mountains. It
was an isolated village that travelers rarely visited. The villagers
lived quietly, honoring their own unique customs so as not to draw the
attention of witches or demons.

In that village lived a peculiar girl. She was a lovely girl with silver
hair tied back and a sharp gaze. Because she usually wore a stern
expression, people often misunderstood and thought she was angry. Her
name was Shatia.

“Shatia. It’s getting late, come inside now.”

“Understood. Mother.”

As the sky began to be dyed a sunset red and Shatia’s mother called out
to her daughter, Shatia replied in a tone quite unbefitting a child.

In appearance, she was a perfectly ordinary girl. Skirts suited her, and
she wore a cute ribbon atop her head. However, her countenance was so
dignified that it was hard to believe she was a child, and her eyes were
as clear as if they could see through the very nature of all things.

“Please, Shatia… Haven’t I told you time and again to speak more like a
girl your age?”

“My apologies, Mother. I am simply more accustomed to this manner of
speaking.”

Shatia’s mother was always cautioning her daughter about her manner of
speech, but for some reason it never improved in the slightest. Usually,
such things change due to the influence of those around them, but
unfortunately, there was no one nearby who spoke in such eccentricities.
Shatia’s mother couldn’t begin to fathom where Shatia had learned to
speak in such a way.

“Goodness, I wonder who she takes after…” Shatia’s mother grumbled under
her breath while heading back inside.

Shatia followed after her, but her eyes suddenly caught a puddle by the
roadside. It must have collected during last night’s rain. Shatia peered
into it. Naturally, what was reflected there was her own form—the face
of a lovely young girl. But upon seeing it, Shatia’s mouth curved into a
small, amused smile.

“I must say, I pulled it off well. To be reincarnated as a human girl…
truly, the path of magic is an infinite frontier that one can never
finish exploring,” Shatia muttered while inspecting herself at various
angles.

Correction—she was a being that was not Shatia; she was the
reincarnation of the witch Shatifahl.

On the verge of death, she had activated a magic for reincarnation that
was still being researched, and found herself reborn as the daughter of
a certain human woman. Reincarnation magic was a forbidden art, and a
magic she hadn’t yet fully mastered at that, but Shatifahl had succeeded
brilliantly. And so, while retaining all her memories, power, and
magecraft, she was able to be reborn into a completely new living being.

“Be that as it may, to think the Hero disappeared… if he believed my
words, then all is well, but I wonder…”

However, even though she had been reincarnated, it wasn’t as if she had
retained her memories from infancy; Shatia had only recently regained
her memories as Shatifahl. Consequently, there was a gap, and she had
not yet been able to process the events that had occurred during these
past few years. Furthermore, she hadn’t fully adjusted to her new form
yet either.

The Hero had vanished. What intentions lay behind such an action? Shatia
placed a hand on her chin and pondered.

“Well, for now, I suppose I shall explore what can be done with this
body.”

She had gone to the trouble of being reincarnated into a young and pure
body. This was to say her mana was uninfluenced, and she was currently
in the most optimal period for growth. Although she had once mastered
all magic as the Witch of Wisdom, Shatia had grasped the chance to gain
experiences anew. There was no reason not to enjoy her situation.

The Witch of Wisdom. Once praised as perfect, she had obtained a new
body and once again descended upon the world. Where would her insatiable
curiosity lead her?

Shatia returned home with a faint smile on her face.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
The next day, while her mother was out, Shatia decided to practice her
magic immediately. No matter how much of her had been reincarnated, it
was impossible to bring forth the power of her former life while
possessing a child’s body. A check on how much she could currently exert
would be needed regardless, so Shatia decided to test some of her magic.

“Indeed… it would appear I can use most of the magic from my former
life.”

After levitating objects, starting fires, and reciting various other
spells, Shatia concluded that her magic was activating normally. And
that she could use all the magic she remembered without issue. Upon
confirming this, Shatia smiled happily.

In that case, next was further exploration. She wanted to learn magic
she didn’t know in her previous life. Specifically, the magic used by
humans. For that, Shatia needed to seek instruction. Because she had
previously been branded as a heretic, she was poorly versed in human
knowledge. Even the magic they used in their daily lives was
fundamentally different from a witch’s. Therefore, she desired knowledge
that could only be obtained through others.

“Mother. I want to study magic.”

“…Eh?”

With a demeanor unbefitting a child, Shatia stated her demand
immediately after her mother returned home.

Caught off guard, Shatia’s mother wore a wry, troubled smile. It was
perfectly natural for a child to be interested in magic. However, Shatia
was still very young. Typically, children only started learning magic
after turning six. Thus, she agonized over how to respond.

But precisely because Shatia was so precocious, it was hard to
definitively say no. Shatia had learned to speak quickly, and as soon as
she could walk, she had begun reading books as well. She was even
considered a brilliant child among the villagers. Because her mother was
so proud of such a daughter, she found herself wanting to grant her
wish. And after careful consideration, she decided to hire someone.

“Then let’s hire a tutor. It’s someone who used to be a court mage in
the royal capital. I’m sure they can teach you what you want to know.”

“That would be most welcome.”

A certain mage lived in this village. He had once been a mage of some
status in the royal capital, but for unknown reasons, he had come to
live in this remote village. Of course, the villagers were generous and
welcomed him warmly.

Shatia’s mother decided to go to him. If it was someone who had been a
mage in the capital, surely it would be good stimulation for Shatia.
That was the reasoning behind her decision. Shatia likewise surmised
that someone from the capital would have abundant knowledge and showed a
joyful expression. However, it wasn’t the pure smile of a child, but
rather a smile as if she were delighted that her own scheme had worked
out.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
A few days later, the tutor arrived at Shatia’s house.

He was a man who still retained some youth. He wore glasses and
possessed an intelligent face. However, he had stubble on his chin and a
somewhat down-and-out appearance. He looked every bit the part of a mage
with a complicated past. Nevertheless, his knowledge was certain. He was
highly valued by the villagers, to the point that he would solve any
problems in the village with magic. Shatia’s eyes sparkled as she gazed
upon him.

“Well then, I’ll be acting as your tutor starting today. Nice to meet
you, Shatia-chan.”

“Umu. I shall entrust my guidance to you.”

In contrast to the tutor’s polite greeting, Shatia maintained her usual
haughty attitude. However, the tutor just smiled dotingly as he
conveniently misinterpreted it as the shy reaction of an eager child.

The lessons began immediately. Mainly, the tutor would demonstrate
magic, and Shatia would recite the spell in the same way. A
straightforward cycle. Not long after starting, the tutor’s expression
turned grim.

Shatia’s learning ability was far too high.

Once the tutor showed a spell, Shatia would mimic it in no time at all.
Even though being able to use magic at this age should have been amazing
in itself, Shatia was absorbing magic reserved for court mages at an
unbelievable pace. Before he knew it, his hands were trembling. The
genius he had always yearned to teach was right before his eyes. It was
only natural for him to be shaken.

“Shatia-chan… you’ve never studied magic before now, right?”

“Hmm? Ah, umu. Well, certainly, I’ve not touched magic since I was
born.”

Since she couldn’t very well say she had studied it in her previous
life, Shatia deflected vaguely. However, it wasn’t a lie, and the tutor
became convinced that she was indeed a genius. In truth, Shatia *was* a
genius. It was for that reason that she was qualified to be a witch. She
wasn’t just a once-in-a-decade genius; she was a once-in-a-century
prodigy.

After the first day’s lesson ended—more specifically, after the tutor
had finished imparting all he knew—Shatia languidly asked, “Teacher. Is
what you showed me today the entirety of what you call ‘royal magic’?”

“Eh? A-Ah… yes, that’s right.” Not understanding the intent behind her
question, the tutor’s response came a beat late.

*As expected, the magic of humans and that of witches is fundamentally
different,* Shatia thought. Since they differed from their very
foundations, it wouldn’t be strange if there were many, many more
offshoots. Greedy as she was, Shatia desired more.

“Are there any other magics? Since you say ‘royal magic,’ I assume there
must be other types or variations.”

“Yes. Royal magic is a special magic studied by court mages. There is
also general magic used by commoners, ancient magic used by sages, and
various others.”

Just as Shatia had imagined, there were many branches of magic. The
magic used by witches was likely categorized under “Witch Magic” or
something similar. She was delighted to hear that there were still many
magics out there for her to learn.

Back when she was a witch, if she even tried to come into contact with
humans, it would inevitably end with stones being thrown at her. Even if
she wanted to know, she couldn’t; that was the state of things back
then. But now it was different. Now that she was human, she could learn
such magic properly. And how wonderful it was.

“Splendid. I certainly wish to thoroughly master those magics as well,”
Shatia muttered while placing a finger to her lips. But those words
reached the ears of her tutor, and a chill ran down his spine.

*Such an insatiable girl,* the mage thought to himself. To learn all of
royal magic in just one day, and yet, far from being satisfied, she was
already demanding even more. It was a feat that should have been
impossible for any person of ordinary mental fortitude. Yet, the girl
before him, far younger than himself, declared it so easily. He felt a
fear that transcended his previous amazement.

“Ma’am. Shatia-chan possesses truly magnificent talent. You should spare
no effort to send her to the Magic Academy in the capital.”

“Eh? That much? But Shatia’s only five, and…”

Immediately after the lesson ended, the tutor lost no time in conveying
Shatia’s brilliance to her mother. Partly due to his excitement, his
explanation was a bit difficult to follow as he used frantic hand
gestures; furthermore, since Shatia’s mother wasn’t particularly
knowledgeable about magic, the sheer scale of that brilliance couldn’t
properly sink in.

“In just one day, she was able to watch and mimic the royal magic I use…
a terrifying talent. I’m certain she will become a legendary sage who
will leave her name in history.”

Being told that much, even the dense mother finally understood how
incredible Shatia was. At the same time, she was struck with worry.
Would she truly be able to raise a daughter with such immense talent
correctly? Would it be better to send her to a more suitable
institution? She couldn’t help but harbor such doubts.

After that, because the tutor had taught everything he could, the
lessons came to an end on the very first day. Nevertheless, at Shatia’s
request, the lessons continued; she desired to broaden her horizons even
beyond the realm of magic.
<br />  
<br />
<br />
“Kukuku, as I suspected, the path of magic is indeed an amusement well
worth exploring,” Shatia monologued in her room after the tutor left.

She repeatedly clenched and opened her hands, confirming the feeling of
the magic she had used today. Then, with a certain sense of fulfillment,
she lay down on her bed with a satisfied look.
