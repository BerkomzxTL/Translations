# Chapter 16 – Farewell

In the end, persuading her mother to let her go to the capital went
surprisingly smoothly. Once it was decided that her tutor would
accompany her, Shatia’s mother felt at ease leaving it in his hands. The
real problem, however, turned out to be her childhood friend, Moffy.
Upon learning that Shatia would be leaving the village for a while,
Moffy clung to her and began to wail.

“No, no, nooo! I don’t wanna be away from Shatiaa!”

“…Moffy, I beg of you, let go. My clothes are getting sticky from your
tears and snot.”

As Moffy thrashed and cried, Shatia sighed helplessly and spoke while
stroking the girl’s head.

She had expected this, but it was still a situation where even Shatia
found herself at a loss. She could have simply left in secret, but
Shatia possessed enough of a conscience to feel that such a betrayal
would be heart-wrenching. Thus, she was searching for a way to leave the
village without leaving Moffy in a state of despair.

“It is not as if I will be gone from the village forever. I’m just going
to live in the royal capital for a mere six or seven years or so. I will
return once my business is finished.”

“Six or seven years is foreverrr! Even Uncle Rob will be dead by then!!”

Shatia tried to reason with her, but Moffy protested by thumping
Shatia’s chest with her small fists. Shatia suddenly remembered that
seven years was a long time for a human. Having been a witch who lived
for over a thousand years, she was somewhat out of touch with the
passage of time. In seven years, the child Moffy would be a young woman.
Thinking of it that way gave Shatia a complicated feeling. However, she
couldn’t not go. She absolutely had to master body creation magic to
revive Emerald. Not even her childhood friend could get in the way of
that resolve.

“Hmm… good grief, this is troublesome.”

At this rate, Moffy looked ready to squat in Shatia’s house and never
let go, even while sleeping. Shatia slumped her shoulders in exhaustion.

She couldn’t exactly resort to force. She had no choice but to provide a
reason that Moffy could accept. Shatia rested her chin on her hand in
thought, then her eyes widened as if struck by inspiration.

“Then let us do this. While I am in the capital, I shall leave Moffy
with some homework.”

“Homework…?” When Shatia said this to the weeping girl, Moffy’s ears
perked up and she lifted her head. Her face was a mess from crying.

Shatia wiped away the tears trickling down Moffy’s cheeks and continued,
“I’ve taught you bits and pieces when you’ve come to visit, have I not?
Magic. I shall specially teach you my magic, Moffy.”

With a small flick of her finger, the mess on Moffy’s face instantly
vanished. The tears and snot on her clothes disappeared as well, leaving
them looking brand new. Moffy blinked in surprise and looked at Shatia,
who offered a gentle smile and patted her head.

Shatia’s magic. In other words, the magic of the legendary figure known
as the Witch of Wisdom. A powerful magecraft capable of rending heaven
and earth. Even the other six witches found it difficult to grasp, and
few had ever mastered it perfectly. Naturally, Moffy couldn’t begin to
comprehend the weight of this, but to Shatia, it was the greatest gift
she could offer.

Shatia pulled a book from her shelf. It was a thick, black volume with
white lines etched into the cover like runes. Holding it out for Moffy
to see, she began to explain, “This is a grimoire I created. It is under
a special enchantment so that only you, Moffy, can read it. If you read
this and study hard, you can become a fine mage.”

“Eh… really!? I can learn to use magic!?” Hearing that she could become
a mage, Moffy’s eyes instantly sparkled as she stared at the book.

Since her childhood friend could use magic, Moffy had naturally wanted
to use it too, and Shatia had taught her a little in the past. However,
Shatia had often found it a bother, so there hadn’t been much progress.
For Moffy, a grimoire was the thing she wanted most in the world.

“Yes. However, it will be impossible unless you study diligently for
seven years… Can you do it?” Shatia asked while holding the book
tantalizingly aloft.

“Yes! I’ll work hard! I’ll work so hard that I’ll be able to use magic
just like Shatia!!” Moffy replied without a moment’s hesitation. Before
she knew it, she was already standing, and her previous crying fit
forgotten, replaced by a radiant smile.

Witnessing her enthusiasm, Shatia couldn’t help but chuckle. “Then it is
a promise. By the time I return from the royal capital, you must be able
to use many magics, understood?”

“Yeah, I got it! It’ll be lonely without Shatia here… but I’ll be
waiting, and I’ll be so good at magic it’ll surprise you!!”

“Kukuku… I look forward to it.”

The two of them vowed with a firm pinky promise.

From Shatia’s perspective, she thought somewhat derisively that Moffy
would have to master weather-manipulation magic to even come close to
her own level, but she decided to say nothing, thinking the girl might
just show unexpected talent.

With Moffy successfully persuaded, Shatia began her preparations to head
to the capital with her tutor.

As a former court mage, the tutor apparently had many connections and
had already begun the paperwork for the academy. All that remained was
for Shatia to go and take the necessary exams. Of course, with Shatia’s
ability, such exams would be child’s play.

While organizing the books she would bring, Shatia grew curious about
something while watching her tutor prepare the documents in her room.
She asked, “By the way… is it truly alright for you to go to the
capital, teacher? I don’t doubt much happened in your past.”

The tutor’s pen stopped dead. Out of habit, he adjusted his glasses,
then spoke with a somewhat weary expression, “Ah, well… a lot of time
has passed. It’s nothing to worry about. Probably… I think it will be
fine as long as I don’t stand out.”

His voice was trembling and lacked any real conviction. In fact, his
eyes were darting about behind his glasses, and his usually
intelligent-looking face was clouded with anxiety. The truth was
painfully obvious.

Shatia gazed at him with half-lidded eyes. She could have pried the
truth out with magic, but instead, she used her “child’s privilege” to
look at him inquisitively. Finally, unable to bear the pressuring
silence, the tutor slumped his shoulders in defeat and began to talk.

“Alright, alright. I’ll tell you… though it’s a boring story that might
be hard for a child like you to understand, Shatia-chan.”

“I do not mind. I am quite fond of such things.”

The tutor wagged his finger in warning, but for Shatia, who had lived
for ages, listening to these kinds of trivial stories was a preferred
way to kill time. She sat on her bed with a look of intense curiosity,
and the tutor began to recount his past bit by bit.

“As you know, I used to live in the royal capital as a court mage… back
then, I was even called the ‘Mage of Extraordinary Genius.’ I was fairly
famous.”

He didn’t say it to boast; rather, he looked embarrassed to mention his
old title.

To begin with, since only a handful of chosen mages could become court
mages, that alone proved his excellence. Shatia had vaguely sensed his
skill as well. When he showed her royal magic, she had been more
impressed by the efficiency of his incantations than the spells
themselves. Even with the same magic, the power changes drastically
based on the limits of one’s mana, the construction of the formula, and
the method of chanting. At the very least, he possessed a level of
technique that impressed even Shatia. She nodded, agreeing that his
title of genius was well-deserved.

“Everything was going so well. I had friends, and there were nobles
willing to invest in my research. Truly, it was a life without a single
hardship.”

He spoke of his memories, reminiscing on happy days. Becoming a court
mage and being able to do what one loves should be the height of
happiness. Yet, his face was troubled. Despite telling a happy story,
his expression was the exact opposite.

“However, I made one mistake. I caught the eye of the most troublesome
woman in the country.”

Shatia blinked blankly hearing his grave tone. She didn’t quite grasp
the meaning, assuming it was just a boast about a romance. But looking
at his face, it was clearly something else.

“…Romantically?” Shatia confirmed.

“Yeah… I’m not bragging. Truly, I ended up involved with a terrifying
person,” the tutor looked down as he clarified.

Shatia thought idly that she had never heard the term “terrifying
person” used quite like that.

The tutor continued his story, cloaked in a heavy atmosphere, “She was
the daughter of an influential noble, with considerable status and
power. I let it get to my head a bit. I didn’t have a lover at the time,
so when she pursued me, we started dating.”

“But,” the tutor raised a hand, pausing his story. He seemed to have a
very painful past, and his mouth twitched as if he found it difficult to
speak. Finally, as if venting long-held frustrations, he let out a great
sigh and resumed.

“But… she had an incredibly extreme personality. She had a desire to
possess whatever she wanted, no matter what. As a result, I was forced
into a life that was practically imprisonment.”

A terrifying word had been dropped casually, and Shatia didn’t know how
to react. They were supposed to be together because they loved each
other; why would the word “imprisonment” come up? Shatia, who still
didn’t fully understand the human heart, could only stare in shock.

“Naturally, I decided to break up with her. But she wouldn’t allow it,
and she used her power to frame me for a crime I didn’t commit. Because
of that, I was driven from the capital and ended up hiding in this
village.”

“How nostalgic,” the tutor said lightly despite the heavy story, leaving
Shatia exasperated and speechless. To lose one’s status as a court mage
and be driven to a remote village all because of a nonsensical romantic
obsession… Shatia shuddered, thinking she would lose her mind if such a
thing happened to her.

“And yet you’re going back? Even if you were framed, you’re still
considered a criminal, are you not?” Upon hearing that he had been
framed, Shatia asked if it was truly safe to return, but the tutor shook
his head and said it wouldn’t be a problem.

“Not officially. Besides, everyone involved knows what that lady’s
personality is like… but because she has so much power, they can only
turn a blind eye.”

“…In other words, she’s roaming free. How troublesome.”

It seemed almost everyone in the royal capital knew about that lady, and
they understood that if she made a scene, it was just her own selfish
imposing. However, because her father was a high-ranking noble, they
were unable to speak up against her.

In short, everyone knew the tutor was innocent, but he couldn’t live
openly because of her. That was why he intended to stay behind the
scenes to support Shatia this time, to avoid being discovered.

“Well, I won’t cause you any trouble, Shatia-chan. I’m just the
intermediary. My job is just to get you to the capital and into the
academy.”

He spoke as if he had no lingering attachments to the capital, showing
no trace of guilt. Shatia nodded after seeing the sincerity in his eyes.

“Yes, I see. I leave it to you then, for the sake of my goal. Teacher.”

“Heh, being called ‘Teacher’ might only last a little longer.”

Upon hearing Shatia’s words, the tutor felt a hint of loneliness.

From his perspective, once she graduated from the academy and became a
court mage like himself, their relationship as teacher and student would
truly be over. In fact, Shatia had already perfectly mastered royal
magic and had completely surpassed him. Yet, at her request, they had
continued their student-teacher relationship. Thinking that it would end
in a few years left the tutor with a feeling of indescribable sadness.
