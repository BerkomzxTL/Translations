# Chapter 29 – Invitation

The creature’s fur was black, with each individual strand as sharp as a
thorn. Its fangs curved into lethal points, and the legs supporting its
massive frame were thick and powerful, as if packed tight with pure
muscle. If Shatia’s memory served correct, it was a Berserker Wolf, a
vicious monster, a predator so ferocious it would even view its own kind
as prey. Recalling this, Shatia swung her arm at the lunging wolf.

“GROOOOAAHHH!!”

Sent flying by a pulse of mana, the Berserker Wolf was pushed back
toward the center of the plaza. Shatia moved to ensure the demon child
wouldn’t be caught in the crossfire and faced the growling wolf.

Observing her enemy intently, she tilted her head and pondered, “A
Berserker Wolf… To think a creature as ferocious as you would be treated
as mere merchandise. Demons truly are greedy.”

A trade system for monsters existed within the Demon Kingdom. They were
trained to obey their master’s orders and forced to fight other monsters
or used as spectacle. Such things were the current trend among them.

As a lover of nature, Shatia didn’t particularly care for such
practices, but since the monsters the demons captured were usually
either vicious or those that attacked towns, she had refrained from
meddling.

However, there were limits. In this case, the limit was that the
monster’s strength far exceeded what the handlers could manage. As its
massive frame suggested, the Berserker Wolf was unlike ordinary
monsters. Since they had the habit of cannibalizing their own kind, any
surviving Berserker Wolf was a powerhouse that had endured the harshest
law of the jungle. That was precisely why it was so dangerous.

“GROOOOAAAAAAAAHHH!!” The Berserker Wolf, which had likely seen its fair
share of combat, let out a roar and swiped away a nearby stall with its
forepaw.

Seeing Shatia use magic, it had grown cautious and instinctively judged
that attacking from a distance would be more advantageous. That,
however, was a mistake. For Shatia, close-quarters combat was what she
avoided most; long-range exchanges were her specialty. Shatia blocked
the flying debris with a mana shield, then traced her fingers through
the air to form a sphere of mana in her palm.

“First, let us silence that noisy mouth of yours.”

With a flick of her hand, she released a barrage of mana orbs forward,
spinning at high speeds. The Berserker Wolf swatted another stall to use
as a makeshift wall, but the attacks wove through the gaps and struck
the wolf directly.

“GUOOAAHH!!”

The wolf flinched for a moment, but the damage wasn’t significant. As
the smoke cleared, the wolf shook its body, and its fur suddenly became
as hard as steel, extending into sharp needles. The needles shot out,
flying toward Shatia. She dodged them at the last second by tilting her
head, then let out a whistle of admiration.

“Hoh. That is a first for me.”

Berserker Wolves hardened their fur like steel when under extreme stress
or when defending against an external threat. Shatia had observed and
known this, but she hadn’t known they could fire them like blowdarts.
Pleased to have added another piece of knowledge to her collection,
Shatia let her cheeks relax into a smile.

The Berserker Wolf hardened its fur again and fired another volley of
needles. This time, however, Shatia didn’t even bother to dodge; she
caught the incoming needles with a single hand.

“But I have already figured it out. I am not so lowly as to be defeated
by something of this level,” Shatia chuckled as she twirled a needle
between her fingertips.

After all, it was merely an improvised weapon made by temporarily
hardening fur to give it killing power. It lacked sufficient speed and
precision. For someone like Shatia, dealing with it was simple once she
had seen it once.

The Berserker Wolf felt fear. Neither its strength nor its needles
worked against the enemy before it. For a creature that had defeated
countless kin, this was the first time it had faced a clearly superior
foe. Yet, it did not retreat. It poured all its strength into its legs
and crouched low.

“GUOOOAAAAAAAAHHH!!”

“*Shackles of Sin*.”

As the wolf lunged forward with bared fangs and a roar, Shatia attached
magical restraints to it. There was little point in draining mana from a
creature with high base physical stats like a Berserker Wolf, but it was
a more than sufficient means to halt its movement. Facing the
immobilized wolf, Shatia ruthlessly raised her palm.

“Hup.”

With a nonchalant grunt, she released a massive blast that echoed with a
thunderous roar. The immobile Berserker Wolf took the full brunt of the
attack. When the dust settled, the wolf stood there, charred black.
However, it wasn’t dead, it just let out a small groan and collapsed on
the spot. Confirming this, Shatia dispelled the restraints.

“Well, that should about do it.”

No matter how fearsome a Berserker Wolf might be, a single one was no
great threat to Shatia. Regardless of how many of its kin it had
devoured, she was a witch who had lived for over a thousand years. The
difference in their power was absolute.

Shatia let out a small sigh, rolled her shoulders, and cracked her neck
to loosen up. When she returned to the market area to check if the child
was safe, she found a crowd of demons had gathered.

“Y-You’re the Silver Traveler, aren’t you! The one with the silver hair
who always wears a hood!”

“The rumors were true! The mysterious traveler who saved the merchants
from bandits… Incredible!”

For some reason, the demons knew that Shatia went by the alias “Silver
Traveler.” No, thinking back, it was inevitable. Shatia had helped
demons numerous times while on her way to the city. Sometimes it was a
merchant parent and child, other times a traveler, or even villagers.
Every time she helped, she had introduced herself as the Silver
Traveler. It was only natural that rumors would spread. However, she
hadn’t expected them to spread *this* far, and she shrunk back in
confusion.

“Please, let us thank you! You’ve saved so many of our people! We must
show our gratitude…!”

“Ah, no… I have business I must attend to.”

The demons pressed closer, offering words of gratitude. While Shatia
appreciated the gesture, she couldn’t allow her face to be seen. She
could have used illusion magic to disguise herself, but she couldn’t
risk casting magic in the middle of such a crowd. There was always the
chance she’d be found out by some fluke. Shatia felt a surge of panic.

“M-My apologies, but I shall take my leave here!”

In the end, Shatia chose to flee. She bolted from the scene, escaping
into a narrow back alley. Several demons gave chase, but once Shatia
evacuated to the rooftops using flight magic, they lost sight of her in
an instant.

After confirming there were no pursuers, Shatia let out a weary sigh and
slumped her shoulders. She pulled her hood back just enough to reveal
her face and looked down at the city with clear eyes.

“…Haah, honestly. I really do have a troublesome personality,” Shatia
murmured with a hint of loneliness.

She remembered someone telling her something similar long ago. From
Shatia’s perspective, she was simply acting according to her conscience,
but that often resulted in things becoming complicated. If she had just
feigned ignorance or ignored it, things would have been fine, yet she
always found herself sticking her nose into others’ business. She sighed
once more and scratched her neck with a fingertip.

Suddenly, she froze. Her gaze sharpened, and she reached for her hood to
pull it low again. Holding her breath, she silently shifted her eyes
toward the back. She had sensed a presence. And just as she thought, a
demon was standing on the roof.

“I take it you are the one called the Silver Traveler.”

The demon was someone Shatia was familiar with. It was Sheris, the
commander of the demons who had been present during the incident with
Emerald, a woman with black hair and crimson eyes.

“…Sheris,” Shatia muttered the name low enough that she wouldn’t be
heard. She stared at Sheris’s face with surprise.

Taking that silence as affirmation, Sheris continued, “His Majesty the
Demon Lord wishes to see you. You are to respond to this summons. Should
you refuse, we will be forced to take appropriate measures,” Sheris took
a paper—evidently a summons—from her breast pocket, unfolded it, and
read it aloud, holding it up for Shatia to see.

“…Hoh,” Shatia narrowed her eyes in amusement, then returned to her
normal expression and slowly stood up, “That is quite an honor. I, too,
wish to meet with the Demon Lord-dono.”

Shatia accepted without hesitation. In fact, it was an opportunity that
offered her nothing but benefits, so she welcomed it. Sheris wore a
confused expression upon hearing Shatia’s manner of speech, but since it
didn’t concern her duty, she ignored it and led Shatia toward the Demon
Lords’s castle.

<br />
<br />
Walking down the road and crossing a long bridge, Shatia was led beneath
the gates of the castle. The massive doors creaked open, and once she
finally stepped inside, she found herself in a massive hall. Countless
pillars stood tall, and a red carpet stretched endlessly into the
depths. Occasionally, demons who appeared to be castle soldiers
patrolled the area, clad in jet-black armor. Shatia followed Sheris
while observing everything intently. All the while, she kept her hood
pulled low and remained vigilant of her surroundings.

Eventually, after walking down a long corridor, Shatia was brought
before a single room. The path ahead didn’t seem to lead to a throne
room. She tilted her head, wondering if this was to be a private meeting
rather than a formal audience.

Sheris gave a soft knock, and a man’s voice answered from the other
side. It was likely the Demon Lord.

“Enter.”

Having received confirmation, Sheris stepped to the side of the door and
signaled to Shatia. It seemed Sheris didn’t intend to follow her inside.
This confirmed it was a private matter. Judging as much, Shatia gave a
small nod, slowly opened the door, and stepped into the room.

It was a truly small guest parlor. A red sofa placed beside a table,
atop which a single candle flickered. A tiny, dim room. And inside that
gloom, a single man sat on the sofa. He was a familiar figure, someone
Shatia had met long ago.

“It has been a long time, Belpheus… Or is it ‘Demon Lord Belpheus’ now?”
She spoke while removing her hood.

“…Shatifahl,” Belpheus narrowed his eyes, as if his suspicions were
confirmed, and stared at Shatia’s young form.

Both had changed significantly. One had grown into a magnificent and
sturdy adult, while the other had become quite small. Their positions
were the complete reverse of when they had first met. Yet, Belpheus
could sense it; what was left unspoken. Although the girl’s form had
changed, the mana lurking within had not diminished. If anything, it
felt even more immense than before.

Belpheus sighed softly, reaffirming once more that witches were indeed
creatures on a whole other level.
