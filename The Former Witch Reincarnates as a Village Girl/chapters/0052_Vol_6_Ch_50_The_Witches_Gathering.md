# Chapter 50: The Witches’ Gathering

\*\*\*

Atop a hill with a magnificent view. All around, herbivorous animals
spent their time in leisure, basking in the sun or grazing on grass; a
scene of idyllic tranquility spread far and wide. In this place, beneath
the shade of a single, giant tree, a group of individuals had arranged
tables and were enjoying an elegant tea party, cups in hand.

“*Phew.*.. How peaceful. Nature truly is wonderful,” the silver-haired
girl, Shatia, murmured as she took a sip of black tea from her cup.

Though her body was small enough to fit snugly into her chair, her
presence was like that of a noble lady, with every single movement
refined and deliberate. However, sitting in a different seat from
Shatia, a purple-haired girl had her face buried against the table as if
exhausted.

“Mother… it is hot. I wish to go indoors,” Vestalis, the Undying Witch,
whined.

Shatia ignored her, dismissing the complaint with a sip of tea,
“Vestalis. You need to bask in the sun a bit more. You spend far too
much time shut away as it is.”

“Uuu… I’m dying… I’m burning to death…”

“If the ‘undying’ witch could actually die from something like this, we
wouldn’t have nearly as much trouble, would we?” Emerald, who was
watching the agonizing Vestalis from the side, commented with a hint of
irony as she added sugar to her tea.

Technically, this spot was under a giant tree, shaded by branches and
leaves, but apparently, even that was insufficient for Vestalis. Due to
her shut-in personality, she was unaccustomed to the outdoor
environment. Still, her reaction was somewhat exaggerated.

After staring blankly at Emerald, she suddenly asked, “Emerald… come to
think of it, why are you so small?”

“You only noticed that *now*…!?” Emerald hurriedly cleared her throat
and sat up straight in her chair, “Well, a lot happened…”

Since explaining from the beginning would be tedious, Emerald gave only
that curt answer. For one thing, she knew she would be teased for
becoming small. She wasn’t about to expose her own weaknesses of her own
accord.

“Hmmm… I see,” Vestalis spoke with a lack of interest while resting her
chin on her hand, looking at Emerald with dull eyes.

Relieved that she had managed to deflect the question for now, Emerald
brought the tea to her lips.

“She used possession magic. After I taught her a lesson, I used body
creation magic to craft a vessel for her. But since the materials were a
bit sparse, she ended up small, as you can see.”

“*Pfft*…! Sh-Shatifahl…!” Hit with Shatia’s blunt revelation, Emerald
accidentally spat out her tea. She hurriedly wiped her mouth with a
handkerchief and turned toward Shatia, who wore a mischievous smile.

“Heh~ so that was it…”

Emerald fought back, “Hmph… appearances shouldn’t matter to us
long-lived witches anyway, right? Besides, you’re quite small yourself,
Shatifahl.”

“My current body is strictly human, so I will eventually grow into an
adult form,” she interjected.

“……….” This time, Emerald truly froze, her face trembling with
indignation and her eyes welling up slightly with tears.

“Kukuku, my apologies. Do not be so angry. If you get too worked up,
your cursed eyes will activate, so have mercy.”

“…It’s because you tease me so much, Shatifahl…!” Emerald said while
wiping away her tears with her hand and using magic to pour more tea
into her cup.

“In the first place, is everyone really going to gather for something
like this? Wasn’t the last time we were all together about twenty years
ago?”

“Was it around then…? Shatifahl, did you call out to everyone?”

“I sent word of the time and place… whether they come or not is up to
them,” Shatia answered the doubts of Vestalis and Emerald while looking
up at the sky.

This tea party wasn’t held just because the weather was nice. There was
a proper purpose, and formal invitations had been sent out. However, she
didn’t know if they would show up. All Shatia could do now was wait in
silence while drinking her tea.

Then, as if perfectly timed, a sudden gust of wind whipped up a short
distance from the table. Emerald hurriedly used magic to keep the items
on the table from blowing away, while Shatia watched the wind with an
amused expression.

“Speak of the devil…” A smirk escaped Shatia’s lips as she murmured.

The gust subsided, and standing there were the Witch Twins, one with red
hair and the other with light blue hair, each tied in a side-tail on
opposite sides.

“Huh, talk about nostalgic faces. Even the shut-in is here.”

“Sister.. you shouldn’t say things like that.”

Upon seeing Vestalis’s face, the red-haired girl, Lili, made a wry face,
while Mimi, who stood quietly beside her, corrected her sister with a
regretful expression.

Sensing their mana, Shatia asked, “Welcome. Lili, Mimi. From the look of
things, your seal has been completely broken?”

“Pretty much. The Hero finally pulled himself together. Hahh—it was a
real pain,” Lili nodded in confirmation. She then rolled her shoulders
as if exhausted and took a seat at the table. Mimi sat down beside her.

“What is the Hero doing now?”

“He’s the same as ever. He recovered, but he’s still on his journey of
atonement. He’s probably out helping people in some country right about
now,” Lili answered Shatia’s question with an indifferent shrug.

It seemed the Hero had managed to recover enough to fully lift the seal
he had placed on Lili and Mimi. However, he apparently still felt a
heavy sense of guilt, and his long journey of atonement continued. He
would likely continue for the rest of his life.

“A guy like that deserves to have his limbs torn off…”

“I think he might actually be happier if I just turned him to stone with
my eyes, though.”

“You two have started saying quite witch-like things, hm?”

Vestalis and Emerald, who had been listening, each murmured these
terrifying things with displeased expressions. It was only natural to
feel that way toward someone who had nearly killed them, but Shatia
couldn’t help but let out a little chuckle at their very witchy remarks.

With two more witches having joined, the tea party continued. Emerald
prepared cups for Lili and Mimi, giving Lili black tea and handing Mimi
a cup poured from a different pot.

Since Mimi couldn’t drink black tea, Emerald handed her the coffee she
had prepared in advance, “Here, Mimi, you can’t handle black tea, right?
This is coffee.”

“Thank you, Emerald,” Mimi smiled warmly and thanked her as she accepted
it.

Because each of the seven witches had such strong personalities, they
lacked much in the way of cooperativeness. Since a single individual
possessed enough power to destroy a nation, such a tendency was only
natural. Among them, Emerald and Mimi had relatively mild temperaments,
so they were close enough to understand each other’s preferences like
this. In fact, Emerald often acted more like a mature older sister than
the actual twin sister, Lili.

Lili downed her tea in a single gulp and asked Shatia with a grumpy
look, “Anyway, Shatifahl, what business do you have calling us here?
Surely it wasn’t just to have a friendly little tea party?”

“Now, do not be in such a rush. There is still one more left…” Shatia
answered while quietly sipping her tea. The witches looked at each other
with complicated expressions.

“The last one…”

“It’s *her*, isn’t it…”

“She’s always picking fights with Shatifahl.”

It was easy to guess who the final person was. It was that Witch of
Inquiry who had challenged Shatifahl since long ago, only to be turned
back every time. While not as extreme as Fantaretta, who had plotted
rebellion, she too had once recognized Shatifahl as an enemy and fought
her. In short, she was a source of various headaches among the witches.

While they harbored such anxieties, a blue-haired woman clad in black
robes suddenly appeared on the center of the table with a sound like
cutting wind. The cups and pots nearby were naturally blown aside, but
Emerald hurriedly used magic to freeze them in mid-air.

Translocation magic. It was the specialty of the Witch of Inquiry and a
symbol of her power. Despite doing something so abrupt that anyone else
would have been startled, the beautiful woman, Cloak, simply looked
around.

“Hmm… what? Am I late?”

Seeing everyone except Shatia staring blankly, Cloak asked to confirm if
she had made a mistake. But what everyone wanted to say was much simpler
than that.

Lili immediately leaned forward and snapped at her, “Cloak! Can’t you
make a more normal entrance? It’s startling when you just pop in like
that!!”

“I just teleported to the coordinates Shatifahl gave me. If you’ve got a
complaint, take it up with the big boss.”

However, Cloak didn’t show the slightest sign of caring; she stepped
down onto the table with a heavy thud and sat in an empty seat. Despite
her beautiful appearance, she was a witch whose personality was quite a
handful.

“You… you really never change!”

Vestalis, who was startled at first, buried her face back on the table
and spoke sluggishly, “The same as ever, Cloak.”

“Well, it is a relief in its own way…” Emerald nodded in agreement, a
small smile appearing on her face as she reflected that some things
staying the same wasn’t all that bad.

“It has been a while, Cloak.”

Looking at the unchanged Cloak, Shatia watched her while toying with her
own hair. Despite Cloak’s attitude, Shatia was honestly happy that she
had come and viewed her as a precious existence.

“Yeah… hey. Long time no see, big boss.”

In contrast, Cloak wore a complicated expression, likely due to what had
happened in the Demon Kingdom. Shatia still recognized Cloak as a fellow
witch and companion, but for Cloak, who had once participated in a
revolt, it wasn’t so easy to return to their original relationship
immediately. Still, Shatia intended to wait until she opened her heart.
That was exactly why she had summoned everyone to this tea party.

“Well then, now that everyone is gathered.”

Shatia clapped her hands, returning the tablecloth Cloak had disturbed
to its original state, while the cups and pots Emerald had been
levitating returned to their places. She then poured black tea into her
own cup and, holding it in one hand, turned to face everyone.

Thinking they had finally reached the main point, Lili asked Shatia once
more, “So, what is the ‘business’ that made you go out of your way to
gather all the witches?”

“Hmm. No, it is nothing much. I just wanted to see everyone’s faces
after so long. I thought we might have a chat over some tea since we are
all here,” Shatia took a sip of tea and then answered as if it were the
most natural thing in the world.

Lili didn’t understand at first and sat there with her mouth agape. Once
she finally processed it, she cried out in exasperation, “Whaaa!? So
it’s exactly like I said at the start! You just wanted to have a tea
party!?”

Shatia retorted, “Indeed. Well, that is about the gist of things. I am
pleased to see we were blessed with good weather.”

Lili was finally so exasperated that she fell backward out of her chair.
Mimi hurried to her side to check if she was okay. Fortunately, she
seemed unhurt.

“Shatifahl has always been the type to suddenly act on a whim…”

“Even so, isn’t this a bit too sudden?”

Vestalis clapped her hands against the table, cackling at Shatia’s
characteristic impulsiveness. It was a sight where it was hard to tell
if she was laughing or just exhausted, but beside her, Emerald was
nodding in agreement.

“Hmph… what a troublesome mother,” Cloak propped her legs up on the
table, as if to say the whole thing was ridiculous.

“Surely you could have predicted this, Cloak? In fact, I thought it
unlikely you would participate in such an event regardless of the
reason.”

“…………”

However, when Shatia pointed this out while staring at Cloak with her
clear eyes, Cloak looked away awkwardly and downed her tea as if to hide
her embarrassment.

“Well, it is only natural for a mother to worry about her children’s
growth. Let us sit down and have a long talk for once,” she continued as
she set down her cup and rested her chin on her folded hands.

Though she had the most childlike appearance among them, Shatia was the
one who united the witches—and the one who could kill them. If she, who
was like a mother to all the others, desired this, her children had no
reason to object.

“I don’t mind. Talking with Shatifahl is the most fun, after all.”

“I… if it’s just talking, I don’t mind staying outside a bit longer…”

“Hmph, well, if Shatifahl says so, I guess I can go along with it.”

“Sister, you’re just saying that even though you really want to talk
about all sorts of things with Shatifahl…”

“I suppose I’ll indulge the big boss’s whims every once in a while.”

Each of the witches spoke up, accepting Shatia’s invitation. Thus, the
witches’ tea party finally began in earnest, and they chatted happily
together with black tea in hand.
