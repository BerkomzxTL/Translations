## Chapter 162: Sword Dance—Encore

"W-Winner!!"

The referee, frozen in place, raised a hand and made the announcement
with a clear tremble in his voice.

It was true that he had witnessed an incredible match. However, more
importantly, he had also just seen Curtis's face from the second-closest
position. It was a miracle he could speak at all.

At his announcement, the audience erupted, and a massive cheer rang out.
In the center of the cheering, Curtis and Letty were still facing each
other.

No, Curtis was staring up at the sky, completely spaced out. *How did
this happen?* While being consumed by such thoughts.

It was then that the front row of the audience began to murmur
restlessly… Noticing the tension between the two figures who hadn't
moved, or perhaps after making out Curtis's face.

"Ah, well... damn it, this ruins everything, doesn't it?"

He muttered this under his breath. His tone was strangely resigned as he
stared up at the sky. After a moment, his mouth twisted grotesquely.

"Hah! Hahaha! This is it? This is everything? Hahahahaha,
hahahahahahahahaha!!!!"

The audience grew silent as maniacal laughter echoed. Confused murmurs,
hushed whispers. All eyes, without exception, were on him.

His neck jerked suddenly.

He turned his face towards Letty and stared at her with vacant eyes.

"It's you. It's you, after all."

"I don't understand what you’re talking about."

Letty tilted her head. Of course, she wasn’t one to let her guard down.
She was ready to react immediately, no matter how he moved. In contrast,
Curtis looked very vulnerable.

Despite Letty's indifference, his lips curled up in amusement.

"It's you, you. Back then, and now. You’re always getting in the
way!!!!!"

The air vibrated as he yelled. This was not a metaphor.

Screams could be heard everywhere, causing the audience to begin to
panic.

It was certainly a loud voice. But of course, it wasn't strong enough to
generate that kind of shockwave. That was simply not something a human
could produce.

"Ah, I see."

Letty alone understood. His scream. And the phenomenon.

Something like a core, hidden in the center of his body, had begun to
run wild. At this close a distance, Letty could feel the raw, intense
magical power emanating from it.

"Back then was one thing, but this time was just a coincidence."

"I don't care! Stop messing around! STOP MESSING AROUND! STOP MESSING
AROUUND!!!"

With a scream, something burst inside him.

A blast of air, like an explosion, tore through the arena, and a
particularly loud shriek rose from the audience.

Riding that gust of wind, Letty smoothly distanced herself. She called
out to the referee, who was standing still in shock.

"Run. And please direct the audience to evacuate."

"Huh!? Ah, y-yes, I understand! B-But what about you!?"

"Me? I..."

She slowly turned her gaze toward the thing that used to be Curtis.

"I am his opponent. Since he singled me out."

"T-That's reckless!"

"But if I run, people will get caught up. I'll buy some time, so please,
do that in the meantime."

The thing that used to be Curtis slowly turned its gaze toward her. Just
being near the focus of that gaze was terrifying. Something inside the
referee told him that staying here would be a pointless death.

"D-Damn it... I'm sorry, don't you dare die!?"

She glanced briefly at the back of the referee, who had started running
and shouting evacuation instructions, before looking back at Curtis.

The muscles were swelling, pressuring the armor from the inside. The
exposed face was warped, and his eyes were sinking into hollow sockets.
There was a snapping sound, and pieces of his armor burst off.

Slowly, his body was transforming into something inhuman.

"Letty-san!!"

Ellie, in the royal box, shouted and threw her beloved shortsword. As
she possessed unimaginable strength for her appearance, it accurately
reached Letty’s hands.

"Thank you! Please handle things over there."

Whether Ellie heard or read her lips, she nodded repeatedly. The aim was
still unclear, but in this situation, the royal family was certainly the
most likely target. Therefore, Ellie, who could use a barrier, and
Dominique, who was even more skilled than Letty, should remain with
them. That had been discussed beforehand. But no one had predicted
Curtis would become like this, and they were, naturally, worried.

Still, they had no choice but to trust Letty as she stood calmly in her
position.

"Well now. You've become quite the manly figure."

Letty sighed softly and drew her shortsword from its sheath. Logically
and emotionally, she knew she should be the one to deal with that thing.
She knew it, but...

"I really think that's taking things too far."

She looked up at what used to be Curtis. His body was covered in swollen
muscles, easily exceeding three meters in height. A colossal physique,
and a stench of rot that made her nose wrinkle; he could be mistaken for
an ogre. All the things he had been hiding had burst forth as he became
consumed by the rampant magical power.

**“Stop messing around... stop messing around…”**

All that leaked from his mouth was resentment. Had he completely fallen
into becoming a revenant?

"I think you're the one who’s messing around. Well, nevermind."

He must have been plotting something sinister. And now, he had abandoned
his human form and turned into a monster. Where that flesh came from,
she couldn't imagine.

Strangely enough, she was calm. Calm enough to think such a ridiculous
thing. Wondering if this was also Dominique's influence, she held her
shortsword in position.

"I'll play along a little longer."

He raised his clenched fist and brought it down. It struck the ground
with a force that seemed to break the sound barrier. But Letty had
already flowed away from the spot. He swung his fist sideways toward the
place she had been, but it missed, narrowly whistling past Letty's nose.
With that momentum, he turned to face her, catching Letty in his sights.

The moment he thought he had her, Letty stepped forward. His reflexive
punch was dodged by a hair's breadth, and her shortsword danced,
slashing his arm once, then twice. The hard, thick skin was sliced open,
and something viscous flowed out.

But perhaps he no longer felt pain; he swung that arm again regardless.
She received the blow by pressing her left hand against the flat of her
blade, letting the force push her backward, putting distance between
them.

"...That's strange. He's faster, but he feels slow."

She muttered to herself. Yes, after growing in size, his strikes were
clearly faster and heavier, yet she could see them even better than
before. The fist, which looked like it could explode on contact, felt
less threatening than his longsword had. He was too reliant on raw
power, and his movements were too transparent. Even his footwork was a
mess.

"I wonder if this feeling is pity."

She didn't know if it was the right emotion to feel toward an enemy. She
was still being cautious in case he was hiding some deeper trick. But
she was smoothly and leisurely evading, deflecting, and avoiding. She
circled him, staying close, then pulling away. With every move, she
carved another wound.

"...I will end this."

She announced this, and once again, transitioned to the offensive.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

The sound of his fists; is it the plaintive wailing of a pathetic
revenant?

*I can’t reach. I can’t reach. I can’t reach.*

She dances calmly, and he cannot touch her.

All the accumulated resentment and misplaced anger strike the ground in
vain.

The curtain will be lowered by her hand.
