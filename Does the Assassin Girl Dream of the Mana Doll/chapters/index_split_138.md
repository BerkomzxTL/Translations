## Chapter 161: Flowing Water Sword Dance

*Kiiiin*, a clear sound rang out.

It was unfamiliar, causing him to involuntarily open his eyes wide.

The sound was coming from the longsword he wielded.

His longsword. It had been crafted by a smith considered one of the best
in the Kingdom of Jurastin, boasting the highest quality a human could
forge. Steel that was uniformly dense, yet retained a desirable level of
flexibility. Hard enough to cut through virtually anything, yet supple
enough not to break.

He was still unable to grasp the properties of that sword.

Yes, still.

"As expected, you defended well."

A calm voice.

The person who spoke those words had already vanished from his sight.
His vision could barely follow the faint afterimage. But his perception
couldn't keep up.

*Whoosh*, a small gust of air.

Was his reaction due to actually seeing it, or was it a coincidence? At
the very least, he hadn't defended with conviction. He knew this better
than anyone.

**"Damn it! Even the attacks are annoying!"**

Grinding out a complaint, he parried the approaching blade. With a speed
that was surely the fastest a human could manage. Yet, bafflingly crude.

Yes, his defense was clearly unrefined compared to his offense. In fact,
it might even be considered clumsy. Still, he was managing to endure
through sheer quickness and good eyesight.

But from there, nothing followed.

He could only endlessly guard, evade, and deflect the blade in front of
him. He could only protect himself from the immediate threat. Most
critically, he didn't even have the leisure to notice his own
predicament.

*Gohn*, a dull sound.

He was struck on the shoulder. Since it was on the armor, it wasn't
counted as a valid hit, but to the man, it was a humiliation equal to
defeat.

He couldn't fully defend. Meaning, she was pushing him. *Him*, of all
people. What else could this be but humiliation?

The moment he tried to glare with hatred, she was already disappearing
from the corner of his vision. When he hastily turned his head, a bright
blade was rushing toward him again. He instinctively parried and tried
to sweep his sword, but she was no longer there.

He could see it. His body was reacting. But something was fatally
insufficient. Slow. Yes, he was slow. He, who had overwhelmed every
opponent he ever faced before.

**"Slow!? Me!?"**

He was aghast at the realization. As if to prove it, he was immediately
outmaneuvered again, forced onto the defensive.

He saw it. He defended. He reacted. But he couldn't counterattack. By
the time he tried to counter, she was already gone.

In the corner of his eye, he saw her trying to circle behind him. The
moment he turned around in a panic, she reversed direction. Now, she was
circling to his right, again vanishing from his sight.

He was being spun around.

Him.

*Why? Why? Why?*

Only doubts ran wild, confusion set in, his reactions dulled. An attack
came from his right side, and he blocked it. But by then, she was
already flowing away from the spot. Attack and movement were intricately
intertwined, never stopping for a moment.

*Whoosh*, a sound of cutting air. An attack aimed at his feet.

His sword wouldn't reach in time.

He instinctively presented his greave and barely managed to block it. On
top of the armor.

A dull pain resonated.

It likely wasn't fatal, but it was a powerful strike.

If this had been a real sword? No, he still would have blocked it. He
wouldn't have died.

His vision caught Letty's attack, and his thoughts were interrupted.
Instinctively, he defended. Just as he thought he had blocked a
horizontal slash aimed at his neck, the blade flipped, and a strike came
from the opposite direction.

He blocked this too. His eyes focused on the blade, returned, and by the
time he tried to look at her, she was flowing sideways again.

He couldn't keep up. His eyes. His body. Most of all, his thoughts.

"You're slow. Come on, next."

He couldn't even afford to be annoyed by Letty's words. He wasn't given
the time.

An attack aimed at his torso. He blocked. The horizontal slash was
heavy, utilizing her body's rotation.

Surprised by the unexpected power, he involuntarily braced his feet.

His movement stalled, and in that moment, she flowed past. A strike to
his back.

It was on the armor. Not a valid hit, but...

As he instinctively turned around, he saw her flowing further to the
right in response. He followed her to the right, and she continued
further right. A circular motion, with him as the center. Even though he
should be faster just by turning.

**"Why! Why is this happening!"**

Letty wasn't kind enough to answer.

What she gave him instead was a strike to his arm.

His sword didn't make it in time, and the blow landed directly on the
armor.

*I see*, he realized in his heart. His problem was the same as hers from
a short while ago.

If he lacked *that*, even he would be forced onto the defensive. Even
he, who should have been overwhelmingly faster.

She used the momentum of the strike to his arm to flow further sideways.
Just as he tried to turn around, she reversed. Since he tended to turn
his neck too far, she aimed for the back of his head. If it landed,
good. If it was blocked, she would reverse again and aim for his feet.

His thoughts were moving seconds ahead of his actions, his body moved
according to those thoughts, and he was spun around exactly as she
envisioned. Something he couldn't see, but Letty could.

The future just a few seconds ahead.

No, she wasn't *seeing* it. It was a future she was actively creating.

He would never dream that this was something forged by human connection.
He must truly have been a tiger in a cage.

"Because you have no one."

**"What are you talking about!?"**

An existence that only gorged on the food given to it. No one approached
him. No one *could* approach him. And he himself never tried to leave.
This surely must be the result.

The strike to the back of his head was blocked, but his feet were struck
sharply. His body swayed; it seemed he wasn't completely inhuman after
all.

She relentlessly circled behind him, now aiming for his hips, then his
legs. She struck and then flowed away, spinning and twirling. She kept
moving fluidly, unleashing strikes within the flow.

Dominique had called it the Flowing Water Sword Dance.

Had she come closer to that state?

**"Damn it! Take this!"**

As if in desperation, and knowing that attacks to the armor wouldn't
count as valid, he threw caution to the wind and swung his sword.

However.

The descending sword was completely visible, and she slipped sideways to
evade.

It would hit the ground with a fierce crash, and it would spring back
up.

It sprang up. A horizontal slash would come next.

"Hah!"

Exhaling sharply, she stopped her feet. Just for an instant. She
converted the inertia of her sideways movement into rotational momentum.
Adding power from her legs and hips, she transmitted the force up
through her core and into her arm.

And then.

A single strike, leaving even the sound of cutting air behind.

It collided head-on with his sword. A shattering roar echoed.

**"Impossible..."**

A look of bewilderment.

He was staring at his hand.

The sword was gone.

No, it was bent and lying on the ground.

His hand was numb and trembling.

It was only a fraction of a difference. His body axis was slightly off.
The swing of his arm was slightly poor. His grip was slightly loose.

As a result of combining these many "slight differences," he was
defeated.

But he wasn't even given the time to understand it.

Letty wouldn't overlook such an opening.

A merciless blow struck his head.

The powerful impact blew his helmet off.

"...Long time no see, handsome."

Letty whispered with a touch of pity.

The face revealed beneath the helmet was that of Curtis, already
beginning to show signs of ugly decay in places.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Exposed to the light of day is a pathetic revenant.

Outside the bounds of human reason, it loses control in its unbound
state.

It screams, thrashes, and tries to sweep everything away, but it’s
swallowed by the flow.
