## Chapter 159: Clash—Human vs. The Inhuman

And so, the final day of the tournament arrived. The audience was
completely packed, chatting loudly and animatedly, discussing their
predictions for the day:

On one side was the fully armored man, who had demonstrated overwhelming
might, allowing no one to get near him. On the other was the lightly
armored female swordswoman, who had shown exquisite skill and footwork,
never allowing her opponents to pin her down.

It was truly a clash of soft and hard styles, and the cityfolk, who had
watched this tournament for a long time, were hotly debating who would
win. The odds were currently leaning in favor of the armored man. It
seemed his strength and speed carried a different kind of conviction.

In contrast, Letty was more popular. This was likely due to sympathy
votes and the desire for her to win. Of course, there were also men
drawn to her appearance and women drawn to her strength.

Because of this, Ellie was filled with mixed feelings, even being overly
nervous about Letty's popularity. Letty herself didn't pay any attention
to such things however.

At any rate, the final day had come, and the time for the championship
match was fast approaching.

"Egret-sama, may fortune favor you in battle!"

"You've come this far, allow me to commend you."

Tseren and Batbayar offered their encouragement. While this could be
seen as blurring public and private roles, Letty was, after all, a royal
recommendation, so it was probably a gray area.

At that moment, Dominique spoke up, "Well, I believe I've told you
everything I needed to. Any issues?"

"No, I'm fine, no issues," Letty nodded in response. She felt she had a
very good grasp of her opponent's true nature. She thought she also knew
her own capabilities. Her physical condition was normal. "I believe I
can do everything I'm capable of."

"Then there's nothing to worry about. Go out there and give it your
all." When she said that, Letty instinctively stared intently at
Dominique.

And, being stared at, Dominique gave a wry smile. "What's with that
face? Is it so surprising that I would say something like that?"

"Yes, it’s very surprising. I'm quite astonished," Letty said frankly,
standing firm, while Dominique frowned. For a moment, tension filled the
room.

But in the next instant, Dominique abruptly burst out laughing, and
Letty smiled softly.

"Kukuku, you've really started speaking your mind, haven't you?"

"Well, that's because you trained me."

"I didn't intend to train *that* part, but oh well, that's fine too."

It hadn't been a long time, but the last month or so had been truly
enjoyable. It was truly fulfilling. Both Dominique and Letty felt the
same way.

Therefore.

"Well then, I'm going now…” Letty hesitated for just a moment, then
announced shyly, “Master."

Dominique, completely caught off guard, was speechless. Seeing that face
was enough to satisfy Letty.

Then, Dominique re-composed herself and grinned. "Go on then, my top
student."

And by the entrance, in the final place to see her off, stood Ellie.

"Please show me your very coolest side."

With a look of complete trust.

"Of course.”

So, Letty returned her best smile. Radiantly.

“I wouldn't want to embarrass my wife, now, would I?"

Then, Letty walked confidently toward the arena.

"T-That is exactly what I'm talking about..."

Leaving behind a completely weak-kneed Ellie.

\*\*\*

Letty then stepped out into the arena. Right into the center of the
venue, where cheers, murmurs, and general noise intermingled.

Amidst the ecstatic, desperate, high-pitched cheers and shouts, she
stood on the stage and waited quietly.

Soon after, her opponent appeared.

*Clank, clank.* The sound of his armor echoed carelessly.

*Clunk, clunk.* His steps were as graceful as a wild beast's.

**"Hey, it's been a while."** A familiar voice called out warmly from
within the helmet.

But Letty deliberately tilted her head, speaking with genuine confusion,
"...Who are you? I don't recall any acquaintance of mine being so
cowardly as to hide their face."

The man's entire demeanor changed. *Hmm, was he always this easy to
provoke?* No, perhaps he was.

**"You've got some nerve, don't you?"**

A heavy voice, on the verge of losing its patience, resonated. Letty
calmly ignored the voice that was strong enough to make the referee
flinch.

Unable to withstand the tension, the referee raised a hand and declared
in a trembling voice:

"B-Begin!"

Taking that as a signal, the man leaped forward before the cheers could
even start. With movements that completely belied the weight of his
metal armor, he covered the three-meter distance in a single jump.

In an instant, he had Letty within his range.

At that moment, the sword he had raised high above his head swung down
with a sharpness that could cut even iron.

But Letty could see it.

She met the terrifyingly fast attack by aligning her shortsword with
his. She cut in before the blade could hit her head, clung to its side,
and deflected it.

The man knew the attack would be parried. He didn't fight the
deflection, allowing himself to be carried along... but because he had
anticipated this, he stopped abruptly. He checked his blade, flipped it
over, and swung it horizontally.

However, Letty was no longer there. The moment she parried, she had
smoothly disengaged from the spot. As if she had already known what he
was going to do next.

After a brief exchange serving as a greeting, the two separated and
stopped, measuring each other's range. The audience, finally realizing
what had happened, cheered loudly.

**"The same as ever, I see."** Calmly ignoring the cheers, the man spoke
in an amused tone.

Letty replied with an exasperated expression, "You, too, are exactly the
same."

Whether he noticed the difference in nuance or not, the man pressed
forward with an air of enjoyment.

Striking down. Sweeping across.

An overwhelming, inhuman violence bearing down.

But all of it is brushed aside. As if she had already seen it.
