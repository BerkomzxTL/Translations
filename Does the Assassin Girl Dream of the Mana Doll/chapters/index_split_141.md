## Chapter 164: At the Tail End of a Scheme

The core burst, the wind exploded outwards, and then everything
subsided.

The people who were attempting to evacuate hunched over to brace against
the wind, stopping their movement.

When they hesitantly looked up, they realized the sound of fighting was
gone.

Slowly, slowly, they turned around.

The monster's form was no longer there; only a single woman stood
quietly.

*Impossible?!* Just as that thought began to surface in their minds.

"Hear me, everyone!!"

At Batbayar's loud voice, everyone straightened their backs. The deep
resonance vibrated everyone's eardrums, forcing the information into
their brains.

"The monster that suddenly appeared and went berserk has been slain!
Yes! By the swordswoman, Egret, who showed preeminent skill, won her
matches unchallenged, and gloriously claimed victory!!!"

Flipping his mantle with a dramatic yet masterful gesture, he swung his
arm and pointed toward Letty. The gaze of the audience shifted in turn.

And they acknowledged her standing figure.

A cheer as great as the recent blast of wind erupted.

\*\*\*

"Phew, that should do it."

"Oh, come now, Your Majesty, you're quite the actor."

"Don't say that, it's all thanks to her setting the stage."

He took his seat, returning a wry smile to Dominique's teasing words.
Yes, because Letty had held the monster back, there was virtually no
damage. Furthermore, she actually defeated it, which was astonishing.

If Letty hadn't been there, and the soldiers in the venue had fought it
instead….

The very thought sent a chill down his spine.

The hero herself, after flinching at the massive cheers, was timidly
waving her hand in response.

"All the guards were diverted to help the audience escape. If she hadn't
been here, I would have had to mobilize all the soldiers in the venue,
take command myself... No, that wouldn't have been enough. Of course,
you could have done the same thing alone, but if she hadn't been here,
you wouldn't have been here either, would you?"

"Haha, I can't deny that."

Indeed, if she hadn't met Egret, she would have continued in the
opposite direction from Cordor and drifted off somewhere. She almost
certainly wouldn't have been here. Thinking about it, it was a strange
twist of fate.

"Well, that's settled for now. Maybe I can finally get some good sleep."

Batbayar scratched his neck in resignation.

However, Dominique curved her lips into a mischievous smile.

"Ah, well, it doesn't seem to be entirely settled yet, does it?"

\*\*\*

"Why. Why. Why."

The man could not believe the sight that had unfolded before his eyes.
He couldn't comprehend it.

Curtis, whom he had brought back to life with ‘Animate Dead’, was
clearly superior in both strength and speed. Although he should have
been the one controlling Curtis, the latter's extraordinary willpower
had wrestled control away, and he moved on his own. In fact, Curtis's
speed was beyond his control; leaving it to him was undoubtedly the
better decision, so that wasn't the issue.

"Why was he beaten in speed? Why was he defeated?"

Curtis was certainly faster, yet he couldn't catch her. Curtis was
stronger, yet he was ultimately defeated. It defied logic. Therefore, he
could not understand it.

What's more…

"Why was he defeated even in *that* form...?"

He had been certain of Curtis's victory from the start. The Oracle had
also told him so. And he had planned to have Curtis strike down Batbayar
the moment he was being praised as the victor. He had considered having
him infiltrate as a retainer, but the man's personality made that
impossible.

The eyes of the entire nation were on them. By assassinating the deeply
beloved and trusted king in this arena, and then instigating Gaschnar,
the country should have been instantly plunged into chaos.

But the unexpected defeat.

Just in case, he had prepared a contingency to make the core embedded
for the Animate Dead spell run wild and cause havoc. Considering
Batbayar's personality, he would have led the troops from the front
line. It should have been easy to eliminate him then.

Yet, even that plan had failed.

Despite becoming faster and stronger, the creature was easily handled
and defeated by that one woman.

"Why... why did this happen?"

If you're fast, you hit. If you're strong, you crush. It should be that
simple.

What unfolded before him was the exact opposite of self-evident truth.

He could not understand it.

He couldn't understand, but what he needed to do hadn't changed.

Now, it was the last resort.

With soldiers diverted to evacuation, Batbayar's guard was thin.

He would do it himself.

He looked up at the royal box.

In that instant, his eyes met those of the elderly woman sitting next to
Batbayar.

He saw her lips curl into a grin, in what seemed like painfully slow
motion.

*"Found you."*

She murmured this. He shouldn't have been able to hear it at this
distance, amid this deafening cheer, yet he did.

Yes. Amidst the cheering, everyone was focused on Letty.

Only he, the one man, had turned to look back at Batbayar.

He was unaware of it, but it was a very conspicuous action. Batbayar had
deliberately incited the crowd to flush him out.

And the elderly woman sitting there was not so lenient as to miss that.

That must have been the reason for his defeat.

He hadn't known Letty. He hadn't known Dominique.

He hadn't bothered to investigate, confident that Curtis's strength was
undeniable.

And that was the fatal mistake.

\*\*\*

*Whoosh*, Dominique's wrist flickered.

What was released was a fishing hook tied to a line.

It snagged the man's hood and pulled.

"Ellie, that's him."

"Yes! Mana Bolt!"

Ellie's eyes flashed.

She instantly targeted the man's limbs and unleashed Mana Bolts.

The luminous bolts, which soared into the air to avoid the spectators,
curved sharply and rained down.

Four shots accurately pierced the man's hands and feet, immobilizing
him.

Fearing they might be caught in the sudden action, the surrounding
spectators quickly began to retreat.

"Gah!? W-Why, when, did you chant!?"

He must never have imagined the attack was from a Mana Doll. He had
believed that no human could possibly use a magic attack to so easily
pierce a demon's magical resistance with just a short incantation.

But in reality, the man was shot and fell to the ground.

"I-In that case..."

It was the moment he tried to unleash his full demon form.

"That's enough, brother."

*Tap*, a cold, heavy blade touched him.

Precisely on top of his core.

"Behave yourself, and I won't take your life."

At those words. At that blade. He realized he was checkmated.

If she truly intended to, she could shatter his core in an instant.

He was made aware of this in a startlingly real sense, and his spine
shuddered, despite her being a frail-looking, elderly woman.

It was over.

Giving up on releasing his demon form, he tried to make his core run
wild and self-destruct. He calculated that even if she pierced it, it
would explode and at least take this old woman with him.

"Ellie."

The old woman's voice. A small wave of her hand.

"Mana Blaster."

Spoken solemnly.

By the time the man fell, the surrounding people had fled, and the line
of fire was clear.

A tiny, highly focused Mana Blaster shot towards the location Dominique
indicated with the tip of her blade.

The man's core was shot through and annihilated before it could
self-destruct.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

The scheme is crushed, and all that remains is a winner who deserves to
be praised.

The audience, the king, the master, and everyone present send her their
applause.

Above all, she who believed in her more than anyone else sends her a
blessing.

Up Next: For Her, Kisses and Blessings

Into the light, she leaps forth silently.
