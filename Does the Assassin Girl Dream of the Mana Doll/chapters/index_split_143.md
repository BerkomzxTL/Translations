## Chapter 166: One Day It Will Bloom

"Hahaha, drink up! Drink up! What's this, you won't drink my alcohol!?"

—Now at the banquet held in celebration of Letty's victory.

A highly cheerful Batbayar was bothersomely mingling with his
subordinates everywhere.

However, he didn't mean it seriously, and his subordinates seemed to
know that well. Most of all, they understood the reason for his high
spirits today.

Swordsmanship tournaments in recent years had been characterized by
swordsmen in full armor stopping their feet and trading blows. It was
certainly a realistic technique.

But today's match was enough to change that perception.

“A truly exhilarating match.” That's what it must be called.

A dizzying, lightning-fast exchange that pushed the limits of human
capability, with offense and defense fiercely alternating, and a blur of
strikes.

Every moment was vivid.

*Can humans truly reach this level?*

It was a match that drove home that realization.

"Indeed, it was truly magnificent, Egret-sama."

"Ah, um, thank you..."

Letty, the guest of honor at the banquet, was constantly surrounded by
nobles, sharp-eyed merchants, and the like. Unaccustomed to such
attention, Letty was rather taciturn. Well, she was quiet to begin with.

"Haha, you were so formidable in the match, but you seem unfamiliar with
this setting. By the way, Egret-sama, my family actually has a son of
marriageable age..."

Just as he started to broach his hidden agenda, Ellie smoothly cut in
between the two.

*Smiling, smiling, smiling.* Her face was full of cheer, but for some
reason, the man who had been speaking took a step back from her
intensity.

"Fufufu, for matters like that, please go through me, her manager and
wife\\"

"W-What, m-manager? Wife??"

The man was confused by Ellie's sudden intrusion. She was certainly the
woman who presented the prize money at the awards ceremony. He
remembered her well because she was so beautiful.

But, manager aside… Wife?

Ignoring the confused man, Ellie linked her arm through Letty's.

"Honestly\\, Letty-san is completely defenseless in situations like
this\\"

"Wait, Ellie, have you been drinking a lot?"

Ellie clung to Letty, no longer paying any attention to the man, and
Letty's expression, as she gently chided her, was completely different
from a moment ago—soft and tender.

*What is going on?* As the man puzzled over this, a voice called out
from behind him.

"Ah, stop it, stop it. Don't go bothering those two."

"What, Your Majesty!? Such a pleasure to see you in such good spirits!"

The man hastily straightened his posture, and Batbayar laughed heartily,
giving the man a firm slap on the back in the process.

…The man couldn't help but cough.

"None of that stiff formality, this is a drinking party! Don't do
anything that will spoil the drinks!"

"Y-Yes, my apologies."

"See? You don't need any of that. It's an informal party!"

Batbayar continued to laugh without being angered by the man's
apologetic demeanor. Perhaps it was contagious, but the man's expression
gradually relaxed a bit.

"If His Majesty says so, then as you wish."

"Still a bit stiff, aren't you... Well, never mind! Anyway, don't mess
with those two unnecessarily. I can't guarantee your life, you know?"

"You must be joking again... or perhaps not...?"

He was a competent nobleman, so he naturally had the ability to read the
atmosphere. Now that he had regained his composure, that ability
returned.

Batbayar nodded in response to his question.

"Ah, look at them. They're more passionate than most married couples out
there. Anyone who interferes with that is asking to be kicked by a
horse, you see."

"I-I see... I understand..."

Batbayar grinned at the man as he nodded in understanding.

"And you'll also be making enemies of Big Sis Dominique, me, and Tseren,
so you'd better be careful."

"What!? Isn't that... practically a death sentence?"

Making enemies of the Whirlwind Lady, whose strength hadn’t waned, the
King, and the wildly popular Princess. If he crossed those three, he
might as well have no place in this country.

Batbayar laughed gleefully at the trembling man.

"Well, that's the kind of people they are. Besides, considering what she
accomplished, this is a small price to pay, wouldn't you say?"

"That is certainly true, Your Majesty..."

The man had been at the arena then, too. But he couldn't do anything.

In front of him, the two whirlwinds danced gracefully. Cool and
dazzling.

Now that he thought about it, his own son was certainly not a man
capable of anchoring such a storm.

"Indeed, perhaps a dancing wind is best viewed from afar."

Once he accepted it, the two of them chatting intimately looked truly
picturesque. He even wondered if this kind of relationship might be
acceptable.

\*\*\*

—On a balcony extending outside, slightly away from the murmur of the
banquet.

Dominique was drinking alone in the secluded space.

For some reason, she was in a good mood.

She wanted to savor that feeling by herself.

The moon illuminating the land of the plains country shone blue in the
clear, biting winter air, unobstructed by anything.

The heat of the day was gone... no, the sounds of the banquet could
still be heard faintly in the distance, so some lingering feeling
remained.

She drank alone, with the air and the moonlight as her companion.

*I must be quite lucky.*

It was an uncharacteristic thought.

Letty's movements that she saw during the day. She could proudly claim
that she taught those.

And she was proud of Letty for having exceeded her expectations, if only
slightly.

Her decision to take a disciple was initially a whim, a passing fancy.

But that spontaneity had led to an extraordinary connection, and it had
grown.

Yes, it had grown. It had definitely connected to something.

She truly felt that from the bottom of her heart, and it made her
inexplicably happy.

She didn't know what it would lead to, but she was sure it wasn't
anything bad.

Just by looking at her disciple, she could believe that without a doubt.

"Am I getting old, finding fulfillment in something like this? It's not
like me."

She grumbled, but she couldn't completely hide her own joyful amusement.

She must be looking forward to the future that awaits her.

She didn't know if she would be able to see it through.

"Well, she's got a solid wife now, so there'll be no mistake."

She said that and raised her cup again, just as she noticed a presence
approaching her. She swallowed the drink and turned to face them.

"Well, well, Princess Tseren, what brings you here?"

"And what brings you here alone, Dominique-sama? My father was looking
for you."

Tseren replied with a quiet laugh.

In her smile, Dominique thought she sensed a strength that was absent
when they first met.

"Haha, my apologies. I just felt like drinking alone for a bit."

"I see, so you have those moments, too."

“It’s not something I understand just yet, though,” Tseren murmured as
she walked closer to Dominique.

*Oh?* Dominique thought. Her footsteps were steady, and upon closer
look, her face wasn't red at all.

"Princess Tseren, you don't seem to have had much to drink today."

"Ah, no, today, how should I put it... I have a request for you,
Dominique-sama. I decided to hold off until I made it."

Tseren turned to face Dominique as if she had made up her mind.

Since it seemed to be a serious matter, Dominique waited quietly without
making any teasing remarks.

*Sigh*, she took a deep breath.

Looking straight into Dominique's eyes.

"Dominique-sama. Would you take me on as an apprentice?"

Dominique raised an eyebrow at the request.

She scrutinized the Princess for a moment, but the expression turned
toward her was utterly serious.

"Hoh… It seems you're not joking... But why?"

"Yes, well, I had actually been considering this for a while. That I
lack the strength to stand on my own. And learning the sword is one
means to achieve that."

It was perfectly reasonable. After all, she was royalty; it was always
better to have the means to protect herself. But if that was the case,
learning from just anyone would suffice.

"However, after seeing Egret-sama today, I changed my mind. I want to be
strong like that, too. Even if it's impossible to be exactly the same, I
want to get as close as possible. For that, I believe being mentored by
you, Dominique-sama, is the best path."

Letty today was strong. And she was dazzling.

Her fighting, which must have captivated much of the audience, had also
deeply touched Tseren. In fact, perhaps seeing Letty up close had made
the impact even deeper. She realized that Letty, who was not
particularly blessed with size or strength compared to herself, could
achieve such feats by concentrating the essence of technique.

It was exhilarating. It was vivid. And she found herself yearning for
it.

"If you aim to be like her, I wouldn't recommend it. She’s at a level
that only someone who can repeat the same thing for many hours every day
can reach."

"Does that mean I can get close through repetition?"

"Even that, I can't guarantee. It might only consume your valuable time,
Princess Tseren."

Dominique smiled faintly at the words that were humble yet utterly
proactive.

She didn't know it herself, but she didn't seem to dislike this kind of
attitude.

And in response to Dominique's advice, Tseren also smiled.

"So you won't say it's impossible, either. In that case, I want to aim
for the possibility of being able to do it. That is the me I want to
become."

She stated this clearly.

And to Dominique, that was dazzling.

And very agreeable.

"Very well. If you insist, I have no objection. I shall teach you
properly!"

With renewed resolve, Dominique bared a decisive smile.

\*\*\*

In later years, the sight of women studying swordsmanship would become
commonplace in Cordor.

One catalyst was Letty's success. The other was... a few years later,
Princess Tseren's appearance in the swordsmanship tournament, where she
put up a good fight.

Women, inspired by their figures, took up the sword and began to learn.

In a culture where the spirit of self-reliance was strong and women had
to survive on their own if necessary, perhaps learning swordsmanship was
simply natural.

As time progressed, most women would pick up a sword at least once.

Later history would record that the greatest contributor to ensuring
this trend was not a passing fad, organizing and transmitting the system
of techniques in an easily understandable way to make swordsmanship
accessible, was the woman called the 'Whirlwind Lady.'
