## Chapter 160: Stopping, Flowing Time

The man began to move as if a switch had been flicked. She could feel,
without needing to be told, that this was his full strength, that he was
coming at her with serious intent.

And he stepped in.

At a speed she had seen once before. But with even greater intensity.

This was the man's full power. The true intent shown by the man who had
attacked relentlessly but failed to break through, and ultimately lost
his life.

It was a strike that left onlookers behind.

Only two people in this arena saw it: Dominique and Letty. They saw the
start of the movement and the trajectory the sword would trace.

*Riiing...* A sound like a ringing bell. It was a sound created by
blades that did not clash but rather were deflected perfectly. The
shortsword, smoothly inserted into the longsword's trajectory,
dissipated its force.

The longsword, having met almost no resistance, struck the ground.

**“Tch!”**

The moment he clicked his tongue, the sword sprang upward. The blade,
aimed at Letty's feet, could not catch her, as she was already moving
while parrying. He closed the distance toward where she had moved and
thrust forward, but it was effortlessly batted aside.

He used brute force to stop the sideways-moving sword and, stepping in
further, swung it horizontally. But Letty’s feet hadn’t stopped moving
for even a moment. She had already vanished from that spot.

He pulled his swinging arm back and thrust at her as she was once again
caught in his sight, but that, too, was easily evaded, and she slipped
away to the side once more.

**"Damn it! Quite scurrying around!"**

"But it would hurt if it hit me, wouldn’t it?"

Uttering this light remark, Letty fluidly drifted sideways again. The
blade passed harmlessly through the space she had occupied just an
instant—no, a *half*-instant—before.

The audience went wild with excitement over this sequence of offense and
defense. After all, there was the attack of the bestial, terrifying man,
whose speed was difficult to follow with the eyes, and Letty, who calmly
and gracefully parried and evaded it. There was a rhythm between them
that was harmonious, like music.

Probably, only one person was truly frustrated at this moment. No, two
people. And that frustration only accelerated the tempo.

A strike from overhead like thunder. Yet, it only made a flashy sound
like a cymbal in a symphony; masterfully avoided.

He sprang the blade that had hit the ground back up and swung it
horizontally once more. It was lightly received, its momentum utilized,
and he was leaped over. He gnashed his teeth at the utter lack of
resistance.

He chased after her, attempting small strikes at her wrist, neck, and
torso, but all his reduced-power attacks were deflected with light
sounds. Attempting slashes, sweeps… His fastest thrusts after feints
were completely read and dodged with footwork alone.

The man was impatient. He was getting frustrated. He was irritated. He,
who should have been able to finish her in one or two strikes, was being
slipped past again and again. Thinking back, she had evaded him then,
too. But was it this effortlessly?

A question surfaced, and the man's movement stalled. The gaze that was
calmly observing him was infuriating.

During this brief standoff—no, this period where one was glaring and the
other was observing calmly—Letty suddenly spoke, "Yes, just as I
thought. You are the same person you were back then."

He assumed his identity was already known. But if so, what was the
meaning of her words? His frustration accelerated, and his voice
transformed into a snarl. **"Huh? What are you talking about now?"**

“I'll tell you something.” Letty's expression toward the man was placid.
Not cold like ice. But quiet, like a still body of water. "You cannot
defeat me. Because you are the same as you were back then. You haven't
moved on."

She stated this not as an argument or a boast, but with a confident
certainty that was deeply humiliating to the man.

**"You've got some nerve... saying that to me!!"**

A furious roar, sharp enough to tear ears, echoed, unleashing a killing
intent that alone could murder a person. Unrest rose from the audience
at the sheer intensity, causing a disruption. But Letty, the direct
target, remained calm.

"Yes, I said it. If you want to deny it, come at me. I'll show you."

She extended her left hand and beckoned him over. The provocation worked
instantly.

**"I'll make sure you never open that mouth again!!"**

The enraged man charged with greater force than before.

However.

That also meant his movements were easier to read than before.

Parrying the storm of attacks with the same calm expression, Letty
recalled Dominique's words from the other day.

\*\*\*

"After all, that guy... he looks like a tiger, but he's not a tiger."

"Um, I don't really understand what you mean..."

Letty spoke for everyone in the room, asking the question on their
minds. It was a reasonable question, but Dominique broke out into a
grin.

"Well... do you know why a tiger is strong?"

Letty tilted her head. "Huh? Uh... why, I mean, isn't it naturally
strong...?"

Dominique shook her head in return.

"That's a bit off. Only the strong ones survive. That's why they *look*
strong. And the strong ones don't just rely on their innate abilities.
I've seen a tiger hunt, and their movements were the result of
accumulated ingenuity and refinement. Conversely, those who neglect that
don't survive. That's the kind of world it is."

After saying that much, she looked toward the arena again. The man was
long gone, but his movements were still vivid in her memory.

"That guy is just an unfortunate tiger given food in a cage. He's
probably never once thought he might die. He's only ever dealt with
people he could scatter with a single swing. If anything, the time he
fought you, Egret, would be the exception... but even then, he probably
didn't think he would die until the very end."

"Now that you mention it, that might be true..."

She recalled that time. He was defiant until the very last moment. He
was arrogant enough not to consider his own death. Thanks to that, she
was able to use a trick on him.

"That's why his movements are sloppy. Because he never needed to polish
them. From my perspective, they're awful. Although he can cover it up
with speed and strength."

"I see...?"

Now that she mentioned it, she realized there was some truth in her
words. Though it had only been a slight feeling of incongruity.

"You should be able to see the sloppy parts of his movements now. Watch
his matches for a while. You’ll see."

Dominique stated this with confidence.

\*\*\*

And it was true.

The current Letty could see the sloppiness in his movements. The
movements that were nearly overwhelming, the sword attacks that were
almost too much to handle. And she could see how to evade them, how to
deflect them.

His frustration grew, and his movements became easier to read. For the
first time, truly the first time, facing an opponent who was his equal
or superior, he was left hopelessly flustered. After all, even though he
was defeated by a trick, Letty back then had definitely been beneath
him.

**"Why! Why won't it hit!"**

The strike swung with his scream was terrifyingly fast and strong... but
it was sloppy. Such an attack could no longer even graze Letty.

*Thud*, a small footfall. Letty, having gained the distance she wanted,
slowly readopted her stance with her shortsword.

"Why, indeed? I won't tell you the answer, though."

Quietly, quietly. Yet, there was a flame burning silently deep within
her heart. That flame urged Letty on.

"Instead, I'll teach you something… defeat."

She stated this smoothly.

And finally, Letty shifted to offense.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

And she attacks.

The relentless sequence of strikes is like a tsunami.

For the first time, the man feels the overwhelming pressure of an
opponent who is coming only for him, threatening to sweep him away.

*Impossible*, the man mutters.
