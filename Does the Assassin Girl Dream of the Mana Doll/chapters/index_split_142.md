## Chapter 165: For Her, Kisses and Blessings

"Victor, step forward!!"

Batbayar's voice rang out clearly from the platform.

Following the voice, Letty stepped forward with quiet steps.

It was once the commotion settled down—

Right after the incident in the arena, there was also the disturbance of
dispatching the hooded man who showed hostility toward the King, leading
to a discussion on how to proceed with the awards ceremony. There were
concerns about the possibility of further attacks.

"No, after seeing *that* display, do you seriously think anyone else
would dare try anything?"

Batbayar dismissed the fears, gesturing toward Dominique. Furthermore,
the audience hadn't gone home, still eager to see the awards ceremony,
and even those who had evacuated were returning. There was concern that
canceling now would deeply disappoint the citizens.

As a result, they frantically cleaned up, put together the bare minimum
for appearance, and pushed ahead with the awards ceremony.

It was, unfortunately, the shabbiest-looking of awards ceremonies. At
least, in Batbayar's experience.

But it was also the most riveting.

Not only the audience but even the guards were wearing joyful
expressions.

*My own face must look similar*, Batbayar murmured to himself.

Only one person in the venue did not have a cheerful expression.

"Hey, hey, the winner shouldn't be making a face like that," Batbayar
teased Letty with a laugh when he saw her face as she ascended the
stage.

In response, Letty could only give him a look of confusion. "...But I'm
not used to this kind of attention..."

It had been a huge commotion when she defeated Curtis. However, the
cheers now being directed at Letty as she stood before Batbayar were
even louder. Letty didn't know what was driving them.

"Well, you'll get used to it, so don't worry. Now then, let's start the
ceremony."

"Eh, no, wait a minute... O-Okay."

Flustered by Batbayar's casualness, she knelt before him as they had
planned beforehand.

And, sensing that the ceremony was about to begin, the audience suddenly
quieted.

…Letty seemed bewildered by even this.

"We shall now conduct the ceremony to honor the champion of the Cordor
Swordsmanship Tournament!!"

A loud cheer erupted, but when Batbayar raised his hand slightly, the
noise instantly subsided.

It was as if they had been trained. In fact, for spectators who saw this
every year, they probably were trained.

"Swordswoman Egret! You crushed your opponents from the preliminaries
and claimed victory with magnificent sword skills! Your performance in
the final match, in particular, was truly splendid!"

Batbayar paused, raised his face, and looked around at the audience.

He took a breath.

"Everyone! I have never seen a better match than that! What do you all
say!!"

Batbayar's question was met with a massive roar: "It was the best!" "You
were amazing!" "So cool!"

Such cries could be heard from mouths all over. ...Perhaps the
higher-pitched cheers were slightly more numerous.

"Exactly! I feel exactly the same way! I am proud to be able to honor
such a champion!!"

Cheers erupted a third time, and Batbayar, who listened to the pleasant
noise for a while, again raised his hand to silence the crowd once the
excitement subsided.

"Furthermore, following her Master Dominique, she is the second woman to
win the championship. This is also splendid! This must be the result of
your tireless efforts under your Master's tutelage! You should be
proud!"

“Oh!” A murmur rose up.

Especially among the elderly who had seen Dominique's matches, voices
were heard saying, "She's her apprentice!" and "The disciple of the
'Whirlwind Lady'!"

Young people who didn't know the name looked puzzled, and old folks gave
them an exaggerated, "Later," implying a long story.

"Haha! The old men and women remember! Yes, she is the disciple of the
Whirlwind Lady! Well, isn't this a fine thing to see before you go to
the grave?"

Laughter broke out, followed by various energetic replies; "Don't kill
us off so soon!" "We're not done yet!"

Batbayar took in their joyful voices, and after another short pause:

"That's right, don't you dare die yet. There are still many good things
I need you all to see!"

The joking words were mixed with laughter and a few slightly tearful
voices. A truly joyful and peaceful atmosphere flowed between Batbayar
and the audience.

"Now, Champion Egret! To honor your achievement, I bestow upon you this
sword!!"

With that cue, Tseren, who was waiting behind, stepped forward, holding
a sword in both hands as an offering.

Seeing the sword being presented, some of the spectators began to buzz.

The sword Tseren carried was a slightly curved, single-edged shortsword.
It resembled Letty's favorite weapon, with a blade slightly longer than
hers.

The swords usually given to the winners of this tournament were almost
always long, straight swords.

"Oh, the tournament veterans here have sharp eyes! Yes, the sword forged
this time is in this shape! Honestly, when I first saw it, I thought the
old man had finally gone senile!"

Batbayar's comment elicited a burst of laughter.

The sword given to the champion was forged using a special method called
the cold forging process. Normally, a block of iron was heated and
hammered into shape... but this method, as the name suggested, involved
hammering the iron while it was cold. It was said that while this method
takes longer, it results in a sword of superior quality, with both
flexibility and strength.

The sword presented to the champion was said to be hammered out by a
master blacksmith over the course of a year. He boasted that the shape
of the blade was made according to a 'divine inspiration' he received at
the beginning of the year.

"Well, looking at the result, the old man wasn't senile after all; he
hit the jackpot! Oh, and while we're at it, let's give the old man a
word of appreciation!!"

A variety of warm voices flew toward the elderly blacksmith standing by
the stage, causing him to narrow his eyes in delight.

"Now, Egret. Draw the sword and raise it high! Show the heavens your
victory!!"

Letty nodded, and smoothly drew the sword.

She thrust the slightly bluish, deep-shining blade straight up toward
the sky.

Bathed in the clear azure, the unobstructed sunlight, the blade
glittered brilliantly.

"Let the Gods in Heaven bear witness! Here stands the greatest
swordswoman under the sun!!"

With those words, a massive cheer, accompanied by thunderous applause,
rang out once more.

And they continued to ring.

After thoroughly basking in the applause, Batbayar raised both hands to
stop the cheering, and Letty put the sword back in its sheath.

"Now, the report to the Gods is over, and it's time for some hard,
worldly business! To the Champion Egret, who achieved a glorious
victory, I award the prize money!"

As he said that, there was some quiet chuckling in the audience.

Following Batbayar's cue, a woman stepped forward, carrying a bag filled
with prize money.

"...Eh."

Yes, it was Ellie.

Seeing Ellie approach with a beaming smile, Letty looked at Batbayar
with a look that seemed to say, *I wasn't told about this*. But Batbayar
just gave her a knowing smirk.

"Well, I thought this was a perfect opportunity to have her do it. Come
on, you're going to split it anyway, aren't you?"

"Well, yes, but..."

To be precise, while the ownership was split, Ellie carried most of it
due to the weight. In any case, it was simpler if Ellie handled it.

"Now, to the victor, the honor! ...And a blessing!"

The bag of prize money was presented… a rather crude interpretation of
'honor.'

However, as Letty pondered inwardly, *A blessing? I didn't hear about
that*, Ellie, who had already handed over the bag, wrapped her arms
around Letty's neck and pulled her close.

Before she could realize what was happening—

*Chup*, a soft sensation touched Letty's cheek.

Low cheers of "Ooh..." and some high-pitched shrieks could be heard from
the audience.

When Letty, her face bright red, looked at Ellie, the latter was smiling
widely, her eyes wet with emotion.

Seeing that expression, Letty couldn't utter a word of complaint.

"Now, everyone! Celebrate! Praise her! The Swordswoman Egret! She who
claimed a magnificent victory!!"

A cheer of blessings was sent to Letty as she was being hugged by the
happily smiling Ellie.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

The fervor of the tournament ends, and life moves toward the ordinary.

It is the same as the days before. Or perhaps there are some
differences.

Setting off once again, towards the other side of the intersection.
