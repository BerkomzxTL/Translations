Nevertheless, thinking of them as deep-sea creatures, this appearance was understandable in a way.

*"The dark world where even starlight does not reach... I hear that we are those who sought light and lowered our tentacles into the shallows, and then onto the land. It seems we have branched out considerably over the long years, and not a few have lost their intellect... but it is said that some of the creatures on this planet share the same roots as us if you trace them back."*

"Roots...?"

*"Originally, we were creatures with even more uncertain bodies... something closer to amoebas, and it's said we changed our bodies based on the creatures we preyed upon... some scholars even say that tentacle genes like ours might be mixed into mammals and humans depending on the case."*

It was a truth of the world she would have preferred not to know.

Regardless of Selene, who could not fully process the shocking fact that she was part tentacle, Nyururun vibrated his tentacle and continued.

*"Well, setting aside such academic theories... the primary reason our ancestors left the side of the Mother Goddess and traveled through space long ago was for paradise."*

"...Paradise?"

*"Yes. While almost everything was fulfilled by the Mother Goddess's side, only the neighbors who would take tentacle with tentacle were missing. It is said that the tentacles, seeking even greater happiness, decided to set out in search of a new paradise and scattered into the vast void."*

While swaying his tentacle, he looked up at the moon floating in the night sky.

*"However, over the long years, to join tentacle with tentacle... although they left home for that purpose, many tentacles that landed on this ground have forgotten it. Days of being exterminated as demons, ghosts, or monsters, or being preyed upon by beasts and living in fear. ...It is said that at one point, there were tentacles that challenged people to battle under the guise of protecting tentacles, but they too were defeated... and the one now known as Yamata-no-Orochi was also one such tentacle."*

What Selene imagined, while her cheek twitched, was a hill-like tentacle.

She thought, *I see, it's not an impossible story depending on how you look at it.*

*"A good tentacle who lives out its life in a harsh world is invited to paradise... in modern times, that has changed into a belief in a paradise after death. I often heard such stories from my parents when I was young."*

Nyururun covered his round eye with his eyelid, making his moist eye sparkle.

He looked up at the moon, as if reminiscing.

*"If I endured difficult days, someday I would reach paradise... on such a moonlit night when I was thinking that, it was Krische-sama who reached out her hand and took tentacle with me. And Bery-sama, the Queen... and Selene-sama, you."*

Plum petals fluttered glitteringly toward Nyururun as he gazed at Selene, and the faintly echoing melody of the flute changed.

Looking up suddenly, she saw countless tentacles with similarly moist eyes peeking down at her; as soon as their gazes met, they immediately hid behind the shadow of the roof.

She wanted to make some sort of retort to those tentacles, but basically, they were dead serious.

Knowing that this performance and atmosphere-building were not out of any ill intent, she found it hard to say anything.

*"That encounter was the final destination of our journey that our ancestors wished for long ago. I came to understand that the tentacles' paradise, Nyuruslen, was not a place or a paradise after death, but the encounter with Selene-sama and the others itself."*

The round eye was moist and teary, directed straight at Selene.

Pressed by that pressure, Selene leaned back slightly.

*“The kind-hearted Krische-sama, Bery-sama, who has abandoned past grudges and given us so much, Her Majesty the Queen, who taught us the joy of serving others, and… Selene-sama, who is sometimes strict but always shows us the right path… All of you are Nyuruslen. All of you, the tentacles believe in.”*

“Ah, I see…”

As Nyururun brought his face closer, Selene leaned back so much her waist arched.

The tentacle swaying in front of her eyes in excitement was indeed intense.

*"If this drama is a story that connects tentacles, fairies, and humans... a depiction of Nyuruslen, then Selene-sama is absolutely necessary. No matter how great an actress someone is, to me, to the tentacles, there is no substitute for Selene-sama."*

"U-um, Nyururun..."

While skillfully maintaining the tray, Nyururun bowed his head (※Note: He only has a head).

*"Please, this is a once-in-a-lifetime request. Could you please accept the role in the drama? If necessary, all the tentacles will support Selene-sama as much as possible."*

"Ugh..."

Countless tentacles slithered down from the roof, surrounding Selene and bowing their heads (※Note: They only have heads).

Surrounded by such tentacles, Selene shifted her gaze, pressed her forehead, and sighed.

After a moment of silence, she shook her head as if giving up.

"...Fine, fine, I get it. In exchange, you're the ones who'll regret it when my acting is as wooden as a board, okay? You understand?"

At those words, Nyururun suddenly raised his head (※Note: He only has a head), looked at the other tentacles, and vibrated his tentacle violently.

The tentacles began to dance a dance of joy, writhing and wriggling, and as she watched them, Selene sighed again as if exasperated and exhausted.

"Really. Don't say I didn't warn you."

And, while saying it, she smiled wryly.
